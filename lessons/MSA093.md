# Day 93 - Intensive listening to Arabic news (Transcription Day 3)

## Lesson

Today, your mission is to tackle slightly longer and more grammatically intertwined sentences. The goal remains the same: to sharpen your ear by "listening" (reading and comprehending) these news-style sentences and actively transcribing them. This intensive practice is key to developing your fluency and confidence in understanding Arabic.

**How to approach transcription:**

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly, multiple times.** Imagine it being spoken naturally and clearly. Try to pronounce it aloud as accurately as you can, even if just softly.
2.  **Try to identify each word.** Break the sentence into smaller chunks if necessary. Look for known verbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and noun phrases.
3.  **Pay close attention to case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) and Sukoon.** These subtle markings carry significant grammatical information that helps you understand who is doing what to whom, or how words relate in a sentence.
4.  **Write down what you hear (or read).** Focus on getting the Harakat (vowel markings) correct for each word.
5.  **Then, compare your transcription with the correct version and explanation.** Reflect on any differences and why they occurred. This self-correction is where the real learning happens!

Remember, this isn't a test for a perfect score, but an exercise to improve your recognition skills. The more you practice, the faster your brain will process these forms and the clearer Arabic will become to you.

## Examples

Here is a complex news-style sentence. Read it, imagine hearing it, and try to identify each word and its ending.

**Arabic Text:**

`يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ مُحَمَّدًا يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَعُودَ إِلَى بَلَدِهِ حَتَّى يَحُلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْمَفْهُومَةَ.`

*(Take your time to read and identify the words and their endings. When you are ready, continue below for the breakdown.)*

---

**Breakdown and Explanation:**

The sentence is: `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ مُحَمَّدًا يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَعُودَ إِلَى بَلَدِهِ حَتَّى يَحُلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْمَفْهُومَةَ.`

Let's break it down word by word, identifying each one and its grammatical features:

1.  **`يَبْدُو` (yabdū):**
    *   **Word:** `يَبْدُو` (it seems / it appears).
    *   **Type:** Present tense verb (Day 80).
    *   **Meaning:** It seems.

2.  **`أَنَّ` (anna):**
    *   **Word:** `أَنَّ` (that).
    *   **Type:** Particle introducing a nominal clause.
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the subject of the following clause take Fatha (Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** that.

3.  **`الْوَزِيرَ` (al-wazeerā):**
    *   **Word:** `وَزِيرٌ` (minister - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) because it's the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause (Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** the minister.

4.  **`مُحَمَّدًا` (Muḥammadān):**
    *   **Word:** `مُحَمَّدٌ` (Muhammad - proper noun).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (proper noun).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًا`) because it's in apposition to `الْوَزِيرَ` (Day 85).
    *   **Meaning:** Muhammad.

5.  **`يُرِيدُ` (yureedū):**
    *   **Word:** `يُرِيدُ` (he wants).
    *   **Type:** Present tense verb (Day 44).
    *   **Meaning:** he wants.

6.  **`أَنْ` (an):**
    *   **Word:** `أَنْ` (to / that).
    *   **Type:** Particle introducing a subjunctive verb.
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the following present tense verb take Fatha (Subjunctive mood) (Day 60).
    *   **Meaning:** to.

7.  **`يَعُودَ` (ya'oodā):**
    *   **Word:** `يَعُودُ` (he returns).
    *   **Conjugation:** Present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha - `ـَ`) because of `أَنْ` (Day 78).
    *   **Meaning:** return.

8.  **`إِلَى` (ilā):**
    *   **Word:** `إِلَى` (to / towards).
    *   **Type:** Preposition.
    *   **Meaning:** to.

9.  **`بَلَدِهِ` (baladihi):**
    *   **Word:** `بَلَدٌ` (country) + `ـهِ` (his).
    *   **Grammar:** Idafa (Day 7), `بَلَدِ` takes Kasra after `إِلَى`.
    *   **Meaning:** his country.

10. **`حَتَّى` (ḥattā):**
    *   **Word:** `حَتَّى` (in order to / so that).
    *   **Type:** Conjunctive particle indicating purpose.
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the following present tense verb take Fatha (Subjunctive mood) (Day 86).
    *   **Meaning:** in order to.

11. **`يَحُلَّ` (yaḥullā):**
    *   **Word:** `يَحُلُّ` (he solves).
    *   **Conjugation:** Present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha - `ـَ`) because of `حَتَّى`. (`يَحُلُّ` is a geminated verb, so its Sukoon becomes Fatha) (Day 50).
    *   **Meaning:** solve.

12. **`الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (al-mushkilatā):**
    *   **Word:** `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the direct object of `يَحُلَّ` (Day 50).
    *   **Meaning:** the problem.

13. **`الْمَفْهُومَةَ` (al-mafhūmatā):**
    *   **Word:** `مَفْهُومَةٌ` (understood - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`), passive participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (Day 88).
    *   **Meaning:** the understood.

**Full Sentence Meaning:** It seems that the minister, Muhammad, wants to return to his country in order to solve the understood problem.
*   *Grammar Connections:* This sentence beautifully combines hedging language, apposition, motion verbs, purpose clauses, and participial modifiers, showing how many different rules can come together in a single, coherent statement.

## Practice

For each practice item below, read the Arabic sentence multiple times, imagine hearing it, then try to identify each word and its exact case or mood ending. Write it down, and then check your answer!

1.  **Arabic Text:** `وَمَعَ أَنَّ السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مُهِمَّةٌ، لَا يَعْتَقِدُ الْمُدِيرُ الْقَدِيمُ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ مَحْلُولَةٌ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `وَمَعَ` (wa-ma'a): Conjunction `وَ` (and) + Preposition `مَعَ` (with).
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): Particle (that), makes subject take Fatha (Day 39).
        *   `السِّيَاسَةَ` (as-siyāsatā): `سِيَاسَةٌ` (policy), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as subject of `أَنَّ` clause (Day 54).
        *   `الْجَدِيدَةَ` (al-jadeedatā): `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new), definite, Accusative (Fatha), adjective agreeing with `السِّيَاسَةَ` (Day 27).
        *   `مُهِمَّةٌ` (muhimmatun): `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important), indefinite, Nominative (Damma) as comment for `السِّيَاسَةَ` (Day 27).
        *   `لَا` (lā): Negation particle for present tense (Day 21).
        *   `يَعْتَقِدُ` (ya'taqidū): `يَعْتَقِدُ` (he believes), present tense verb (Day 64).
        *   `الْمُدِيرُ` (al-mudeerū): `مُدِيرٌ` (manager), definite, Nominative (Damma) as subject of `يَعْتَقِدُ` (Day 79).
        *   `الْقَدِيمُ` (al-qadeemū): `قَدِيمٌ` (old), definite, Nominative (Damma), adjective agreeing with `الْمُدِيرُ` (Day 27).
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): Particle (that), makes subject take Fatha (Day 39).
        *   `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (al-mushkilatā): `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as subject of `أَنَّ` clause (Day 50).
        *   `مَحْلُولَةٌ` (maḥloolatun): `مَحْلُولَةٌ` (solved), indefinite, Nominative (Damma), passive participle acting as comment for `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (Day 88).
        *   **Translation:** Although the new policy is important, the old manager does not believe that the problem is solved.

2.  **Arabic Text:** `سَأَلَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ سَارَةُ الْمُدَرِّسَ عَلِيًّا عَنِ الرِّسَالَةِ الْمَكْتُوبَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `سَأَلَتِ` (sa'alatī): `سَأَلَ` (he asked) + `ـتْ` (she), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 79).
        *   `الطَّالِبَةُ` (aṭ-ṭālibatū): `طَالِبَةٌ` (student - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as subject.
        *   `سَارَةُ` (Sāratū): `سَارَةُ` (Sarah - proper noun), definite, Nominative (Damma), appositive to `الطَّالِبَةُ` (Day 85). (Note: `سَارَةُ` is a diptote, so it takes Damma, Fatha, or Fatha, not Kasra in the genitive.)
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسَ` (al-mudarrisā): `مُدَرِّسٌ` (teacher - m.), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as direct object.
        *   `عَلِيًّا` (ʿaliyyān): `عَلِيٌّ` (Ali - proper noun), definite, Accusative (Fatha), appositive to `الْمُدَرِّسَ` (Day 85).
        *   `عَنِ` (ʿanī): Preposition `عَنْ` (about), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 79).
        *   `الرِّسَالَةِ` (ar-risālatī): `رِسَالَةٌ` (message), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `عَنِ` (Day 45).
        *   `الْمَكْتُوبَةِ` (al-maktūbatī): `مَكْتُوبَةٌ` (written - f.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), passive participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الرِّسَالَةِ` (Day 88).
        *   `الْجَدِيدَةِ` (al-jadeedatī): `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new - f.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), adjective agreeing with `الرِّسَالَةِ` (Day 27).
        *   **Translation:** The student, Sarah, asked the teacher, Ali, about the new written message.

3.  **Arabic Text:** `رُبَّمَا يُعْلِنُ الرَّئِيسُ مَشْرُوعًا مُشْتَرَكًا، لِذَلِكَ مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَزُورَ مِصْرَ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā): Adverb (perhaps / maybe) (Day 80).
        *   `يُعْلِنُ` (yu'linū): `يُعْلِنُ` (he announces), present tense verb (Day 64).
        *   `الرَّئِيسُ` (ar-ra'eecū): `رَئِيسٌ` (president), definite, Nominative (Damma) as subject.
        *   `مَشْرُوعًا` (mashroo'ān): `مَشْرُوعٌ` (project), indefinite, Accusative (Fatha) as direct object (Day 79).
        *   `مُشْتَرَكًا` (mushtarakān): `مُشْتَرَكٌ` (joint), indefinite, Accusative (Fatha), participial adjective agreeing with `مَشْرُوعًا` (Day 67).
        *   `لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika): Discourse marker (therefore) (Day 69).
        *   `مِنَ` (mina): Preposition `مِنْ` (from), takes Kasra (Day 69).
        *   `الْمُحْتَمَلِ` (al-muḥtamalī): `مُحْتَمَلٌ` (probable), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `مِنَ` (Day 80).
        *   `أَنْ` (an): Particle (to/that), makes verb take Fatha (Subjunctive) (Day 60).
        *   `يَزُورَ` (yazoorā): `يَزُورُ` (he visits), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `أَنْ` (Day 87).
        *   `مِصْرَ` (Miṣrā): `مِصْرٌ` (Egypt), takes Fatha as a diptote, in Accusative case as direct object of `يَزُورَ` (Day 87).
        *   **Translation:** Perhaps the president will announce a joint project, therefore it is probable that he will visit Egypt.
