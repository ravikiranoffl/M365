# Day 47 - Writing practice

## Lesson

Today, your main task is to **write in Arabic**. Writing helps you remember vocabulary, practice connecting letters, correctly place Harakat, and apply all the grammar rules you've learned.

**How to approach this lesson:**

1.  **Get ready:** Grab a pen and paper, or open a document on your computer where you can type in Arabic.
2.  **Focus on accuracy:** Try to write each word correctly, paying attention to:
    *   **Letter shapes:** How letters connect.
    *   **Harakat:** The small vowels (Fatha, Kasra, Damma, Sukoon, Shaddah). These are very important for meaning and pronunciation!
    *   **Grammar rules:** Making sure nouns have the right case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra, or their Tanween forms), verbs are conjugated correctly, and adjectives match their nouns.
3.  **Don't rush:** Take your time with each word and sentence. It's okay to go back and check your notes or previous lessons!

I will give you a few sentences in English, and your job is to translate and write them in complete Arabic, including all the Harakat.

## Examples

Let's do a couple of examples together first, so you see what I expect.

**Example 1: "The student is smart."**

*   First, think about the words: `الطَّالِبُ` (the student), `ذَكِيٌّ` (smart).
*   Then, combine them, remembering this is an "X is Y" sentence where the topic is definite and the comment is indefinite:
*   **Your writing should look like this:** `الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu dhakiyyun.)

**Example 2: "He reads the book now."**

*   Words: `هُوَ` (he), `يَقْرَأُ` (he reads), `الْكِتَابَ` (the book - object, takes Fatha), `الْآنَ` (now).
*   Combine:
*   **Your writing should look like this:** `هُوَ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ الْآنَ.` (Huwa yaqra'u al-kitāba al-āna.)

## Practice
