# Day 65 - Listening practice test

## Lesson

A listening test helps you recognize words and structures quickly when you hear them, even if you don't see the text. Since I cannot provide actual audio in this format, we will simulate a listening test.

**How this will work:**

1.  **I will "speak" an Arabic sentence or short phrase.** In a real listening test, this would be an audio recording. Here, I will write `[Arabic audio will play here]` to indicate where the audio would be.
2.  **I will then ask you questions about what you "heard."** You should try to answer based on your understanding, without seeing the Arabic text.
3.  **After you provide your answer, I will reveal the actual Arabic text (the "script")** so you can check your understanding and see the Harakat and spellings. I will also provide the correct answer and a brief explanation.

This is a great way to practice active listening and comprehension. Don't worry if you don't get everything right the first time; the goal is to practice and improve!

Ready? Let's start listening!

## Practice

For each practice item:
1.  Read the `[Arabic audio will play here]` prompt.
2.  Imagine hearing the sentence.
3.  Answer the question.
4.  Then I will reveal the script and the correct answer.

**Practice Item 1:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** What did he not write?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`لَمْ يَكْتُبْ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Lam yaktub ar-risālata.)

**Correct Answer:** He did not write the message.
*   *Explanation:* `لَمْ` (did not) made `يَكْتُبُ` (he writes) become `يَكْتُبْ` (Jussive with Sukoon). `الرِّسَالَةَ` (the message) is the direct object.

**Practice Item 2:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** Who calls for peace?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`الرَّئِيسُ يَدْعُو إِلَى السَّلَامِ.` (Ar-ra'eecu yad'ū ilā as-salāmi.)

**Correct Answer:** The president calls for peace.
*   *Explanation:* `الرَّئِيسُ` (the president) is the subject. `يَدْعُو إِلَى` (he calls for) is the verb and preposition. `السَّلَامِ` (peace) takes Kasra after `إِلَى`.

**Practice Item 3:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** What is not big?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`لَيْسَتِ الْمَدْرَسَةُ كَبِيرَةً.` (Laysati al-madrasatu kabeeratan.)

**Correct Answer:** The school is not big.
*   *Explanation:* `لَيْسَتْ` (she is not) is used because `الْمَدْرَسَةُ` (the school) is feminine. `كَبِيرَةً` (big) takes Tanween Fatha as the predicate of `لَيْسَ`.

**Practice Item 4:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** Where are the new numbers?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`الْأَرْقَامُ الْجَدِيدَةُ عَلَى الشَّاشَةِ.` (Al-arqāmu al-jadeedatū 'alā ash-shāshati.)

**Correct Answer:** The new numbers are on the screen.
*   *Explanation:* `الْأَرْقَامُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` (the new numbers - non-human plural, adjective is feminine singular). `عَلَى` (on) is a preposition, `الشَّاشَةِ` (the screen) takes Kasra.

**Practice Item 5:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** What does the company announce about the environment?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`الشَّرِكَةُ تُعْلِنُ أَنَّهَا سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Ash-sharikatū tu'linu annahā sa-taḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.)

**Correct Answer:** The company announces that it will protect the environment.
*   *Explanation:* `الشَّرِكَةُ تُعْلِنُ` (the company announces). `أَنَّهَا` (that it - referring to the company). `سَتَحْفَظُ` (she will protect - future tense). `الْبِيئَةَ` (the environment - object).

**Practice Item 6:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** What is known about health?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الصِّحَّةَ مُهِمَّةٌ لِلْإِنْسَانِ.` (Mina al-ma'rūfi anna aṣ-ṣiḥḥata muhimmatun lil-insāni.)

**Correct Answer:** It is known that health is important for humans.
*   *Explanation:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (it is known that). `الصِّحَّةَ` (health) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`. `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important). `لِلْإِنْسَانِ` (for humans - `لِـ` + `الْإِنْسَانِ`).

**Practice Item 7:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** Who will not fail the exam?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`الطَّالِبُ النَّاجِحُ لَنْ يَرْسُبَ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu an-nājiḥu lan yarsuba fī al-imtiḥāni.)

**Correct Answer:** The successful student will not fail the exam.
*   *Explanation:* `الطَّالِبُ النَّاجِحُ` (the successful student). `لَنْ` (will not) makes `يَرْسُبَ` (he fails) Subjunctive (Fatha). `فِي الِامْتِحَانِ` (in the exam).

**Practice Item 8:**

`[Arabic audio will play here]`

*   **Question:** What is the student doing on the computer?

*(Pause for your answer)*

**The script for what you "heard" was:**
`الطَّالِبُ يَسْتَخْدِمُ الْحَاسُوبَ لِأَخْذِ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yastakhdimu al-ḥāsūba li'akhdhi al-ma'lūmāti.)

**Correct Answer:** The student uses the computer to receive information.
*   *Explanation:* `الطَّالِبُ يَسْتَخْدِمُ الْحَاسُوبَ` (the student uses the computer). `لِأَخْذِ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ` (in order to receive information - `لِـ` + Masdar `أَخْذِ` (receiving) + Idafa `الْمَعْلُومَاتِ`).
