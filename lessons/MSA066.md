# Day 66 - Arabic idioms

## Lesson

Idioms are common in every language, and Arabic has many beautiful and wise ones. When you understand idioms, it's a sign that you are truly starting to think in Arabic, not just translate word-for-word.

For each idiom, I will:
1.  Show you the Arabic phrase and its pronunciation.
2.  Give you the **literal translation** (what the words mean by themselves).
3.  Explain the **idiomatic meaning** (what the phrase really means).
4.  If there are any new single words within the idiom, I will introduce them first.

Let's dive into some common Arabic idioms!

### 1. `يَدٌ بِيَدٍ` (yadun bi-yadin) - Hand in Hand

*   **New Word for the idiom:**
    *   **`يَدٌ` (yadun) - Hand** (feminine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ya-dun'.
        *   *Example:* `الْيَدُ صَغِيرَةٌ.` (Al-yadu ṣagheeratun.) - The hand is small.
*   **The Idiom:** `يَدٌ بِيَدٍ` (yadun bi-yadin)
    *   *Literal translation:* "Hand with hand."
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "Hand in hand," "cooperating," "together." It means working together or being united.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `نَحْنُ نَعْمَلُ يَدًا بِيَدٍ لِحِفْظِ الْبِيئَةِ.` (Naḥnu na'malu yadan bi-yadin li-ḥifẓi al-bī'ati.)
    *   *Translation:* We work hand in hand to protect the environment.
    *   *Breakdown:* `نَعْمَلُ` (we work - from Day 29 for 'work'). `يَدًا بِيَدٍ` is the idiom. `لِحِفْظِ الْبِيئَةِ` (to protect the environment - purpose clause with Masdar and Idafa from Day 56, 64).

### 2. `عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ` (ala ra'sihi reeshatun) - He has a feather on his head

*   **New Words for the idiom:**
    *   **`رَأْسٌ` (ra'sun) - Head** (masculine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ra'-sun'.
        *   *Example:* `الرَّأْسُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Ar-ra'su kabeerun.) - The head is big.
    *   **`رِيشَةٌ` (reeshatun) - Feather** (feminine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ree-sha-tun'.
        *   *Example:* `الرِّيشَةُ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Ar-reeshatu jameelatun.) - The feather is beautiful.
*   **The Idiom:** `عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ` (ala ra'sihi reeshatun)
    *   *Literal translation:* "On his head is a feather."
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "He is arrogant," "he acts superior," "he thinks he's special." It often describes someone who is overly proud or conceited.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `لَيْسَ الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيًّا فَقَطْ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ.` (Laysa aṭ-ṭālibu dhakiyyan faqaṭ, wa-lākin 'alā ra'sihi reeshatun.)
    *   *Translation:* The student is not just smart, but he is arrogant (literally: on his head is a feather).
    *   *Breakdown:* `لَيْسَ الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيًّا` (The student is not smart - Day 61). `فَقَطْ` (only - new adverb here, let's replace to maintain strictness).
    *   *Revised Example:* `الْوَلَدُ ذَكِيٌّ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ.` (Al-waladu dhakiyyun, wa-lākin 'alā ra'sihi reeshatun.)
    *   *Revised Translation:* The boy is smart, but he is arrogant (literally: on his head is a feather).
    *   *Breakdown:* `الْوَلَدُ ذَكِيٌّ` (The boy is smart - Day 11, 37). `وَلَكِنْ` (but - Day 28). `عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ` is the idiom.

### 3. `قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ` (qalbuhu ṭayyibun) - His heart is kind

*   **New Words for the idiom:**
    *   **`قَلْبٌ` (qalb) - Heart** (masculine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'qalb'.
        *   *Example:* `الْقَلْبُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-qalbu muhimun.) - The heart is important.
    *   **`طَيِّبٌ` (ṭayyibun) - Good, Kind, Pleasant** (masculine adjective).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ṭay-yi-bun'. (Feminine: `طَيِّبَةٌ` - 'ṭay-yi-ba-tun').
        *   *Example:* `الرَّجُلُ طَيِّبٌ.` (Ar-rajulu ṭayyibun.) - The man is kind.
*   **The Idiom:** `قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ` (qalbuhu ṭayyibun)
    *   *Literal translation:* "His heart is good / kind."
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "He has a kind heart," "he is good-hearted." This describes someone who is compassionate and kind.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `الطَّبِيبُ قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ، وَهُوَ يُحِبُّ الْمَرْضَى.` (Aṭ-ṭabeebu qalbuhu ṭayyibun, wa-huwa yuḥibbu al-marḍā.)
    *   *Translation:* The doctor has a kind heart, and he loves the patients.
    *   *Breakdown:* `الطَّبِيبُ` (the doctor - Day 57). `قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ` is the idiom (`ـهُ` is 'his', Day 7). `وَهُوَ` (and he - Day 6). `يُحِبُّ` (he loves - from Day 29). `الْمَرْضَى` (the patients - plural of `مَرِيضٌ`, Day 57, takes Fatha as object).

### 4. `فِي نَفْسِ الْوَقْتِ` (fī nafsi al-waqti) - At the same time

*   **New Words for the idiom:**
    *   **`نَفْسٌ` (nafsun) - Self, Same** (feminine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'naf-sun'. (Often used in Idafa to mean 'same').
        *   *Example:* `هَذَا هُوَ نَفْسُ الْكِتَابِ.` (Hādhā huwa nafsu al-kitābi.) - This is the same book. (`نَفْسُ` is first part of Idafa).
    *   **`وَقْتٌ` (waqtun) - Time** (masculine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'waq-tun'.
        *   *Example:* `الْوَقْتُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-waqtu muhimun.) - Time is important.
*   **The Idiom:** `فِي نَفْسِ الْوَقْتِ` (fī nafsi al-waqti)
    *   *Literal translation:* "In the self of the time." (Meaning "in the very time itself").
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "At the same time." It means concurrently or simultaneously.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `هُوَ يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ وَ يَسْمَعُ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ فِي نَفْسِ الْوَقْتِ.` (Huwa yaqra'u al-akhbāra wa yasma'u al-ma'lūmāti fī nafsi al-waqti.)
    *   *Translation:* He reads the news and listens to the information at the same time.
    *   *Breakdown:* `يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ` (he reads the news - Day 45). `وَيَسْمَعُ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ` (and he listens to the information - Day 45, 55). `فِي نَفْسِ الْوَقْتِ` is the idiom. (`نَفْسِ الْوَقْتِ` is an Idafa, with `نَفْسِ` taking Kasra after `فِي`).

These idioms add a wonderful layer of meaning to your Arabic. Remember, the key is to understand their special meaning, not just the individual words!

## Examples

Let's try some more sentences using these new idioms.

1.  **"The new company works hand in hand to solve the problem of pollution."**
    *   `الشَّرِكَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ تَعْمَلُ يَدًا بِيَدٍ لِحَلِّ مُشْكِلَةِ التَّلَوُّثِ.` (Ash-sharikatū al-jadeedatū ta'malu yadan bi-yadin li-ḥalli mushkilati at-talawwuthi.)
        *   *Notice:* `تَعْمَلُ` (she works) for `الشَّرِكَةُ`. `لِحَلِّ` (to solve - Day 40, 52). `مُشْكِلَةِ التَّلَوُّثِ` (problem of pollution - Idafa chain Day 50, 56).

2.  **"The minister (f.) is smart, but she is arrogant."**
    *   `الْوَزِيرَةُ ذَكِيَّةٌ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَى رَأْسِهَا رِيشَةٌ.` (Al-wazeeratū dhakiyyatun, wa-lākin 'alā ra'sihā reeshatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْوَزِيرَةُ` (the minister - f. from Day 48). `ذَكِيَّةٌ` (smart - f.). `رَأْسِهَا` (her head - Day 7).

3.  **"We believe that the judge has a kind heart."**
    *   `نَحْنُ نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْقَاضِيَ قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ.` (Naḥnu na'taqidu anna al-qāḍiya qalbuhu ṭayyibun.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (we believe that - Day 64). `الْقَاضِيَ` (the judge) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`. `قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ` is a new nominal sentence acting as the comment for `الْقَاضِيَ`.

4.  **"The student (f.) studies the lesson and uses the computer at the same time."**
    *   `الطَّالِبَةُ تَدْرُسُ الدَّرْسَ وَ تَسْتَخْدِمُ الْحَاسُوبَ فِي نَفْسِ الْوَقْتِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibatū tadrusu ad-darsa wa tastakhdimu al-ḥāsūba fī nafsi al-waqti.)
        *   *Notice:* `تَدْرُسُ الدَّرْسَ` (she studies the lesson - Day 58). `تَسْتَخْدِمُ الْحَاسُوبَ` (she uses the computer - Day 55).

5.  **"It is known that the good doctor has a kind heart."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الطَّبِيبَ الطَّيِّبَ قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ.` (Mina al-ma'rūfi anna aṭ-ṭabeeba aṭ-ṭayyiba qalbuhu ṭayyibun.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (it is known that - Day 64). `الطَّبِيبَ الطَّيِّبَ` (the good doctor) takes Fatha because of `أَنَّ`. `قَلْبُهُ طَيِّبٌ` is the comment.
