# Day 124 - Fluency: Environment reporting

## Lesson

"Environment reporting" involves using specific vocabulary and phrases to describe the natural world, its problems, and efforts to protect it. This topic is increasingly important in global discussions, and knowing these terms will help you engage with a wide range of Arabic media and conversations.

Let's learn some new and essential vocabulary for environment reporting today!

### 1. `بِيئَةٌ` (bee'atun) - Environment

This is the fundamental term for "environment." It is a feminine noun.

*   **New Word:** `بِيئَةٌ` (bee'atun) - 'bee-a-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Environment (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْبِيئَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-bee'atu muhimmatun.) - The environment is important.

### 2. `تَلَوُّثٌ` (talawwuthun) - Pollution

This is the Masdar (verbal noun) for "pollution." It's a masculine noun.

*   **New Word:** `تَلَوُّثٌ` (talawwuthun) - 'ta-law-wu-thun'
    *   *Meaning:* Pollution (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `تَلَوُّثُ الْهَوَاءِ خَطَرٌ.` (Talawwuthu al-hawā'i khaṭarun.) - Air pollution is a danger. (We will learn `هَوَاءٌ` for "air" soon).

### 3. `مُنَاخٌ` (munākhun) - Climate

This noun refers to the prevailing weather conditions in a general area over a long period. It is a masculine noun.

*   **New Word:** `مُنَاخٌ` (munākhun) - 'mu-nā-khun'
    *   *Meaning:* Climate (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمُنَاخُ يَتَغَيَّرُ.` (Al-munākhu yataghayyaru.) - The climate is changing. (`يَتَغَيَّرُ` - he changes, from `تَغَيُّرٌ` - change, Day 113).

### 4. `يَحْمِي` (yaḥmee) / `تَحْمِي` (taḥmee) - He/She protects (Present Tense Verb)

This verb means "to protect" or "to guard." You know `يَحْفَظُ` (he protects/memorizes) from Day 112, `يَحْمِي` is another common verb for protection, especially for tangible things.

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `يَحْمِي` (yaḥmee) - 'yaḥ-mee'
    *   *Meaning:* He protects / is protecting.
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `تَحْمِي` (taḥmee) - 'taḥ-mee'
    *   *Meaning:* She protects / is protecting.
    *   *Example:* `الْحُكُومَةُ تَحْمِي الْبِيئَةَ.` (Al-ḥukūmatu taḥmee al-bee'ata.) - The government protects the environment.

### 5. `حِمَايَةٌ` (ḥimāyatun) - Protection (Masdar)

This is the verbal noun (Masdar) of `يَحْمِي`, meaning "protection." It is a feminine noun.

*   **New Word:** `حِمَايَةٌ` (ḥimāyatun) - 'ḥi-mā-ya-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Protection (feminine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `حِمَايَةُ الْبِيئَةِ وَاجِبٌ.` (Ḥimāyatu al-bee'ati wājibun.) - Environmental protection is a duty.

### 6. `مُلَوَّثٌ` (mulawwathun) / `مُلَوَّثَةٌ` (mulawwathatun) - Polluted (Adjective)

This adjective is the passive participle of the verb `لَوَّثَ` (to pollute), meaning "polluted."

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `مُلَوَّثٌ` (mulawwathun) - 'mu-law-wa-thun'
    *   *Meaning:* Polluted.
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `مُلَوَّثَةٌ` (mulawwathatun) - 'mu-law-wa-tha-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Polluted.
    *   *Example:* `الْهَوَاءُ مُلَوَّثٌ.` (Al-hawā'u mulawwathun.) - The air is polluted.

### 7. `نَظِيفٌ` (naẓeefun) / `نَظِيفَةٌ` (naẓeefatun) - Clean (Adjective)

This is a common adjective meaning "clean."

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `نَظِيفٌ` (naẓeefun) - 'na-ẓee-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* Clean.
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `نَظِيفَةٌ` (naẓeefatun) - 'na-ẓee-fa-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Clean.
    *   *Example:* `الْمَاءُ نَظِيفٌ.` (Al-mā'u naẓeefun.) - The water is clean.

### 8. `مَوَارِدُ` (mawāridu) - Resources (Plural Noun)

This noun refers to assets or supplies, especially natural resources. It is a broken plural and a **diptote** (Day 119), so it takes a Fatha instead of a Kasra when in the Genitive case and indefinite. Its adjectives will be feminine singular (Day 27).

*   **New Word:** `مَوَارِدُ` (mawāridu) - 'ma-wā-ri-du'
    *   *Meaning:* Resources (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example:* `الْمَوَارِدُ الطَّبِيعِيَّةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-mawāridu aṭ-ṭabee'iyyatū muhimmatun.) - Natural resources are important. (`طَبِيعِيَّةٌ` - natural, feminine adjective).

### 9. `مَاءٌ` (mā'un) - Water

This is the essential noun for "water." It is a masculine noun.

*   **New Word:** `مَاءٌ` (mā'un) - 'mā-un'
    *   *Meaning:* Water (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمَاءُ حَيَاةٌ.` (Al-mā'u ḥayātun.) - Water is life. (`حَيَاةٌ` - life, feminine noun, Day 106).

### 10. `هَوَاءٌ` (hawā'un) - Air

This is the essential noun for "air." It is a masculine noun.

*   **New Word:** `هَوَاءٌ` (hawā'un) - 'ha-wā-un'
    *   *Meaning:* Air (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `نُرِيدُ هَوَاءً نَظِيفًا.` (Nureedu hawā'an naẓeefan.) - We want clean air. (`نُرِيدُ` - we want, `هَوَاءً` is Accusative direct object, `نَظِيفًا` is Accusative adjective).

### 11. `أَرْضٌ` (arḍun) - Earth / Land

This noun refers to the ground, land, or our planet Earth. It is a feminine noun.

*   **New Word:** `أَرْضٌ` (arḍun) - 'ar-ḍun'
    *   *Meaning:* Earth, land (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْأَرْضُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-arḍu kabeeratun.) - The Earth is big.

### 12. `تَقْلِيلٌ` (taqleelun) - Reduction / Decrease (Masdar)

This is the verbal noun for "reduction" or "decrease." It's a masculine noun, and it comes from a Form II verb `قَلَّلَ` (to reduce), which you can infer.

*   **New Word:** `تَقْلِيلٌ` (taqleelun) - 'taq-lee-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Reduction, decrease (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `تَقْلِيلُ التَّلَوُّثِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Taqleelu at-talawwuthi muhimun.) - Reduction of pollution is important.

These terms are crucial for discussing environmental issues and solutions in Arabic.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new environment words and phrases into sentences, using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the pollution of the air is a big danger to the environment."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ تَلَوُّثَ الْهَوَاءِ خَطَرٌ كَبِيرٌ لِلْبِيئَةِ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna talawwatha al-hawā'i khaṭarun kabeerun li-l-bee'ati.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `تَلَوُّثَ الْهَوَاءِ` (new Masdar `تَلَوُّثَ`, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, first term of Idafa; new word `الْهَوَاءِ` in Genitive, Day 124). `خَطَرٌ كَبِيرٌ` (Day 105, 54). `لِلْبِيئَةِ` (new word `الْبِيئَةِ` in Genitive after `لِـ`, Day 124).

2.  **"Despite the fact that the climate is changing, the government wants to protect natural resources."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُنَاخَ يَتَغَيَّرُ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحْمِيَ الْمَوَارِدَ الطَّبِيعِيَّةَ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-munākha yataghayyaru, tureedu al-ḥukūmatu an taḥmiya al-mawārida aṭ-ṭabee'iyyata.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Day 107). `الْمُنَاخَ يَتَغَيَّرُ` (new word `الْمُنَاخَ`, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `يَتَغَيَّرُ` (he changes) from `تَغَيُّرٌ` (change) Day 113). `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحْمِيَ` (Day 98, 101; new verb `تَحْمِيَ` in Subjunctive, Day 124). `الْمَوَارِدَ الطَّبِيعِيَّةَ` (new plural `الْمَوَارِدَ`, Accusative object, Day 124; `الطَّبِيعِيَّةَ` - natural, feminine singular adjective for non-human plural, Day 27).

3.  **"The scientific report indicates a big increase in polluted water. Moreover, the experts believe that the protection of the Earth is important."**
    *   `التَّقْرِيرُ الْعِلْمِيُّ يُشِيرُ إِلَى زِيَادَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ فِي الْمَاءِ الْمُلَوَّثِ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ أَنَّ حِمَايَةَ الْأَرْضِ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (At-taqrīru al-ʿilmiyyu yusheeru ilā ziyādatin kabeeratin fī al-mā'i al-mulawwathi. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, yaʿtaqidu al-khubarā'u anna ḥimāyata al-arḍi muhimmatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `التَّقْرِيرُ الْعِلْمِيُّ يُشِيرُ إِلَى` (Day 123 for `يُشِيرُ إِلَى`). `زِيَادَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ فِي` (Day 115). `الْمَاءِ الْمُلَوَّثِ` (new word `الْمَاءِ` in Genitive after `فِي`; new adjective `الْمُلَوَّثِ` in Genitive, Day 124). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ أَنَّ` (Day 123 for `الْخُبَرَاءُ`). `حِمَايَةَ الْأَرْضِ` (new Masdar `حِمَايَةَ` as Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, first term of Idafa; new word `الْأَرْضِ` in Genitive, Day 124).

4.  **"In light of that, everyone wants clean air and water. And accordingly, the reduction of pollution became a national goal."**
    *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ هَوَاءً وَمَاءً نَظِيفَيْنِ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، أَصْبَحَ تَقْلِيلُ التَّلَوُّثِ هَدَفًا وَطَنِيًّا.` (Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, yureedu al-jamee'u hawā'an wa-mā'an naẓeefayni. Wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika, aṣbaḥa taqleelu at-talawwuthi hadafan waṭaniyan.)
        *   *Notice:* `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (Day 108). `يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ` (Day 110). `هَوَاءً وَمَاءً نَظِيفَيْنِ` (new words `هَوَاءً` and `مَاءً` as Accusative direct objects; `نَظِيفَيْنِ` - dual Accusative adjective, new for today, but student has seen dual on Day 32). `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `أَصْبَحَ تَقْلِيلُ التَّلَوُّثِ هَدَفًا وَطَنِيًّا` (`أَصْبَحَ` Day 98; new Masdar `تَقْلِيلُ`, Nominative subject of `أَصْبَحَ`, first term of Idafa; `التَّلَوُّثِ` new word in Genitive, Day 124; `هَدَفًا` new word `هَدَفٌ` Accusative predicate of `أَصْبَحَ`, Day 117; `وَطَنِيًّا` - national, Accusative adjective, Day 113).

These examples show how you can combine new environmental vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences.
