**نَصُّ الْيَوْمِ (Naṣṣu al-yawmi) - Today's Text**

1.  `السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ.`
2.  `مَا اسْمُكَ؟ اِسْمِي أَحْمَدُ.`
3.  `أَنَا طَالِبٌ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.`
4.  `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ، أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ فِي الصَّبَاحِ.`
5.  `أَدْرُسُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ الْجَمِيلَةِ.`
6.  `مَكْتَبِي هُنَاكَ وَ قَلَمِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.`
7.  `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ لَكِنْ لَا أَكْتُبُ الْآنَ.`
8.  `الْآنَ، آكُلُ الطَّعَامَ وَ أَشْرَبُ الْمَاءَ.`
9.  `فِي الظُّهْرِ، أَرْجِعُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ.`
10. `فِي الْمَسَاءِ، أَكْتُبُ قِصَّةً جَدِيدَةً فِي كِتَابِي الصَّغِيرِ.`
11. `غَدًا، سَأَدْرُسُ كُتُبًا كَبِيرَةً.`
12. `الْكُتُبُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ.`
13. `أَأَنْتَ طَالِبٌ أَوْ مُدَرِّسٌ؟`

***

Let's go through it sentence by sentence:

1.  `السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ.` (As-salāmu 'alaykum.)
    *   **Translation:** Peace be upon you. (Our basic greeting from Day 13!)

2.  `مَا اسْمُكَ؟ اِسْمِي أَحْمَدُ.` (Mā ismuka? Ismī Aḥmadu.)
    *   **Translation:** What is your name? My name is Ahmad. (Question from Day 13, and `اِسْمِي` uses an attached pronoun from Day 8.) `أَحْمَدُ` is a proper name.

3.  `أَنَا طَالِبٌ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (Ana ṭālibun fī al-madrasati al-kabeerati.)
    *   **Translation:** I am a student in the big school. (Uses `أَنَا` from Day 7, nominal sentence from Day 6, `طَالِبٌ` (student) from Day 23. `فِي` (in) from Day 10, and `الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ` is a definite feminine noun with a definite feminine adjective, both taking Kasra due to `فِي` from Day 11 and Day 10.)

4.  `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ، أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ فِي الصَّبَاحِ.` (Kulla yawmin, adhhabu ilā al-madrasati fī aṣ-ṣabāḥi.)
    *   **Translation:** Every day, I go to the school in the morning. (Uses `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ` (every day), `أَذْهَبُ` (I go) from Day 29. `إِلَى` (to) makes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` take Kasra. `فِي الصَّبَاحِ` (in the morning) is a time adverbial phrase from Day 29.)

5.  `أَدْرُسُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ الْجَمِيلَةِ.` (Adrusu fī al-ghurfati al-jameelati.)
    *   **Translation:** I study in the beautiful room. (`أَدْرُسُ` (I study) from Day 20. `فِي` makes `الْغُرْفَةِ الْجَمِيلَةِ` (the beautiful room) take Kasra. `الْجَمِيلَةِ` is an adjective matching `الْغُرْفَةِ` in definiteness, gender, and case from Day 11.)

6.  `مَكْتَبِي هُنَاكَ وَ قَلَمِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Maktabī hunāka wa qalamī fawqa al-maktabi.)
    *   **Translation:** My desk is there and my pen is on top of the desk. (`مَكْتَبِي` (my desk) and `قَلَمِي` (my pen) use the Idafa construct with attached pronouns from Day 8 and Day 25. `هُنَاكَ` (there) is an adverb of place from Day 27. `وَ` (and) is a conjunction from Day 28. `فَوْقَ` (on top of) is an adverb of place from Day 27, and it makes `الْمَكْتَبِ` (the desk) take Kasra.)

7.  `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ لَكِنْ لَا أَكْتُبُ الْآنَ.` (Ana aktubu kulla yawmin lakin lā aktubu al-āna.)
    *   **Translation:** I write every day but I am not writing now. (`أَكْتُبُ` (I write) from Day 20. `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ` (every day). `لَكِنْ` (but) is a conjunction from Day 28. `لَا أَكْتُبُ` (I am not writing) uses negation from Day 21. `الْآنَ` (now) is an adverb of time from Day 27.)

8.  `الْآنَ، آكُلُ الطَّعَامَ وَ أَشْرَبُ الْمَاءَ.` (Al-āna, aakulu aṭ-ṭa'āma wa ashrabu al-mā'a.)
    *   **Translation:** Now, I am eating the food and drinking the water. (`الْآنَ` (now). `آكُلُ` (I eat) and `أَشْرَبُ` (I drink) are verbs from Day 29. `الطَّعَامَ` (the food) and `الْمَاءَ` (the water) are objects, so they take Fatha from Day 29.)

9.  `فِي الظُّهْرِ، أَرْجِعُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ.` (Fī aẓ-ẓuhri, arji'u ilā al-bayti.)
    *   **Translation:** At noon, I return to the house. (`فِي الظُّهْرِ` (at noon) from Day 29. `أَرْجِعُ` (I return) from Day 29. `إِلَى` makes `الْبَيْتِ` (the house) take Kasra. `بَيْتٌ` from Day 26.)

10. `فِي الْمَسَاءِ، أَكْتُبُ قِصَّةً جَدِيدَةً فِي كِتَابِي الصَّغِيرِ.` (Fī al-masā'i, aktubu qiṣṣatan jadeedatan fī kitaabī aṣ-ṣagheeri.)
    *   **Translation:** In the evening, I write a new story in my small book. (`فِي الْمَسَاءِ` (in the evening) from Day 29. `أَكْتُبُ` (I write). `قِصَّةً جَدِيدَةً` (a new story) is an indefinite feminine object with a matching adjective, both taking Tanween Fatha from Day 11 and Day 20. `فِي كِتَابِي الصَّغِيرِ` (in my small book) is a complex Idafa/adjective phrase where `كِتَابِي` (my book) is made definite by the attached pronoun, and `الصَّغِيرِ` (the small) is an adjective describing `كِتَابِي`, matching in definiteness, gender, and taking Kasra because of `فِي` from Day 26.)

11. `غَدًا، سَأَدْرُسُ كُتُبًا كَبِيرَةً.` (Ghadan, sa'adrusu kutuban kabeeratan.)
    *   **Translation:** Tomorrow, I will study big books. (`غَدًا` (tomorrow) from Day 27. `سَأَدْرُسُ` (I will study) uses the future tense prefix `سَـ` from Day 22. `كُتُبًا كَبِيرَةً` (big books) uses `كُتُبٌ` (books) which is a broken plural from Day 24. Since `كُتُبٌ` is a non-human broken plural, its adjective `كَبِيرَةً` (big) is in the feminine singular form, both taking Fatha Tanween as objects from Day 24.)

12. `الْكُتُبُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ.` (Al-kutubu fī al-ghurfati.)
    *   **Translation:** The books are in the room. (`الْكُتُبُ` (the books) is a broken plural used as the subject. `فِي الْغُرْفَةِ` (in the room) uses the preposition `فِي` making `الْغُرْفَةِ` take Kasra.)

13. `أَأَنْتَ طَالِبٌ أَوْ مُدَرِّسٌ؟` (A'anta ṭālibun aw mudarrisun?)
    *   **Translation:** Are you a student or a teacher? (`أَـ` is a question particle from Day 12. `أَنْتَ` (you-m) from Day 7. `أَوْ` (or) is a conjunction from Day 28. `مُدَرِّسٌ` (teacher) from Day 23.)

## Examples

Let's revisit some key phrases from our text to highlight the rules you've learned.

1.  **Showing Possession (Idafa/Attached Pronoun):**
    *   `اِسْمِي أَحْمَدُ.` (Ismī Aḥmadu.) - My name is Ahmad.
    *   `مَكْتَبِي هُنَاكَ.` (Maktabī hunāka.) - My desk is there.
    *   `قَلَمِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Qalamī fawqa al-maktabi.) - My pen is on top of the desk. (`فَوْقَ` also behaves like the first noun in an Idafa, making `الْمَكْتَبِ` take Kasra.)
    *   `فِي كِتَابِي الصَّغِيرِ.` (Fī kitaabī aṣ-ṣagheeri.) - In my small book. (Complex Idafa with adjective.)

2.  **Verbs in Different Tenses and Negation:**
    *   `أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Adhhabu ilā al-madrasati.) - I go to the school. (Present Tense)
    *   `لَا أَكْتُبُ الْآنَ.` (Lā aktubu al-āna.) - I am not writing now. (Present Tense Negation)
    *   `سَأَدْرُسُ كُتُبًا كَبِيرَةً.` (Sa'adrusu kutuban kabeeratan.) - I will study big books. (Future Tense)
    *   `أَرْجِعُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ.` (Arji'u ilā al-bayti.) - I return to the house. (Present Tense)

3.  **Adjectives with Plurals (especially non-human broken plurals):**
    *   `الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (Al-madrasati al-kabeerati.) - The big school. (Singular noun, singular adjective matching gender, definiteness, case.)
    *   `كُتُبًا كَبِيرَةً.` (Kutuban kabeeratan.) - Big books. (`كُتُبٌ` is a non-human broken plural, so `كَبِيرَةً` is feminine singular.)

4.  **Conjunctions:**
    *   `مَكْتَبِي هُنَاكَ وَ قَلَمِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Maktabī hunāka wa qalamī fawqa al-maktabi.) - My desk is there **and** my pen is on top of the desk. (`وَ` = and)
    *   `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ لَكِنْ لَا أَكْتُبُ الْآنَ.` (Ana aktubu kulla yawmin lakin lā aktubu al-āna.) - I write every day **but** I am not writing now. (`لَكِنْ` = but)
    *   `أَأَنْتَ طَالِبٌ أَوْ مُدَرِّسٌ؟` (A'anta ṭālibun aw mudarrisun?) - Are you a student **or** a teacher? (`أَوْ` = or)
