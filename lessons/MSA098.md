# Day 98 - Light verb constructions

## Lesson

"Light verb constructions" in Arabic are like building blocks. You take a verb that has a fairly general meaning (like "to make" or "to do"), and you combine it with another noun or an adjective to express a richer, more specific idea. This is a very common and natural way to speak in Modern Standard Arabic.

Let's look at three important examples:

### 1. `جَعَلَ / يَجْعَلُ` (ja'ala / yaj'alu) - To make / To render / To cause to be

This verb means "to make something become something else" or "to cause something to be in a certain state." It's powerful because it shows a change happening.

*   **New Verb (Past Tense):** `جَعَلَ` (ja'ala) - 'ja-a-la' (he made, he rendered, he caused to be).
*   **New Verb (Present Tense):** `يَجْعَلُ` (yaj'alu) - 'yaj-a-lu' (he makes, he renders, he causes to be).
*   **How it works:** This verb takes *two* words after it that describe the result of the action. Both of these words will be in the **Accusative case** (ending in Fatha `ـَ` or `ـًـَ`).
    1.  The first word is the thing being changed or made.
    2.  The second word is the new state or identity it's changed into.

*   **Example 1: `جَعَلَ الدَّرْسَ سَهْلًا.`** (Ja'ala ad-darsa sahlan.)
    *   *Translation:* He made the lesson easy.
    *   *Breakdown:* `جَعَلَ` (he made). `الدَّرْسَ` (the lesson) is the first object, taking Fatha. `سَهْلًا` (easy) is the second object (or predicate of the action), also taking Fatha. `سَهْلٌ` (easy) is a new adjective.

*   **Example 2: `يَجْعَلُ الْمَاءَ بَارِدًا.`** (Yaj'alu al-mā'a bāridan.)
    *   *Translation:* He makes the water cold.
    *   *Breakdown:* `يَجْعَلُ` (he makes). `الْمَاءَ` (the water) is the first object, taking Fatha. `بَارِدًا` (cold) is the second object, also taking Fatha.

### 2. `قَامَ / يَقُومُ بِـ` (qāma / yaqūmu bi-) - To undertake / To carry out / To do

This is a very common way to express that someone "performs" or "carries out" an action. It's often used for formal actions, like performing a duty or undertaking a project.

*   **New Verb (Past Tense):** `قَامَ` (qāma) - 'qaa-ma' (he undertook, he carried out, he stood up).
*   **New Verb (Present Tense):** `يَقُومُ` (yaqūmu) - 'ya-qoo-mu' (he undertakes, he carries out, he stands up).
*   **How it works:** This verb is almost always followed by the preposition `بِـ` (with/by means of), which attaches to a noun (often a Masdar/verbal noun) to specify the action being done. The noun after `بِـ` will be in the **Genitive case** (taking Kasra `ـِ`).

*   **Example 1: `قَامَ بِالْعَمَلِ.`** (Qāma bi-l-'amali.)
    *   *Translation:* He carried out the work.
    *   *Breakdown:* `قَامَ` (he carried out). `بِـ` (by/with). `الْعَمَلِ` (the work) takes Kasra because of `بِـ`.

*   **Example 2: `تَقُومُ الشَّرِكَةُ بِإِعْلَانِ الْخُطَّةِ.`** (Taqūmu ash-sharikatū bi-i'lāni al-khuṭṭati.)
    *   *Translation:* The company performs the announcement of the plan / The company announces the plan.
    *   *Breakdown:* `تَقُومُ` (she performs). `الشَّرِكَةُ` (the company - subject, Damma). `بِإِعْلَانِ` (by the announcement - `إِعْلَانٌ` is a Masdar, Genitive after `بِـ`). `الْخُطَّةِ` (the plan - Genitive, second term of Idafa `إِعْلَانِ الْخُطَّةِ`).

### 3. `أَصْبَحَ / يُصْبِحُ` (aṣbaḥa / yuṣbiḥu) - To become

This verb is used to express a change of state, meaning something "becomes" something else. It works very similarly to `كَانَ` (he was) that you learned on Day 40.

*   **New Verb (Past Tense):** `أَصْبَحَ` (aṣbaḥa) - 'aṣ-ba-ḥa' (he became).
*   **New Verb (Present Tense):** `يُصْبِحُ` (yuṣbiḥu) - 'yuṣ-bi-ḥu' (he becomes).
*   **How it works:** Like `كَانَ`, `أَصْبَحَ` takes a subject in the **Nominative case** (Damma `ـُ`) and a predicate (the thing it became) in the **Accusative case** (Fatha `ـَ` or `ـًـَ`).

*   **Example 1: `أَصْبَحَ الطَّقْسُ جَمِيلًا.`** (Aṣbaḥa aṭ-ṭaqsu jamīlan.)
    *   *Translation:* The weather became beautiful.
    *   *Breakdown:* `أَصْبَحَ` (it became). `الطَّقْسُ` (the weather) is the subject, taking Damma. `جَمِيلًا` (beautiful) is the predicate, taking Fatha.

*   **Example 2: `تُصْبِحُ الْمُشْكِلَةُ صَعْبَةً.`** (Tuṣbiḥu al-mushkilatū ṣa'batan.)
    *   *Translation:* The problem becomes difficult.
    *   *Breakdown:* `تُصْبِحُ` (it becomes - feminine verb). `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` (the problem) is the subject, Damma. `صَعْبَةً` (difficult - feminine) is the predicate, Fatha.

These light verb constructions add a great deal of nuance and formality to your Arabic. Mastering them will significantly enhance your expressive capabilities!

## Examples

Let's see these light verb constructions in more complex sentences, combining them with grammar you've already mastered.

1.  **"The minister (m.) makes the lesson easy for the student."**
    *   `يَجْعَلُ الْوَزِيرُ الدَّرْسَ سَهْلًا لِلطَّالِبِ.` (Yaj'alu al-wazeeru ad-darsa sahlan li-ṭ-ṭālibi.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَجْعَلُ` (he makes). `الدَّرْسَ سَهْلًا` (the lesson easy - both Accusative). `لِلطَّالِبِ` (for the student).

2.  **"We know that the president will carry out the new project."**
    *   `نَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الرَّئِيسَ سَيَقُومُ بِالْمَشْرُوعِ الْجَدِيدِ.` (Na'lamu anna ar-ra'eeca sayaqūmu bi-l-mashroo'i al-jadeedi.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَعْلَمُ أَنَّ` (we know that - Day 79). `الرَّئِيسَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `سَيَقُومُ بِالْمَشْرُوعِ الْجَدِيدِ` (will carry out the new project - `سَـ` for future, `الْمَشْرُوعِ الْجَدِيدِ` Genitive after `بِـ`).

3.  **"It seems that the economy became important for the country."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الِاقْتِصَادَ أَصْبَحَ مُهِمًّا لِلْبَلَدِ.` (Yabdū anna al-iqtiṣāda aṣbaḥa muhimman li-l-baladi.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that - Day 80). `الِاقْتِصَادَ` (the economy - Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `أَصْبَحَ مُهِمًّا` (it became important - `مُهِمًّا` is Accusative as predicate of `أَصْبَحَ`).

4.  **"The knowing student, Ali, wants to make his work successful."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ الطَّالِبُ الْعَالِمُ عَلِيٌّ أَنْ يَجْعَلَ عَمَلَهُ نَاجِحًا.` (Yureedu aṭ-ṭālibu al-'ālimu 'Aliyyun an yaj'ala 'amalahu nājiḥan.)
        *   *Notice:* `الطَّالِبُ الْعَالِمُ عَلِيٌّ` (apposition with active participle, Day 85, 88). `أَنْ يَجْعَلَ` (to make - `يَجْعَلَ` is Subjunctive due to `أَنْ`, Day 60). `عَمَلَهُ نَاجِحًا` (his work successful - both Accusative).

5.  **"Although the problem is difficult, the minister (f.) will undertake the search for a solution."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ صَعْبَةٌ، سَتَقُومُ الْوَزِيرَةُ بِالْبَحْثِ عَنِ الْحَلِّ.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-mushkilata ṣa'batun, satqūmu al-wazeeratu bi-l-baḥthi 'ani al-ḥalli.)
        *   *Notice:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (although - Day 81). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ صَعْبَةٌ` (Accusative subject for `أَنَّ`, Nominative comment). `سَتَقُومُ` (she will undertake - `سَـ` for future, feminine verb). `بِالْبَحْثِ عَنِ الْحَلِّ` (by searching for the solution - `بَحْثٌ` is Masdar of `بَحَثَ`, Day 97).

6.  **"Perhaps the news will become clear tomorrow."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا تُصْبِحُ الْأَخْبَارُ وَاضِحَةً غَدًا.` (Rubbamā tuṣbiḥu al-akhbāru wāḍiḥatan ghadan.)
        *   *New word:* `وَاضِحٌ` (wāḍiḥun) - clear (masculine adjective). `وَاضِحَةٌ` (feminine).
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (perhaps - Day 80). `تُصْبِحُ` (it becomes - feminine verb, `أَخْبَارٌ` is non-human plural). `الْأَخْبَارُ` (the news - subject, Damma). `وَاضِحَةً` (clear - Accusative predicate).
