# Day 80 - Hedging language

## Lesson

Hedging language helps you express degrees of certainty or possibility. It's like adding "maybe," "possibly," or "it seems that" to your sentences in English. This is important for being precise and polite in Arabic.

We'll learn a few key phrases and words today that are commonly used for hedging:

### 1. `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ` (mina al-muḥtamali anna) - It is probable/likely that...

This phrase is used to say that something is likely to happen or is probably true, but not 100% certain.

*   **New Word for the phrase:**
    *   **`مُحْتَمَلٌ` (muḥtamalun) - Probable, Likely** (masculine adjective / passive participle).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'muḥ-ta-ma-lun'. (Feminine: `مُحْتَمَلَةٌ` - muḥtamalatun).
        *   *Example:* `الْأَمْرُ مُحْتَمَلٌ.` (Al-amru muḥtamalun.) - The matter is probable. (`أَمْرٌ` - matter, thing, command, masculine noun from Day 22).
*   **The Hedging Phrase:** `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ` (mina al-muḥtamali anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "From the probable that."
    *   *How to use:* This phrase is similar in structure to `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (it is known that, Day 64) and `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (it is clear that, Day 69). It always introduces `أَنَّ`, so the subject of the clause following `أَنَّ` will take a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   *Example sentence:*
        `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ الطَّقْسَ سَيَكُونُ جَمِيلًا.` (Mina al-muḥtamali anna aṭ-ṭaqsa sa-yakoonu jameelan.)
        *   *Translation:* It is probable that the weather will be beautiful.
        *   *Breakdown:* `أَنَّ` makes `الطَّقْسَ` (the weather - Day 79) take Fatha. `سَيَكُونُ` (it will be - `كَانَ` with `سَـ`). `جَمِيلًا` (beautiful) is the predicate of `كَانَ` and takes Fatha.

### 2. `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā) - Perhaps / Maybe

This is a simple adverb you can use to express possibility or uncertainty.

*   **New Word:** `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā) - 'rub-ba-maa'
    *   *Meaning:* "Perhaps," "maybe."
    *   *How to use:* You can place `رُبَّمَا` at the beginning of a sentence, or before the verb. It does not affect the case endings or verb moods of other words in the sentence.
    *   *Example sentence:*
        `رُبَّمَا يَجِيءُ الْوَزِيرُ الْيَوْمَ.` (Rubbamā yajī'u al-wazeeru al-yawma.)
        *   *Translation:* Perhaps the minister will come today.
        *   *Breakdown:* `يَجِيءُ` (he comes - Day 78). `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister) is the subject. `الْيَوْمَ` (today) is an adverb of time.

### 3. `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (yabdū anna) - It seems that... / It appears that...

This phrase is used to express an observation or an assumption about something, indicating that it *appears* to be true, without stating it as a definite fact.

*   **New Verb: "To Appear / To Seem"**
    *   **Root:** `ب د و` (b-d-w)
    *   **Present Tense (it appears / it seems):** `يَبْدُو` (yabdū) - 'yab-doo'. (This is a weak verb, ending in `ـو` or `ـى` in its root, which changes its conjugation in certain contexts. For now, just remember `يَبْدُو` for "it seems").
*   **The Hedging Phrase:** `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (yabdū anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "It appears that."
    *   *How to use:* This phrase also introduces `أَنَّ`, so the subject of the clause following `أَنَّ` will take a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   *Example sentence:*
        `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Yabdū anna al-mushkilata kabeeratun.)
        *   *Translation:* It seems that the problem is big.
        *   *Breakdown:* `أَنَّ` makes `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (the problem - Day 50) take Fatha. `كَبِيرَةٌ` (big) is the comment.

### Review: `أَظُنُّ أَنَّ` (aẓunnu anna) - I think/assume that...

You learned `يَظُنُّ` (he thinks/assumes) on Day 79. This is an excellent phrase for hedging, as it expresses your personal opinion or assumption rather than a confirmed fact.

*   **Example sentence:**
    `أَظُنُّ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ سَيَنْجَحُ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Aẓunnu anna aṭ-ṭāliba sa-yanjaḥu fī al-imtiḥāni.)
    *   *Translation:* I think that the student will succeed in the exam.
    *   *Breakdown:* `أَظُنُّ` (I think). `أَنَّ` makes `الطَّالِبَ` (the student) take Fatha. `سَيَنْجَحُ` (he will succeed - future tense, Day 58).

These phrases give you more ways to express uncertainty and possibility, making your Arabic sound more natural and sophisticated!

## Examples

Let's combine our new hedging phrases with grammar and vocabulary you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **"It is probable that the government will protect the environment."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali anna al-ḥukoomata sa-taḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ` (that the government) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`. `سَتَحْفَظُ` (she will protect - Day 56).

2.  **"Perhaps the minister (f.) will return to her country tomorrow."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا تَعُودُ الْوَزِيرَةُ إِلَى بَلَدِهَا غَدًا.` (Rubbamā ta'ūdu al-wazeeratū ilā baladihā ghadan.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` at the beginning. `تَعُودُ` (she returns - Day 78). `الْوَزِيرَةُ` (the minister - f.) is the subject. `إِلَى بَلَدِهَا` (to her country - Day 78).

3.  **"It seems that the new policy is important."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Yabdū anna as-siyāsata al-jadeedata muhimmatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (that the new policy) takes Fatha for both noun and adjective. `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important - feminine) is the comment.

4.  **"I think that it is necessary to ask about the prices."**
    *   `أَظُنُّ أَنَّهُ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَسْأَلَ عَنِ الْأَسْعَارِ.` (Aẓunnu annahu yajibu an nas'ala 'ani al-as'āri.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَظُنُّ أَنَّهُ` (I think that it is). `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَسْأَلَ` (it is necessary to ask - Day 67, 79, Subjunctive). `عَنِ الْأَسْعَارِ` (about the prices - Day 79).

5.  **"It is probable that the old computer is not useful."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ الْحَاسُوبَ الْقَدِيمَ غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali anna al-ḥāsūba al-qadeema ghayru mufīdin.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ الْحَاسُوبَ الْقَدِيمَ` takes Fatha. `غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ` (not useful - Day 73).

6.  **"Perhaps the problem was solved yesterday."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا حُلَّتِ الْمُشْكِلَةُ أَمْسِ.` (Rubbamā ḥullati al-mushkilatu amsi.)
        *   *Notice:* `حُلَّتِ` (it was solved - passive past tense for feminine `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` - Day 72). `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`. `أَمْسِ` (yesterday - Day 27).

7.  **"It seems that the student wants not to fail."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ يُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا يَرْسُبَ.` (Yabdū anna aṭ-ṭāliba yureedu an lā yarsuba.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ` takes Fatha. `يُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا يَرْسُبَ` (he wants not to fail - Day 73, Subjunctive).
