# Day 40 - Purpose clauses

## Lesson

1.  **What are Purpose Clauses?**
    *   A purpose clause tells you the **reason** or **goal** behind an action. It answers the question "Why?"
    *   In English, we often use phrases like "to," "in order to," or "so that."
    *   For example: "I study **to learn**." (The purpose of studying is learning.)
    *   "He goes to the market **to buy** food." (The purpose of going to the market is buying food.)

2.  **Introducing `لِـ` (li-) for Purpose**
    *   In Arabic, the most common way to express "to" or "in order to" when describing the purpose of an action is by using the particle **`لِـ` (li-)**.
    *   This is the letter `ل` (Laam) with a Kasra ( ِ ) underneath it. It always attaches directly to the verb that follows it.

3.  **The Fatha Rule with `لِـ`**
    *   This is a crucial rule, similar to what you learned with `أَنْ` (an) on Day 38!
    *   When `لِـ` (li-) comes directly before a **present tense verb**, it makes that verb change its ending vowel from a Damma ( ُ ) to a **Fatha ( َ )**.
    *   Think of `لِـ` as giving the present tense verb a little "push" that changes its sound! This is called the *Subjunctive Mood*.

    *   Let's look at some verbs you know and how they change with `لِـ`:
        *   `يَكْتُبُ` (yaktubu - he writes) becomes `لِيَكْتُبَ` (li-yaktuba - in order for him to write)
        *   `تَكْتُبُ` (taktubu - she writes) becomes `لِتَكْتُبَ` (li-taktuba - in order for her to write)
        *   `أَكْتُبُ` (aktubu - I write) becomes `لِأَكْتُبَ` (li-aktuba - in order for me to write)
        *   `يَدْرُسُ` (yadrusu - he studies) becomes `لِيَدْرُسَ` (li-yadrusa - in order for him to study)
        *   `يَذْهَبُ` (yadhhabu - he goes) becomes `لِيَذْهَبَ` (li-yadhhaba - in order for him to go)
        *   `يَرْجِعُ` (yarji'u - he returns) becomes `لِيَرْجِعَ` (li-yarji'a - in order for him to return)
        *   `يَأْكُلُ` (ya'kulu - he eats) becomes `لِيَأْكُلَ` (li-ya'kula - in order for him to eat)
        *   `آكُلُ` (aakulu - I eat) becomes `لِآكُلَ` (li-ākula - in order for me to eat)
            *   *Notice:* The `آ` (long 'aa') stays, and the `ل` gets the Fatha.
        *   `يَشْرَبُ` (yashrabu - he drinks) becomes `لِيَشْرَبَ` (li-yashraba - in order for him to drink)

4.  **Putting it Together: The Sentence Structure**
    *   You'll usually have a main action, and then the `لِـ` + Fatha-ending verb to state its purpose.
    *   **Structure:** `[Subject] + [Main Action Verb] + لِـ + [Purpose Verb (present tense, ending in Fatha)] + [Optional Object/Details]`

This new grammatical tool allows you to explain the reasons behind actions, making your sentences much more descriptive!

## Examples

Let's put `لِـ` and the Fatha rule into practice with sentences using vocabulary you already know.

1.  **I study to write the story.**
    *   `أَنَا أَدْرُسُ لِأَكْتُبَ الْقِصَّةَ.` (Ana adrusu li'aktuba al-qisṣata.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَدْرُسُ` is the main verb ("I study"). `لِأَكْتُبَ` is the purpose verb ("in order for me to write"), with the Fatha ending.

2.  **He goes to the school to study.**
    *   `هُوَ يَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ لِيَدْرُسَ.` (Huwa yadhhabu ilā al-madrasati li-yadrusa.)
        *   *Notice*: `يَذْهَبُ` is the main verb. `لِيَدْرُسَ` is the purpose verb, with the Fatha ending.

3.  **She drinks the water to eat the food.**
    *   `هِيَ تَشْرَبُ الْمَاءَ لِتَأْكُلَ الطَّعَامَ.` (Hiya tashrabu al-mā'a li-ta'kula aṭ-ṭa'āma.)
        *   *Notice*: `تَشْرَبُ` is the main verb. `لِتَأْكُلَ` is the purpose verb, with the Fatha ending.

4.  **We return to the house to drink tea.**
    *   `نَحْنُ نَرْجِعُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ لِنَشْرَبَ الشَّايَ.` (Naḥnu narji'u ilā al-bayti li-nashraba ash-shāya.)
        *   *Notice*: `نَرْجِعُ` is the main verb. `لِنَشْرَبَ` is the purpose verb, with the Fatha ending.

5.  **He will go to the university tomorrow to write his name.**
    *   `هُوَ سَيَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ غَدًا لِيَكْتُبَ اِسْمَهُ.` (Huwa sa-yadhhabu ilā al-jāmi'ati ghadan li-yaktuba ismahu.)
        *   *Notice*: `سَيَذْهَبُ` is the main verb (future tense). `لِيَكْتُبَ` is the purpose verb.

6.  **The teacher (feminine) studies every day to write.**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ تَدْرُسُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ لِتَكْتُبَ.` (Al-mudarrisatu tadrusu kulla yawmin li-taktuba.)
        *   *Notice*: `تَدْرُسُ` is the main verb. `لِتَكْتُبَ` is the purpose verb.

You can now add reasons and goals to your actions in Arabic! This makes your communication much more complete and sophisticated.
