## Lesson

1.  **What is a Verb?**
    *   So far, most of our sentences have been "X *is* Y" (nominal sentences). Now, we're going to learn about sentences that include an **action**.
    *   A **verb (فِعْلٌ - fi'lun)** is a word that tells us what someone or something **did**, **is doing**, or **will do**.
    *   Today, we focus on the **past tense**, for actions that already finished.

2.  **The Root as the Base for Verbs**
    *   Remember our `ك ت ب` root from yesterday? We saw that `كَتَبَ` means "he wrote." This is actually the most basic form of a verb in Arabic!
    *   Most Arabic verbs come from a three-letter root. The simplest past tense form (called **Form I**) is usually just these three letters with short vowel sounds.
    *   The "he" form is often the starting point from which all other verb forms are made.

3.  **The Past Tense Verb for "He" (هُوَ)**
    *   The basic three-letter past tense verb is always for "he" (هُوَ).
    *   **Structure:** `فَعَلَ` (faʿala) - This is a common pattern for many "Form I" past tense verbs. It usually has a Fatha ( َ ) on the first letter, a Fatha ( َ ) on the second letter, and a Fatha ( َ ) on the third letter.
    *   **Example:**
        *   `كَتَبَ` (kataba) - **He wrote.**
            *   *Notice*: `ك` has Fatha, `ت` has Fatha, `ب` has Fatha.
        *   When you want to say "He wrote," you just say `هُوَ كَتَبَ.` (Huwa kataba.)

4.  **The Past Tense Verb for "She" (هِيَ)**
    *   To change the "he" form to the "she" form, we add a special ending to the verb.
    *   **Structure:** Add a **Sukooned Taa (تْ)** to the end of the "he" form.
    *   **Example:**
        *   Take `كَتَبَ` (he wrote).
        *   Add `تْ` to get `كَتَبَتْ` (katabat) - **She wrote.**
            *   *Notice*: The last root letter (ب) gets a Sukoon ( ْ ) to make space for the new `تْ`.
        *   When you want to say "She wrote," you just say `هِيَ كَتَبَتْ.` (Hiya katabat.)

5.  **The Past Tense Verb for "They" (هُُمْ) (Masculine/Mixed Plural)**
    *   To change the "he" form to the "they" (masculine or mixed group plural) form, we add another special ending.
    *   **Structure:** Add a **Damma ( ُ )** to the last root letter, and then add **Waw Alif (وْا)**. The Alif ( ا ) at the very end is usually silent, it's there for grammatical reasons that you'll learn later.
    *   **Example:**
        *   Take `كَتَبَ` (he wrote).
        *   Change the last Fatha on `ب` to Damma, then add `وْا` to get `كَتَبُوا` (katabū) - **They wrote.**
            *   *Notice*: The `ب` now has Damma, followed by the long `ـُو` sound (Waw) and a silent Alif.
        *   When you want to say "They wrote," you just say `هُمْ كَتَبُوا.` (Hum katabū.)

6.  **Introducing Another Verb: `دَرَسَ` (Darasa) - He studied**
    *   Let's practice with another common verb from the root `د ر س` (Dāl-Rā-Sīn), meaning "study" or "learn."
    *   **He studied:** `دَرَسَ` (darasa)
    *   **She studied:** `دَرَسَتْ` (darasat)
    *   **They studied:** `دَرَسُوا` (darasū)

This might feel like a lot, but the patterns are very consistent! Once you learn the "he" form of a Form I verb, you'll know how to create the "she" and "they" forms easily.

## Examples

Let's put these new verb forms into practice with the pronouns you learned on Day 7!

1.  **He wrote.**
    *   `هُوَ كَتَبَ.` (Huwa kataba.)

2.  **She wrote.**
    *   `هِيَ كَتَبَتْ.` (Hiya katabat.)

3.  **They wrote.**
    *   `هُمْ كَتَبُوا.` (Hum katabū.)

4.  **He studied.**
    *   `هُوَ دَرَسَ.` (Huwa darasa.)

5.  **She studied.**
    *   `هِيَ دَرَسَتْ.` (Hiya darasat.)

6.  **They studied.**
    *   `هُمْ دَرَسُوا.` (Hum darasū.)

7.  **The teacher (masculine) wrote the book.**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُ كَتَبَ الْكِتَابَ.` (Al-mudarrisu kataba al-kitaaba.)
        *   *Notice*: The subject `الْمُدَرِّسُ` (the teacher) takes the place of `هُوَ`. The `الْكِتَابَ` (the book) here takes a Fatha because it is the object of the verb, meaning "what was written." Don't worry about this new Fatha rule for now; just focus on the verb.

8.  **The student (feminine) studied in the school.**
    *   `الطَّالِبَةُ دَرَسَتْ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (At-taalibatu darasat fī al-madrasati.)
        *   *Notice*: `الطَّالِبَةُ` (the student - feminine) takes the place of `هِيَ`. The preposition `فِي` (in) still makes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` end with a Kasra.

You've just learned how to express actions in the past! This is a massive leap in your Arabic communication skills.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   فِعْلٌ (fi'lun) - verb
*   الْمَاضِي (al-maadii) - the past (part of "past tense")
*   كَتَبَ (kataba) - he wrote
*   كَتَبَتْ (katabat) - she wrote
*   كَتَبُوا (katabū) - they (masculine/mixed plural) wrote
*   دَرَسَ (darasa) - he studied
*   دَرَسَتْ (darasat) - she studied
*   دَرَسُوا (darasū) - they (masculine/mixed plural) studied
