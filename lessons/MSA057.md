# Day 57 - Health vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns, adjectives, and a new verb related to health. Pay close attention to the gender of each noun and adjective, as this will affect how they are used in sentences!

1.  **`صِحَّةٌ` (ṣiḥḥatun) - Health**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ṣiḥ-ḥa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الصِّحَّةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Aṣ-ṣiḥḥatū muhimmatun.) - Health is important. (Using `مُهِمَّةٌ` - important, feminine from Day 43).

2.  **`طَبِيبٌ` (ṭabeebun) - Doctor (masculine)**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ṭa-bee-bun'.
    *   Example: `الطَّبِيبُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Aṭ-ṭabeebu dhakiyyun.) - The doctor is smart. (Using `ذَكِيٌّ` - smart, from Day 37).

3.  **`طَبِيبَةٌ` (ṭabeebatun) - Doctor (feminine)**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun (just add `ـَةٌ` to `طَبِيبٌ`).
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ṭa-bee-ba-tun'.
    *   Example: `الطَّبِيبَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭabeebatū jadeedatun.) - The doctor (f.) is new. (Using `جَدِيدَةٌ` - new, feminine from Day 3).

4.  **`مَرِيضٌ` (mareeḍun) - Sick / Patient (masculine)**
    *   This can be a **masculine adjective** meaning "sick," or a **masculine noun** meaning "a patient."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ma-ree-ḍun'.
    *   Example: `الرَّجُلُ مَرِيضٌ.` (Ar-rajulu mareeḍun.) - The man is sick. (`الرَّجُلُ` is 'the man' from Day 28).
    *   Example: `الطَّبِيبُ يَرَى الْمَرِيضَ.` (Aṭ-ṭabeebu yarā al-mareeḍa.) - The doctor sees the patient. (New verb 'to see' not introduced yet, so let's simplify).
    *   *Revised Example:* `الْوَلَدُ مَرِيضٌ.` (Al-waladu mareeḍun.) - The boy is sick. (`الْوَلَدُ` is 'the boy' from Day 11).

5.  **`مَرِيضَةٌ` (mareeḍatun) - Sick / Patient (feminine)**
    *   This can be a **feminine adjective** meaning "sick," or a **feminine noun** meaning "a patient."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ma-ree-ḍa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمَرْأَةُ مَرِيضَةٌ.` (Al-mar'atu mareeḍatun.) - The woman is sick. (`الْمَرْأَةُ` is 'the woman' from Day 28).

6.  **`مُسْتَشْفَى` (mustashfā) - Hospital**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It ends with an Alif Maqsura (ى), which means it acts like a masculine noun for agreement.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mus-tash-faa'.
    *   Example: `الْمُسْتَشْفَى كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-mustashfā kabeerun.) - The hospital is big. (Using `كَبِيرٌ` - big, masculine from Day 3).

7.  **`دَوَاءٌ` (dawā'un) - Medicine**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'da-waa-un'.
    *   Example: `الدَّوَاءُ مُفِيدٌ.` (Ad-dawā'u mufīdun.) - The medicine is useful. (Using `مُفِيدٌ` - useful, masculine from Day 43).

8.  **`أَلَمٌ` (alamun) - Pain**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'a-la-mun'.
    *   Example: `الْأَلَمُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-alamu kabeerun.) - The pain is big.

9.  **New Verb: "To Feel"**
    *   This verb is very useful for expressing feelings. It takes the preposition `بِـ` (bi-) which means "with" or "by." So, "to feel sick" is `يَشْعُرُ بِالْمَرَضِ` (literally "he feels with the sickness"). For now, we will just use it to say "he feels."
    *   **Root:** `ش ع ر` (sh-'-r)
    *   **Past Tense (he felt):** `شَعَرَ` (sha'ara) - 'sha-a-ra'.
    *   **Present Tense (he feels):** `يَشْعُرُ` (yash'uru) - 'yash-u-ru'.
    *   **Present Tense (I feel):** `أَشْعُرُ` (ash'uru) - 'ash-u-ru'.
    *   **Present Tense (she feels / you-m.s. feel):** `تَشْعُرُ` (tash'uru) - 'tash-u-ru'.
    *   Example: `هُوَ يَشْعُرُ.` (Huwa yash'uru.) - He feels.

10. **New Verbal Noun (Masdar): `شُعُورٌ` (shu'ūrun) - Feeling**
    *   This is the Masdar for the verb `شَعَرَ` (to feel). It is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'shu-oo-run'.
    *   Example: `الشُّعُورُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Ash-shu'ūru muhimun.) - The feeling is important.

You now have a foundational vocabulary to begin discussing health-related topics! Remember to pay attention to the gender and case endings of these words, especially when they combine with other grammar rules you know.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **Health is important for the people.** (Nominal sentence + preposition)
    *   `الصِّحَّةُ مُهِمَّةٌ لِلشَّعْبِ.` (Aṣ-ṣiḥḥatū muhimmatun li-sh-sha'bi.)
        *   *Notice:* `الصِّحَّةُ` (health) is feminine, so `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important) is feminine. `لِلشَّعْبِ` (for the people) uses the preposition `لِـ` (Day 10), so `الشَّعْبِ` (people - Day 48) takes Kasra.

2.  **The doctor (m.) is in the big hospital.** (Nominal sentence + prepositional phrase)
    *   `الطَّبِيبُ فِي الْمُسْتَشْفَى الْكَبِيرِ.` (Aṭ-ṭabeebu fī al-mustashfā al-kabeeri.)
        *   *Notice:* `فِي الْمُسْتَشْفَى الْكَبِيرِ` (in the big hospital). `الْمُسْتَشْفَى` takes Kasra after `فِي`, and its adjective `الْكَبِيرِ` (big) is masculine and takes Kasra to match.

3.  **She feels the pain.** (New verb + direct object)
    *   `هِيَ تَشْعُرُ بِالْأَلَمِ.` (Hiya tash'uru bi-l-alami.)
        *   *Notice:* The verb `يَشْعُرُ` (to feel) always takes the preposition `بِـ` (bi-). So `الْأَلَمِ` (the pain) takes Kasra after `بِـ`.

4.  **Indeed, the new medicine is certainly useful.** (Emphasis particles `إِنَّ` and `لَـ`)
    *   `إِنَّ الدَّوَاءَ الْجَدِيدَ لَمُفِيدٌ.` (Inna ad-dawā'a al-jadeeda la-mufīdun.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` (Day 37) makes `الدَّوَاءَ الْجَدِيدَ` (the new medicine) take Fatha. `لَمُفِيدٌ` (certainly useful) has `لَـ` attached, and is masculine to match `الدَّوَاءَ`.

5.  **The sick woman wants to go to the doctor (f.).** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `الْمَرْأَةُ الْمَرِيضَةُ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَذْهَبَ إِلَى الطَّبِيبَةِ.` (Al-mar'atu al-mareeḍatū tureedu an tadhhaba ilā aṭ-ṭabeebati.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْمَرْأَةُ الْمَرِيضَةُ` (the sick woman) is the topic. `تُرِيدُ` (she wants - Day 44). `أَنْ تَذْهَبَ` (to go - `تَذْهَبُ` from Day 20, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `إِلَى الطَّبِيبَةِ` (to the doctor - f.) is a prepositional phrase.

6.  **He said that the patient (m.) is in the hospital.** (Reported speech + `أَنَّ`)
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْمَرِيضَ فِي الْمُسْتَشْفَى.` (Qāla anna al-mareeḍa fī al-mustashfā.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَ` (he said - Day 39). `أَنَّ` makes `الْمَرِيضَ` (the patient) take Fatha. `فِي الْمُسْتَشْفَى` (in the hospital) is the comment.

7.  **I want to protect my health.** (Possessive pronoun + new verb)
    *   `أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَحْفَظَ صِحَّتِي.` (Ureedu an aḥfaẓa ṣiḥḥatī.)
        *   *Notice:* `أُرِيدُ` (I want - Day 44). `أَنْ أَحْفَظَ` (to protect - `أَحْفَظُ` from Day 56, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `صِحَّتِي` (my health) is an Idafa, with `صِحَّةَ` taking Fatha as object of the verb, and `ي` (-ī) as the possessive pronoun for 'my'.

8.  **The feeling of pain is big.** (Masdar in Idafa)
    *   `شُعُورُ الْأَلَمِ كَبِيرٌ.` (Shu'ūru al-alami kabeerun.)
        *   *Notice:* `شُعُورُ` (feeling of - new Masdar) is the first noun of the Idafa, masculine, so `كَبِيرٌ` (big) is masculine. `الْأَلَمِ` (the pain) is the second noun, taking Kasra.
