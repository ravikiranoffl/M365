# Day 67 - Diplomatic phrasing

## Lesson

Diplomatic language is all about expressing strong ideas in a way that encourages cooperation and understanding. It often involves expressing hope, stating necessities, or referring to shared goals.

We'll learn a few key phrases and words today that are very common in diplomatic contexts:

### 1. Expressing Hope (`نَأْمُلُ أَنَّ` or `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ`) - We hope that/to...

In diplomacy, expressing hope for a positive outcome is very common.

*   **New Verb: "To Hope"**
    *   **Root:** `أ م ل` (a-m-l)
    *   **Present Tense (he hopes):** `يَأْمُلُ` (ya'mulu) - 'ya'-mu-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I hope):** `آمُلُ` (āmulū) - 'aa-mu-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (we hope):** `نَأْمُلُ` (na'mulu) - 'na'-mu-lu'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `أَمَلٌ` (amalun) - hope (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb is typically followed by either:
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna - that, Day 39) when expressing hope about a *fact or state*. Remember, `أَنَّ` makes the noun after it take a Fatha.
            *   *Example:* `نَأْمُلُ أَنَّ السَّلَامَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Na'mulu anna as-salāma muhimun.) - We hope that peace is important.
        *   `أَنْ` (an - to, Day 38) when expressing hope about an *action*. Remember, `أَنْ` makes the present tense verb take a Fatha (Subjunctive mood).
            *   *Example:* `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ يَدًا بِيَدٍ.` (Na'mulu an na'mala yadan bi-yadin.) - We hope to work hand in hand. (`نَعْمَلَ` takes Fatha because of `أَنْ`).

### 2. Stating Necessity (`يَجِبُ أَنْ`) - It is necessary to/that...

This is a powerful way to state that something is required or crucial.

*   **New Impersonal Verb: "It is necessary"**
    *   **`يَجِبُ` (yajibu) - 'ya-ji-bu'**
    *   *How to use:* `يَجِبُ` is an impersonal verb, meaning it doesn't have a specific "he" or "she" subject. It always means "it is necessary." It is *always* followed by `أَنْ` (an - to/that), which makes the next present tense verb take a Fatha (Subjunctive mood).
    *   *Example:* `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Yajibu an naḥfaẓa al-bī'ata.) - It is necessary to protect the environment. (`نَحْفَظَ` takes Fatha because of `أَنْ`).

### 3. Introducing a Topic (`بِخُصُوصِ`) - Regarding / Concerning...

When shifting to a new point or specific subject in a formal discussion, this phrase is very useful.

*   **New Preposition-like word: `بِخُصُوصِ` (bi-khuṣūṣi) - 'bi-khu-ṣoo-ṣi'**
    *   *How to use:* This word functions like a preposition and always makes the noun immediately following it take a **Kasra**.
    *   *Example:* `بِخُصُوصِ السَّلَامِ، نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ.` (Bi-khuṣūṣi as-salāmi, na'mulu an na'mala.) - Regarding peace, we hope to work.

### 4. Describing Shared Efforts (`مُشْتَرَكٌ`) - Joint / Mutual

Diplomacy often focuses on common interests and cooperation.

*   **New Adjective: "Joint / Common / Mutual"**
    *   **`مُشْتَرَكٌ` (mushtarakun) - 'mush-ta-ra-kun'** (masculine singular)
    *   **`مُشْتَرَكَةٌ` (mushtarakatun) - 'mush-ta-ra-ka-tun'** (feminine singular)
    *   *How to use:* This is an adjective, so it needs to agree in gender, definiteness, and case with the noun it describes.
    *   *Example:* `هَذَا اتِّفَاقٌ مُشْتَرَكٌ.` (Hādhā ittifāqun mushtarakun.) - This is a joint agreement. (`اتِّفَاقٌ` is agreement, masculine from Day 50).
    *   *Example:* `الْمَصَالِحُ مُشْتَرَكَةٌ.` (Al-maṣāliḥu mushtarakatun.) - The interests are mutual/common. (New plural: `مَصَالِحُ` (maṣāliḥu) - interests, a non-human broken plural, so its adjective `مُشْتَرَكَةٌ` is feminine singular).

These phrases will significantly boost your ability to understand and participate in formal Arabic discussions. Practice combining them with all the grammar you already know!

## Examples

Let's combine our new diplomatic phrases with grammar and vocabulary you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **"We hope that the new agreement is important."**
    *   `نَأْمُلُ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ الْجَدِيدَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Na'mulu anna al-ittifāqa al-jadeeda muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَأْمُلُ` (we hope). `أَنَّ` makes `الِاتِّفَاقَ الْجَدِيدَ` (the new agreement) take Fatha.

2.  **"It is necessary to understand the problem."**
    *   `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَفْهَمَ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Yajibu an nafham al-mushkilata.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَجِبُ أَنْ` (it is necessary to). `نَفْهَمَ` (we understand - `نَفْهَمُ` from Day 48, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (the problem - Day 50) takes Fatha as object.

3.  **"Regarding the health of the people, it is important."**
    *   `بِخُصُوصِ صِحَّةِ الشَّعْبِ، هِيَ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Bi-khuṣūṣi ṣiḥḥati ash-sha'bi, hiya muhimmatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِخُصُوصِ` is the new phrase. `صِحَّةِ الشَّعْبِ` (health of the people) is an Idafa (Day 57, 48), and `صِحَّةِ` takes Kasra after `بِخُصُوصِ`. `هِيَ مُهِمَّةٌ` (it is important - referring to health).

4.  **"The minister calls for a joint solution."**
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يَدْعُو إِلَى حَلٍّ مُشْتَرَكٍ.` (Al-wazeeru yad'ū ilā ḥallin mushtarakin.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَدْعُو إِلَى` (he calls for - Day 64). `حَلٍّ مُشْتَرَكٍ` (a joint solution) takes Tanween Kasra for both noun (`حَلٌّ` solution - Masdar Day 52) and adjective after `إِلَى`.

5.  **"We believe that joint work is useful."**
    *   `نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْعَمَلَ الْمُشْتَرَكَ مُفِيدٌ.` (Na'taqidu anna al-'amala al-mushtaraka mufīdun.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (we believe that - Day 64). `الْعَمَلَ الْمُشْتَرَكَ` (the joint work) takes Fatha for both noun (`الْعَمَلُ` work - Day 29) and adjective due to `أَنَّ`.

6.  **"It is known that it is necessary to protect the environment."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّهُ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Mina al-ma'rūfi annahu yajibu an naḥfaẓa al-bī'ata.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّهُ` (it is known that it is - `أَنَّ` + `ـهُ` referring to the necessity). `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ` (it is necessary to protect the environment) is the embedded clause.

7.  **"They (m.p.) hope that the company will not fail."**
    *   `هُمْ يَأْمُلُونَ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ لَنْ تَرْسُبَ.` (Hum ya'muloona anna ash-sharikatū lan tarsuba.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَأْمُلُونَ` (they hope - plural form of `يَأْمُلُ`). `أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ` (that the company) takes Fatha. `لَنْ تَرْسُبَ` (she will not fail - Day 60, Subjunctive).

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   أَمَلٌ (amalun) - hope (masculine noun / Masdar)
*   يَأْمُلُ (ya'mulu) - he hopes (present tense verb)
*   آمُلُ (āmulū) - I hope (present tense verb)
*   نَأْمُلُ (na'mulu) - we hope (present tense verb)
*   يَجِبُ (yajibu) - it is necessary (impersonal verb)
*   بِخُصُوصِ (bi-khuṣūṣi) - regarding, concerning (preposition-like word)
*   مُشْتَرَكٌ (mushtarakun) - joint, common, mutual (masculine adjective)
*   مُشْتَرَكَةٌ (mushtarakatun) - joint, common, mutual (feminine adjective)
*   مَصْلَحَةٌ (maṣlaḥatun) - interest, benefit (feminine noun) (singular of `مَصَالِحُ`)
