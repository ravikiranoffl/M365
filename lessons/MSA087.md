# Day 87 - Headline compression

## Lesson

News headlines need to grab attention quickly and convey a lot of information in very few words. Arabic has several clever ways to "compress" sentences for this purpose. Let's look at the most common strategies.

### 1. Omitting `كَانَ` (kāna) - "Was/Were"

You know that in a past tense nominal sentence (like "The house was big"), you usually use `كَانَ` (Day 40). In headlines, especially when talking about something that happened in the past, `كَانَ` is very often left out! The reader understands it's implied.

*   **Normal sentence:** `كَانَ الْوَزِيرُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ.` (Kāna al-wazeeru fī al-qāhirati.) - The minister was in Cairo.
*   **Headline version:** `الْوَزِيرُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ.` (Al-wazeeru fī al-qāhirati.) - Minister in Cairo.
    *   *Notice:* `كَانَ` is simply removed, and the sentence remains a nominal sentence. The subject `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister) still takes Damma. `الْقَاهِرَةُ` (al-Qāhiratu) is the Arabic name for **Cairo**, a new word today.

### 2. Using Masdars (Verbal Nouns) for Actions

You've learned about Masdars (verbal nouns) like `ذَهَابٌ` (going), `مَجِيءٌ` (coming), `حِفْظٌ` (protection), `إِعْلَانٌ` (announcement), `سُؤَالٌ` (question), `ظَنٌّ` (thought) (Day 78, 79, 82, 86). These are perfect for headlines because they describe an action or event without needing a full verb and subject.

*   **New verb:** `زَارَ` (zāra) - he visited (past tense). `يَزُورُ` (yazūru) - he visits (present tense).
*   **New Masdar:** `زِيَارَةٌ` (ziyāratun) - visit (feminine noun).
*   **Normal sentence:** `زَارَ الرَّئِيسُ مِصْرَ.` (Zāra ar-ra'eecu Miṣra.) - The president visited Egypt.
*   **Headline version:** `زِيَارَةُ الرَّئِيسِ لِمِصْرَ.` (Ziyāratu ar-ra'eeci li-Miṣra.) - Visit of the President to Egypt / President's visit to Egypt.
    *   *Notice:* `زِيَارَةُ الرَّئِيسِ` is an Idafa (Day 15). `لِـ` (to/for) is used here instead of `إِلَى` (Day 17, 60). `مِصْرَ` (Egypt) is a diptote, so it takes Fatha here instead of Kasra after `لِـ`.
*   **Normal sentence:** `أُعْلِنَتِ الْخُطَّةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ.` (U'linati al-khuṭṭatū al-jadeedatu.) - The new plan was announced. (Passive voice, Day 72).
*   **Headline version:** `إِعْلَانُ الْخُطَّةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (I'lānu al-khuṭṭatī al-jadeedati.) - Announcement of the new plan / New plan announcement.
    *   *Notice:* `إِعْلَانُ الْخُطَّةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` is an Idafa. `إِعْلَانُ` is the Masdar (Day 64). `الْخُطَّةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (the new plan) takes Kasra because it's the second term of the Idafa and its adjective matches.

### 3. Using `بِـ` (bi-) for "Because of"

You know `بِسَبَبِ` (because of, Day 86) is followed by a noun in the Genitive case. In headlines, sometimes just `بِـ` (the prefix meaning "with" or "by means of," Day 10) is used on its own to imply "because of" or "due to."

*   **Normal sentence:** `لَمْ يَأْتِ الطَّبِيبُ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (Lam ya'ti aṭ-ṭabeebu bi-sababi al-mushkilati.) - The doctor did not come because of the problem.
*   **Headline version:** `غِيَابُ الطَّبِيبِ بِالْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (Ghiyābu aṭ-ṭabeebi bi-l-mushkilati.) - Doctor's absence due to problem.
    *   *New word for explanation:* `غِيَابٌ` (ghiyābun) - absence (masculine noun, Masdar of `غَابَ` to be absent).
    *   *Notice:* `غِيَابُ الطَّبِيبِ` (doctor's absence) is an Idafa. `بِالْمُشْكِلَةِ` uses `بِـ` + `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (the problem) directly to convey "because of the problem." `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` takes Kasra because of `بِـ`.

These strategies make headlines very short and direct, focusing only on the most important information.

## Examples

Let's see how these compression rules apply to different headlines!

1.  **"President in Cairo."** (Implied "was")
    *   `الرَّئِيسُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ.` (Ar-ra'eecu fī al-qāhirati.)
        *   *Normal sentence:* `كَانَ الرَّئِيسُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ.` (The president was in Cairo.)

2.  **"Visit of the Minister to the company."** (Action summarized with Masdar)
    *   `زِيَارَةُ الْوَزِيرِ لِلشَّرِكَةِ.` (Ziyāratu al-wazeeri li-sh-sharika.)
        *   *Normal sentence:* `زَارَ الْوَزِيرُ الشَّرِكَةَ.` (The minister visited the company.)

3.  **"Announcement of a new policy."** (Action summarized with Masdar)
    *   `إِعْلَانُ سِيَاسَةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ.` (I'lānu siyāsatīn jadeedatin.)
        *   *Normal sentence (passive):* `أُعْلِنَتْ سِيَاسَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (A new policy was announced.)

4.  **"Problem solved."** (Implied "is/was")
    *   `مُشْكِلَةٌ مَحْلُولَةٌ.` (Mushkilatun maḥloolatun.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `مَحْلُولٌ` (maḥloolun) - solved (masculine adjective / passive participle). `مَحْلُولَةٌ` (feminine).
        *   *Normal sentence (passive):* `تُحَلُّ الْمُشْكِلَةُ.` (The problem is solved.)

5.  **"Agreement due to joint work."** (Using `بِـ` for "because of")
    *   `اِتِّفَاقٌ بِالْعَمَلِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ.` (Ittifāqun bi-l-'amali al-mushtaraki.)
        *   *Normal sentence:* `حُقِّقَ الِاتِّفَاقُ بِسَبَبِ الْعَمَلِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ.` (The agreement was achieved because of the joint work.)

6.  **"Student in library."** (Implied "is/was")
    *   `طَالِبٌ فِي الْمَكْتَبَةِ.` (Ṭālibun fī al-maktabati.)
        *   *Normal sentence:* `الطَّالِبُ فِي الْمَكْتَبَةِ.` (The student is in the library.) or `كَانَ الطَّالِبُ فِي الْمَكْتَبَةِ.` (The student was in the library.)
