# Day 74 - Emphasis via word order

## Lesson

In English, we often use our voice to show emphasis (e.g., "The *student* reads the book" vs. "The student reads the *book*"). In Arabic, while voice is important, you can also change the order of words in a sentence to highlight what you want the listener to focus on.

Let's look at the most common, "neutral" word orders first, and then how we can change them for emphasis.

### 1. Default Word Order (Neutral Emphasis)

*   **For Nominal Sentences (Noun is Adjective / Noun is Noun):**
    *   The **Topic** (the `X` part) usually comes first, then the **Comment** (the `Y` part).
    *   *Example:* `الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-baytu kabeerun.) - The house is big.
        *   Here, `الْبَيْتُ` (the house) is the Topic, and `كَبِيرٌ` (big) is the Comment. This is the normal way to say it, with no special emphasis.
        *   *(Note: For strong emphasis in nominal sentences, you often use `إِنَّ` (indeed) at the beginning, like `إِنَّ الْبَيْتَ كَبِيرٌ.` (Indeed, the house is big), which you learned on Day 37.)*

*   **For Verbal Sentences (Verb-Subject-Object):**
    *   The **Verb** comes first, then the **Subject**, then the **Object**.
    *   *Example:* `يَقْرَأُ الطَّالِبُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Yaqra'u aṭ-ṭālibu al-kitāba.) - The student reads the book.
        *   Here, `يَقْرَأُ` (reads) is the verb, `الطَّالِبُ` (the student) is the subject, and `الْكِتَابَ` (the book) is the object. This is the normal, most common order for a verbal sentence.

### 2. Shifting Word Order for Emphasis

Now, let's see how moving words around can change the focus of a verbal sentence.

#### A. Emphasizing the Subject

If you want to emphasize *who* is doing the action, you can place the **Subject before the Verb**. When you do this, the sentence technically becomes a nominal sentence (Topic + Verbal sentence as Comment).

*   *Default:* `يَقْرَأُ الطَّالِبُ الْكِتَابَ.` (The student reads the book.)
*   *Emphasize Subject:* `الطَّالِبُ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yaqra'u al-kitāba.) - **The student** reads the book. (Not someone else, but *the student*.)
    *   *Notice:* `الطَّالِبُ` (the student) now starts the sentence, becoming the Topic. `يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ` (reads the book) is the verbal sentence acting as the Comment. The verb `يَقْرَأُ` still agrees with `الطَّالِبُ` in gender (masculine singular).

#### B. Emphasizing the Object

If you want to emphasize *what* is being acted upon, you can place the **Object before the Verb or Subject**. This is very common in Arabic!

*   *Default:* `يَقْرَأُ الطَّالِبُ الْكِتَابَ.` (The student reads the book.)
*   *Emphasize Object:* `الْكِتَابَ يَقْرَأُ الطَّالِبُ.` (Al-kitāba yaqra'u aṭ-ṭālibu.) - The student reads **the book** (not something else, but *the book*).
    *   *Notice:* `الْكِتَابَ` (the book) now comes first and still has a Fatha, showing it is still the object. The verb `يَقْرَأُ` and subject `الطَّالِبُ` follow.

#### C. Emphasizing Adverbial Phrases (Time, Place, Manner)

If you want to emphasize *when*, *where*, or *how* an action happens, you can place the **adverbial phrase at the beginning of the sentence**.

*   *Default (from Day 59, sentence 1):* `يَسْتَخْدِمُ الطَّالِبُ الْحَاسُوبَ كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ.` (The student uses the computer every morning.)
*   *Emphasize Time:* `كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ يَسْتَخْدِمُ الطَّالِبُ الْحَاسُوبَ.` (Kulla ṣabāḥin yastakhdimu aṭ-ṭālibu al-ḥāsūba.) - **Every morning** the student uses the computer (not just sometimes, but *every morning*).
    *   *Notice:* `كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ` (every morning) comes first. It still takes Fatha because it's an adverbial phrase of time.

The key takeaway is this: **The word that comes first in an Arabic sentence, especially if it's not in the default position, is usually the most emphasized word!** The case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) still apply to tell you the word's job, even if its position changes.

This flexibility makes Arabic very expressive and allows speakers to highlight what's most important in their message!

## Examples

Let's practice with more sentences you already know, showing how emphasis changes with word order.

1.  **Default (Verb-Subject-Object):**
    *   `يَحُلُّ الْوَزِيرُ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Yaḥullu al-wazeeru al-mushkilata.) - The minister solves the problem. (Neutral, focus on the action.)
    *   **Emphasize Subject:** `الْوَزِيرُ يَحُلُّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Al-wazeeru yaḥullu al-mushkilata.) - **The minister** solves the problem. (It's *him*, not someone else.)
    *   **Emphasize Object:** `الْمُشْكِلَةَ يَحُلُّ الْوَزِيرُ.` (Al-mushkilata yaḥullu al-wazeeru.) - The minister solves **the problem.** (It's *the problem* that gets solved.)

2.  **Default (Verb-Subject-Object-Time):**
    *   `تُرْسِلُ الشَّرِكَةُ رِسَالَةً الْيَوْمَ.` (Tursilu ash-sharikatū risālatan al-yawma.) - The company sends a message today. (Neutral.)
    *   **Emphasize Time:** `الْيَوْمَ تُرْسِلُ الشَّرِكَةُ رِسَالَةً.` (Al-yawma tursilu ash-sharikatū risālatan.) - **Today** the company sends a message. (It's happening *today*.)
    *   **Emphasize Subject:** `الشَّرِكَةُ تُرْسِلُ رِسَالَةً الْيَوْمَ.` (Ash-sharikatū tursilu risālatan al-yawma.) - **The company** sends a message today. (It's *the company* doing it.)
    *   **Emphasize Object:** `رِسَالَةً تُرْسِلُ الشَّرِكَةُ الْيَوْمَ.` (Risālatan tursilu ash-sharikatū al-yawma.) - The company sends **a message** today. (It's *a message*, not a report.)

3.  **Default (Verb-Subject-Prepositional Phrase):**
    *   `يَدْعُو الرَّئِيسُ إِلَى السَّلَامِ.` (Yad'ū ar-ra'eecu ilā as-salāmi.) - The president calls for peace. (Neutral.)
    *   **Emphasize Subject:** `الرَّئِيسُ يَدْعُو إِلَى السَّلَامِ.` (Ar-ra'eecu yad'ū ilā as-salāmi.) - **The president** calls for peace.
    *   **Emphasize Prepositional Phrase:** `إِلَى السَّلَامِ يَدْعُو الرَّئِيسُ.` (Ilā as-salāmi yad'ū ar-ra'eecu.) - The president calls for **peace.** (It's *peace* he's calling for, specifically.)

4.  **Nominal Sentence Example with `لَيْسَ` (Day 61):**
    *   `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرًا.` (Laysa al-baytu kabeeran.) - The house is not big. (Default for negation).
    *   **Emphasize the negated quality (using `بِـ` for emphasis - Day 61):** `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ بِكَبِيرٍ.` (Laysa al-baytu bi-kabeerin.) - The house is *not* big (at all).
        *   *Note:* While `لَيْسَ` itself provides negation, adding `بِـ` (Day 61) before its predicate adds emphasis to the negation, rather than just changing word order. Word order changes in `لَيْسَ` sentences are less common for emphasis.

You can see how just moving a word to the beginning of the sentence automatically puts a spotlight on it!
