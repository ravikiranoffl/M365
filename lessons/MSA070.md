# Day 70 - Discourse markers

## Lesson

Discourse markers are crucial for making your language flow naturally. They tell you if you're about to hear a contrasting idea, an example, a conclusion, or an additional point.

We'll learn a few very common and useful discourse markers today:

### 1. `مَعَ ذَلِكَ` (ma'a dhālika) - Nevertheless / However

This phrase is used to introduce an idea that contrasts with or is despite what was just said. It's similar to "but" (`لَكِنْ`, Day 28) but often implies a stronger "despite that" meaning.

*   **Arabic phrase:** `مَعَ ذَلِكَ` (ma'a dhālika)
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ma-a dhaa-li-ka'.
    *   *Literal translation:* "With that." (You know `مَعَ` - with, Day 27, and `ذَلِكَ` - that, Day 11).
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "Nevertheless," "however," "despite that."
*   **How to use:** Place it at the beginning of a sentence or clause to show a contrast or concession.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `الْمُشْكِلَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ، مَعَ ذَلِكَ يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَحُلَّهَا.` (Al-mushkilatu kabeeratun, ma'a dhālika yureedu al-wazeeru an yaḥullahā.)
    *   *Translation:* The problem is big; nevertheless, the minister wants to solve it.
    *   *Breakdown:* `الْمُشْكِلَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ` (the problem is big - Day 50, 3). `يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَحُلَّهَا` (the minister wants to solve it - Day 44, 50).

### 2. `عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ` (ala sabeeli al-mithāli) - For example

This phrase is used to introduce an illustration or instance that supports a previous statement.

*   **New Words for the phrase:**
    *   **`سَبِيلٌ` (sabeelun) - Way, Path** (masculine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'sa-bee-lun'.
        *   *Example:* `هَذَا سَبِيلٌ جَدِيدٌ.` (Hādhā sabeelun jadeedun.) - This is a new way.
    *   **`مِثَالٌ` (mithālun) - Example** (masculine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mi-thā-lun'.
        *   *Example:* `هَذَا مِثَالٌ كَبِيرٌ.` (Hādhā mithālun kabeerun.) - This is a big example.
*   **Arabic phrase:** `عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ` (ala sabeeli al-mithāli)
    *   *Literal translation:* "On the way of the example." (You know `عَلَى` - on, Day 52).
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "For example."
*   **How to use:** Place it before or after the example you are providing. It's often used at the beginning of a sentence or clause. `سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ` is an Idafa, taking Kasra for `سَبِيلِ` after `عَلَى`.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `هُنَاكَ مُشْكِلَاتٌ كَثِيرَةٌ، عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ، التَّلَوُّثُ.` (Hunāka mushkilātun katheeratun, 'alā sabeeli al-mithāli, at-talawwuthu.)
    *   *Translation:* There are many problems; for example, pollution.
    *   *Breakdown:* `هُنَاكَ` (there are - Day 15). `مُشْكِلَاتٌ كَثِيرَةٌ` (many problems - `مُشْكِلَةٌ` plural, Day 50; `كَثِيرَةٌ` many, feminine singular for non-human plural, Day 15). `التَّلَوُّثُ` (the pollution - Day 56).

### 3. `فِي الْخِتَامِ` (fī al-khitāmi) - In conclusion / In summary

This phrase is used to introduce a concluding statement, summary, or final thought in a formal way.

*   **New Word for the phrase:**
    *   **`خِتَامٌ` (khitāmun) - Conclusion, End** (masculine noun, also a Masdar).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'khi-tā-mun'.
        *   *Example:* `الْخِتَامُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-khitāmu muhimun.) - The conclusion is important.
*   **Arabic phrase:** `فِي الْخِتَامِ` (fī al-khitāmi)
    *   *Literal translation:* "In the conclusion." (You know `فِي` - in, Day 10).
    *   *Idiomatic meaning:* "In conclusion," "in summary," "finally."
*   **How to use:** Place it at the beginning of the concluding sentence or paragraph. It takes Kasra for `الْخِتَامِ` after `فِي`.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `فِي الْخِتَامِ، يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الصِّحَّةَ.` (Fī al-khitāmi, yajibu an naḥfaẓa aṣ-ṣiḥḥata.)
    *   *Translation:* In conclusion, it is necessary to protect health.
    *   *Breakdown:* `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الصِّحَّةَ` (it is necessary to protect health - Day 67).

### Review: `لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika) - Therefore / Thus

You learned this on Day 69, and it's a great example of a discourse marker that shows cause and effect.

*   **Arabic word:** `لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika)
    *   *Meaning:* "Therefore," "thus," "for that reason."
*   **How to use:** Connects a cause to its effect or a premise to its conclusion.
*   **Example sentence:**
    `الْبِيئَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ، لِذَلِكَ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَهَا.` (Al-bī'atu muhimmatun, li-dhālika yajibu an naḥfaẓahā.)
    *   *Translation:* The environment is important; therefore, it is necessary to protect it.

These discourse markers will help you create more sophisticated and logical connections in your Arabic sentences. Keep practicing how to use them to guide your reader or listener!

## Examples

Let's combine our new discourse markers with grammar and vocabulary you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **"The new law is difficult, nevertheless the judge wants to understand it."**
    *   `الْقَانُونُ الْجَدِيدُ صَعْبٌ، مَعَ ذَلِكَ يُرِيدُ الْقَاضِي أَنْ يَفْهَمَهُ.` (Al-qānūnu al-jadeedu ṣa'bun, ma'a dhālika yureedu al-qāḍī an yafhamahu.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْقَانُونُ الْجَدِيدُ صَعْبٌ` (the new law is difficult - Day 51, 3, 27). `يُرِيدُ الْقَاضِي أَنْ يَفْهَمَهُ` (the judge wants to understand it - Day 44, 48).

2.  **"There are many new ideas, for example, the idea of joint work."**
    *   `هُنَاكَ أَفْكَارٌ جَدِيدَةٌ كَثِيرَةٌ، عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ، فِكْرَةُ الْعَمَلِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ.` (Hunāka afkārun jadeedatun katheeratun, 'alā sabeeli al-mithāli, fikratu al-'amali al-mushtaraki.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `فِكْرَةٌ` (fikratun) - idea (feminine noun). `أَفْكَارٌ` (afkārun) - ideas (non-human broken plural). This is new, so I'll simplify.
        *   *Revised Example 2:* **"There are many new solutions, for example, the joint solution."**
            `هُنَاكَ حُلُولٌ جَدِيدَةٌ كَثِيرَةٌ، عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ، الْحَلُّ الْمُشْتَرَكُ.` (Hunāka ḥulūlun jadeedatun katheeratun, 'alā sabeeli al-mithāli, al-ḥallu al-mushtaraka.)
            *   *Notice:* `حُلُولٌ جَدِيدَةٌ كَثِيرَةٌ` (many new solutions - `حَلٌّ` solution, plural `حُلُولٌ`, Day 52; `كَثِيرَةٌ` many, feminine singular). `الْحَلُّ الْمُشْتَرَكُ` (the joint solution - Day 67).

3.  **"In conclusion, we believe that education is important for the people."**
    *   `فِي الْخِتَامِ، نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الدِّرَاسَةَ مُهِمَّةٌ لِلشَّعْبِ.` (Fī al-khitāmi, na'taqidu anna ad-dirāsata muhimmatun li-sh-sha'bi.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (we believe that - Day 64). `الدِّرَاسَةَ` (studying/education - Masdar Day 43) takes Fatha. `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important - feminine for `الدِّرَاسَةَ`).

4.  **"It is clear that the student wants to succeed, therefore he studies a lot."**
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَنْجَحَ، لِذَلِكَ يَدْرُسُ كَثِيرًا.` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna aṭ-ṭāliba yureedu an yanjaḥa, li-dhālika yadrusu katheeran.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (it is clear that - Day 69). `الطَّالِبَ` takes Fatha. `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَنْجَحَ` (he wants to succeed - Day 44, 58). `يَدْرُسُ كَثِيرًا` (he studies a lot - Day 19, `كَثِيرًا` adverb Day 27).

5.  **"The company announced that it will protect the environment, for example, the clean water."**
    *   `الشَّرِكَةُ أَعْلَنَتْ أَنَّهَا سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ، عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ، الْمَاءَ النَّظِيفَ.` (Ash-sharikatū a'lanat annahā sa-taḥfaẓu al-bī'ata, 'alā sabeeli al-mithāli, al-mā'a an-naẓīfa.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَعْلَنَتْ أَنَّهَا سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ` (she announced that she will protect the environment - Day 64). `الْمَاءَ النَّظِيفَ` (the clean water - Day 56) is the example, taking Fatha to match the implied object (or simply being listed).
