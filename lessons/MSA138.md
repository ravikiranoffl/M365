# Day 138 - Fluency: Environment reporting

## Lesson

"Fluency: Environment reporting" today is all about integrated application and advanced synthesis. You already possess a strong vocabulary for discussing `بِيئَةٌ` (environment), `تَلَوُّثٌ` (pollution), `مُنَاخٌ` (climate), `حِمَايَةٌ` (protection), `مُلَوَّثٌ` (polluted), `نَظِيفٌ` (clean), `مَوَارِدُ` (resources), `مَاءٌ` (water), `هَوَاءٌ` (air), `أَرْضٌ` (Earth), and `تَقْلِيلٌ` (reduction). The goal now is to use these terms within even more complex sentences, connecting environmental ideas to political, economic, scientific, or cultural contexts, and employing your full range of advanced connectors, stance markers, and argumentation phrases.

Think of yourself as a seasoned analyst, capable of crafting insightful reports or discussions where you seamlessly blend environmental observations with analytical points, drawing deep connections between different aspects of a society.

To achieve this high level of fluency, remember to consistently apply and integrate:

1.  **Your `Environment Reporting` Vocabulary (Days 124 & 131):**
    *   `بِيئَةٌ` (environment)
    *   `تَلَوُّثٌ` (pollution)
    *   `مُنَاخٌ` (climate)
    *   `يَحْمِي` / `تَحْمِي` (he/she protects)
    *   `حِمَايَةٌ` (protection)
    *   `مُلَوَّثٌ` / `مُلَوَّثَةٌ` (polluted)
    *   `نَظِيفٌ` / `نَظِيفَةٌ` (clean)
    *   `مَوَارِدُ` (resources)
    *   `مَاءٌ` (water)
    *   `هَوَاءٌ` (air)
    *   `أَرْضٌ` (earth, land)
    *   `تَقْلِيلٌ` (reduction, decrease)
    *   `تَنْمِيَةٌ مُسْتَدَامَةٌ` (sustainable development)
    *   `دُوَلٌ` (countries)
    *   `تَقِلُّ` (she decreases)
    *   `عَمَلٌ جَمَاعِيٌّ دَوْلِيٌّ` (collective international work)
    *   `كَوْكَبٌ` (planet)
    *   `بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ` (in a negative way)

2.  **Your `Argumentation Structures` (Days 125 & 132):** Use these to make claims, provide evidence, acknowledge contrasts, and draw conclusions.
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (It is certain that...)
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (It is known that...)
    *   `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ` (There is no doubt that...)
    *   `يَعْتَقِدُ ... أَنَّ` (... believes that...)
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (It seems that...)
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (It is clear that...)
    *   `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ` (It cannot be denied that...)
    *   `وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ` (And the evidence for that is that...)
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Unfortunately...)
    *   `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (It is strange that...)

3.  **Your `Advanced Connectors` (Days 107-108):** Ensure smooth transitions and logical relationships between ideas.
    *   `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover...)
    *   `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (On the other hand...)
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that...)
    *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (In light of that...)
    *   `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (And accordingly...)
    *   `خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ` (Especially since...)
    *   `وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (At the same time...)
    *   `لِذَلِكَ` (Therefore...)

4.  **Impeccable `Grammatical Accuracy` (Days 118-119):** Pay meticulous attention to noun case endings, adjective agreement (especially with non-human plurals and diptotes), verb-subject agreement, Idafa constructions, `أَنَّ` vs `أَنْ`, and proper conjugation.

This session will push you to articulate nuanced and sophisticated insights about the environment, linking it to various aspects of society and demonstrating a high level of Arabic proficiency.

## Examples

Let's look at some examples that seamlessly integrate environment reporting with other learned concepts. Carefully read each English sentence, then the Arabic translation, and pay close attention to the breakdown of how different elements are combined.

1.  **"It cannot be denied that climate change is a global challenge. And the evidence for that is that scientific research shows a big increase in air pollution. Therefore, environmental protection needs more collective international work."**
    *   `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ تَحَدٍّ عَالَمِيٌّ. وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ. لِذَلِكَ، تَحْتَاجُ حِمَايَةُ الْبِيئَةِ إِلَى مَزِيدٍ مِنَ الْعَمَلِ الْجَمَاعِيِّ الدَّوْلِيِّ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ` (It cannot be denied that - Day 132).
        *   `تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ تَحَدٍّ عَالَمِيٌّ` (climate change is a global challenge - `تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ` (change of climate, Day 113/124) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `تَحَدٍّ عَالَمِيٌّ` (global challenge, Day 105/106) Nominative predicate).
        *   `وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ` (And the evidence for that is that - Day 132).
        *   `الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ` (scientific research shows a big increase in air pollution - `الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ` (scientific research, Day 123) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `تُظْهِرُ` (she shows, Day 115) feminine verb for non-human plural; `زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً` (big increase, Day 115/54); `فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ` (in air pollution, Day 124) Genitive after `فِي`).
        *   `لِذَلِكَ، تَحْتَاجُ حِمَايَةُ الْبِيئَةِ إِلَى مَزِيدٍ مِنَ الْعَمَلِ الْجَمَاعِيِّ الدَّوْلِيِّ` (Therefore, environmental protection needs more collective international work - `لِذَلِكَ` (Day 132); `تَحْتَاجُ إِلَى` (she needs, Day 132); `حِمَايَةُ الْبِيئَةِ` (environmental protection, Day 124) Nominative subject Idafa; `مَزِيدٍ مِنَ` (more of, Day 110); `الْعَمَلِ الْجَمَاعِيِّ الدَّوْلِيِّ` (collective international work, Day 131)).

2.  **"Despite the fact that clean water resources are decreasing noticeably, the government wants to promote sustainable development. On the other hand, many countries believe that new technologies can help solve this problem."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ مَوَارِدَ الْمَاءِ النَّظِيفِ تَقِلُّ بِشَكْلٍ مَلْحُوظٍ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تُعَزِّزَ التَّنْمِيَةَ الْمُسْتَدَامَةَ. مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، تَعْتَقِدُ دُوَلٌ كَثِيرَةٌ أَنَّ التِّقْنِيَّاتِ الْحَدِيثَةَ يُمْكِنُ أَنْ تُسَاعِدَ فِي حَلِّ هَذِهِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that - Day 107).
        *   `مَوَارِدَ الْمَاءِ النَّظِيفِ تَقِلُّ بِشَكْلٍ مَلْحُوظٍ` (clean water resources are decreasing noticeably - `مَوَارِدَ الْمَاءِ النَّظِيفِ` (clean water resources, Day 124/54) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `تَقِلُّ` (she decreases, Day 131); `بِشَكْلٍ مَلْحُوظٍ` (noticeably, Day 125/113)).
        *   `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تُعَزِّزَ التَّنْمِيَةَ الْمُسْتَدَامَةَ` (the government wants to promote sustainable development - `تُرِيدُ` (Day 79); `الْحُكُومَةُ` (Day 101); `أَنْ تُعَزِّزَ` (to promote, Day 127) Subjunctive; `التَّنْمِيَةَ الْمُسْتَدَامَةَ` (sustainable development, Day 131) Accusative object).
        *   `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (On the other hand - Day 108).
        *   `تَعْتَقِدُ دُوَلٌ كَثِيرَةٌ أَنَّ التِّقْنِيَّاتِ الْحَدِيثَةَ يُمْكِنُ أَنْ تُسَاعِدَ فِي حَلِّ هَذِهِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (many countries believe that new technologies can help solve this problem - `تَعْتَقِدُ` (she believes, Day 79); `دُوَلٌ كَثِيرَةٌ` (many countries, Day 131/110) Nominative subject; `أَنَّ التِّقْنِيَّاتِ الْحَدِيثَةَ` (that modern technologies, Day 130) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `يُمْكِنُ أَنْ تُسَاعِدَ` (can help - `يُمْكِنُ أَنْ` (Day 101); `تُسَاعِدَ` (to help, Day 116) Subjunctive); `فِي حَلِّ هَذِهِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (in solving this problem - `حَلٌّ` (solution/solving, Day 105), `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem, Day 105) Genitive Idafa).

3.  **"Unfortunately, the polluted environment affects cultural traditions in a negative way. In light of that, it seems that there is an urgent need for new scientific experiments for pollution reduction."**
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، تُؤَثِّرُ الْبِيئَةُ الْمُلَوَّثَةُ فِي التَّقَالِيدِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ. فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يَبْدُو أَنَّ هُنَاكَ حَاجَةً مُلِحَّةً لِتَجَارِبَ عِلْمِيَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ لِتَقْلِيلِ التَّلَوُّثِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Unfortunately - Day 99).
        *   `تُؤَثِّرُ الْبِيئَةُ الْمُلَوَّثَةُ فِي التَّقَالِيدِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ` (the polluted environment affects cultural traditions in a negative way - `تُؤَثِّرُ فِي` (she affects, Day 122); `الْبِيئَةُ الْمُلَوَّثَةُ` (polluted environment, Day 124) Nominative subject; `التَّقَالِيدِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ` (cultural traditions, Day 122) Genitive after `فِي`; `بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ` (in a negative way, Day 131) adverbial phrase).
        *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (In light of that - Day 108).
        *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ هُنَاكَ حَاجَةً مُلِحَّةً لِتَجَارِبَ عِلْمِيَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ لِتَقْلِيلِ التَّلَوُّثِ` (it seems that there is an urgent need for new scientific experiments for pollution reduction - `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that, Day 125); `هُنَاكَ حَاجَةً مُلِحَّةً` (there is an urgent need, Day 133) Accusative predicate + subject of `أَنَّ`; `لِتَجَارِبَ عِلْمِيَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ` (for new scientific experiments - `تَجَارِبَ` (experiments, Day 123) Genitive diptote, takes Fatha; `عِلْمِيَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ` (scientific new, Day 123/54) Genitive feminine singular adjectives for non-human plural; `لِتَقْلِيلِ التَّلَوُّثِ` (for pollution reduction, Day 124) Genitive Idafa).
