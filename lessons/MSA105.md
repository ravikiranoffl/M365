# Day 105 - Conflict vocabulary

## Lesson

Understanding terms related to conflict is crucial for anyone engaging with Arabic media or formal discussions about global events. These words often appear in news reports about international relations, security, and humanitarian issues.

Let's learn some new and essential conflict vocabulary!

### 1. `حَرْبٌ` (ḥarbun) - War

This noun refers to a state of armed conflict between different countries or different groups within a country.

*   **New Word:** `حَرْبٌ` (ḥarbun) - 'ḥar-bun'
    *   *Meaning:* War (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْحَرْبُ صَعْبَةٌ.` (Al-ḥarbu ṣa'batun.) - The war is difficult.

### 2. `أَزْمَةٌ` (azmatun) - Crisis

This noun refers to a time of intense difficulty, trouble, or danger. You already know `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem) from Day 50, but `أَزْمَةٌ` implies something more severe or urgent.

*   **New Word:** `أَزْمَةٌ` (azmatun) - 'az-ma-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Crisis (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْأَزْمَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-azmatu kabeeratun.) - The crisis is big.

### 3. `خَطَرٌ` (khaṭarun) - Danger

This noun refers to the possibility of suffering harm or injury.

*   **New Word:** `خَطَرٌ` (khaṭarun) - 'kha-ṭa-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Danger (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `هَذَا خَطَرٌ.` (Hādhā khaṭarun.) - This is a danger.

### 4. `تَهْدِيدٌ` (tahdeedun) - Threat

This noun refers to a statement of an intention to inflict pain, injury, damage, or other hostile action on someone in retribution for something done or not done.

*   **New Word:** `تَهْدِيدٌ` (tahdeedun) - 'tah-dee-dun'
    *   *Meaning:* Threat (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْقَرَارُ تَهْدِيدٌ.` (Al-qarāru tahdeedun.) - The decision is a threat.

### 5. `هُجُومٌ` (hujūmun) - Attack

This noun refers to an aggressive and violent act against a person or place.

*   **New Word:** `هُجُومٌ` (hujūmun) - 'hu-joo-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Attack (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْهُجُومُ قَوِيٌّ.` (Al-hujūmu qawiyyun.) - The attack is strong. (Remember `قَوِيٌّ` - strong, from Day 70).

### 6. `دِفَاعٌ` (difāʿun) - Defense

This noun refers to the action of defending or protecting from attack. It is the opposite of `هُجُومٌ`.

*   **New Word:** `دِفَاعٌ` (difāʿun) - 'di-fā-ʿun'
    *   *Meaning:* Defense (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الدِّفَاعُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Ad-difāʿu muhimun.) - The defense is important.

### 7. `ضَحِيَّةٌ` (ḍaḥiyyatun) - Victim

This noun refers to a person harmed, injured, or killed as a result of a crime, accident, or other event or action.

*   **New Word:** `ضَحِيَّةٌ` (ḍaḥiyyatun) - 'ḍa-ḥee-ya-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Victim (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `هِيَ ضَحِيَّةٌ.` (Hiya ḍaḥiyyatun.) - She is a victim.

### 8. `لَاجِئٌ` (lāji'un) - Refugee

This noun refers to a person who has been forced to leave their country in order to escape war, persecution, or natural disaster.

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `لَاجِئٌ` (lāji'un) - 'lā-ji-un'
    *   *Meaning:* Refugee (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `لَاجِئَةٌ` (lāji'atun) - 'lā-ji-a-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Refugee (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `هُوَ لَاجِئٌ.` (Huwa lāji'un.) - He is a refugee.

### 9. `عُنْفٌ` (ʿunfun) - Violence

This noun refers to behavior involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill someone or something.

*   **New Word:** `عُنْفٌ` (ʿunfun) - 'ʿun-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* Violence (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ لَا عُنْفَ.` (Yureedu al-jamee'u lā 'unfa.) - Everyone wants no violence. (`لَا عُنْفَ` - no violence, similar to `لَا شَكَّ` from Day 99).

### 10. `إِرْهَابٌ` (irhābun) - Terrorism

This noun refers to the unlawful use of violence and intimidation, especially against civilians, in the pursuit of political aims.

*   **New Word:** `إِرْهَابٌ` (irhābun) - 'ir-hā-bun'
    *   *Meaning:* Terrorism (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْإِرْهَابُ خَطَرٌ.` (Al-irhābu khaṭarun.) - Terrorism is a danger.

Remember the words you already know that fit into this context: `صِرَاعٌ` (conflict, Day 104), `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem, Day 50), `حَلٌّ` (solution, Day 97), and `سَلَامٌ` (peace, Day 94).

These terms are essential for understanding news and discussions about international politics, security, and humanitarian situations.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new conflict words into sentences using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the war became difficult."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْحَرْبَ أَصْبَحَتْ صَعْبَةً.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-ḥarba aṣbaḥat ṣa'batan.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْحَرْبَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `أَصْبَحَتْ صَعْبَةً` (became difficult - feminine verb for feminine subject, Day 98).

2.  **"The government wants to solve the big crisis."**
    *   `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحُلَّ الْأَزْمَةَ الْكَبِيرَةَ.` (Tureedu al-ḥukūmatu an taḥulla al-azmata al-kabeerata.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ` (known, Day 101). `أَنْ تَحُلَّ` (to solve - Day 93, Subjunctive). `الْأَزْمَةَ الْكَبِيرَةَ` (the big crisis - Accusative).

3.  **"Perhaps the threat will make the relations difficult."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا يَجْعَلُ التَّهْدِيدُ الْعَلَاقَاتِ صَعْبَةً.` (Rubbamā yaj'alu at-tahdeedu al-'alāqāti ṣa'batan.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (Day 80). `يَجْعَلُ` (makes - Day 98). `التَّهْدِيدُ` (the threat - subject). `الْعَلَاقَاتِ صَعْبَةً` (the relations difficult - both Accusative, `عَلَاقَاتٍ` is sound feminine plural, so `ـاتِ` for Accusative, Day 36; `صَعْبَةً` is feminine singular adjective for non-human plural, Day 27).

4.  **"It is known that violence is a danger to the state."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الْعُنْفَ خَطَرٌ عَلَى الدَّوْلَةِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna al-'unfa khaṭarun 'alā ad-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْعُنْفَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `خَطَرٌ` (a danger - Nominative comment). `عَلَى الدَّوْلَةِ` (to the state - `عَلَى` preposition, Day 17).

5.  **"The ambassador (m.) believes that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs will undertake the defense."**
    *   `يَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرُ أَنَّ وِزَارَةَ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ سَتَقُومُ بِالدِّفَاعِ.` (Ya'taqidu as-safeeru anna wizārata al-khārijiyyati sataqūmu bi-d-difāʿi.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرُ أَنَّ` (Day 79, Day 101). `وِزَارَةَ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ` (Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Idafa, Day 104). `سَتَقُومُ بِالدِّفَاعِ` (will undertake the defense - feminine verb for `وِزَارَةُ`, Day 98).

6.  **"Unfortunately, many refugees are victims of the terrorism."**
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ اللَّاجِئِينَ ضَحَايَا الْإِرْهَابِ.` (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, al-katheeru mina al-lāji'eena ḍaḥāyā al-irhābi.)
        *   *New words for explanation:* `اللَّاجِئِينَ` (refugees - plural of `لَاجِئٌ`, Accusative/Genitive form, Day 36). `ضَحَايَا` (ḍaḥāyā) - victims (plural of `ضَحِيَّةٌ`). This is a new plural noun for `ضحية`. Let's rephrase to use only singular forms to simplify, as plural of `ضَحِيَّةٌ` is irregular.
        *   **Revised 6:** **"Unfortunately, the refugee (m.) is a victim of the terrorism."**
        *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، اللَّاجِئُ ضَحِيَّةُ الْإِرْهَابِ.` (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, al-lāji'u ḍaḥiyyatu al-irhābi.)
            *   *Notice:* `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Day 99). `اللَّاجِئُ` (the refugee - subject). `ضَحِيَّةُ الْإِرْهَابِ` (victim of terrorism - Idafa).

These examples show how you can combine new vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences about conflict and related issues.
