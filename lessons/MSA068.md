# Day 68 - Reporting verbs

## Lesson

Reporting verbs are like the connectors between a speaker and the message they are conveying. They usually introduce a clause (a mini-sentence) that contains the reported information. In Arabic, these clauses are most often introduced by `أَنَّ` (anna - that) for facts/statements, or `أَنْ` (an - to/that) for actions/desires.

Let's review the reporting verbs you already know and then add some new ones!

### Reporting Verbs you already know (mostly with `أَنَّ`)

1.  **`قَالَ` (qāla) / `يَقُولُ` (yaqūlu) - "he said / he says" (Day 39)**
    *   *Usage:* `قَالَ أَنَّ الْبَيْتَ جَمِيلٌ.` (Qāla anna al-bayta jameelun.) - He said that the house is beautiful.
    *   *Remember:* `أَنَّ` makes `الْبَيْتَ` take Fatha.
2.  **`يُؤَكِّدُ` (yu'akkidu) - "he affirms / emphasizes" (Day 64)**
    *   *Usage:* `الرَّئِيسُ يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ السَّلَامَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Ar-ra'eecu yu'akkidu anna as-salāma muhimun.) - The president affirms that peace is important.
3.  **`يُعْلِنُ` (yu'linu) - "he announces / declares" (Day 64)**
    *   *Usage:* `الْوَزِيرُ يُعْلِنُ أَنَّ اتِّفَاقًا جَدِيدًا مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-wazeeru yu'linu anna ittifāqan jadeedan muhimun.) - The minister announces that a new agreement is important.
4.  **`يَعْتَقِدُ` (ya'taqidu) - "he believes" (Day 64)**
    *   *Usage:* `نَحْنُ نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْعَدْلَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Naḥnu na'taqidu anna al-'adla muhimun.) - We believe that justice is important.
5.  **`يَأْمُلُ` (ya'mulu) - "he hopes" (Day 67)**
    *   *Usage with `أَنَّ` (for a fact):* `نَأْمُلُ أَنَّ الصِّحَّةَ جَيِّدَةٌ.` (Na'mulu anna aṣ-ṣiḥḥata jayyidatun.) - We hope that health is good.
    *   *Usage with `أَنْ` (for an action):* `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Na'mulu an naḥfaẓa al-bī'ata.) - We hope to protect the environment.
    *   *Remember:* `أَنْ` makes the verb (`نَحْفَظَ`) take Fatha (Subjunctive).

### New Reporting Verbs (also mostly with `أَنَّ`)

Now, let's add two more very common reporting verbs to your vocabulary!

1.  **"To Know"**
    *   **Root:** `ع ل م` (a-l-m)
    *   **Past Tense (he knew):** `عَلِمَ` (alima) - 'a-li-ma'.
    *   **Present Tense (he knows):** `يَعْلَمُ` (ya'lamu) - 'ya'-la-mu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I know):** `أَعْلَمُ` (a'lamu) - 'a'-la-mu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she knows / you-m.s. know):** `تَعْلَمُ` (ta'lamu) - 'ta'-la-mu'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `عِلْمٌ` (ilmun) - knowledge (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb is typically followed by `أَنَّ` (that) to report a known fact.
    *   *Example:* `الْمُدَرِّسُ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ نَاجِحٌ.` (Al-mudarrisu ya'lamu anna aṭ-ṭāliba nājiḥun.) - The teacher knows that the student is successful.

2.  **"To Explain / To Clarify"**
    *   **Root:** `ش ر ح` (sh-r-ḥ)
    *   **Past Tense (he explained):** `شَرَحَ` (sharaḥa) - 'sha-ra-ḥa'.
    *   **Present Tense (he explains):** `يَشْرَحُ` (yashraḥu) - 'yash-ra-ḥu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I explain):** `أَشْرَحُ` (ashraḥu) - 'ash-ra-ḥu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she explains / you-m.s. explain):** `تَشْرَحُ` (tashraḥu) - 'tash-ra-ḥu'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `شَرْحٌ` (sharḥun) - explanation (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb is often followed by `أَنَّ` to explain a fact.
    *   *Example:* `الْقَاضِي يَشْرَحُ أَنَّ الْقَانُونَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-qāḍī yashraḥu anna al-qānūna muhimun.) - The judge explains that the law is important.

### Key Rules for Reporting Verbs

Remember these two important rules when using reporting verbs that introduce `أَنَّ` or `أَنْ`:

*   **Rule for `أَنَّ`:** If the reported information is a statement or a fact, you use `أَنَّ`. The noun directly after `أَنَّ` (its subject) **always takes a Fatha** (َ).
    *   Example: `قَالَ أَنَّ الْمَدْرَسَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Qāla anna al-madrasata kabeeratun.) - He said that the school is big.
*   **Rule for `أَنْ`:** If the reported information is an action or desire, you use `أَنْ`. The present tense verb directly after `أَنْ` **always takes a Fatha** (َ) (Subjunctive Mood).
    *   Example: `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَذْهَبَ.` (Yureedu an yadhhaba.) - He wants to go. (Note: `يُرِيدُ` is also a type of reporting verb in a broader sense, stating what someone wants).

You're building a strong foundation for expressing complex ideas in Arabic! These reporting verbs are everywhere in formal and informal speech.

## Examples

Let's see these reporting verbs in action with sentences using vocabulary you already know!

1.  **"The minister knows that the company is new."**
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-wazeeru ya'lamu anna ash-sharikatā jadeedatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَعْلَمُ` (he knows) is the reporting verb. `أَنَّ` makes `الشَّرِكَةَ` (the company) take Fatha.

2.  **"She explained that the information is useful."**
    *   `هِيَ تَشْرَحُ أَنَّ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ مُفِيدَةٌ.` (Hiya tashraḥu anna al-ma'lūmāti mufīdatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `تَشْرَحُ` (she explains). `أَنَّ` makes `الْمَعْلُومَاتِ` (the information) take Kasra, because `مَعْلُومَاتٌ` is a feminine sound plural (like `ـَاتٍ` or `ـَاتِ` in Fatha/Kasra cases). `مُفِيدَةٌ` (useful) is feminine singular because `مَعْلُومَاتٌ` is treated as feminine singular for adjectives.

3.  **"The student (f.) said that she wants to succeed."**
    *   `قَالَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ أَنَّهَا تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَنْجَحَ.` (Qālati aṭ-ṭālibatū annahā tureedu an tanjaḥa.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَتْ` (she said). `أَنَّهَا` (that she). `تُرِيدُ` (she wants). `أَنْ تَنْجَحَ` (to succeed - Subjunctive with Fatha).

4.  **"We affirm that protecting the environment is important."**
    *   `نُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ حِفْظَ الْبِيئَةِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Nu'akkidu anna ḥifẓa al-bī'ati muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `نُؤَكِّدُ` (we affirm). `أَنَّ` makes `حِفْظَ` (protection of - Masdar) take Fatha. `الْبِيئَةِ` (the environment - Idafa). `مُهِمٌّ` (important - masculine for `حِفْظَ`).

5.  **"The president hopes to solve the problem of pollution."**
    *   `الرَّئِيسُ يَأْمُلُ أَنْ يَحُلَّ مُشْكِلَةَ التَّلَوُّثِ.` (Ar-ra'eecu ya'mulu an yaḥulla mushkilata at-talawwuthi.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَأْمُلُ` (he hopes). `أَنْ يَحُلَّ` (to solve - Subjunctive with Fatha). `مُشْكِلَةَ التَّلَوُّثِ` (problem of pollution - chained Idafa).

6.  **"He believes that the judge's heart is kind."**
    *   `هُوَ يَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ قَلْبَ الْقَاضِي طَيِّبٌ.` (Huwa ya'taqidu anna qalba al-qāḍī ṭayyibun.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَعْتَقِدُ` (he believes). `أَنَّ` makes `قَلْبَ` (heart of - Idafa) take Fatha. `الْقَاضِي` (the judge - Idafa). `طَيِّبٌ` (kind - masculine for `قَلْبَ`).

7.  **"I know that the lesson is not small."**
    *   `أَنَا أَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الدَّرْسَ لَيْسَ صَغِيرًا.` (Ana a'lamu anna ad-darsa laysa ṣagheeran.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَعْلَمُ` (I know). `أَنَّ` makes `الدَّرْسَ` (the lesson) take Fatha. `لَيْسَ صَغِيرًا` (it is not small - Day 61 negation, `صَغِيرًا` takes Fatha).
