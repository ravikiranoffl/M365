# Day 114 - Speech listening practice

## Lesson

"Speech listening practice" is exactly what it sounds like: training your ear to understand spoken Arabic. Even though we're learning through text, we can simulate this by presenting sentences and encouraging you to "listen" to them (read them aloud or in your head) before seeing the translation or breakdown. This helps your brain process Arabic as a sound rather than just a collection of letters.

The goal today is to integrate *all* your recent knowledge into active listening. This means:

1.  **Listen for the big picture:** Try to get the overall meaning of the sentence first.
2.  **Identify familiar vocabulary:** Can you pick out the nouns (like `حُكُومَةٌ`, `اتِّفَاقٌ`, `اِقْتِصَادٌ`), verbs (like `يُرِيدُ`, `يَقُومُ`, `أَصْبَحَ`), and adjectives (like `مُهِمٌّ`, `صَعْبٌ`)?
3.  **Recognize grammatical structures:** Can you hear the `أَنَّ` clause opening? The prepositional phrases like `بِسَبَبِ` or `عَنْ`? The stance markers like `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ`? The advanced connectors like `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ`? The active (`دَارِسٌ`) or passive (`مَدْرُوسٌ`) participles?
4.  **Pay attention to Harakat (vowels):** The Harakat tell you the case endings, which are vital for understanding who is doing what to whom, and how words relate to each other in Idafa constructions.

For each example below, I want you to:
*   **Read the Arabic sentence aloud (or clearly in your mind) at least twice.** Pretend you are hearing it spoken.
*   **Try to understand the full meaning.** Don't rush to the translation.
*   **Mentally (or on paper) break it down.** What words do you recognize? What structures?
*   **Only then, look at the English translation and the breakdown.** Compare your understanding.

This active "listening" will significantly boost your comprehension skills!

## Examples

Here are some complex Arabic sentences combining various structures and vocabulary you've learned. Remember the steps: **Listen first, understand, then check.**

1.  **Arabic Sentence:**
    `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ الْعَلَاقَاتِ الدَّوْلِيَّةَ قَوِيَّةً.`

    *   **Your Turn:** Read aloud and understand.
    *   **English Translation:** It is certain that the government wants to make the international relations strong.
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (It is certain that - Stance marker, Day 99).
        *   `الْحُكُومَةَ` (the government - Accusative, subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 101).
        *   `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ` (wants to make - Light verb, Day 98, Subjunctive).
        *   `الْعَلَاقَاتِ الدَّوْلِيَّةَ` (the international relations - `الْعَلَاقَاتِ` is Accusative object of `تَجْعَلَ`, sound feminine plural, Day 104; `الدَّوْلِيَّةَ` (international) is Accusative feminine adjective, from `دَوْلَةٌ`, Day 101).
        *   `قَوِيَّةً` (strong - Accusative second object of `تَجْعَلَ`, feminine singular adjective for non-human plural `الْعَلَاقَاتِ`, Day 27).

2.  **Arabic Sentence:**
    `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْأَزْمَةَ صَعْبَةٌ، يَرْغَبُ الْقَاضِي الْعَالِمُ فِي تَطْبِيقِ الْعَدَالَةِ.`

    *   **Your Turn:** Read aloud and understand.
    *   **English Translation:** Despite the fact that the crisis is difficult, the knowing judge desires the application of justice.
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `الْأَزْمَةَ صَعْبَةٌ` (the crisis is difficult - `الْأَزْمَةَ` is Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 105; `صَعْبَةٌ` is Nominative comment, feminine adjective, Day 27).
        *   `يَرْغَبُ الْقَاضِي الْعَالِمُ فِي` (the knowing judge desires - `يَرْغَبُ فِي` is Verb + preposition collocation, Day 97; `الْقَاضِي الْعَالِمُ` is Apposition, `الْقَاضِي` is Nominative, Day 102; `الْعَالِمُ` is active participle, Nominative, Day 112).
        *   `تَطْبِيقِ الْعَدَالَةِ` (the application of justice - Idafa, `تَطْبِيقِ` (application) is Masdar, Genitive after `فِي`, Day 113; `الْعَدَالَةِ` is Genitive, Day 102).

3.  **Arabic Sentence:**
    `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، الْإِرْهَابُ خَطَرٌ كَبِيرٌ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، لَمْ تَحْفَظِ الشُّرْطَةُ الْأَمْنَ فِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ.`

    *   **Your Turn:** Read aloud and understand.
    *   **English Translation:** Unfortunately, terrorism is a great danger. Moreover, the police did not protect security at the same time.
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Unfortunately - Stance marker, Day 99).
        *   `الْإِرْهَابُ خَطَرٌ كَبِيرٌ` (terrorism is a great danger - `الْإِرْهَابُ` is Nominative, Day 105; `خَطَرٌ كَبِيرٌ` is Nominative predicate, Day 105, 54).
        *   `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `لَمْ تَحْفَظِ الشُّرْطَةُ الْأَمْنَ` (the police did not protect security - `لَمْ تَحْفَظِ` is Jussive, Day 60; `الشُّرْطَةُ` is Nominative, Day 102; `الْأَمْنَ` is Accusative, direct object, Day 106).
        *   `فِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (at the same time - Advanced connector, Day 108).

4.  **Arabic Sentence:**
    `يَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرُ مُحَمَّدٌ أَنَّ دِرَاسَةَ الِاقْتِصَادِ مُهِمَّةٌ، خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ الْمَالُ لَيْسَ كَافِيًا لِتَحْقِيقِ النَّجَاحِ.`

    *   **Your Turn:** Read aloud and understand.
    *   **English Translation:** Ambassador Muhammad believes that the study of the economy is important, especially since the money is not enough to achieve success.
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `يَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرُ مُحَمَّدٌ أَنَّ` (Ambassador Muhammad believes that - `يَعْتَقِدُ` is Verb, Day 79; `السَّفِيرُ مُحَمَّدٌ` is Apposition, Nominative, Day 101, 85).
        *   `دِرَاسَةَ الِاقْتِصَادِ مُهِمَّةٌ` (the study of the economy is important - `دِرَاسَةَ` is Masdar, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, first term of Idafa, Day 111; `الِاقْتِصَادِ` is Genitive, second term of Idafa, Day 103; `مُهِمَّةٌ` is Nominative comment, feminine adjective, Day 27).
        *   `خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ` (especially since - Advanced connector, Day 108).
        *   `الْمَالُ لَيْسَ كَافِيًا لِتَحْقِيقِ النَّجَاحِ` (the money is not enough to achieve success - `الْمَالُ` is Nominative, subject of `أَنَّ`'s clause, Day 103; `لَيْسَ` (is not) is a negative verb, Day 113. It takes `كَافِيًا` (enough - Accusative predicate of `لَيْسَ`, Day 108). `لِتَحْقِيقِ النَّجَاحِ` (to achieve success - `لِـ` preposition, `تَحْقِيقِ` (achieving) is Masdar, Genitive, Day 113; `النَّجَاحِ` is Genitive, Day 107).
