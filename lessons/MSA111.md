# Day 111 - Morphology practice Part 1

## Lesson

**Morphology** (in Arabic, it's called `صَرْف` - ṣarf) is the study of *how words are built* and *how they change*. It's like looking at the internal structure of words. In Arabic, this is incredibly powerful because most words are derived from a basic three-letter root. These three letters carry a core meaning, and by applying different patterns to them, we can create related verbs, nouns, and adjectives!

Think of the three-letter root as a building block. We can then add different "shapes" or "patterns" around these blocks to create a whole family of words with related meanings. This helps you understand new vocabulary and also connect words you already know in a deeper way.

Let's start with the most common and basic type of root and its derivations, which is called **Form I** in Arabic grammar. We'll use roots that are already familiar to you from words you've learned.

### The Three-Letter Root (الجَذْرُ)

Most Arabic words come from a **three-letter root** (like `د ر س` or `ك ت ب`). We don't write the vowels for roots, just the three consonants. These consonants carry the fundamental meaning.

Let's take a familiar root and see how different word types are formed from it:

### 1. Root `د ر س` (D-R-S) - related to studying, teaching, lessons

This root is behind words like `دَرْسٌ` (lesson). Let's see its common derivations in Form I:

*   **Past Tense Verb (فِعْلٌ مَاضٍ - fi'lun māḍin):** This tells us who *did* the action in the past.
    *   `دَرَسَ` (darasa) - He studied. (This follows a common pattern `فَعَلَ`).
*   **Present Tense Verb (فِعْلٌ مُضَارِعٌ - fi'lun muḍāri'un):** This tells us who *does* or *is doing* the action now or in the future.
    *   `يَدْرُسُ` (yadrusu) - He studies / is studying. (This follows a common pattern `يَفْعُلُ`).
*   **Active Participle (اسْمُ فَاعِلٍ - ismu fā'ilin):** This is like an adjective that means "the doer of the action." You already know this concept from Day 88!
    *   `دَارِسٌ` (dārisun) - Studying (one who studies) / Student. (This follows a common pattern `فَاعِلٌ`).
*   **Passive Participle (اسْمُ مَفْعُولٍ - ismu maf'ūlin):** This is like an adjective that means "the one being subjected to the action." You also know this concept from Day 88!
    *   `مَدْرُوسٌ` (madrūsun) - Studied (one being studied). (This follows a common pattern `مَفْعُولٌ`).
*   **Verbal Noun / Masdar (مَصْدَرٌ - maṣdarun):** This is the noun form of the verb, meaning "the act of" the verb. You've seen Masdars like `إِعْلَانٌ` (announcement) or `بَحْثٌ` (search).
    *   `دِرَاسَةٌ` (dirāsatun) - The act of studying / Study. (Masdars have many patterns, this is one common one).

### 2. Root `ك ت ب` (K-T-B) - related to writing, books

This root is behind words like `كِتَابٌ` (book). Let's see its common derivations in Form I:

*   **Past Tense Verb:**
    *   `كَتَبَ` (kataba) - He wrote.
*   **Present Tense Verb:**
    *   `يَكْتُبُ` (yaktubu) - He writes / is writing.
*   **Active Participle:**
    *   `كَاتِبٌ` (kātibun) - Writer (one who writes) / Writing.
*   **Passive Participle:** You've seen this word many times already!
    *   `مَكْتُوبٌ` (maktūbun) - Written (one being written).
*   **Verbal Noun / Masdar:**
    *   `كِتَابَةٌ` (kitābatun) - The act of writing / Writing.

**Why is this important?**
Understanding these root-and-pattern relationships is fundamental to Arabic. It allows you to:
*   **Guess the meaning of new words:** If you know the root and the pattern, you can often infer the meaning.
*   **Expand your vocabulary efficiently:** Learning one root unlocks a whole family of related words.
*   **Reinforce your grammar:** You see how verbs, participles, and Masdars are systematically linked.

This is just the beginning of exploring Arabic morphology, but it's a very powerful start!

## Examples

Let's see these morphological derivations in sentences, combining them with the grammar and vocabulary you've already mastered.

1.  **"The studying student wrote the studied lesson."**
    *   `الطَّالِبُ الدَّارِسُ كَتَبَ الدَّرْسَ الْمَدْرُوسَ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu ad-dārisu kataba ad-darsa al-madrūsa.)
        *   *Notice:* `الدَّارِسُ` (active participle, Day 88, here in apposition to `الطَّالِبُ`). `كَتَبَ` (new past tense verb from `ك ت ب`). `الْمَدْرُوسَ` (passive participle, from `د ر س`, acting as an adjective).

2.  **"The teacher writes the lesson (that is) studied because of the problem."**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُ يَكْتُبُ الدَّرْسَ الْمَدْرُوسَ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (Al-mudarrisu yaktubu ad-darsa al-madrūsa bi-sababi al-mushkilati.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَكْتُبُ` (new present tense verb from `ك ت ب`). `الْمَدْرُوسَ` (passive participle, Accusative as adjective). `بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (cause/effect, Day 86).

3.  **"Studying the book is important for writing the message."**
    *   `دِرَاسَةُ الْكِتَابِ مُهِمَّةٌ لِكِتَابَةِ الرِّسَالَةِ.` (Dirāsatū al-kitābi muhimmatun li-kitābatu ar-risālati.)
        *   *Notice:* `دِرَاسَةُ الْكِتَابِ` (Idafa, `دِرَاسَةٌ` is the Masdar of `د ر س`). `مُهِمَّةٌ` (feminine adjective for `دِرَاسَةُ`). `لِكِتَابَةِ الرِّسَالَةِ` (preposition `لِـ` + Idafa, `كِتَابَةٌ` is the Masdar of `ك ت ب`).

4.  **"The minister (f.) wants to make the writing clear for the judge."**
    *   `تُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ الْكِتَابَةَ وَاضِحَةً لِلْقَاضِي.` (Tureedu al-wazeeratu an taj'ala al-kitābata wāḍiḥatan li-l-qāḍī.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ` (light verb construction, Day 98). `الْكِتَابَةَ وَاضِحَةً` (Masdar `كِتَابَةٌ` as first object, `وَاضِحَةً` as second, both Accusative). `لِلْقَاضِي` (for the judge, Day 102).

5.  **"It is known that the state desires the study of the law."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الدَّوْلَةَ تَرْغَبُ فِي دِرَاسَةِ الْقَانُونِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna ad-dawlata targhabu fī dirāsatī al-qānooni.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (stance marker, Day 99). `الدَّوْلَةَ` (subject of `أَنَّ`, Accusative). `تَرْغَبُ فِي` (verb + preposition collocation, Day 97). `دِرَاسَةِ الْقَانُونِ` (Idafa, `دِرَاسَةٌ` in Genitive after `فِي`).
