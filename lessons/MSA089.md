# Day 89 - Review

## Lesson

Today, our goal is to bring all these pieces of grammar and vocabulary together. We'll revisit the core ideas of each lesson with a brief reminder and an example, and then we'll have a comprehensive practice session.

Here's a quick recap of the main points from the past week and a half:

1.  **Motion Verbs (Day 78):** Verbs like `جَاءَ` (he came), `عَادَ` (he returned), `دَخَلَ` (he entered), `خَرَجَ` (he exited). They often use `إِلَى` (to) or `مِنْ` (from).
    *   *Example:* `جَاءَ الْوَزِيرُ إِلَى الْمَكْتَبِ.` (The minister came to the office.)
    *   *Example:* `خَرَجَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ مِنَ الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (The teacher (f.) exited from the school.)

2.  **Thinking and Speaking Verbs (Day 79):** `يَظُنُّ` (he thinks/assumes) and `يَسْأَلُ` (he asks).
    *   *Example:* `هُوَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ الطَّقْسَ جَمِيلٌ.` (He thinks that the weather is beautiful.)
    *   *Example:* `يَسْأَلُ الطَّالِبُ الْمُدَرِّسَةَ.` (The student asks the teacher (f.).)

3.  **Hedging Language (Day 80):** `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ` (it is probable that), `رُبَّمَا` (perhaps), `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that). These express uncertainty.
    *   *Example:* `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (It is probable that the problem is big.)
    *   *Example:* `رُبَّمَا تَعُودُ الْوَزِيرَةُ غَدًا.` (Perhaps the minister (f.) will return tomorrow.)

4.  **Contrast Structures (Day 81):** `بَيْنَمَا` (while/whereas), `عَلَى الْعَكْسِ` (in contrast), `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (although).
    *   *Example:* `يَقْرَأُ هُوَ، بَيْنَمَا تَكْتُبُ هِيَ.` (He reads, while she writes.)
    *   *Example:* `الْقَانُونُ جَدِيدٌ. عَلَى الْعَكْسِ، السِّيَاسَةُ قَدِيمَةٌ.` (The law is new. In contrast, the policy is old.)

5.  **Interview Language (Day 82):** `حَدِّثْنِي عَنْ نَفْسِكَ` (tell me about yourself), `لَدَيَّ خِبْرَةٌ` (I have experience), `مُهْتَمٌّ بِـ` (interested in), `هَدَفٌ` (goal), `وَظِيفَةٌ` (job).
    *   *Example:* `لَدَيَّ خِبْرَةٌ جَيِّدَةٌ فِي التَّعْلِيمِ.` (I have good experience in education.)
    *   *Example:* `أَنَا مُهْتَمٌّ بِهَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ.` (I am interested in this job.)

6.  **Quotation Strategies (Day 84):** Direct quotes use `قَالَ: "..."` without `أَنَّ`. Indirect quotes use `قَالَ أَنَّ...` with `أَنَّ` affecting the following noun.
    *   *Direct:* `قَالَتْ: "الْمُشْكِلَةُ صَغِيرَةٌ."` (She said: "The problem is small.")
    *   *Indirect:* `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ صَغِيرَةٌ.` (She said that the problem is small.)

7.  **Apposition Structures (Day 85):** A noun clarifying another, agreeing in case and definiteness.
    *   *Example:* `الْوَزِيرُ مُحَمَّدٌ ذَكِيٌّ.` (The minister, Muhammad, is smart.)
    *   *Example:* `رَأَيْتُ الطَّبِيبَةَ فَاطِمَةَ.` (I saw the doctor (f.), Fatima.)

8.  **Cause/Effect Connectors (Day 86):** `بِسَبَبِ` (because of + noun), `لِأَنَّ` (because + clause), `فَـ` (so/then), `حَتَّى` (in order to + subjunctive verb).
    *   *Example:* `لَمْ يَأْتِ الطَّبِيبُ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (The doctor did not come because of the problem.)
    *   *Example:* `نَدْرُسُ حَتَّى نَنْجَحَ.` (We study in order to succeed.)

9.  **Headline Compression (Day 87):** Omitting `كَانَ`, using Masdars for actions, `بِـ` for "due to."
    *   *Example:* `الرَّئِيسُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ.` (President in Cairo. - Implied "was")
    *   *Example:* `زِيَارَةُ الْوَزِيرِ لِمِصْرَ.` (Minister's visit to Egypt. - Using Masdar `زِيَارَةٌ`)

10. **Participial Modifiers (Day 88):** Active participles (`فَاعِلٌ` pattern - "doer of X") and Passive participles (`مَفْعُولٌ` pattern - "receiver of X"). They act like adjectives.
    *   *Example:* `الطَّالِبُ الْكَاتِبُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (The writing student is smart.)
    *   *Example:* `الْكِتَابُ الْمَكْتُوبُ جَدِيدٌ.` (The written book is new.)

## Examples

Here are a few sentences that bring together multiple grammatical points from the past two weeks. Try to identify all the different rules at play in each one!

1.  `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ مُحَمَّدًا عَادَ إِلَى بَلَدِهِ بِسَبَبِ مُشْكِلَةٍ مَحْلُولَةٍ.`
    *   (Yabdū anna al-wazeera Muḥammadan 'āda ilā baladihi bi-sababi mushkilatin maḥloolatin.)
    *   **Translation:** It seems that the minister, Muhammad, returned to his country because of a solved problem.
    *   **Breakdown:** `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (hedging language, Day 80). `الْوَزِيرَ مُحَمَّدًا` (apposition, Fatha due to `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `عَادَ` (motion verb, Day 78). `إِلَى بَلَدِهِ` (to his country, Day 78). `بِسَبَبِ` (cause/effect connector, Day 86). `مُشْكِلَةٍ مَحْلُولَةٍ` (passive participle `مَحْلُولَةٌ` as an adjective agreeing with `مُشْكِلَةٍ`, both taking Kasra after `بِسَبَبِ`, Day 88).

2.  `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الطَّبِيبَةَ سَارَةَ مُهْتَمَّةٌ بِوَظِيفَتِهَا، لَنْ تَسْأَلَ الْمُدِيرَ عَنِ الْأَسْعَارِ حَتَّى لَا تَظُنَّ أَنَّهَا قَارِئَةٌ غَيْرُ فَاهِمَةٍ.`
    *   (Wa-ma'a anna aṭ-ṭabeebata Sārata muhtammatun bi-waẓīfatihā, lan tas'ala al-mudeera 'ani al-as'āri ḥattā lā taẓunna annahā qāri'atun ghayru fāhimatin.)
    *   **Translation:** Although the doctor, Sarah, is interested in her job, she will not ask the manager about the prices so that she does not think that she is a non-understanding reader.
    *   **Breakdown:** `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (contrast structure, Day 81). `الطَّبِيبَةَ سَارَةَ` (apposition, Fatha due to `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `مُهْتَمَّةٌ بِوَظِيفَتِهَا` (interview language, Day 82). `لَنْ تَسْأَلَ` (negated thinking verb, Jussive mood for `لَنْ`, Day 79, 60). `الْمُدِيرَ عَنِ الْأَسْعَارِ` (asking with preposition, Day 79). `حَتَّى لَا تَظُنَّ` (cause/effect `حَتَّى` + negation `لَا` + subjunctive `تَظُنَّ`, Day 86, 73). `أَنَّهَا` (indirect quotation, Day 84). `قَارِئَةٌ غَيْرُ فَاهِمَةٍ` (active participles `قَارِئَةٌ` and `فَاهِمَةٌ`, negated with `غَيْرُ`, Day 88, 73).

3.  `الرَّئِيسُ الْكَاتِبُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ الْيَوْمَ، لِذَلِكَ يَعْلَمُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنَّ إِعْلَانَ الْخُطَّةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ مُحْتَمَلٌ.`
    *   (Ar-ra'eecu al-kātibu fī al-qāhirati al-yawma, li-dhālika ya'lamu al-jamee'u anna i'lāna al-khuṭṭati al-jadeedati muḥtamalun.)
    *   **Translation:** The writing president is in Cairo today, therefore everyone knows that the announcement of the new plan is probable.
    *   **Breakdown:** `الرَّئِيسُ الْكَاتِبُ` (active participle `الْكَاتِبُ` as adjective, Day 88). `فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ الْيَوْمَ` (headline compression for `كَانَ` + location/time, Day 87). `لِذَلِكَ` (cause/effect connector, Day 86). `يَعْلَمُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنَّ` (thinking verb, indirect quote, Day 79, 84). `إِعْلَانَ الْخُطَّةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (Masdar `إِعْلَانُ` used in headline style, takes Fatha due to `أَنَّ`, Day 87, 39). `مُحْتَمَلٌ` (hedging vocabulary, Day 80).
