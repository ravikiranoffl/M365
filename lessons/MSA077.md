## Lesson

Today, our goal is to bring all these pieces of grammar and vocabulary together. We'll revisit the core ideas of each lesson with a brief reminder and an example, and then we'll have a comprehensive practice session.

Here's a quick recap of the main points from the past week and a half:

1.  **Embedded Clauses (Day 62):** These are small sentences inside bigger ones.
    *   `الْكِتَابُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُهُ الطَّالِبُ جَدِيدٌ.` (The book which the student reads is new.)
    *   `تُرِيدُ هِيَ أَنْ تَكْتُبَ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (She wants to write the message.)
2.  **Complex Nominal Sentences (Day 63):** When the "X" or "Y" part of an "X is Y" sentence is an entire phrase or clause.
    *   `الطَّالِبُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ ذَكِيٌّ.` (The student who reads the news is smart.) - The Topic is a relative clause.
    *   `رَئِيسُ الدَّوْلَةِ يَحُلُّ مُشْكِلَةَ التَّلَوُّثِ.` (The president of the state solves the problem of pollution.) - The Comment is a verbal sentence.
3.  **Speech Acts in Formal Arabic (Day 64):** Specific verbs to perform actions with words.
    *   `الرَّئِيسُ يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ السَّلَامَ مُهِمٌّ.` (The president affirms that peace is important.)
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يَدْعُو إِلَى فَهْمِ الْقَانُونِ.` (The minister calls for understanding the law.)
4.  **Arabic Idioms (Day 66):** Phrases with non-literal meanings.
    *   `نَحْنُ نَعْمَلُ يَدًا بِيَدٍ لِحِفْظِ الْبِيئَةِ.` (We work hand in hand to protect the environment.)
    *   `الْوَلَدُ ذَكِيٌّ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ.` (The boy is smart, but he is arrogant.)
5.  **Diplomatic Phrasing (Day 67):** Polite and formal expressions.
    *   `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ يَدًا بِيَدٍ.` (We hope to work hand in hand.)
    *   `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ.` (It is necessary to protect the environment.)
6.  **Reporting Verbs (Day 68):** Verbs to report what someone said, thought, or knew.
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ نَاجِحٌ.` (The teacher knows that the student is successful.)
7.  **Persuasive Language (Day 69):** Words to strengthen arguments.
    *   `الْبِيئَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ، لِذَلِكَ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَهَا.` (The environment is important, therefore it is necessary to protect it.)
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ التَّلَوُّثَ مُشْكِلَةٌ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (It is clear that pollution is a big problem.)
8.  **Discourse Markers (Day 70):** Words to connect ideas.
    *   `الْمُشْكِلَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ، مَعَ ذَلِكَ يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَحُلَّهَا.` (The problem is big; nevertheless, the minister wants to solve it.)
    *   `فِي الْخِتَامِ، نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ يَدًا بِيَدٍ.` (In conclusion, we hope to work hand in hand.)
9.  **Advanced Passive Structures (Day 72):** When the verb focuses on what happened, not who did it.
    *   `كُتِبَتِ الرِّسَالَةُ.` (The message was written.)
    *   `يُفْهَمُ الدَّرْسُ.` (The lesson is understood.)
10. **Complex Negation Patterns (Day 73):** More nuanced ways to say "not."
    *   `الْكِتَابُ غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ.` (The book is not useful.) - Using `غَيْرُ`.
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْبَيْتَ لَيْسَ كَبِيرًا.` (He said that the house is not big.) - `لَيْسَ` inside `أَنَّ` clause.
    *   `لَمْ يُكْتَبِ الْكِتَابُ.` (The book was not written.) - Negating passive verb.
11. **Emphasis via Word Order (Day 74):** Placing important words first.
    *   `الطَّالِبُ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ.` (The **student** reads the book.) - Emphasizes the subject.
    *   `الْكِتَابَ يَقْرَأُ الطَّالِبُ.` (The student reads **the book**.) - Emphasizes the object.

## Examples

Here are a few sentences that bring together multiple grammatical points from the past two weeks. Try to identify all the different rules at play in each one!

1.  `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الْبِيئَةَ مُهِمَّةٌ، لِذَلِكَ يَجِبُ أَنْ تُحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةُ الْمُشْتَرَكَةُ يَدًا بِيَدٍ مِنَ الشَّعْبِ.`
    *   (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna al-bī'ata muhimmatun, li-dhālika yajibu an tuḥfaẓa al-bī'atu al-mushtarakatū yadan bi-yadin mina ash-sha'bi.)
    *   **Translation:** It is clear that the environment is important, therefore it is necessary that the joint environment be protected hand in hand by the people.
    *   **Breakdown:** `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (persuasive language). `الْبِيئَةَ` (Fatha due to `أَنَّ`). `لِذَلِكَ` (persuasive language, discourse marker). `يَجِبُ أَنْ تُحْفَظَ` (diplomatic phrasing, `تُحْفَظَ` is passive present subjunctive with Fatha). `الْبِيئَةُ الْمُشْتَرَكَةُ` (`نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` for `تُحْفَظَ`, takes Damma, with agreeing adjective). `يَدًا بِيَدٍ` (idiom). `مِنَ الشَّعْبِ` (specifying agent of passive verb).

2.  `الْوَزِيرَةُ الَّتِي تُعْلِنُ أَنَّ الْقَرَارَ لَيْسَ صَغِيرًا، تَدْعُو إِلَى حَلٍّ غَيْرِ مُمْكِنٍ مَعَ ذَلِكَ.`
    *   (Al-wazeeratū allatī tu'linu anna al-qarāra laysa ṣagheeran, tad'ū ilā ḥallin ghayri mumkinin ma'a dhālika.)
    *   **Translation:** The minister who announces that the decision is not small, calls for an impossible solution nevertheless.
    *   **Breakdown:** `الْوَزِيرَةُ الَّتِي تُعْلِنُ...` (Complex nominal sentence, relative clause, reporting verb). `أَنَّ الْقَرَارَ لَيْسَ صَغِيرًا` (embedded `أَنَّ` clause, `لَيْسَ` negating its predicate). `تَدْعُو إِلَى` (speech act verb). `حَلٍّ غَيْرِ مُمْكِنٍ` (Idafa with `غَيْرُ` for complex negation, `حَلٍّ` takes Kasra after `إِلَى`). `مَعَ ذَلِكَ` (discourse marker).

3.  `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ لَا تُرْسَلَ الرَّسَائِلُ الْقَدِيمَةُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ فِي الْخِتَامِ.`
    *   (Na'mulu an lā tursala ar-rasā'ilū al-qadeematū kulla yawmin fī al-khitāmi.)
    *   **Translation:** We hope that the old messages are not sent every day in conclusion.
    *   **Breakdown:** `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ لَا تُرْسَلَ` (diplomatic phrasing, `أَنْ لَا` for complex negation, `تُرْسَلَ` is passive present subjunctive with Fatha). `الرَّسَائِلُ الْقَدِيمَةُ` (`نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`, broken plural `رَسَائِلُ` takes Damma, adjective `الْقَدِيمَةُ` is feminine singular). `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ` (adverbial phrase, can be emphasized by initial placement, but here it's in a more neutral position within the clause). `فِي الْخِتَامِ` (discourse marker).
