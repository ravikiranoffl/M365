# Day 63 - Complex nominal sentences

## Lesson

You already know that a simple nominal sentence (an "X is Y" sentence) has a **Topic** (the `X` part, called `الْمُبْتَدَأُ`) and a **Comment** (the `Y` part, called `الْخَبَرُ`). Both usually end with a Damma (ُ).

*   *Example:* `الْبَيْتُ جَمِيلٌ.` (Al-baytu jameelun.) - The house is beautiful.
    *   `الْبَيْتُ` is the Topic.
    *   `جَمِيلٌ` is the Comment.

Now, a **Complex Nominal Sentence** is when either the **Topic** or the **Comment** (or both!) are not just single words, but entire phrases or even mini-sentences (which we call "clauses" - remember Day 62?). You've actually been learning the building blocks for these for a long time!

Let's break down how we can make our nominal sentences more complex:

### 1. Complex Topics (the 'X' part)

The Topic (`الْمُبْتَدَأُ`) can be more than just one noun. It can be:

*   **A Noun with an Adjective:**
    *   Instead of `الْبَيْتُ` (the house), you can say `الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ` (the big house). This whole phrase is your Topic.
    *   Example: `الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ جَمِيلٌ.` (The big house is beautiful.)

*   **An Idafa Construct:** (Day 25)
    *   Instead of `الْبَيْتُ` (the house), you can say `بَيْتُ الْمُدَرِّسِ` (the teacher's house). This whole Idafa is your Topic.
    *   Example: `بَيْتُ الْمُدَرِّسِ جَمِيلٌ.` (The teacher's house is beautiful.)

*   **A Relative Clause:** (Day 31, Day 62)
    *   This is where a whole mini-sentence describes the noun. The whole "noun + its description" becomes your Topic.
    *   Example: `الْكِتَابُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُهُ الطَّالِبُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-kitābu alladhī yaqra'uhu aṭ-ṭālibu jadeedun.) - The book **which the student reads** is new.
        *   Here, `الْكِتَابُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُهُ الطَّالِبُ` is the entire Topic.

### 2. Complex Comments (the 'Y' part)

The Comment (`الْخَبَرُ`) can also be more than just one adjective. It can be:

*   **A Noun with an Adjective:**
    *   Instead of `جَمِيلٌ` (beautiful), you can say `مَكَانٌ جَمِيلٌ` (a beautiful place).
    *   Example: `الْبَيْتُ مَكَانٌ جَمِيلٌ.` (The house is a beautiful place.)

*   **A Prepositional Phrase:** (Day 10)
    *   Instead of an adjective, the comment can tell you *where* something is.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu fī al-madrasati.) - The student is in the school.

*   **A Verbal Sentence:**
    *   This is a very common type of complex comment! The comment is actually a verb and its subject/object. In English, we might say "The student, he reads." In Arabic, it's just "The student reads."
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yaqra'u al-kitāba.) - The student reads the book.
        *   Here, `يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ` (reads the book) is the entire Comment for `الطَّالِبُ`.

*   **A Nominal Sentence (often introduced by `أَنَّ` or `أَنْ`):** (Day 39, 44, 62)
    *   When you say, "The news is **that the government is new**," the "that..." part is a full nominal sentence acting as the comment.
    *   Example: `الْأَخْبَارُ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-akhbāru anna al-ḥukūmata jadeedatun.) - The news is that the government is new.
        *   Here, `أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ جَدِيدَةٌ` is the entire Comment for `الْأَخْبَارُ`.

### 3. How Particles (`إِنَّ`, `لَيْسَ`) Affect Complex Nominal Sentences

The rules for `إِنَّ` (Day 37) and `لَيْسَ` (Day 61) still apply, but they affect the *first word* of the Topic or Comment if they are phrases.

*   **With `إِنَّ`:**
    *   The **Topic** (or the first word of the Topic phrase) takes a **Fatha**. The Comment (or its first word) keeps its Damma.
    *   Example: `إِنَّ الْبَيْتَ الْكَبِيرَ جَمِيلٌ.` (Indeed, the big house is beautiful.)
        *   `الْبَيْتَ` has a Fatha because of `إِنَّ`. `الْكَبِيرَ` follows in Fatha because it's an adjective. `جَمِيلٌ` stays Damma.

*   **With `لَيْسَ`:**
    *   The **Topic** (or its first word) keeps its **Damma**. The **Comment** (or its first word) takes a **Fatha** (or Kasra with `بِـ`).
    *   Example: `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ جَمِيلًا.` (The big house is not beautiful.)
        *   `الْبَيْتُ` keeps Damma. `الْكَبِيرُ` also keeps Damma. `جَمِيلًا` takes Fatha.

The main idea is that Arabic nominal sentences are very flexible! You can layer information by making the Topic and Comment more detailed using all the structures you've learned.

## Examples

Let's see these complex nominal sentences in action!

1.  **"The big house of the minister is in the new city."** (Complex Topic, Complex Comment)
    *   `بَيْتُ الْوَزِيرِ الْكَبِيرُ فِي الْمَدِينَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Baytu al-wazeeri al-kabeeru fī al-madeenati al-jadeedati.)
        *   *Topic:* `بَيْتُ الْوَزِيرِ الْكَبِيرُ` (The big house of the minister). This is an Idafa (`بَيْتُ الْوَزِيرِ`) modified by an adjective (`الْكَبِيرُ`). Notice `الْوَزِيرِ` takes Kasra (Idafa), and `الْكَبِيرُ` matches `بَيْتُ` (masculine, definite, Damma) but describes it from outside the Idafa link. This specific phrasing is trickier; let's adjust for clarity.
        *   *Revised Example 1:* **"The big house (of the) minister is in the new city."** (Idafa for Topic, but adjective describes the first part).
            `بَيْتُ الْوَزِيرِ كَبِيرٌ فِي الْمَدِينَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Baytu al-wazeeri kabeerun fī al-madeenati al-jadeedati.) - The minister's house is big in the new city. (This is clearer, "big" is an adjective for the house.)
        *   *Alternative (complex topic):* **"The big house is for the minister."**
            `الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ لِلْوَزِيرِ.` (Al-baytu al-kabeeru li-l-wazeeri.)
            *   Topic: `الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ` (The big house - Noun + Adjective).
            *   Comment: `لِلْوَزِيرِ` (for the minister - Prepositional Phrase).

2.  **"The student who reads the news is smart."** (Topic is a Relative Clause)
    *   `الطَّالِبُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu alladhī yaqra'u al-akhbāra dhakiyyun.)
        *   *Topic:* `الطَّالِبُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ` (The student who reads the news).
        *   *Comment:* `ذَكِيٌّ` (smart - Adjective).

3.  **"The president of the state solves the problem of pollution."** (Comment is a Verbal Sentence)
    *   `رَئِيسُ الدَّوْلَةِ يَحُلُّ مُشْكِلَةَ التَّلَوُّثِ.` (Ra'eecu ad-dawlati yaḥullu mushkilata at-talawwuthi.)
        *   *Topic:* `رَئِيسُ الدَّوْلَةِ` (The president of the state - Idafa).
        *   *Comment:* `يَحُلُّ مُشْكِلَةَ التَّلَوُّثِ` (solves the problem of pollution - Verbal Sentence, with a chained Idafa as object).

4.  **"Indeed, the new information is that the agreement is important."** (With `إِنَّ`, Comment is a Nominal Clause with `أَنَّ`)
    *   `إِنَّ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةَ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Inna al-ma'lūmāti al-jadeedata anna al-ittifāqa muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` makes `الْمَعْلُومَاتِ` (the information) take Fatha (it's a sound feminine plural, so it's `ـاتِ` in Fatha/Kasra). Its adjective `الْجَدِيدَةَ` is feminine singular Fatha.
        *   *Comment:* `أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ مُهِمٌّ` (that the agreement is important). `الِاتِّفَاقَ` takes Fatha because of `أَنَّ`.

5.  **"It is not probable that the minister is a rich person."** (With `لَيْسَ`, Topic is `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ` phrase, Comment is a nominal sentence)
    *   `لَيْسَ مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْوَزِيرُ رَجُلًا غَنِيًّا.` (Laysa mina al-muḥtamali an yakūna al-wazeeru rajulan ghaniyyan.)
        *   This is very complex! `لَيْسَ` negates the entire "probable" idea. `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْوَزِيرُ رَجُلًا غَنِيًّا` is the predicate of `لَيْسَ`, meaning "the probability that the minister is a rich man." Here, `رَجُلًا غَنِيًّا` takes Fatha because it's the predicate of `يَكُونَ` (which is like `كَانَ`, acting similarly to `لَيْسَ` in structure).

6.  **"The health of the people is not a small problem."** (With `لَيْسَ`, Topic is Idafa, Comment is Noun + Adjective)
    *   `لَيْسَتْ صِحَّةُ الشَّعْبِ مُشْكِلَةً صَغِيرَةً.` (Laysat ṣiḥḥatu ash-sha'bi mushkilatan ṣagheeratan.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَيْسَتْ` because `صِحَّةُ` (health) is feminine. `صِحَّةُ الشَّعْبِ` (the health of the people) is the Idafa Topic, keeping Damma. `مُشْكِلَةً صَغِيرَةً` (a small problem) is the Comment, taking Tanween Fatha for both noun and adjective.

These examples show how you can combine all your learned grammatical tools to build long, descriptive, and nuanced sentences. The core "X is Y" structure remains, but X and Y become powerful containers for more complex ideas!

## Practice

Now, let's practice forming some complex nominal sentences! Remember to pay attention to case endings and noun/adjective agreements.

1.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The beautiful sea is clean." (`الْبَحْرُ`, `جَمِيلٌ`, `نَظِيفٌ`).
2.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The teacher's new lesson is important." (`مُدَرِّسٌ`, `دَرْسٌ`, `جَدِيدٌ`, `مُهِمٌّ`).
3.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The ambassador who came to the meeting is smart." (`سَفِيرٌ`, `جَاءَ إِلَى`, `اجْتِمَاعٌ`, `ذَكِيٌّ`). *Hint: `جَاءَ` (jā'a) - he came (past tense).*
4.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The company wants to protect the environment." (`شَرِكَةٌ`, `تُرِيدُ`, `تَحْفَظَ`, `بِيئَةٌ`). (Remember the verb for 'protect' is `يَحْفَظُ`).
5.  **Translate to Arabic:** "Indeed, the law of the state is beautiful." (`إِنَّ`, `قَانُونٌ`, `دَوْلَةٌ`, `جَمِيلٌ`).
6.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The big mountain is not near the small river." (`جَبَلٌ`, `كَبِيرٌ`, `قَرِيبٌ مِنْ` (near to - masculine adjective), `نَهْرٌ`, `صَغِيرٌ`).
7.  **Translate to Arabic:** "My computer, it uses the internet." (`حَاسُوبٌ` + `ي`, `يَسْتَخْدِمُ`, `إِنْتَرْنِت`). *Hint: The comment is a verbal sentence.*
8.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The news is that the price is small." (`أَخْبَارٌ`, `أَنَّ`, `سِعْرٌ`, `صَغِيرٌ`).
