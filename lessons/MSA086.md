# Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors

## Lesson

Cause and effect connectors are like bridges between ideas. They tell the listener or reader how one event leads to another. This is crucial for explaining things, making arguments, and describing sequences of events.

We will learn about new ways to express "because," "so," and "in order to."

### 1. `لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika) - Therefore (Review)

You already know this important connector from Day 69! It shows that what comes after it is a *result* or *effect* of what was said before.
*   **Meaning:** "Therefore," "for that reason."
*   **How to use:** It introduces the consequence of a previous statement.
*   *Example:* `الْبِيئَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ، لِذَلِكَ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَهَا.` (Al-bī'atu muhimmatun, li-dhālika yajibu an naḥfaẓahā.) - The environment is important, therefore it is necessary to protect it.

### 2. `بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi) - Because of / Due to

This phrase is used to give a reason *for* something, and it's always followed by a **noun** or a **Masdar** (verbal noun) in the Genitive case (taking Kasra).

*   **New Word:** `سَبَبٌ` (sababun) - 'sa-ba-bun'
    *   *Meaning:* Cause, reason (masculine noun).
*   **The Phrase:** `بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi)
    *   *Literal translation:* "With the reason of." (`بِـ` means "with" or "by means of," Day 10).
    *   *How to use:* It acts like a prepositional phrase, so the word following `بِسَبَبِ` will take a **Kasra** (ِ).
    *   *Example:* `لَمْ يَأْتِ الطَّبِيبُ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (Lam ya'ti aṭ-ṭabeebu bi-sababi al-mushkilati.)
        *   *Translation:* The doctor did not come because of the problem.
        *   *Breakdown:* `لَمْ يَأْتِ` (he did not come - `يَأْتِي` becomes Jussive, Day 60, 78). `الطَّبِيبُ` (the doctor). `بِسَبَبِ` (because of). `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (the problem) takes Kasra because it follows `بِسَبَبِ`.

### 3. `لِأَنَّ` (li-anna) - Because (followed by a clause)

This connector is used to give a reason *when that reason is a full sentence or clause*. It's a combination of `لِـ` (for/because) and `أَنَّ` (that).

*   **The Connector:** `لِأَنَّ` (li-anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "For that."
    *   *How to use:* Just like `أَنَّ` (Day 39), `لِأَنَّ` makes the subject of the clause immediately following it take a **Fatha** (َ). It introduces a full explanation.
    *   *Example:* `أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ لِأَنَّ الدَّرْسَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Adhhabu ilā al-madrasati li-anna ad-darsa muhimun.)
        *   *Translation:* I go to school because the lesson is important.
        *   *Breakdown:* `أَذْهَبُ` (I go - Day 78). `إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (to school - Day 38). `لِأَنَّ` (because). `الدَّرْسَ` (the lesson) takes Fatha because it's the subject of the `لِأَنَّ` clause. `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is its comment.

### 4. `فَـ` (fa-) - So / Then / As a result

This is a very common and versatile conjunction. It's a prefix that attaches to the next word and often indicates a sequence of events or a direct consequence.

*   **The Connector:** `فَـ` (fa-)
    *   *Meaning:* "So," "then," "as a result," "consequently."
    *   *How to use:* It connects two clauses, showing that the second one is a direct result or follow-up of the first. It does not change the case endings or verb moods of the words that follow it.
    *   *Example:* `قَرَأَ الْكِتَابَ فَفَهِمَ الدَّرْسَ.` (Qara'a al-kitāba fa-fahima ad-darsa.)
        *   *Translation:* He read the book, so he understood the lesson.
        *   *Breakdown:* `قَرَأَ الْكِتَابَ` (he read the book - Day 45). `فَـ` (so). `فَهِمَ الدَّرْسَ` (he understood the lesson - Day 45).

### 5. `حَتَّى` (ḥattā) - In order to / So that (for purpose)

This word is used to express the *purpose* or *goal* of an action. It tells you *why* something is being done, focusing on the desired *effect*.

*   **New Word:** `حَتَّى` (ḥattā) - 'ḥat-taa'
    *   *Meaning:* "In order to," "so that," "until." (When followed by a verb, it means "in order to" or "so that.")
    *   *How to use:* When `حَتَّى` is followed by a present tense verb, it forces that verb into the **Subjunctive mood** (ending in Fatha, or Nūn-drop). This is similar to `أَنْ` or `لِـ` (for purpose, Day 60).
    *   *Example:* `نَدْرُسُ حَتَّى نَنْجَحَ.` (Nadrusu ḥattā nanjaḥa.)
        *   *Translation:* We study in order to succeed.
        *   *Breakdown:* `نَدْرُسُ` (we study - Day 58). `حَتَّى` (in order to). `نَنْجَحَ` (we succeed) takes Fatha (Subjunctive mood) because of `حَتَّى`.

These five connectors give you a strong set of tools to clearly express cause and effect in your Arabic sentences!

## Examples

Let's use these cause/effect connectors in more complex sentences, integrating grammar you've already learned.

1.  **"It is clear that the agreement is important, therefore the president will announce it."**
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ مُهِمٌّ، لِذَلِكَ سَيُعْلِنُهُ الرَّئِيسُ.` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna al-ittifāqa muhimun, li-dhālika sayu'linuhu ar-ra'eecu.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (Day 69). `لِذَلِكَ` (Day 69). `سَيُعْلِنُهُ` (he will announce it - Day 64, 22).

2.  **"The minister (f.) returned to her country because of a big problem."**
    *   `عَادَتِ الْوَزِيرَةُ إِلَى بَلَدِهَا بِسَبَبِ مُشْكِلَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ.` (ʿĀdati al-wazeeratū ilā baladihā bi-sababi mushkilatin kabeeratin.)
        *   *Notice:* `عَادَتِ` (she returned - Day 78). `بَلَدِهَا` (her country - Day 78). `بِسَبَبِ` (because of). `مُشْكِلَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ` (a big problem) takes Kasra for both noun and adjective after `بِسَبَبِ`.

3.  **"We hope to enter the market because it is important for the company."**
    *   `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَدْخُلَ السُّوقَ لِأَنَّهُ مُهِمٌّ لِلشَّرِكَةِ.` (Na'mulu an nadkhula as-sūqa li-annahu muhimun li-sh-sharika.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَدْخُلَ` (we hope to enter - Day 67, 78). `لِأَنَّهُ` (because it is - `لِأَنَّ` + `هُوَ`). `مُهِمٌّ لِلشَّرِكَةِ` (important for the company).

4.  **"The student (f.) studied well, so she succeeded in the exam."**
    *   `دَرَسَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ جَيِّدًا فَوَجَدَتِ النَّجَاحَ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Darasati aṭ-ṭālibatū jayyidan fa-wajadatī an-najāḥa fī al-imtiḥāni.)
        *   *New verb for explanation:* `وَجَدَ` / `يَجِدُ` (to find). We've used `نَجَحَ` (succeed) and `تَحْقِيقُ النَّجَاحِ` (achieving success). Let's stick to what's known: `نَجَحَتْ`.
        *   *Revised Example 4:* **"The student (f.) studied well, so she succeeded in the exam."**
            `دَرَسَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ جَيِّدًا فَنَجَحَتْ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Darasati aṭ-ṭālibatū jayyidan fa-najaḥat fī al-imtiḥāni.)
            *   *Notice:* `دَرَسَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ جَيِّدًا` (the student studied well - Day 58, 59). `فَـ` (so). `نَجَحَتْ` (she succeeded - Day 58). `فِي الِامْتِحَانِ` (in the exam - Day 58).

5.  **"He wants to ask the manager (f.) in order to understand the new plan."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَسْأَلَ الْمُدِيرَةَ حَتَّى يَفْهَمَ الْخُطَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ.` (Yureedu an yas'ala al-mudeeratā ḥattā yafhama al-khuṭṭata al-jadeedata.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَسْأَلَ` (he wants to ask - Day 79, Subjunctive). `الْمُدِيرَةَ` (the manager - f.) is the object. `حَتَّى` (in order to). `يَفْهَمَ` (he understands) takes Fatha (Subjunctive). `الْخُطَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (the new plan) is the object of `يَفْهَمَ`.

6.  **"Perhaps the health of the people is not good, because of the pollution."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا صِحَّةُ الشَّعْبِ غَيْرُ جَيِّدَةٍ، بِسَبَبِ التَّلَوُّثِ.` (Rubbamā ṣiḥḥatu ash-sha'bi ghayru jayyidatin, bi-sababi at-talawwuthi.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (perhaps - Day 80). `صِحَّةُ الشَّعْبِ` (health of the people - Idafa, Day 50). `غَيْرُ جَيِّدَةٍ` (not good - Day 73). `بِسَبَبِ` (because of). `التَّلَوُّثِ` (the pollution - Day 69) takes Kasra.

7.  **"The minister (m.) left the office early, so he returned to his house."**
    *   `خَرَجَ الْوَزِيرُ مِنَ الْمَكْتَبِ مُبَكِّرًا فَعَادَ إِلَى بَيْتِهِ.` (Kharaja al-wazeeru mina al-maktabi mubakkiran fa-'āda ilā baytihi.)
        *   *New word:* `مُبَكِّرًا` (mubakkiran) - early (adverb of time, takes Fatha).
        *   *Notice:* `خَرَجَ الْوَزِيرُ مِنَ الْمَكْتَبِ` (the minister exited the office - Day 78). `فَـ` (so). `عَادَ إِلَى بَيْتِهِ` (he returned to his house - Day 78).

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   سَبَبٌ (sababun) - cause, reason (masculine noun)
*   بِسَبَبِ (bi-sababi) - because of, due to
*   لِأَنَّ (li-anna) - because (followed by a nominal clause)
*   فَـ (fa-) - so, then, as a result (conjunction, prefix)
*   حَتَّى (ḥattā) - in order to, so that (conjunctive particle, makes verb subjunctive)
*   مُبَكِّرًا (mubakkiran) - early (adverb)
