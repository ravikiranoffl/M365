# Day 122 - Fluency: Culture reporting

## Lesson

"Culture reporting" involves using specific vocabulary and phrases to describe the various aspects of culture, from general concepts to specific events and artistic expressions. This topic allows you to explore a rich dimension of Arabic language and the Arab world.

Let's learn some new and essential vocabulary for culture reporting today!

### 1. `ثَقَافَةٌ` (thaqāfatun) - Culture

This is the fundamental term for "culture." It is a feminine noun.

*   **New Word:** `ثَقَافَةٌ` (thaqāfatun) - 'tha-qā-fa-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Culture (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الثَّقَافَةُ الْعَرَبِيَّةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Ath-thaqāfatū al-ʿarabiyyatū kabeeratun.) - Arab culture is great. (`عَرَبِيَّةٌ` - Arab, feminine adjective, Day 87).

### 2. `حَدَثٌ` (ḥadathun) / `أَحْدَاثٌ` (aḥdāthun) - Event / Events

This word refers to occurrences or happenings, often significant ones, and is very useful when reporting on specific cultural *events*. `أَحْدَاثٌ` is a broken plural, so its adjectives will be feminine singular (Day 27).

*   **New Word (Singular):** `حَدَثٌ` (ḥadathun) - 'ḥa-da-thun'
    *   *Meaning:* Event (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `أَحْدَاثٌ` (aḥdāthun) - 'aḥ-dā-thun'
    *   *Meaning:* Events (masculine plural, broken plural).
    *   *Example:* `الْأَحْدَاثُ الثَّقَافِيَّةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-aḥdāthū ath-thaqāfiyyatū muhimmatun.) - The cultural events are important. (`ثَقَافِيَّةٌ` - cultural, feminine adjective, agreeing with `أَحْدَاثٌ` as non-human plural).

### 3. `تَقْلِيدٌ` (taqleedun) / `تَقَالِيدُ` (taqāleedu) - Tradition / Traditions

This refers to customs or beliefs passed down through generations. `تَقَالِيدُ` is a broken plural *and* a diptote (Day 119), meaning it will take a Fatha instead of a Kasra when it's in the Genitive case and indefinite. Its adjectives will be feminine singular.

*   **New Word (Singular):`** `تَقْلِيدٌ` (taqleedun) - 'taq-lee-dun'
    *   *Meaning:* Tradition (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):`** `تَقَالِيدُ` (taqāleedu) - 'ta-qā-lee-du'
    *   *Meaning:* Traditions (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example:* `هُنَاكَ تَقَالِيدُ قَدِيمَةٌ.` (Hunāka taqāleedu qadeematun.) - There are old traditions. (`قَدِيمَةٌ` - old, feminine adjective).
    *   *Example (Genitive):* `تَكَلَّمْتُ عَنْ تَقَالِيدَ جَمِيلَةٍ.` (Takallamtu 'an taqāleeda jameelatun.) - I spoke about beautiful traditions. (`تَقَالِيدَ` takes Fatha because it's diptote in Genitive; `جَمِيلَةٍ` takes Kasra Tanween).

### 4. `فَنٌّ` (fannun) / `فُنُونٌ` (funoonun) - Art / Arts

This general term covers various forms of creative expression. `فُنُونٌ` is a broken plural, so its adjectives will be feminine singular.

*   **New Word (Singular):`** `فَنٌّ` (fannun) - 'fan-nun'
    *   *Meaning:* Art (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):`** `فُنُونٌ` (funoonun) - 'fu-noo-nun'
    *   *Meaning:* Arts (masculine plural, broken plural).
    *   *Example:* `هَذِهِ فُنُونٌ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Hādhihi funoonun jameelatun.) - These are beautiful arts.

### 5. `مُهْرَجَانٌ` (muhrajānun) / `مُهْرَجَانَاتٌ` (muhrajānātun) - Festival / Festivals

This noun is frequently used in cultural reporting to refer to celebrations or events. `مُهْرَجَانَاتٌ` is a sound feminine plural.

*   **New Word (Singular):`** `مُهْرَجَانٌ` (muhrajānun) - 'muh-ra-jā-nun'
    *   *Meaning:* Festival (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):`** `مُهْرَجَانَاتٌ` (muhrajānātun) - 'muh-ra-jā-nā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Festivals (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمُهْرَجَانَاتُ الْجَدِيدَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-muhrajānātū al-jadeedatū muhimmatun.) - The new festivals are important.

### 6. `تَأْثِيرٌ` (ta'theerun) - Influence / Impact (Masdar)

This is the verbal noun (Masdar) of `أَثَّرَ` (to influence). It's crucial for discussing how cultural elements affect people or other cultures.

*   **New Word:** `تَأْثِيرٌ` (ta'theerun) - 'ta'-thee-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Influence, impact (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `لِلثَّقَافَةِ تَأْثِيرٌ كَبِيرٌ.` (Li-th-thaqāfati ta'theerun kabeerun.) - Culture has a big influence.

### 7. `يُؤَثِّرُ فِي` (yu'aththiru fī) - He influences / affects (Verb + Preposition)

This is the present tense verb for "to influence" or "to affect," and it typically takes the preposition `فِي`. It's a Form IV verb.

*   **New Word (Verb):** `يُؤَثِّرُ فِي` (yu'aththiru fī) - 'yu'-ath-thi-ru fee' (masculine) / `تُؤَثِّرُ فِي` (tu'aththiru fī) - 'tu'-ath-thi-ru fee' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she influences, affects.
    *   *Example:* `الثَّقَافَةُ تُؤَثِّرُ فِي الْجَمِيعِ.` (Ath-thaqāfatū tu'aththiru fī al-jamee'i.) - Culture influences everyone.

### 8. `يُمَثِّلُ` (yumaththilu) - He represents (Present Tense Verb)

This verb means "to stand for" or "to symbolize." It's a Form II verb.

*   **New Word (Verb):** `يُمَثِّلُ` (yumaththilu) - 'yu-math-thi-lu' (masculine) / `تُمَثِّلُ` (tumaththilu) - 'tu-math-thi-lu' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she represents.
    *   *Example:* `هَذَا الْفَنُّ يُمَثِّلُ تَارِيخًا قَدِيمًا.` (Hādhā al-fannu yumaththilu tārīkhan qadeeman.) - This art represents an old history. (`تَارِيخٌ` - history, masculine noun, Day 106).

### 9. `اهْتِمَامٌ` (ihtimāmun) - Interest (Masdar)

This is the verbal noun (Masdar) for "interest." It often takes the preposition `بِـ` (in).

*   **New Word:** `اهْتِمَامٌ` (ihtimāmun) - 'ih-ti-mā-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Interest (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `هُنَاكَ اهْتِمَامٌ كَبِيرٌ بِالثَّقَافَةِ.` (Hunāka ihtimāmun kabeerun bi-th-thaqāfati.) - There is big interest in culture.

### 10. `يَهْتَمُّ بِـ` (yahtammu bi-) - He is interested in (Verb + Preposition)

This is the present tense verb for "to be interested in," and it takes the preposition `بِـ`. It's a Form VIII verb.

*   **New Word (Verb):** `يَهْتَمُّ بِـ` (yahtammu bi-) - 'yah-tam-mu bi-' (masculine) / `تَهْتَمُّ بِـ` (tahtammu bi-) - 'tah-tam-mu bi-' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she is interested in.
    *   *Example:* `الْجَمِيعُ يَهْتَمُّ بِالْفُنُونِ.` (Al-jamee'u yahtammu bi-l-funooni.) - Everyone is interested in the arts.

### 11. `مُتَنَوِّعٌ` (mutanawwi'un) / `مُتَنَوِّعَةٌ` (mutanawwi'atun) - Diverse / Varied (Active Participle)

This adjective is perfect for describing the rich nature of many cultures or artistic expressions. It's an active participle form.

*   **New Word (Masculine):`** `مُتَنَوِّعٌ` (mutanawwi'un) - 'mu-ta-naw-wi'-un'
    *   *Meaning:* Diverse, varied.
*   **New Word (Feminine):`** `مُتَنَوِّعَةٌ` (mutanawwi'atun) - 'mu-ta-naw-wi'-a-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Diverse, varied.
    *   *Example:* `الثَّقَافَةُ الْعَرَبِيَّةُ مُتَنَوِّعَةٌ.` (Ath-thaqāfatū al-ʿarabiyyatū mutanawwi'atun.) - Arab culture is diverse.

These terms will significantly enhance your ability to describe and report on cultural aspects.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new cultural reporting words and phrases into sentences, using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the cultural events represent a big influence on society."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْأَحْدَاثَ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ تُمَثِّلُ تَأْثِيرًا كَبِيرًا عَلَى الْمُجْتَمَعِ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-aḥdātha ath-thaqāfiyyata tumaththilu ta'theeran kabeeran 'ala al-mujtama'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْأَحْدَاثَ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ` (new plural `الْأَحْدَاثَ`, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 122; `الثَّقَافِيَّةَ` new adjective). `تُمَثِّلُ` (new verb, Day 122). `تَأْثِيرًا كَبِيرًا` (new Masdar `تَأْثِيرًا`, Accusative object, Day 122). `عَلَى الْمُجْتَمَعِ` (on society, `الْمُجْتَمَعُ` - society, masculine noun, Day 106).

2.  **"Despite the fact that the traditions are old, many people are interested in new arts."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ التَّقَالِيدَ قَدِيمَةٌ، يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ النَّاسِ بِالْفُنُونِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna at-taqāleeda qadeematun, yahtammu al-katheeru mina an-nāsi bi-l-funooni al-jadeedati.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Day 107). `التَّقَالِيدَ قَدِيمَةٌ` (new diptote plural `التَّقَالِيدَ`, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 122). `يَهْتَمُّ بِـ` (new verb + preposition, Day 122). `الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ النَّاسِ` (many people, Day 110). `بِالْفُنُونِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (new plural `الْفُنُونِ`, Genitive after `بِـ`, Day 122).

3.  **"The report shows a big increase in cultural festivals. Moreover, the government wants to make the diverse culture clear."**
    *   `يُظْهِرُ التَّقْرِيرُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ الثَّقَافَةَ الْمُتَنَوِّعَةَ وَاضِحَةً.` (Yuẓhiru at-taqrīru ziyādatan kabeeratan fī al-muhrajānāti ath-thaqāfiyyati. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, tureedu al-ḥukūmatu an taj'ala ath-thaqāfata al-mutanawwi'ata wāḍiḥatan.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُظْهِرُ التَّقْرِيرُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي` (Day 115). `الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ` (new plural `الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ` + adjective, Genitive after `فِي`, Day 122). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ` (Day 98, 101). `الثَّقَافَةَ الْمُتَنَوِّعَةَ وَاضِحَةً` (new word `الثَّقَافَةَ`, `الْمُتَنَوِّعَةَ` new adjective, Accusative first object, Day 122; `وَاضِحَةً` Accusative second object).

4.  **"In light of that, everyone wants to protect national traditions and arts. At the same time, foreign influences are increasing."**
    *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنْ يَحْفَظَ التَّقَالِيدَ وَالْفُنُونَ الْوَطَنِيَّةَ. وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ، تَزْدَادُ التَّأْثِيرَاتُ الْأَجْنَبِيَّةُ.` (Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, yureedu al-jamee'u an yaḥfaẓa at-taqāleeda wa-l-funūna al-waṭaniyyata. Wa-fī al-waqti nafsihi, tazdādu at-ta'theerātū al-ajnabiyyatū.)
        *   *Notice:* `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (Day 108). `يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنْ يَحْفَظَ` (Day 112). `التَّقَالِيدَ وَالْفُنُونَ الْوَطَنِيَّةَ` (new plurals `التَّقَالِيدَ` and `الْفُنُونَ` as Accusative objects, Day 122; `الْوَطَنِيَّةَ` as adjective, Day 113). `وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (Day 108). `تَزْدَادُ التَّأْثِيرَاتُ الْأَجْنَبِيَّةُ` (`تَزْدَادُ` Day 115; `التَّأْثِيرَاتُ` - influences, plural of `تَأْثِيرٌ`, new word, Day 122; `الْأَجْنَبِيَّةُ` - foreign, Day 113).

These examples show how you can combine new cultural vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences.

*   مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْأَحْدَاثَ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ تُمَثِّلُ تَأْثِيرًا كَبِيرًا عَلَى الْمُجْتَمَعِ. (It is certain that the cultural events represent a big influence on society.)
*   بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ التَّقَالِيدَ قَدِيمَةٌ، يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ النَّاسِ بِالْفُنُونِ الْجَدِيدَةِ. (Despite the fact that the traditions are old, many people are interested in new arts.)
*   يُظْهِرُ التَّقْرِيرُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ الثَّقَافَةَ الْمُتَنَوِّعَةَ وَاضِحَةً. (The report shows a big increase in cultural festivals. Moreover, the government wants to make the diverse culture clear.)
*   فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنْ يَحْفَظَ التَّقَالِيدَ وَالْفُنُونَ الْوَطَنِيَّةَ. وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ، تَزْدَادُ التَّأْثِيرَاتُ الْأَجْنَبِيَّةُ. (In light of that, everyone wants to protect national traditions and arts. At the same time, foreign influences are increasing.)
