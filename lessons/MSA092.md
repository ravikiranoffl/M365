# Day 92 - Intensive listening to Arabic news (Transcription Day 2)

## Lesson

Today, your goal is to further sharpen your ear by "listening" (reading and comprehending) slightly more complex news-style sentences and actively transcribing them. This exercise forces you to engage deeply with the Arabic text, recognizing not just words, but their precise grammatical forms.

**How to approach transcription:**

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly, multiple times.** Imagine it being spoken naturally. Try to pronounce it aloud as accurately as you can, even if just softly.
2.  **Try to identify each word.** Break the sentence into smaller chunks if necessary.
3.  **Pay close attention to case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) and Sukoon.** These are often the hardest to catch but are essential for correct grammar.
4.  **Write down what you hear.** You're essentially creating a very detailed copy of the text. Focus on getting the Harakat (vowel markings) correct for each word.
5.  **Then, compare your transcription with the correct version and explanation.** Reflect on any differences and why they occurred.

Remember, this is not a test of perfect memory, but an exercise in training your recognition. The more you practice, the faster your brain will process these forms.

## Examples

Here is a short news-style sentence. Read it, imagine hearing it, and try to identify each word and its ending.

**Arabic Text:**

`يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْمُدِيرَةَ فَاطِمَةَ عَادَتْ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ.`

*(Take your time to read and identify the words and their endings. When you are ready, continue below for the breakdown.)*

---

**Breakdown and Explanation:**

The sentence is: `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْمُدِيرَةَ فَاطِمَةَ عَادَتْ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ.`

Let's break it down word by word, identifying each one and its grammatical features:

1.  **`يَبْدُو` (yabdū):**
    *   **Word:** `يَبْدُو` (it seems / it appears).
    *   **Type:** Present tense verb (Day 80).
    *   **Meaning:** It seems.

2.  **`أَنَّ` (anna):**
    *   **Word:** `أَنَّ` (that).
    *   **Type:** Particle introducing a nominal clause.
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the subject of the following clause take Fatha (Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** that.

3.  **`الْمُدِيرَةَ` (al-mudeeratā):**
    *   **Word:** `مُدِيرَةٌ` (manager - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) because it's the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause (Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** the manager.

4.  **`فَاطِمَةَ` (Fāṭimatā):**
    *   **Word:** `فَاطِمَةُ` (Fatima - proper noun).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (proper noun).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) because it's in apposition to `الْمُدِيرَةَ`, and `فَاطِمَةُ` is a diptote (Day 85).
    *   **Meaning:** Fatima.

5.  **`عَادَتْ` (ʿādat):**
    *   **Word:** `عَادَ` (he returned).
    *   **Conjugation:** Past tense, third person feminine singular (`عَادَتْ` - she returned). Note the Sukoon on the `تْ` (Day 78).
    *   **Meaning:** she returned.

6.  **`مِنَ` (mina):**
    *   **Word:** `مِنْ` (from).
    *   **Type:** Preposition. It takes Kasra `ـِ` here to avoid two Sukoons (the original Sukoon on `نْ` and the Sukoon on the `لْ` of `الْقَاهِرَةِ`).
    *   **Meaning:** from.

7.  **`الْقَاهِرَةِ` (al-qāhiratī):**
    *   **Word:** `قَاهِرَةٌ` (Cairo).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Genitive (Kasra - `ـِ`) because it follows the preposition `مِنَ` (Day 87).
    *   **Meaning:** Cairo.

**Full Sentence Meaning:** It seems that the manager, Fatima, returned from Cairo.
*   *Grammar Connection:* This sentence combines hedging language (`يَبْدُو أَنَّ`), apposition (`الْمُدِيرَةَ فَاطِمَةَ`), a motion verb (`عَادَتْ`), and prepositions with a proper noun (`مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ`).

## Practice

For each practice item below, read the Arabic sentence multiple times, imagine hearing it, then try to identify each word and its exact case or mood ending. Write it down, and then check your answer!

1.  **Arabic Text:** `يَعْتَقِدُ الطَّالِبُ الْفَاهِمُ أَنَّ الدَّرْسَ مَفْهُومٌ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `يَعْتَقِدُ` (ya'taqidū): `يَعْتَقِدُ` (he believes), present tense verb (Day 64).
        *   `الطَّالِبُ` (aṭ-ṭālibū): `طَالِبٌ` (student), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject.
        *   `الْفَاهِمُ` (al-fāhimū): `فَاهِمٌ` (understanding), definite, Nominative (Damma), active participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الطَّالِبُ` (Day 88).
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): Particle (that), makes subject take Fatha (Day 39).
        *   `الدَّرْسَ` (ad-darsā): `دَرْسٌ` (lesson), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause.
        *   `مَفْهُومٌ` (mafhūmun): `مَفْهُومٌ` (understood), indefinite, Nominative (Damma), passive participle acting as the comment for `الدَّرْسَ` (Day 88).
        *   **Translation:** The understanding student believes that the lesson is understood.

2.  **Arabic Text:** `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ نَسْأَلَ الْمُدِيرَ عَنِ الْخُطَّةِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `مِنَ` (mina): Preposition `مِنْ` (from), takes Kasra (Day 69).
        *   `الْمُحْتَمَلِ` (al-muḥtamalī): `مُحْتَمَلٌ` (probable), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `مِنَ` (Day 80).
        *   `أَنْ` (an): Particle (to/that), makes verb take Fatha (Subjunctive) (Day 60).
        *   `نَسْأَلَ` (nas'alā): `نَسْأَلُ` (we ask), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `أَنْ` (Day 79).
        *   `الْمُدِيرَ` (al-mudeerā): `مُدِيرٌ` (manager), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as direct object of `نَسْأَلَ` (Day 79).
        *   `عَنِ` (ʿanī): Preposition `عَنْ` (about), takes Kasra (Day 79).
        *   `الْخُطَّةِ` (al-khuṭṭatī): `خُطَّةٌ` (plan), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `عَنِ` (Day 79).
        *   **Translation:** It is probable that we will ask the manager about the plan.

3.  **Arabic Text:** `قَالَتِ الْوَزِيرَةُ: "لَا أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَدْخُلَ السُّوقَ الْآنَ بِسَبَبِ الزِّيَارَةِ."`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `قَالَتِ` (qālatī): `قَالَ` (he said) + `ـتْ` (she), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 39).
        *   `الْوَزِيرَةُ` (al-wazeeratū): `وَزِيرَةٌ` (minister - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as subject.
        *   `لَا` (lā): Negation particle for present tense (Day 21).
        *   `أُرِيدُ` (ureedū): `أُرِيدُ` (I want), present tense verb (Day 44).
        *   `أَنْ` (an): Particle (to/that), makes verb take Fatha (Subjunctive) (Day 60).
        *   `أَدْخُلَ` (adkhulā): `أَدْخُلُ` (I enter), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `أَنْ` (Day 78).
        *   `السُّوقَ` (as-sūqā): `سُوقٌ` (market), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as direct object of `أَدْخُلَ` (Day 51).
        *   `الْآنَ` (al-āna): Adverb of time (now), takes Fatha (Day 44).
        *   `بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi): Prepositional phrase (because of), followed by Genitive (Day 86).
        *   `الزِّيَارَةِ` (az-ziyāratī): `زِيَارَةٌ` (visit), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `بِسَبَبِ` (Day 87).
        *   **Translation:** The minister (f.) said: "I do not want to enter the market now because of the visit."
