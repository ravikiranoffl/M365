# Day 27 - Adverbs of Time and Place

## Lesson

1.  **What are Adverbs of Time and Place?**
    *   Think of these words as answering the questions "When?" (`مَتَى؟`) or "Where?" (`أَيْنَ؟`) in a sentence.
    *   They tell us more information about the action or the location of something.

2.  **Adverbs of Place (ظُرُوفُ الْمَكَانِ - dhurūfu al-makāni)**
    *   These words tell us *where* something is or where an action takes place.
    *   Let's learn four common ones:

    *   **هُنَا (hunā)**: This means **"here"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'hu-naa', with a long 'aa'.
        *   Example: `الْكِتَابُ هُنَا.` (Al-kitaabu hunā.) - The book is here.

    *   **هُنَاكَ (hunāka)**: This means **"there"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'hu-naa-ka', with a long 'aa'.
        *   Example: `الْمُدَرِّسُ هُنَاكَ.` (Al-mudarrisu hunāka.) - The teacher is there.

    *   **فَوْقَ (fawqa)**: This means **"above"** or **"on top of"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'faw-qa'.
        *   **Important!** When you use `فَوْقَ` (or `تَحْتَ` below), it functions like the first noun (`مُضَافٌ`) in an Idafa Construct! This means the noun that follows `فَوْقَ` will be the second noun (`مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ`) and will take a **Kasra ( ِ )** at the end.
        *   Example: `الْقَلَمُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Al-qalamu fawqa al-maktabi.) - The pen is above the desk.
            *   *Notice*: `الْمَكْتَبِ` ends with a Kasra.

    *   **تَحْتَ (taḥta)**: This means **"below"** or **"under"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'taḥ-ta'.
        *   Like `فَوْقَ`, `تَحْتَ` also functions like `مُضَافٌ` in an Idafa. The noun that follows it will take a **Kasra ( ِ )** at the end.
        *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (At-taalibu taḥta al-maktabi.) - The student is under the desk.
            *   *Notice*: `الْمَكْتَبِ` ends with a Kasra.

3.  **Adverbs of Time (ظُرُوفُ الزَّمَانِ - dhurūfu az-zamāni)**
    *   These words tell us *when* something happens.
    *   Let's learn four common ones:

    *   **الْآنَ (al-āna)**: This means **"now"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'al-aa-na', with a long 'aa'. It usually ends with a Fatha.
        *   Example: `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ الْآنَ.` (Ana aktubu al-āna.) - I am writing now.

    *   **الْيَوْمَ (al-yawma)**: This means **"today"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'al-yaw-ma'. It usually ends with a Fatha.
        *   Example: `هِيَ تَدْرُسُ الْيَوْمَ.` (Hiya tadrusu al-yawma.) - She is studying today.

    *   **غَدًا (ghadan)**: This means **"tomorrow"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'gha-dan'. It ends with Tanween Fatha.
        *   Example: `هُوَ سَيَدْرُسُ غَدًا.` (Huwa sayadrusu ghadan.) - He will study tomorrow.

    *   **أَمْسِ (amsi)**: This means **"yesterday"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'am-si'. It usually ends with a Kasra.
        *   Example: `هُمْ دَرَسُوا أَمْسِ.` (Hum darasū amsi.) - They studied yesterday.

You can combine these adverbs with the sentences you already know how to build, using nouns, adjectives, and verbs in the past, present, and future tenses!

## Examples

Let's put these new adverbs of time and place into practice!

1.  **The big book is here.**
    *   `الْكِتَابُ الْكَبِيرُ هُنَا.` (Al-kitaabu al-kabeeru hunā.)

2.  **The new teachers (masculine) are there.**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُونَ الْجَدِيدُونَ هُنَاكَ.` (Al-mudarrisūna al-jadeedūna hunāka.)

3.  **My pen is on top of my book.**
    *   `قَلَمِي فَوْقَ كِتَابِي.` (Qalamī fawqa kitaabī.)
        *   *Notice*: `كِتَابِي` is `كِتَابٌ` + `ـِي`, so it naturally ends in a Kasra sound (`ـِي` works as `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ`).

4.  **The small car is under the new desk.**
    *   `السَّيَّارَةُ الصَّغِيرَةُ تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ الْجَدِيدِ.` (As-sayyaaratu as-sagheeratu taḥta al-maktabi al-jadeedi.)
        *   *Notice*: `تَحْتَ` makes `الْمَكْتَبِ` take Kasra, and `الْجَدِيدِ` (new) is an adjective describing `الْمَكْتَبِ`, so it also takes Kasra and matches in definiteness and gender.

5.  **I am writing the new story now.**
    *   `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ الْقِصَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ الْآنَ.` (Ana aktubu al-qisṣata al-jadeedata al-āna.)
        *   *Notice*: `الْقِصَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` takes Fatha because it's the object of the verb.

6.  **We will study in the university tomorrow.**
    *   `نَحْنُ سَنَدْرُسُ فِي الْجَامِعَةِ غَدًا.` (Naḥnu sanadrusu fī al-jaami'ati ghadan.)
        *   *You could also say:* `نَحْنُ سَوْفَ نَدْرُسُ فِي الْجَامِعَةِ غَدًا.`

7.  **She wrote her name yesterday.**
    *   `هِيَ كَتَبَتْ اِسْمَهَا أَمْسِ.` (Hiya katabat ismahā amsi.)
        *   *Notice*: `اِسْمَهَا` takes Fatha as it's the object of the verb.

8.  **The students (feminine) studied the big books today.**
    *   `الطَّالِبَاتُ دَرَسْنَ الْكُتُبَ الْكَبِيرَةَ الْيَوْمَ.` (At-taalibātu darasna al-kutuba al-kabeerata al-yawma.)
        *   *Note*: The verb `دَرَسْنَ` is the conjugation for "they (feminine) studied". `الْكُتُبَ الْكَبِيرَةَ` is the object, `الْكُتُبَ` is a broken plural, so `الْكَبِيرَةَ` is feminine singular. Both take Fatha as objects.

You can now add precise information about when and where things are happening or happened in Arabic! This makes your sentences much richer.
