# Day 79 - Thinking and speaking verbs

## Lesson

"Thinking and speaking verbs" are verbs that describe mental processes (like knowing, believing) and communication (like saying, announcing, asking). These verbs are often followed by `أَنَّ` (that) for facts or beliefs, or `أَنْ` (to/that) for intended actions, creating embedded clauses.

Let's quickly review the "thinking and speaking" verbs you already know, and then we'll add two new ones!

### I. Review of Known Thinking and Speaking Verbs

Here's a quick reminder of the key verbs you've mastered:

*   **`قَالَ` (qāla) / `يَقُولُ` (yaqūlu) - He said / He says (Day 39)**
    *   *Usage:* `قَالَ أَنَّ الْبَيْتَ كَبِيرٌ.` (He said that the house is big.)
*   **`يَعْتَقِدُ` (ya'taqidu) - He believes (Day 64)**
    *   *Usage:* `نَحْنُ نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ السَّلَامَ مُهِمٌّ.` (We believe that peace is important.)
*   **`يَعْلَمُ` (ya'lamu) - He knows (Day 68)**
    *   *Usage:* `الْمُدَرِّسُ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ نَاجِحٌ.` (The teacher knows that the student is successful.)
*   **`يَشْرَحُ` (yashraḥu) - He explains (Day 68)**
    *   *Usage:* `الْقَاضِي يَشْرَحُ أَنَّ الْقَانُونَ مُهِمٌّ.` (The judge explains that the law is important.)
*   **`يَأْمُلُ` (ya'mulu) - He hopes (Day 67)**
    *   *Usage:* `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ يَدًا بِيَدٍ.` (We hope to work hand in hand.)
*   **`يُؤَكِّدُ` (yu'akkidu) - He affirms (Day 64)**
    *   *Usage:* `الرَّئِيسُ يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ مُهِمٌّ.` (The president affirms that the agreement is important.)
*   **`يُعْلِنُ` (yu'linu) - He announces (Day 64)**
    *   *Usage:* `الشَّرِكَةُ تُعْلِنُ أَنَّهَا سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (The company announces that it will protect the environment.)

Remember that `أَنَّ` makes the noun after it take a Fatha, and `أَنْ` makes the present tense verb after it take a Fatha (Subjunctive mood).

### II. New Thinking and Speaking Verbs

Let's add two new, very common verbs to your vocabulary!

#### 1. To Think / To Assume (`ظَنَّ` / `يَظُنُّ`)

This verb is used when you think something is true, or you assume something.

*   **New Verb: "To Think / To Assume"**
    *   **Root:** `ظ ن ن` (ẓ-n-n)
    *   **Past Tense (he thought/assumed):** `ظَنَّ` (ẓanna) - 'ẓan-na'. (This is a geminated verb, so the last two letters are the same).
    *   **Present Tense (he thinks/assumes):** `يَظُنُّ` (yaẓunnu) - 'ya-ẓun-nu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she thinks/assumes / you-m.s. think/assume):** `تَظُنُّ` (taẓunnu) - 'ta-ẓun-nu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I think/assume):** `أَظُنُّ` (aẓunnu) - 'a-ẓun-nu'.
    *   **Present Tense (we think/assume):** `نَظُنُّ` (naẓunnu) - 'na-ẓun-nu'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `ظَنٌّ` (ẓannun) - thought, assumption (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb is typically followed by `أَنَّ` (that) to express an assumption or a belief, just like `يَعْتَقِدُ`.
    *   *Example:* `هُوَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ الطَّقْسَ جَمِيلٌ.` (Huwa yaẓunnu anna aṭ-ṭaqsa jameelun.) - He thinks that the weather is beautiful.
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ` makes `الطَّقْسَ` (the weather - new word) take Fatha.

#### 2. To Ask (`سَأَلَ` / `يَسْأَلُ`)

This verb is used when you ask a question or ask someone for something.

*   **New Verb: "To Ask"**
    *   **Root:** `س أ ل` (s-'-l) - This verb has a Hamza (أ) in its root.
    *   **Past Tense (he asked):** `سَأَلَ` (sa'ala) - 'sa-a-la'.
    *   **Present Tense (he asks):** `يَسْأَلُ` (yas'alu) - 'yas-a-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she asks / you-m.s. ask):** `تَسْأَلُ` (tas'alu) - 'tas-a-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I ask):** `أَسْأَلُ` (as'alu) - 'as-a-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (we ask):** `نَسْأَلُ` (nas'alu) - 'nas-a-lu'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `سُؤَالٌ` (su'ālun) - question (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb usually takes the person being asked as a direct object, and sometimes `عَنْ` (about) for what is being asked. For simplicity today, we'll mostly use it with the person being asked as the object.
    *   *Example:* `الطَّالِبُ يَسْأَلُ الْمُدَرِّسَ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yas'alu al-mudarrisa.) - The student asks the teacher.
        *   *Notice:* `الْمُدَرِّسَ` (the teacher) takes Fatha as the direct object.
    *   *Example with `عَنْ`:* `هُوَ يَسْأَلُ عَنِ الْأَخْبَارِ.` (Huwa yas'alu 'ani al-akhbāri.) - He asks about the news.
        *   *Notice:* `عَنِ` (about) makes `الْأَخْبَارِ` take Kasra.

These two new verbs, `يَظُنُّ` and `يَسْأَلُ`, are very common and will greatly expand your ability to talk about thoughts and communication!

## Examples

Let's combine these new verbs with the grammar and vocabulary you've already learned.

1.  **"I think that the problem is big."**
    *   `أَنَا أَظُنُّ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Ana aẓunnu anna al-mushkilata kabeeratun.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَظُنُّ` (I think). `أَنَّ` makes `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (the problem) take Fatha.

2.  **"The minister (f.) asks the president (m.)."**
    *   `تَسْأَلُ الْوَزِيرَةُ الرَّئِيسَ.` (Tas'alu al-wazeeratū ar-ra'eeca.)
        *   *Notice:* `تَسْأَلُ` (she asks). `الْوَزِيرَةُ` is the subject. `الرَّئِيسَ` (the president) is the direct object.

3.  **"We thought that the agreement was not important."**
    *   `نَحْنُ ظَنَنَّا أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ لَيْسَ مُهِمًّا.` (Naḥnu ẓanannā anna al-ittifāqa laysa muhimman.)
        *   *Notice:* `ظَنَنَّا` (we thought - past tense, `نَحنُ` subject). `أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ` (that the agreement) with Fatha. `لَيْسَ مُهِمًّا` (it is not important - Day 73).

4.  **"The student (m.) wants to ask the teacher (f.) about the lesson."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ الطَّالِبُ أَنْ يَسْأَلَ الْمُدَرِّسَةَ عَنِ الدَّرْسِ.` (Yureedu aṭ-ṭālibu an yas'ala al-mudarrisata 'ani ad-darsi.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَسْأَلَ` (he wants to ask - `يَسْأَلَ` is Subjunctive). `الْمُدَرِّسَةَ` is the direct object. `عَنِ الدَّرْسِ` (about the lesson - `عَنِ` makes `الدَّرْسِ` take Kasra).

5.  **"It is clear that the doctor thinks that health is important."**
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الطَّبِيبَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ الصِّحَّةَ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna aṭ-ṭabeeba yaẓunnu anna aṣ-ṣiḥḥata muhimmatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (Day 69). `الطَّبِيبَ` takes Fatha. `يَظُنُّ أَنَّ` (he thinks that). `الصِّحَّةَ` takes Fatha.

6.  **"The company announced that it will ask about the new project."**
    *   `الشَّرِكَةُ تُعْلِنُ أَنَّهَا سَتَسْأَلُ عَنِ الْمَشْرُوعِ الْجَدِيدِ.` (Ash-sharikatū tu'linu annahā satas'alu 'ani al-mashroo'i al-jadeedi.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُعْلِنُ أَنَّهَا` (she announces that she). `سَتَسْأَلُ` (she will ask - future tense). `عَنِ الْمَشْرُوعِ الْجَدِيدِ` (about the new project).

7.  **"We hope to enter the market and ask about the prices."**
    *   `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَدْخُلَ السُّوقَ وَ نَسْأَلَ عَنِ الْأَسْعَارِ.` (Na'mulu an nadkhula as-sūqa wa nas'ala 'ani al-as'āri.)
        *   *New word:* `أَسْعَارٌ` (as'ārun) - prices (non-human broken plural for `سِعْرٌ` price).
        *   *Notice:* `نَأْمُلُ أَنْ` (we hope to). Both `نَدْخُلَ` (we enter) and `نَسْأَلَ` (we ask) are Subjunctive. `السُّوقَ` (the market) is the object of `نَدْخُلَ`. `عَنِ الْأَسْعَارِ` (about the prices).
