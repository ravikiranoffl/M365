# Day 84 - Quotation strategies

## Lesson

When we want to show what someone said or thought, we have different ways to do it.

### 1. Direct Quotation

This is when you repeat the exact words someone said, word for word.

*   **How it works:** You use a verb like `قَالَ` (he said) or `قَالَتْ` (she said), and then you present the exact words. In Arabic, quotation marks (like " ") are sometimes used, but not always in the same way as English. Often, a colon (:) is used, or the quoted speech simply follows the verb.
*   **The key rule:** When you use direct quotation, you **DO NOT** use `أَنَّ` (that) or `أَنْ` (to/that) before the quoted sentence. The sentence inside the quote keeps its original structure, as if it were a standalone sentence.
*   **Example:** Imagine someone says: "The house is big."
    *   `قَالَ: "الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ."` (Qāla: "Al-baytu kabeerun.") - He said: "The house is big."
    *   *Notice:* The sentence `الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ` is a normal nominal sentence (Topic `الْبَيْتُ` with Damma, Comment `كَبِيرٌ` with Damma). It does not change because it's a direct quote.

### 2. Indirect Quotation (Review)

This is when you report what someone said, but you don't use their exact words. You often summarize or rephrase it, and you use words like "that" in English. You are already very familiar with this from our lessons on `أَنَّ` and reporting verbs!

*   **How it works:** You use a reporting verb (like `قَالَ`, `يَعْتَقِدُ`, `يُعْلِنُ`, `يَشْرَحُ`) followed by `أَنَّ` (that) for statements of fact/belief, or `أَنْ` (to/that) for desired/intended actions.
*   **The key rule:** `أَنَّ` (or `أَنْ`) will affect the word that comes immediately after it.
    *   After `أَنَّ`, the subject of the embedded clause takes a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   After `أَنْ`, the present tense verb takes a **Fatha** (َ) (Subjunctive mood).
*   **Example:** Imagine someone said: "The house is big."
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْبَيْتَ كَبِيرٌ.` (Qāla anna al-bayta kabeerun.) - He said that the house is big.
    *   *Notice:* We use `أَنَّ`. The word `الْبَيْتَ` now takes a Fatha because it is the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause.

### Summary of Differences:

| Feature           | Direct Quotation                               | Indirect Quotation (Reporting Verbs)                            |
| :---------------- | :--------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Connector**     | Often a colon (:) or nothing; NO `أَنَّ` / `أَنْ` | `أَنَّ` (for facts/beliefs) or `أَنْ` (for desired actions)      |
| **Word Changes**  | Quoted sentence keeps its original structure.  | The word after `أَنَّ` takes Fatha. The verb after `أَنْ` takes Fatha (Subjunctive). |
| **Exact Words?**  | Yes, word for word.                            | No, reported or summarized.                                     |

Most of the "Thinking and Speaking Verbs" you learned on Day 79 (`يَعْتَقِدُ`, `يَعْلَمُ`, `يَشْرَحُ`, `يَظُنُّ`, `يُؤَكِّدُ`, `يُعْلِنُ`, `يَأْمُلُ`) naturally lead to indirect quotation because they describe someone's thoughts, beliefs, or announcements *about* something. The verb `قَالَ` (to say) is unique because it can introduce *both* direct and indirect speech.

Understanding this difference is a big step in expressing yourself clearly and accurately in Arabic!

## Examples

Let's see more examples of direct and indirect quotation strategies.

1.  **Direct:** **"The student said: 'I read the book.'"**
    *   `قَالَ الطَّالِبُ: "أَنَا أَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ."` (Qāla aṭ-ṭālibu: "Ana aqra'u al-kitāba.")
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَا أَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ` is the exact sentence. No `أَنَّ`.

2.  **Indirect:** **"The student said that he reads the book."**
    *   `قَالَ الطَّالِبُ أَنَّهُ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Qāla aṭ-ṭālibu annahu yaqra'u al-kitāba.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّهُ` (that he) because of `أَنَّ`. `يَقْرَأُ` keeps its Damma because `أَنَّ` does not change verb endings.

3.  **Direct:** **"She said: 'The problem is big.'"**
    *   `قَالَتْ: "الْمُشْكِلَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ."` (Qālat: "Al-mushkilatu kabeeratun.")
        *   *Notice:* `الْمُشْكِلَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ` is a normal nominal sentence.

4.  **Indirect:** **"She said that the problem is big."**
    *   `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Qālat anna al-mushkilata kabeeratun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` takes Fatha due to `أَنَّ`.

5.  **Direct:** **"The minister (m.) announced: 'We will protect the environment.'"**
    *   `أَعْلَنَ الْوَزِيرُ: "نَحْنُ سَنَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ."` (A'lana al-wazeeru: "Naḥnu sa-naḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.")
        *   *Notice:* `نَحْنُ سَنَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ` is the direct sentence.

6.  **Indirect:** **"The minister (m.) announced that he will protect the environment."**
    *   `أَعْلَنَ الْوَزِيرُ أَنَّهُ سَيَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (A'lana al-wazeeru annahu sa-yaḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّهُ` (that he). The `ـهُ` pronoun takes Fatha due to `أَنَّ`. `سَيَحْفَظُ` (he will protect) keeps its Damma.

7.  **Direct:** **"The teacher (f.) asked: 'Why are you (m.s.) not reading the magazine?'"**
    *   `سَأَلَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ: "لِمَاذَا لَا تَقْرَأُ الْمَجَلَّةَ؟"` (Sa'alati al-mudarrisatū: "Limādhā lā taqra'u al-majallata?")
        *   *Notice:* `لِمَاذَا لَا تَقْرَأُ الْمَجَلَّةَ؟` is the exact question.

8.  **Indirect:** **"The teacher (f.) asked why he is not reading the magazine."**
    *   `سَأَلَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ لِمَاذَا هُوَ لَا يَقْرَأُ الْمَجَلَّةَ.` (Sa'alati al-mudarrisatū limādhā huwa lā yaqra'u al-majallata.)
        *   *Notice:* Here, `لِمَاذَا` acts as the connector, not `أَنَّ`. The rest of the sentence (the reported question) keeps its normal structure. This is a common way to report questions.
