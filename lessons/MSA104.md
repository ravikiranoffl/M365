# Day 104 - Diplomacy vocabulary

## Lesson

Understanding diplomatic terms is crucial for anyone engaging with Arabic media or formal discussions about international affairs. These words often appear in news reports about international relations, peace talks, and political agreements.

Let's learn some new and essential diplomacy vocabulary!

### 1. `عَلَاقَةٌ` (ʿalāqatun) - Relation / Relationship

This noun refers to the way in which two or more people, groups, or countries are connected or interact.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `عَلَاقَةٌ` (ʿalāqatun) - 'a-lā-qa-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Relation, relationship (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْعَلَاقَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-'alāqatu muhimmatun.) - The relation is important.
*   **New Word (Plural):** `عَلَاقَاتٌ` (ʿalāqātun) - 'a-lā-qā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Relations, relationships (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْعَلَاقَاتُ جَيِّدَةٌ.` (Al-'alāqātu jayyidatun.) - The relations are good. (Remember non-human plurals take feminine singular adjectives, Day 27).

### 2. `مُفَاوَضَاتٌ` (mufāwaḍātun) - Negotiations

This noun refers to formal discussions between people or groups with the aim of reaching an agreement. It is usually used in the plural form.

*   **New Word (Plural):** `مُفَاوَضَاتٌ` (mufāwaḍātun) - 'mu-fā-wa-ḍā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Negotiations (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمُفَاوَضَاتُ صَعْبَةٌ.` (Al-mufāwaḍātu ṣa'batun.) - The negotiations are difficult.

### 3. `صِرَاعٌ` (ṣirāʿun) - Conflict / Struggle

This noun refers to a serious disagreement or argument, often between countries or groups, that can lead to war. It's the opposite of peace (`سَلَامٌ`, Day 94).

*   **New Word:** `صِرَاعٌ` (ṣirāʿun) - 'ṣi-rā-ʿun'
    *   *Meaning:* Conflict, struggle (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `تُرِيدُ الدَّوْلَةُ أَنْ تَحُلَّ الصِّرَاعَ.` (Tureedu ad-dawlatu an taḥulla aṣ-ṣirāʿa.) - The state wants to solve the conflict. (Using `يَحُلُّ` - to solve, Day 50, Day 93).

### 4. `اتِّفَاقٌ` (ittifāqun) - Agreement

This noun refers to a mutual understanding or arrangement, often formal or contractual, between two or more parties.

*   **New Word:** `اتِّفَاقٌ` (ittifāqun) - 'it-ti-fā-qun'
    *   *Meaning:* Agreement (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْحُكُومَةُ تُرِيدُ اتِّفَاقًا جَدِيدًا.` (Al-ḥukūmatu tureedu ittifāqan jadeedan.) - The government wants a new agreement.

### 5. `مُمَثِّلٌ` (mumatthilun) - Representative

This noun refers to a person chosen to act or speak on behalf of another person or a group, especially in politics or diplomacy.

*   **New Word:** `مُمَثِّلٌ` (mumatthilun) - 'mu-mat-thi-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Representative (masculine noun). (Feminine: `مُمَثِّلَةٌ` - `mumatthilatun`).
    *   *Example:* `الْمُمَثِّلُ سَيَتَكَلَّمُ عَنِ الدَّوْلَةِ.` (Al-mumatthilu sayatakallamu 'ani ad-dawlati.) - The representative will speak about the state.

### 6. `وِزَارَةُ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ` (wizāratu al-khārijiyyati) - Ministry of Foreign Affairs

This is the government department responsible for a country's relationships with foreign countries. It's an Idafa construction using `وِزَارَةٌ` (ministry, Day 101) and `الْخَارِجِيَّةُ` (foreign, feminine adjective).

*   **New Phrase:** `وِزَارَةُ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ` (wizāratu al-khārijiyyati) - 'wi-zā-ra-tu al-khā-ri-jee-yya-ti'
    *   *Meaning:* Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Idafa construction).
    *   *Example:* `وِزَارَةُ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ مُهِمَّةٌ لِلدَّوْلَةِ.` (Wizāratu al-khārijiyyati muhimmatun li-d-dawlati.) - The Ministry of Foreign Affairs is important for the state.

These terms are foundational for understanding news and discussions about international politics and relations.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new diplomacy words into sentences using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the relations became difficult because of the conflict."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْعَلَاقَاتِ أَصْبَحَتْ صَعْبَةً بِسَبَبِ الصِّرَاعِ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-'alāqāti aṣbaḥat ṣa'batan bi-sababi aṣ-ṣirāʿi.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْعَلَاقَاتِ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`, Day 36 for sound feminine plural Accusative). `أَصْبَحَتْ صَعْبَةً` (became difficult - feminine verb for non-human plural subject, Day 98). `بِسَبَبِ الصِّرَاعِ` (because of the conflict - Day 86).

2.  **"The minister (m.) wants to undertake negotiations for a new agreement."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَقُومَ بِمُفَاوَضَاتٍ لِاتِّفَاقٍ جَدِيدٍ.` (Yureedu al-wazeeru an yaqūma bi-mufāwaḍātin li-ittifāqin jadeedin.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ` (known). `أَنْ يَقُومَ بِـ` (wants to undertake - Day 98, Subjunctive). `مُفَاوَضَاتٍ` (negotiations - Genitive with Kasra Tanween because it's a sound feminine plural after `بِـ`). `لِاتِّفَاقٍ جَدِيدٍ` (for a new agreement - `لِـ` preposition, then `اتِّفَاقٍ` and `جَدِيدٍ` in Genitive).

3.  **"Perhaps the representative (f.) will speak about the Ministry of Foreign Affairs."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا تَتَكَلَّمُ الْمُمَثِّلَةُ عَنْ وِزَارَةِ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ.` (Rubbamā tatakallamu al-mumatthilatū 'an wizārati al-khārijiyyati.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (Day 80). `تَتَكَلَّمُ عَنْ` (speaks about - Day 97). `الْمُمَثِّلَةُ` (feminine representative). `وِزَارَةِ الْخَارِجِيَّةِ` (Ministry of Foreign Affairs - Idafa, Genitive after `عَنْ`).

4.  **"It is known that the big conflict is important for the relations of the state."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الصِّرَاعَ الْكَبِيرَ مُهِمٌّ لِعَلَاقَاتِ الدَّوْلَةِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna aṣ-ṣirāʿa al-kabeera muhimun li-'alāqāti ad-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الصِّرَاعَ الْكَبِيرَ` (the big conflict - Accusative as subject of `أَنَّ`). `مُهِمٌّ` (important). `لِعَلَاقَاتِ الدَّوْلَةِ` (for the relations of the state - Idafa, Genitive after `لِـ`).

5.  **"The ambassador (m.) believes that the government made the agreement easy."**
    *   `يَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرُ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ جَعَلَتِ الِاتِّفَاقَ سَهْلًا.` (Ya'taqidu as-safeeru anna al-ḥukūmata ja'alati al-ittifāqa sahlan.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرُ أَنَّ` (Day 79, Day 101). `الْحُكُومَةَ` (Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 101). `جَعَلَتِ` (she made - feminine verb, Day 98). `الِاتِّفَاقَ سَهْلًا` (the agreement easy - both Accusative, Day 98).

6.  **"Although the negotiations are long, the representative (m.) wants a good relation."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْمُفَاوَضَاتِ طَوِيلَةٌ، يُرِيدُ الْمُمَثِّلُ عَلَاقَةً جَيِّدَةً.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-mufāwaḍāti ṭaweelatun, yureedu al-mumatthilu 'alāqatan jayyidatan.)
        *   *Notice:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (Day 81). `الْمُفَاوَضَاتِ طَوِيلَةٌ` (negotiations are long - Accusative subject for `أَنَّ`, Nominative comment). `يُرِيدُ الْمُمَثِّلُ` (the representative wants). `عَلَاقَةً جَيِّدَةً` (a good relation - Accusative).

These examples show how you can combine new vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences about diplomacy.
