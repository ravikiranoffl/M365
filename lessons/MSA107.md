# Day 107 - Advanced connectors Part 1

## Lesson

As you progress in Arabic, you'll encounter and want to use connectors that go beyond simple "and" or "but." These advanced connectors help you build more elaborate sentences, express logical relationships, and structure your arguments in a clear and formal way. They are crucial for reading news, academic texts, and engaging in sophisticated conversations.

Let's learn three important advanced connectors today!

### 1. `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (bi-r-raghmi min anna) - Despite the fact that / Although

This phrase is a more formal and emphatic way to express contrast or concession, similar to `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (Day 81), but often carrying a stronger sense of "in spite of" or "despite."

*   **New Phrase:** `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (bi-r-raghmi min anna) - 'bi-r-ragh-mi min an-na'
    *   *Literal translation:* "In spite of the fact that."
    *   *How it works:*
        *   `بِـ` is a preposition (meaning "in/with").
        *   `الرَّغْمِ` is a noun in the Genitive case because it follows `بِـ`.
        *   `مِنْ` is another preposition (meaning "from").
        *   `أَنَّ` is the particle that introduces a nominal clause (Day 39).
        *   Therefore, the **subject of the clause immediately following `أَنَّ` will take the Accusative case** (Fatha - `ـَ` or `ـًـَ`).
*   *Example:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الطَّقْسَ سَيِّئٌ، خَرَجَ الْمُدِيرُ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna aṭ-ṭaqsa sayyi'un, kharaja al-mudeeru.)
    *   *Translation:* Despite the fact that the weather is bad, the manager exited.
    *   *Breakdown:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (despite the fact that). `الطَّقْسَ` (the weather - Accusative, subject of `أَنَّ`). `سَيِّئٌ` (bad - Nominative, comment). `خَرَجَ الْمُدِيرُ` (the manager exited - Day 78).

### 2. `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (ʿalāwatan ʿalā dhālika) - Moreover / Furthermore / In addition to that

This connector is used to add more information or another point to what has already been said. It signals that you are building upon a previous statement.

*   **New Phrase:** `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (ʿalāwatan ʿalā dhālika) - 'a-lā-wa-tan 'a-lā dhā-li-ka'
    *   *Literal translation:* "Addition upon that."
    *   *How it works:* `عَلَاوَةً` is an adverb (taking Fatha Tanween). `عَلَى` is a preposition (Day 17) and `ذَلِكَ` (that) is a demonstrative pronoun (Day 14). This phrase typically starts a new sentence or a new clause. It does not directly affect the case endings of the following words, which will be determined by the grammar of that new clause.
*   *Example:* `الِاسْتِثْمَارُ مُهِمٌّ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ النَّجَاحَ.` (Al-istihtmāru muhimun. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, yureedu al-jamee'u an-najāḥa.)
    *   *Translation:* Investment is important. Moreover, everyone wants success.
    *   *Breakdown:* `الِاسْتِثْمَارُ مُهِمٌّ` (investment is important - Day 103). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (moreover). `يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ النَّجَاحَ` (everyone wants success - `نَجَاحٌ` is Masdar of `يَنْجَحُ`, Day 58).

### 3. `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (wa-binā'an ʿalā dhālika) - And accordingly / Based on that / Therefore

This connector is used to introduce a consequence or a logical conclusion based on a previous statement. It's similar to `لِذَلِكَ` (therefore, Day 86) but can be more formal or emphasize the basis of the conclusion.

*   **New Phrase:** `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (wa-binā'an ʿalā dhālika) - 'wa-bi-nā-an 'a-lā dhā-li-ka'
    *   *Literal translation:* "And building upon that."
    *   *How it works:* `وَ` is "and." `بِنَاءً` is an adverb (taking Fatha Tanween). `عَلَى` is a preposition and `ذَلِكَ` is a demonstrative pronoun. Like `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ`, this phrase typically starts a new sentence or clause and does not directly affect the case endings of the following words.
*   *Example:* `جَاءَ الْوَزِيرُ إِلَى مِصْرَ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، سَيَتَكَلَّمُ عَنِ الْعَلَاقَاتِ.` (Jā'a al-wazeeru ilā Miṣra. Wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika, sayatakallamu 'ani al-'alāqāti.)
    *   *Translation:* The minister came to Egypt. And accordingly, he will speak about relations.
    *   *Breakdown:* `جَاءَ الْوَزِيرُ إِلَى مِصْرَ` (the minister came to Egypt - Day 78, 87, 101). `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (and accordingly). `سَيَتَكَلَّمُ عَنِ الْعَلَاقَاتِ` (he will speak about relations - Day 97, 104).

These advanced connectors will significantly enhance your ability to express complex ideas and link sentences in a coherent and formal manner.

## Examples

Let's see these advanced connectors in more complex sentences, combining them with grammar and vocabulary you've already mastered.

1.  **"Despite the fact that the negotiations are long, the government wants a new agreement."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُفَاوَضَاتِ طَوِيلَةٌ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ اتِّفَاقًا جَدِيدًا.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-mufāwaḍāti ṭaweelatun, tureedu al-ḥukūmatu ittifāqan jadeedan.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (new connector). `الْمُفَاوَضَاتِ` (Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, feminine plural, Day 104, Day 36). `طَوِيلَةٌ` (Nominative comment, feminine singular adjective for non-human plural, Day 27). `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ` (the government wants, Day 101). `اتِّفَاقًا جَدِيدًا` (a new agreement, Day 104).

2.  **"The crisis is big. Moreover, terrorism is a danger to the state."**
    *   `الْأَزْمَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، الْإِرْهَابُ خَطَرٌ عَلَى الدَّوْلَةِ.` (Al-azmatu kabeeratun. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, al-irhābu khaṭarun 'alā ad-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْأَزْمَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ` (the crisis is big, Day 105). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (moreover, new connector). `الْإِرْهَابُ خَطَرٌ عَلَى الدَّوْلَةِ` (terrorism is a danger to the state, Day 105).

3.  **"The court made the law clear. And accordingly, the judge announced the decision."**
    *   `جَعَلَتِ الْمَحْكَمَةُ الْقَانُونَ وَاضِحًا. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، أَعْلَنَ الْقَاضِي الْقَرَارَ.` (Ja'alati al-maḥkamatu al-qānoona wāḍiḥan. Wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika, a'lana al-qāḍī al-qarāra.)
        *   *Notice:* `جَعَلَتِ الْمَحْكَمَةُ الْقَانُونَ وَاضِحًا` (the court made the law clear, Day 98, 102). `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (and accordingly, new connector). `أَعْلَنَ الْقَاضِي الْقَرَارَ` (the judge announced the decision, Day 64, 102).

4.  **"It is certain that the ambassador, Fatima, desires to undertake a big project. Moreover, she will speak about the economy."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ السَّفِيرَةَ فَاطِمَةَ تَرْغَبُ فِي أَنْ تَقُومَ بِمَشْرُوعٍ كَبِيرٍ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، سَتَتَكَلَّمُ عَنِ الِاقْتِصَادِ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna as-safeerata Fāṭimata targhabu fī an taqūma bi-mashroo'in kabeerin. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, satatakallamu 'ani al-iqtiṣādi.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `السَّفِيرَةَ فَاطِمَةَ` (Accusative apposition, Day 101, Day 85). `تَرْغَبُ فِي أَنْ تَقُومَ بِـ` (desires to undertake, Day 97, 98). `مَشْرُوعٍ كَبِيرٍ` (a big project, Genitive after `بِـ`). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (moreover). `سَتَتَكَلَّمُ عَنِ الِاقْتِصَادِ` (she will speak about the economy, Day 97, 103).

5.  **"Despite the fact that the work is important, the student wants to return home. And accordingly, he will not study."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْعَمَلَ مُهِمٌّ، يُرِيدُ الطَّالِبُ أَنْ يَعُودَ إِلَى بَيْتِهِ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، لَنْ يَدْرُسَ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-'amala muhimun, yureedu aṭ-ṭālibu an ya'ooda ilā baytihi. Wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika, lan yadrusa.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (new connector). `الْعَمَلَ مُهِمٌّ` (the work is important, Accusative subject, Nominative comment). `يُرِيدُ الطَّالِبُ أَنْ يَعُودَ إِلَى بَيْتِهِ` (the student wants to return home, Day 78, 60). `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (and accordingly). `لَنْ يَدْرُسَ` (he will not study, Day 60).
