# Day 35 - Weekly speaking test

## Lesson

Mudarris365 here! For this test, I want you to **say your answers aloud** as much as possible, just as if we were in a classroom together. Even if you're not speaking to anyone, forming the words and sentences with your mouth helps solidify them in your memory. Don't worry about mistakes; the goal is to practice!

I will ask you to:
1.  Read some Arabic sentences aloud.
2.  Translate sentences from English to Arabic.
3.  Explain some grammar points.
4.  Answer questions in Arabic.

Take your time with each question. Ready? Let's begin!

### Section 1: Reading and Comprehension

**Task 1: Read the following sentences aloud with correct pronunciation.**
*   Sentence A: `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ الْقِصَّةَ الَّتِي دَرَسَتْ.`
*   Sentence B: `إِذَا ذَهَبَ الطَّالِبُ، فَهُوَ سَيَكْتُبُ.`
*   Sentence C: `الْمَدْرَسَةُ أَكْبَرُ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ لِأَنَّهَا جَدِيدَةٌ.`

**Task 2: Now, translate each of those three sentences (A, B, C) into English.**

### Section 2: Sentence Construction (English to Arabic)

**Task 3: Translate the following English sentences into Arabic. Say the Arabic sentence aloud.**

1.  "I eat the food and drink the water every day."
    *(Hint: Use `الطَّعَامَ` and `الْمَاءَ`)*
2.  "The book that is on the desk is small."
    *(Hint: "on the desk" is `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ`)*
3.  "If she studies, then she will go to the university."
    *(Hint: "she studies" is `دَرَسَتْ` for the condition, "she will go" is `سَتَذْهَبُ` for the result, "the university" is `الْجَامِعَةِ`)*
4.  "The teacher (masculine) is smarter than the student (masculine)."
5.  "These are the most beautiful cars."
    *(Hint: "these are" uses `هَذِهِ`, "most beautiful" uses `أَجْمَلُ` as part of an Idafa, "cars" is `سَيَّارَاتٍ`)*

### Section 3: Grammar Spot Check

**Task 4: For each Arabic sentence below, answer the question aloud.**

1.  `هَذِهِ الْكُتُبُ الْكَبِيرَةُ.`
    *   Question: Why does `الْكَبِيرَةُ` (big) appear to be feminine singular, even though it describes `الْكُتُبُ` (books), which is plural? What is the rule here?

2.  `الْقَلَمُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.`
    *   Question: Why does `الْمَكْتَبِ` (the desk) end with a Kasra? What grammatical rule is at play here?

3.  `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ.`
    *   Question: If I wanted to say "I **did not** go to the school every day," how would the sentence change? What word would I add, and where?

4.  `هُوَ طَالِبٌ. كِتَابُهُ جَدِيدٌ.`
    *   Question: What does the attached pronoun `ـهُ` (his) in `كِتَابُهُ` refer back to?

### Section 4: Putting It All Together

**Task 5: Formulate and say aloud an Arabic sentence that includes:**
*   A `Relative Clause` (using `الَّذِي` or `الَّتِي`)
*   An `Idafa Construct` (e.g., "my car" or "the student's book")
*   An `Adverb of Time` (e.g., `الْآنَ`, `غَدًا`)

*(Example for you, don't copy: `الْقَلَمُ الَّذِي فَوْقَ كِتَابِي صَغِيرٌ الْآنَ.` - The pen that is on my book is small now.)*
