# Day 123 - Fluency: Science reporting

## Lesson

"Science reporting" involves using specific vocabulary and phrases to describe scientific research, findings, and developments. This topic is essential for engaging with modern Arabic news, academic discussions, and formal reports. You already know `عِلْمٌ` (knowledge/science) from Day 112, which is our starting point!

Let's learn some new and essential vocabulary for science reporting today!

### 1. `عِلْمِيٌّ` (ʿilmiyyun) / `عِلْمِيَّةٌ` (ʿilmiyyatun) - Scientific (Adjective)

This is the adjective form of `عِلْمٌ` (science/knowledge). It follows the "nisba" adjective pattern.

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `عِلْمِيٌّ` (ʿilmiyyun) - 'ʿil-miy-yun'
    *   *Meaning:* Scientific.
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `عِلْمِيَّةٌ` (ʿilmiyyatun) - 'ʿil-miy-ya-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Scientific.
    *   *Example:* `الْبَحْثُ عِلْمِيٌّ.` (Al-baḥthu ʿilmiyyun.) - The research is scientific. (`بَحْثٌ` - search/research, Day 97).

### 2. `بَحْثٌ` (baḥthun) / `أَبْحَاثٌ` (abḥāthun) - Research (Singular/Plural)

You know `بَحْثٌ` as "search" or "research" (Masdar) from Day 97. We'll now formally introduce its plural, which is frequently used for "research projects" or "studies." `أَبْحَاثٌ` is a broken plural and a diptote, so its adjectives will be feminine singular (Day 27).

*   **Known Word (Singular):** `بَحْثٌ` (baḥthun) - 'baḥ-thun'
    *   *Meaning:* Research (masculine Masdar/noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `أَبْحَاثٌ` (abḥāthun) - 'ab-ḥā-thun'
    *   *Meaning:* Research projects, studies (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example (Plural):* `الْأَبْحَاثُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-abḥāthū al-ʿilmiyyatū muhimmatun.) - The scientific research projects are important. (`عِلْمِيَّةٌ` is feminine singular adjective for non-human plural).
    *   *Example (Genitive, indefinite):* `فِي أَبْحَاثَ جَدِيدَةٍ.` (Fī abḥātha jadeedatin.) - In new research projects. (`أَبْحَاثَ` takes Fatha because it's a diptote in Genitive; `جَدِيدَةٍ` takes Kasra Tanween).

### 3. `تَجْرِبَةٌ` (tajribatun) / `تَجَارِبُ` (tajāribu) - Experiment / Experiments

This noun is crucial for discussing scientific methodology. `تَجَارِبُ` is a broken plural and a diptote.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `تَجْرِبَةٌ` (tajribatun) - 'taj-ri-ba-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Experiment (feminine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `تَجَارِبُ` (tajāribu) - 'ta-jā-ri-bu'
    *   *Meaning:* Experiments (feminine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example:* `التَّجَارِبُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (At-tajāribu jadeedatun.) - The experiments are new.
    *   *Example (Genitive, indefinite):* `عَنْ تَجَارِبَ مُهِمَّةٍ.` (ʿAn tajāriba muhimmatin.) - About important experiments. (`تَجَارِبَ` takes Fatha because it's a diptote in Genitive).

### 4. `اكْتِشَافٌ` (iktishāfun) - Discovery (Masdar) / `يَكْتَشِفُ` (yaktashifu) - He discovers (Verb)

These words are used to talk about new findings. `اكْتِشَافٌ` is a Masdar, and `يَكْتَشِفُ` is its present tense verb (Form VIII, similar to `يَهْتَمُّ بِـ` from Day 122).

*   **New Word (Noun):** `اكْتِشَافٌ` (iktishāfun) - 'ik-ti-shā-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* Discovery (masculine Masdar/noun).
*   **New Word (Verb):** `يَكْتَشِفُ` (yaktashifu) - 'yak-ta-shi-fu' (masculine) / `تَكْتَشِفُ` (taktashifu) - 'tak-ta-shi-fu' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she discovers / is discovering.
    *   *Example (Noun):* `الِاكْتِشَافُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-iktishāfu muhimmun.) - The discovery is important.
    *   *Example (Verb):* `الْعَالِمُ يَكْتَشِفُ شَيْئًا جَدِيدًا.` (Al-ʿālimu yaktashifu shay'an jadeedan.) - The scholar discovers something new. (`شَيْئًا` - something, indefinite accusative).

### 5. `نَتِيجَةٌ` (nateejatun) / `نَتَائِجُ` (natā'iju) - Result / Results

This word is used to describe the outcome of research or experiments. `نَتَائِجُ` is a broken plural and a diptote.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `نَتِيجَةٌ` (nateejatun) - 'na-tee-ja-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Result (feminine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `نَتَائِجُ` (natā'iju) - 'na-tā-i-ju'
    *   *Meaning:* Results (feminine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example:* `النَّتَائِجُ وَاضِحَةٌ.` (An-natā'iju wāḍiḥatun.) - The results are clear.
    *   *Example (Genitive, indefinite):* `تَكَلَّمْنَا عَنْ نَتَائِجَ مُهِمَّةٍ.` (Takallamnā ʿan natā'ija muhimmatin.) - We spoke about important results. (`نَتَائِجَ` takes Fatha because it's a diptote in Genitive).

### 6. `تَطَوُّرٌ` (taṭawwur_un) - Development (Masdar) / `يَتَطَوَّرُ` (yataṭawwaru) - He develops (Verb)

These words are used for progress or advancement in a scientific field. `تَطَوُّرٌ` is a Masdar, and `يَتَطَوَّرُ` is its present tense verb (Form V, similar to `يَتَطَلَّبُ` from Day 113).

*   **New Word (Noun):** `تَطَوُّرٌ` (taṭawwur_un) - 'ta-ṭaw-wu-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Development (masculine Masdar/noun).
*   **New Word (Verb):** `يَتَطَوَّرُ` (yataṭawwaru) - 'ya-ta-ṭaw-wa-ru' (masculine) / `تَتَطَوَّرُ` (tataṭawwaru) - 'ta-ta-ṭaw-wa-ru' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she develops / is developing.
    *   *Example (Noun):* `هُنَاكَ تَطَوُّرٌ كَبِيرٌ فِي الْعِلْمِ.` (Hunāka taṭawwur_un kabeerun fī al-ʿilmi.) - There is a big development in science.
    *   *Example (Verb):* `الْعِلْمُ يَتَطَوَّرُ.` (Al-ʿilmu yataṭawwaru.) - Science is developing.

### 7. `خَبِيرٌ` (khabeerun) / `خُبَرَاءُ` (khubarā'u) - Expert / Experts

This noun is important for referring to specialists in scientific fields. `خُبَرَاءُ` is a broken plural and a diptote (for Nominative, Accusative, and Genitive without `الـ`).

*   **New Word (Singular):** `خَبِيرٌ` (khabeerun) - 'kha-bee-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Expert (masculine noun). (Feminine: `خَبِيرَةٌ` - `khabeeratun`).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `خُبَرَاءُ` (khubarā'u) - 'khu-ba-rā-u'
    *   *Meaning:* Experts (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example:* `الْخُبَرَاءُ يَتَكَلَّمُونَ عَنِ الْأَبْحَاثِ.` (Al-khubarā'u yatakallamoona ʿani al-abḥāthi.) - The experts are speaking about the research. (`يَتَكَلَّمُونَ` - plural verb for plural human subject, Day 118).
    *   *Example (Genitive, indefinite):* `تَكَلَّمْتُ مَعَ خُبَرَاءَ جَدُدٍ.` (Takallamtu maʿa khubarā'a jududin.) - I spoke with new experts. (`خُبَرَاءَ` takes Fatha because it's diptote in Genitive).

### 8. `يُثْبِتُ` (yuthbitu) - He proves / establishes (Verb)

This verb is vital for conveying that scientific findings confirm or demonstrate something. It's a Form IV verb.

*   **New Word (Verb):** `يُثْبِتُ` (yuthbitu) - 'yuth-bi-tu' (masculine) / `تُثْبِتُ` (tuthbitu) - 'tuth-bi-tu' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she proves, establishes.
    *   *Example:* `التَّجْرِبَةُ تُثْبِتُ النَّتِيجَةَ.` (At-tajribatu tuthbitu an-nateejata.) - The experiment proves the result.

### 9. `تُشِيرُ إِلَى` (tusheeru ilā) - She indicates / points to (Verb + Preposition)

This phrase is very common in scientific reporting to introduce what data or findings suggest. `يُشِيرُ إِلَى` is a Form IV verb.

*   **New Word (Verb + Prep):** `يُشِيرُ إِلَى` (yusheeru ilā) - 'yu-shee-ru i-lā' (masculine) / `تُشِيرُ إِلَى` (tusheeru ilā) - 'tu-shee-ru i-lā' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she indicates, points to.
    *   *Example:* `الْبَيَانَاتُ تُشِيرُ إِلَى زِيَادَةٍ.` (Al-bayānātu tusheeru ilā ziyādatin.) - The data indicates an increase. (`تُشِيرُ` is feminine singular for non-human plural `الْبَيَانَاتُ`).

These terms are crucial for discussing scientific topics, research, and results in Arabic.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new science reporting words and phrases into sentences, using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the new scientific research shows important discoveries."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ تُظْهِرُ اكْتِشَافَاتٍ مُهِمَّةً.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-abḥātha al-ʿilmiyyata al-jadeedata tuẓhiru iktishāfātin muhimmatan.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (new plural `الْأَبْحَاثَ`, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 123; `الْعِلْمِيَّةَ` new adjective; `الْجَدِيدَةَ` Day 54). `تُظْهِرُ` (Day 115, feminine singular verb for non-human plural subject). `اكْتِشَافَاتٍ مُهِمَّةً` (`اكْتِشَافَاتٌ` - discoveries, plural of `اكْتِشَافٌ`, new word, feminine sound plural Accusative object, Day 123; `مُهِمَّةً` Accusative adjective).

2.  **"Despite the fact that the experiments are difficult, the experts believe that the results are positive."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ التَّجَارِبَ صَعْبَةٌ، يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ أَنَّ النَّتَائِجَ إِيجَابِيَّةٌ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna at-tajāriba ṣa'batun, yaʿtaqidu al-khubarā'u anna an-natā'ija eejaabiyyatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Day 107). `التَّجَارِبَ صَعْبَةٌ` (new diptote plural `التَّجَارِبَ`, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 123). `يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ` (new plural `الْخُبَرَاءُ` for experts, Nominative subject of `يَعْتَقِدُ`, Day 123). `أَنَّ النَّتَائِجَ إِيجَابِيَّةٌ` (new diptote plural `النَّتَائِجَ` as Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 123; `إِيجَابِيَّةٌ` - positive, feminine adjective, Day 113 context, here used for non-human plural).

3.  **"The scientific report indicates a big development in the field of medicine. Moreover, the new data proves that the percentage is increasing."**
    *   `التَّقْرِيرُ الْعِلْمِيُّ يُشِيرُ إِلَى تَطَوُّرٍ كَبِيرٍ فِي مَجَالِ الطِّبِّ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُثْبِتُ الْبَيَانَاتُ الْجَدِيدَةُ أَنَّ النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ تَزْدَادُ.` (At-taqrīru al-ʿilmiyyu yusheeru ilā taṭawwur_in kabeer_in fī majāli aṭ-ṭibbi. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, tuthbitu al-bayānātū al-jadeedatū anna an-nisbata al-mi'awiyyata tazdādu.)
        *   *Notice:* `التَّقْرِيرُ الْعِلْمِيُّ` (`التَّقْرِيرُ` Day 115, `الْعِلْمِيُّ` new adjective, Day 123). `يُشِيرُ إِلَى` (new verb + preposition, Day 123). `تَطَوُّرٍ كَبِيرٍ` (new Masdar `تَطَوُّرٍ`, Genitive after `إِلَى`, Day 123). `مَجَالِ الطِّبِّ` (field of medicine, `مَجَالٌ` Day 116, `طِبٌّ` - medicine, new noun for this context, Masdar for Idafa). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `تُثْبِتُ الْبَيَانَاتُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` (new verb `تُثْبِتُ`, Day 123; `الْبَيَانَاتُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` Day 115). `أَنَّ النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ تَزْدَادُ` (`النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ` Day 115; `تَزْدَادُ` Day 115).

4.  **"In light of that, many scholars are interested in new discoveries. At the same time, the government wants to make scientific progress clear for everyone."**
    *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ الْعُلَمَاءِ بِالِاكْتِشَافَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةِ. وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ التَّقَدُّمَ الْعِلْمِيَّ وَاضِحًا لِلْجَمِيعِ.` (Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, yahtammu al-katheeru mina al-ʿulamā'i bi-l-iktishāfāt_i al-jadeedat_i. Wa-fī al-waqti nafsihi, tureedu al-ḥukūmatu an taj'ala at-taqadduma al-ʿilmiyya wāḍiḥan li-l-jamee'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (Day 108). `يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ الْعُلَمَاءِ بِـ` (`يَهْتَمُّ بِـ` Day 122; `الْعُلَمَاءِ` - scholars, plural of `عَالِمٌ` (Day 112), broken plural diptote, here in Genitive after `مِنَ`). `الِاكْتِشَافَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (`الِاكْتِشَافَاتِ` new plural for discovery, Genitive after `بِـ`, Day 123). `وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (Day 108). `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ` (Day 98, 101). `التَّقَدُّمَ الْعِلْمِيَّ وَاضِحًا` (`التَّقَدُّمَ` - progress, Day 113, Accusative first object; `الْعِلْمِيَّ` new adjective, Accusative; `وَاضِحًا` Accusative second object, Day 98).

These examples show how you can combine new scientific vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences.

## 🗣️ Key Sentences
*   الْبَحْثُ عِلْمِيٌّ. (The research is scientific.)
*   الْأَبْحَاثُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ مُهِمَّةٌ. (The scientific research projects are important.)
*   فِي أَبْحَاثَ جَدِيدَةٍ. (In new research projects.)
*   التَّجَارِبُ جَدِيدَةٌ. (The experiments are new.)
*   عَنْ تَجَارِبَ مُهِمَّةٍ. (About important experiments.)
*   الِاكْتِشَافُ مُهِمٌّ. (The discovery is important.)
*   الْعَالِمُ يَكْتَشِفُ شَيْئًا جَدِيدًا. (The scholar discovers something new.)
*   النَّتَائِجُ وَاضِحَةٌ. (The results are clear.)
*   تَكَلَّمْنَا عَنْ نَتَائِجَ مُهِمَّةٍ. (We spoke about important results.)
*   هُنَاكَ تَطَوُّرٌ كَبِيرٌ فِي الْعِلْمِ. (There is a big development in science.)
*   الْعِلْمُ يَتَطَوَّرُ. (Science is developing.)
*   الْخُبَرَاءُ يَتَكَلَّمُونَ عَنِ الْأَبْحَاثِ. (The experts are speaking about the research.)
*   تَكَلَّمْتُ مَعَ خُبَرَاءَ جَدُدٍ. (I spoke with new experts.)
*   التَّجْرِبَةُ تُثْبِتُ النَّتِيجَةَ. (The experiment proves the result.)
*   الْبَيَانَاتُ تُشِيرُ إِلَى زِيَادَةٍ. (The data indicates an increase.)
*   مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ تُظْهِرُ اكْتِشَافَاتٍ مُهِمَّةً. (It is certain that the new scientific research shows important discoveries.)
*   بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ التَّجَارِبَ صَعْبَةٌ، يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ أَنَّ النَّتَائِجَ إِيجَابِيَّةٌ. (Despite the fact that the experiments are difficult, the experts believe that the results are positive.)
*   التَّقْرِيرُ الْعِلْمِيُّ يُشِيرُ إِلَى تَطَوُّرٍ كَبِيرٍ فِي مَجَالِ الطِّبِّ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُثْبِتُ الْبَيَانَاتُ الْجَدِيدَةُ أَنَّ النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ تَزْدَادُ. (The scientific report indicates a big development in the field of medicine. Moreover, the new data proves that the percentage is increasing.)
*   فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ الْعُلَمَاءِ بِالِاكْتِشَافَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةِ. وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ التَّقَدُّمَ الْعِلْمِيَّ وَاضِحًا لِلْجَمِيعِ. (In light of that, many scholars are interested in new discoveries. At the same time, the government wants to make scientific progress clear for everyone.)
