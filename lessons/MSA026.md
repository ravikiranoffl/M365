# Day 26 - Complex Idafa (Multiple nouns)

## Lesson

1.  **Review: The Simple Idafa Construct**
    *   Remember from yesterday: an Idafa links two nouns to show possession or "of" relationship.
    *   It has `مُضَافٌ` (Noun 1, the possessed item) first, then `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ` (Noun 2, the possessor).
    *   **Rules for Simple Idafa:**
        *   `مُضَافٌ` (Noun 1): No `الْـ` at the beginning, no `التَّنْوِينُ` at the end. Its ending vowel depends on its role in the sentence (we mostly used Damma for now).
        *   `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ` (Noun 2): Always definite (`الْـ` or attached pronoun or proper noun) and always ends with **Kasra ( ِ )** or **Tanween Kasra ( ٍ )**.

2.  **Building a Complex Idafa (Chaining More Than Two Nouns)**
    *   Now, what if the possessor *itself* is possessed by *another* noun? This creates a chain!
    *   Example: "the book of the student of the university"
        *   The book belongs to the student.
        *   The student belongs to the university.
    *   The chain will be: **Book <-> Student <-> University**
        *   `كِتَابُ` (Book) is the `مُضَافٌ` of `طَالِبِ` (Student).
        *   `طَالِبِ` (Student) is the `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ` of `كِتَابُ`, *but it is also* the `مُضَافٌ` of `الْجَامِعَةِ` (University).
        *   `الْجَامِعَةِ` (University) is the `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ` of `طَالِبِ`.

3.  **The Rules for Complex Idafa (Extending the Rules!)**

    The rules you learned for simple Idafa apply to *every single link* in the chain:

    *   **The First Noun in the entire chain (the `مُضَافٌ`):**
        *   It has no `الْـ` and no `التَّنْوِينُ`.
        *   Its ending vowel (Damma, Fatha, or Kasra) depends on its role in the *overall sentence*. For our examples, it will often be Damma.

    *   **Any Noun in the MIDDLE of the chain (it's both a `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ` to the noun before it, AND a `مُضَافٌ` to the noun after it):**
        *   It has no `الْـ` and no `التَّنْوِينُ`.
        *   It **ALWAYS** ends with a **Kasra ( ِ )**. (This is because it's always a `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ` to the noun before it.)

    *   **The LAST Noun in the entire chain (the final `مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ`):**
        *   It is always **definite** (with `الْـ`, an attached pronoun, or a proper noun).
        *   It **ALWAYS** ends with a **Kasra ( ِ )** or **Tanween Kasra ( ٍ )**.

    **In simple terms:**
    *   Only the *first* word in the chain can have a Damma/Fatha (depending on its sentence role).
    *   Only the *last* word in the chain can have `الْـ` or Tanween (specifically Tanween Kasra).
    *   **ALL nouns from the second word to the second-to-last word will have a single Kasra ( ِ ) and no `الْـ`!**

4.  **Adjectives in Complex Idafa**
    *   An adjective always follows the noun it describes.
    *   If an adjective describes one of the nouns *within* the Idafa, it will follow *that specific noun's rules* for definiteness, gender, and case.
    *   Example: "the door of the new school's room" (the room of the new school)
        *   `بَابُ غُرْفَةِ الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.`
        *   `الْجَدِيدَةِ` (the new) describes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (the school). It matches `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` in being definite (`الْـ`), feminine (`ـَة`), and having Kasra (`ـِ`).

This might seem like a lot, but the patterns are very consistent once you understand them. Let's look at some examples!

## Examples

Let's build some complex Idafa constructs using the rules above. We'll introduce one new word: `مُدِيرٌ` (mudeerun) meaning "director" or "principal".

1.  **The book of the student of the university.**
    *   `كِتَابُ طَالِبِ الْجَامِعَةِ.` (kitaabu taalibi al-jaami'ati.)
        *   `كِتَابُ`: First noun. No `الْـ`, no Tanween, Damma.
        *   `طَالِبِ`: Middle noun. No `الْـ`, no Tanween, Kasra.
        *   `الْجَامِعَةِ`: Last noun. Has `الْـ`, Kasra.
    *   (Literally: book student the-university)

2.  **The door of the teacher's room.** (Referring to a masculine teacher)
    *   `بَابُ غُرْفَةِ الْمُدَرِّسِ.` (baabu ghurfati al-mudarrisi.)
        *   `بَابُ`: First noun. Damma.
        *   `غُرْفَةِ`: Middle noun. Kasra.
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسِ`: Last noun. Has `الْـ`, Kasra.

3.  **The desk of the director of the school.**
    *   `مَكْتَبُ مُدِيرِ الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (maktabu mudeeri al-madrasati.)
        *   `مَكْتَبُ`: First noun. Damma.
        *   `مُدِيرِ`: Middle noun. No `الْـ`, no Tanween, Kasra.
        *   `الْمَدْرَسَةِ`: Last noun. Has `الْـ`, Kasra.

4.  **The car of my university teacher.** (Meaning "the car of the teacher of my university")
    *   `سَيَّارَةُ مُدَرِّسِ جَامِعَتِي.` (sayyaaraatu mudarrisi jaami'atī.)
        *   `سَيَّارَةُ`: First noun. Damma.
        *   `مُدَرِّسِ`: Middle noun. No `الْـ`, no Tanween, Kasra.
        *   `جَامِعَتِي`: Last noun. Has attached pronoun `ـِي`, so it's definite, and the `تِ` has Kasra before the `ـي`. (Remember `جَامِعَةٌ` + `ـِي` = `جَامِعَتِي`).

5.  **The new book of the student of the big school.**
    *   `كِتَابُ الطَّالِبِ الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ الْجَدِيدِ.` This is getting long! Let's simplify.
    *   **The new book of the student of the big school.** (Let's make it the new *book* itself)
        *   `الْكِتَابُ الْجَدِيدُ لِطَالِبِ الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (The new book *for* the student of the big school). This is simpler, but uses a preposition.
        *   Let's stick to strict Idafa: **The book of the student of the big school.** (The adjective `الْكَبِيرَةِ` will describe `الْمَدْرَسَةِ`).
        *   `كِتَابُ طَالِبِ الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (kitaabu taalibi al-madrasati al-kabeerati.)
            *   `كِتَابُ`: First noun. Damma.
            *   `طَالِبِ`: Middle noun. Kasra.
            *   `الْمَدْرَسَةِ`: Noun before adjective. Has `الْـ`, Kasra.
            *   `الْكَبِيرَةِ`: Adjective describing `الْمَدْرَسَةِ`. Matches `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` in definite, feminine, Kasra.

This is truly advanced grammar, and you're doing great by just recognizing the patterns!

## Practice

Now, let's practice forming these complex Idafa constructs. I will give you the English phrase, and you will combine the nouns (and maybe an adjective) into a correct Arabic Idafa.

1.  "The pen of the director of the university." (`قَلَمٌ` + `مُدِيرٌ` + `الْجَامِعَةُ`)
2.  "The door of the small room." (`بَابٌ` + `غُرْفَةٌ` + `صَغِيرَةٌ`)
3.  "The teacher's new car." (meaning "the car of the new teacher") (`سَيَّارَةٌ` + `مُدَرِّسٌ` + `جَدِيدٌ`)
4.  "The book of my school's student." (meaning "the book of the student of my school") (`كِتَابٌ` + `طَالِبٌ` + `مَدْرَسَةٌ` + `ـِي`)
5.  "The room of her big house." (Let's introduce `بَيْتٌ` (baytun) - house (masculine). So "her house" would be `بَيْتُهَا`. "Big" is `كَبِيرٌ`).
    *   (`غُرْفَةٌ` + `بَيْتٌ` + `ـهَا` + `كَبِيرٌ`) - The adjective `الْكَبِيرِ` will describe `بَيْتِهَا`.
