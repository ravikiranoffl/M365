# Day 102 - Legal vocabulary

## Lesson

Just like in government, the world of law has its own set of specialized terms. Knowing these words will allow you to read news articles, follow legal reports, and understand discussions related to justice and crime.

Let's learn some new and essential legal vocabulary!

### 1. `مَحْكَمَةٌ` (maḥkamatun) - Court

This is the place where legal cases are heard and justice is administered.

*   **New Word:** `مَحْكَمَةٌ` (maḥkamatun) - 'maḥ-ka-ma-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Court (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمَحْكَمَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-maḥkamatu jadeedatun.) - The court is new.

### 2. `الْقَاضِي` (al-qāḍī) - The Judge

This is the person who presides over court proceedings, hears cases, and makes decisions. In Arabic, this word is a bit special. When it's definite (with `الـ`), it ends with a long `ـِي` sound. When it's indefinite and in Nominative or Genitive case, it drops the `ي` and takes a Kasra Tanween (like `قَاضٍ`), but we'll focus on the definite form for now, which is easier!

*   **New Word:** `الْقَاضِي` (al-qāḍī) - 'al-qaa-ḍee'
    *   *Meaning:* The judge (masculine, definite noun).
    *   *Feminine (definite):* `الْقَاضِيَةُ` (al-qāḍiyatū) - 'al-qaa-ḍee-ya-tu' - The judge (f.).
    *   *How it works in cases:*
        *   Nominative: `الْقَاضِي` (al-qāḍī) - The judge (subject, Damma is implied but not written on the `ي`).
        *   Accusative: `الْقَاضِيَ` (al-qāḍiya) - The judge (object, Fatha is written on the `ي`).
        *   Genitive: `الْقَاضِي` (al-qāḍī) - The judge (after preposition, Kasra is implied but not written on the `ي`).
    *   *Example:* `الْقَاضِي عَادِلٌ.` (Al-qāḍī 'ādilun.) - The judge is just. (`عَادِلٌ` - just/fair, masculine adjective, new for this sentence).

### 3. `الْمُحَامِي` (al-muḥāmī) - The Lawyer

This is the person who represents clients in court and provides legal advice. This word also follows the same special pattern as `الْقَاضِي`.

*   **New Word:** `الْمُحَامِي` (al-muḥāmī) - 'al-mu-ḥaa-mee'
    *   *Meaning:* The lawyer (masculine, definite noun).
    *   *Feminine (definite):* `الْمُحَامِيَةُ` (al-muḥāmiyatū) - 'al-mu-ḥaa-mee-ya-tu' - The lawyer (f.).
    *   *How it works in cases (same as `الْقَاضِي`):*
        *   Nominative: `الْمُحَامِي` (al-muḥāmī)
        *   Accusative: `الْمُحَامِيَ` (al-muḥāmiya)
        *   Genitive: `الْمُحَامِي` (al-muḥāmī)
    *   *Example:* `الْمُحَامِي جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-muḥāmī jadeedun.) - The lawyer is new.

### 4. `جَرِيمَةٌ` (jareematun) - Crime

This refers to an action that is against the law.

*   **New Word:** `جَرِيمَةٌ` (jareematun) - 'ja-ree-ma-tun'

    *   *Example:* `أَعْلَنَتِ الشُّرْطَةُ عَنِ الْجَرِيمَةِ.` (A'lanati ash-shurṭatu 'ani al-jareemati.) - The police announced the crime. ### 5. `عَدَالَةٌ` (ʿadālatun) - Justice

This is the concept of fairness and righteousness, especially in the application of law.

*   **New Word:** `عَدَالَةٌ` (ʿadālatun) - 'a-dā-la-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Justice (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْقَاضِي يُرِيدُ الْعَدَالَةَ.` (Al-qāḍī yureedu al-'adālata.) - The judge wants justice.

### 6. `حَقٌّ` (ḥaqqun) - Right (as in a legal entitlement)

This refers to something that a person is legally or morally entitled to have or do.

*   **New Word:** `حَقٌّ` (ḥaqqun) - 'ḥaq-qun'
    *   *Meaning:* Right (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `لِكُلِّ شَخْصٍ حَقٌّ.` (Li-kulli shakhṣin ḥaqqun.) - Every person has a right. (`لِكُلِّ شَخْصٍ` - to every person, new for this sentence). I need to rephrase this without new vocab.
    *   *Revised Example:* `هَذَا حَقٌّ مُهِمٌّ.` (Hādhā ḥaqqun muhimun.) - This is an important right.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new legal words into sentences using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the court will announce the new law."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْمَحْكَمَةَ سَتُعْلِنُ الْقَانُونَ الْجَدِيدَ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-maḥkamata satu'linu al-qānoona al-jadeeda.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْمَحْكَمَةَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `سَتُعْلِنُ` (will announce - Day 64). `الْقَانُونَ الْجَدِيدَ` (new law - Accusative, Day 54).

2.  **"The judge (m.) wants to make justice clear for everyone."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ الْقَاضِي أَنْ يَجْعَلَ الْعَدَالَةَ وَاضِحَةً لِلْجَمِيعِ.` (Yureedu al-qāḍī an yaj'ala al-'adālata wāḍiḥatan li-l-jamee'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ الْقَاضِي` (the judge wants). `أَنْ يَجْعَلَ` (to make - Day 98, Subjunctive). `الْعَدَالَةَ وَاضِحَةً` (justice clear - both Accusative). `لِلْجَمِيعِ` (for everyone).

3.  **"Perhaps the lawyer (f.) will speak about the big crime."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا تَتَكَلَّمُ الْمُحَامِيَةُ عَنِ الْجَرِيمَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (Rubbamā tatakallamu al-muḥāmiyatū 'ani al-jareemati al-kabeerati.)

4.  **"It is known that the state protects human rights."** (I need `human` and `protects`. `protects` is `يَحْفَظُ` (Day 86) and `إِنْسَانٌ` - human being. I will teach `حُقُوقُ الْإِنْسَانِ` as a set phrase).
    *   **Revised 4:** **"It is known that the state wants to protect human rights."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الدَّوْلَةَ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَحْفَظَ حُقُوقَ الْإِنْسَانِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna ad-dawlata tureedu an taḥfaẓa ḥuqūqa al-insāni.)

        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الدَّوْلَةَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَحْفَظَ` (wants to protect - Day 44, Day 60, Day 86). `حُقُوقَ الْإِنْسَانِ` (human rights - Accusative direct object).

5.  **"Although the minister (m.) is a politician, he believes that the court's decision is just."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ سِيَاسِيٌّ، يَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ قَرَارَ الْمَحْكَمَةِ عَادِلٌ.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-wazeera siyāsiyyun, ya'taqidu anna qarāra al-maḥkamati 'ādilun.)
