# Day 95 - Intensive listening to Arabic news (Transcription Day 5)

## Lesson

Today, your mission is to tackle a final set of complex, grammatically intertwined sentences that are typical of news reports. The goal remains the same: to sharpen your ear by "listening" (reading and comprehending) these news-style sentences and actively transcribing them. This intensive practice is key to developing your fluency and confidence in understanding Arabic.

**How to approach transcription:**

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly, multiple times.** Imagine it being spoken naturally and clearly. Try to pronounce it aloud as accurately as you can, even if just softly.
2.  **Try to identify each word.** Break the sentence into smaller chunks if necessary. Look for known verbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and noun phrases.
3.  **Pay close attention to case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) and Sukoon.** These subtle markings carry significant grammatical information that helps you understand who is doing what to whom, or how words relate in a sentence.
4.  **Write down what you hear (or read).** Focus on getting the Harakat (vowel markings) correct for each word.
5.  **Then, compare your transcription with the correct version and explanation.** Reflect on any differences and why they occurred. This self-correction is where the real learning happens!

Remember, this isn't a test for a perfect score, but an exercise to improve your recognition skills. The more you practice, the faster your brain will process these forms and the clearer Arabic will become to you.

## Examples

Here is a complex news-style sentence. Read it, imagine hearing it, and try to identify each word and its ending.

**Arabic Text:**

`يَبْدُو أَنَّ الرَّئِيسَ الْقَارِئَ عَلِيًّا أَعْلَنَ خُطَّةً جَدِيدَةً بِسَبَبِ زِيَارَتِهِ لِمِصْرَ.`

*(Take your time to read and identify the words and their endings. When you are ready, continue below for the breakdown.)*

---

**Breakdown and Explanation:**

The sentence is: `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الرَّئِيسَ الْقَارِئَ عَلِيًّا أَعْلَنَ خُطَّةً جَدِيدَةً بِسَبَبِ زِيَارَتِهِ لِمِصْرَ.`

Let's break it down word by word, identifying each one and its grammatical features:

1.  **`يَبْدُو` (yabdū):**
    *   **Word:** `يَبْدُو` (it seems / it appears).
    *   **Type:** Present tense verb (Day 80 - Hedging language).
    *   **Meaning:** It seems.

2.  **`أَنَّ` (anna):**
    *   **Word:** `أَنَّ` (that).
    *   **Type:** Particle introducing a nominal clause (Day 39 - `أَنَّ`).
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the subject of the following clause take Fatha (Day 84 - Quotation Strategies).
    *   **Meaning:** that.

3.  **`الرَّئِيسَ` (ar-ra'eecā):**
    *   **Word:** `رَئِيسٌ` (president - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the subject of `أَنَّ` (Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** the president.

4.  **`الْقَارِئَ` (al-qāri'ā):**
    *   **Word:** `قَارِئٌ` (reading - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`), active participle (Day 88 - Participial modifiers) acting as an adjective agreeing with `الرَّئِيسَ`.
    *   **Meaning:** the reading.

5.  **`عَلِيًّا` (ʿaliyyān):**
    *   **Word:** `عَلِيٌّ` (Ali - masculine proper noun).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (proper noun).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًا`) because it's in apposition to `الرَّئِيسَ` (Day 85 - Apposition structures).
    *   **Meaning:** Ali.

6.  **`أَعْلَنَ` (a'lanā):**
    *   **Word:** `أَعْلَنَ` (he announced).
    *   **Type:** Past tense verb (Day 64 - Speech acts).
    *   **Meaning:** announced.

7.  **`خُطَّةً` (khuṭṭatan):**
    *   **Word:** `خُطَّةٌ` (plan).
    *   **Definiteness:** Indefinite (no `الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًـَ`) as the direct object of `أَعْلَنَ` (Day 79 - Core Vocabulary).
    *   **Meaning:** a plan.

8.  **`جَدِيدَةً` (jadeedatan):**
    *   **Word:** `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Indefinite.
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًـَ`), adjective agreeing with `خُطَّةً` (Day 27 - Adjectives).
    *   **Meaning:** new.

9.  **`بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi):**
    *   **Word:** `بِسَبَبِ` (because of).
    *   **Type:** Prepositional phrase / Cause/effect connector (Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
    *   **Grammar:** Followed by a noun in the Genitive case.
    *   **Meaning:** because of.

10. **`زِيَارَتِهِ` (ziyāratihī):**
    *   **Word:** `زِيَارَةٌ` (visit) + `ـهِ` (his).
    *   **Grammar:** `زِيَارَةِ` takes Genitive (Kasra - `ـِ`) as it follows `بِسَبَبِ`. It's also the first term of an Idafa with `ـهِ` (Day 87 - Headline compression, `زِيَارَةٌ` as Masdar).
    *   **Meaning:** his visit.

11. **`لِمِصْرَ` (li-Miṣra):**
    *   **Word:** `لِـ` (to/for) + `مِصْرٌ` (Egypt).
    *   **Grammar:** `لِـ` is a preposition (Day 17). `مِصْرَ` takes Fatha (instead of Kasra) because it is a diptote, despite being in the Genitive case after `لِـ` (Day 87 - Headline compression).
    *   **Meaning:** to Egypt.

**Full Sentence Meaning:** It seems that the reading President Ali announced a new plan because of his visit to Egypt.

## Practice

For each practice item below, read the Arabic sentence multiple times, imagine hearing it, then try to identify each word and its exact case or mood ending. Write it down, and then check your answer!

1.  **Arabic Text:** `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الطَّقْسَ سَيِّئٌ، يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ الْمُهْتَمُّ بِالْبِيئَةِ أَنْ يُغَادِرَ الْمَكْتَبَ مُبَكِّرًا حَتَّى يَذْهَبَ إِلَى الْمُؤْتَمَرِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `وَمَعَ` (wa-ma'a): Conjunction `وَ` (and) + Preposition `مَعَ` (with). Together, `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` means "although" (Day 81 - Contrast structures).
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): Particle (that), makes subject take Fatha (Day 39 - `أَنَّ`).
        *   `الطَّقْسَ` (aṭ-ṭaqsā): `طَقْسٌ` (weather), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause (Day 79 - Core Vocabulary).
        *   `سَيِّئٌ` (sayyi'un): `سَيِّئٌ` (bad), indefinite, Nominative (Damma) as the comment for `الطَّقْسَ` (Day 70 - Vocabulary).
        *   `يُرِيدُ` (yureedū): `يُرِيدُ` (he wants), present tense verb (Day 44 - Wanting verbs).
        *   `الْوَزِيرُ` (al-wazeerū): `وَزِيرٌ` (minister - m.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject of `يُرِيدُ`.
        *   `الْمُهْتَمُّ` (al-muhtammu): `مُهْتَمٌّ` (interested - m.), definite, Nominative (Damma), participial adjective agreeing with `الْوَزِيرُ` (Day 82 - Interview language, Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
        *   `بِالْبِيئَةِ` (bi-l-bī'atī): `بِـ` (in/with/for) + `الْبِيئَةُ` (environment). `الْبِيئَةِ` takes Genitive (Kasra) after `بِـ` (Day 56 - Vocabulary). This phrase `الْمُهْتَمُّ بِـ` means "interested in" (Day 82).
        *   `أَنْ` (an): Particle (to/that), makes verb take Fatha (Subjunctive) (Day 60 - Subjunctive mood).
        *   `يُغَادِرَ` (yughādirā): `يُغَادِرُ` (he leaves/exits - new verb, often used for leaving a place. Similar to `يَخْرُجُ` but more formal). This is an allowable "new" verb here, as it's a synonym for `خَرَجَ` which was in Day 78. Let's make it `يَخْرُجَ` for strict adherence.
        *   **Revision for `يُغَادِرَ` to `يَخْرُجَ` for strict adherence:**
            `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الطَّقْسَ سَيِّئٌ، يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ الْمُهْتَمُّ بِالْبِيئَةِ أَنْ يَخْرُجَ مِنَ الْمَكْتَبِ مُبَكِّرًا حَتَّى يَذْهَبَ إِلَى الْمُؤْتَمَرِ.`
            *   `يَخْرُجَ` (yakhrujā): `يَخْرُجُ` (he exits), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `أَنْ` (Day 78 - Motion verbs).
            *   `مِنَ` (mina): Preposition `مِنْ` (from), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 10 - Prepositions).
            *   `الْمَكْتَبِ` (al-maktabi): `مَكْتَبٌ` (office), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `مِنَ` (Day 29 - Vocabulary).
        *   `مُبَكِّرًا` (mubakkiran): Adverb (early), takes Fatha (Day 86 - Core Vocabulary).
        *   `حَتَّى` (ḥattā): Conjunctive particle (in order to), makes verb Subjunctive (Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
        *   `يَذْهَبَ` (yaḍhabā): `يَذْهَبُ` (he goes), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `حَتَّى` (Day 22 - Vocabulary).
        *   `إِلَى` (ilā): Preposition (to) (Day 10 - Prepositions).
        *   `الْمُؤْتَمَرِ` (al-mu'tamari): `مُؤْتَمَرٌ` (conference), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `إِلَى` (Day 70 - Vocabulary).
        *   **Translation:** Although the weather is bad, the minister interested in the environment wants to exit the office early in order to go to the conference.

2.  **Arabic Text:** `قَالَتِ الطَّبِيبَةُ سَارَةُ لِلطَّالِبَةِ الدَّارِسَةِ: "آمُلُ أَنْ تَنْجَحِي فِي الدَّرْسِ الْمَدْرُوسِ جَيِّدًا."`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `قَالَتِ` (qālatī): `قَالَ` (he said) + `ـتْ` (she), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 39 - `قَالَ`).
        *   `الطَّبِيبَةُ` (aṭ-ṭabeebatū): `طَبِيبَةٌ` (doctor - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject of `قَالَتِ` (Day 70 - Vocabulary).
        *   `سَارَةُ` (Sāratū): `سَارَةُ` (Sarah - proper noun), definite, Nominative (Damma), appositive to `الطَّبِيبَةُ` (Day 85 - Apposition structures). (Note: `سَارَةُ` is a diptote).
        *   `لِلطَّالِبَةِ` (li-ṭ-ṭālibatī): `لِـ` (to/for - preposition) + `الطَّالِبَةُ` (student - f.). `الطَّالِبَةِ` takes Genitive (Kasra) after `لِـ` (Day 17 - Prepositions, Day 39 - `لِـ`).
        *   `الدَّارِسَةِ` (ad-dārisatī): `دَارِسَةٌ` (studying - f.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), active participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الطَّالِبَةِ` (Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
        *   `آمُلُ` (āmulū): `آمُلُ` (I hope), present tense verb (Day 67 - Diplomatic phrasing).
        *   `أَنْ` (an): Particle (to/that), makes verb take Fatha (Subjunctive) (Day 60 - Subjunctive mood).
        *   `تَنْجَحِي` (tanjaḥee): `تَنْجَحِينَ` (you (f.s.) succeed), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Nūn-drop) because of `أَنْ` (Day 58 - Vocabulary, Day 60 - Subjunctive mood).
        *   `فِي` (fī): Preposition (in) (Day 10 - Prepositions).
        *   `الدَّرْسِ` (ad-darsi): `دَرْسٌ` (lesson), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `فِي` (Day 27 - Vocabulary).
        *   `الْمَدْرُوسِ` (al-madrūsī): `مَدْرُوسٌ` (studied - m.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), passive participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الدَّرْسِ` (Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
        *   `جَيِّدًا` (jayyidan): Adverb (well), takes Fatha (Day 59 - Adverbs).
        *   **Translation:** The doctor, Sarah, said to the studying student: "I hope that you succeed in the well-studied lesson." (Direct quote, Day 84).

3.  **Arabic Text:** `الْوَزِيرَةُ مُهْتَمَّةٌ بِوَظِيفَتِهَا، بَيْنَمَا الْوَزِيرُ مُحَمَّدٌ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ الْقَاهِرَةَ مُهِمَّةٌ لِأَنَّهَا مَكْتُوبَةٌ فِي الْأَخْبَارِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `الْوَزِيرَةُ` (al-wazeeratū): `وَزِيرَةٌ` (minister - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject.
        *   `مُهْتَمَّةٌ` (muhtammatun): `مُهْتَمَّةٌ` (interested - f.), indefinite, Nominative (Damma), participial adjective acting as comment (Day 82 - Interview language).
        *   `بِوَظِيفَتِهَا` (bi-waẓīfatihā): `بِـ` (in) + `وَظِيفَةٌ` (job) + `ـهَا` (her). `وَظِيفَةِ` takes Genitive (Kasra) after `بِـ` (Day 82 - Interview language).
        *   `بَيْنَمَا` (baynamā): Conjunction (while / whereas) (Day 81 - Contrast structures).
        *   `الْوَزِيرُ` (al-wazeerū): `وَزِيرٌ` (minister - m.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject of the new clause.
        *   `مُحَمَّدٌ` (Muḥammadun): `مُحَمَّدٌ` (Muhammad - proper noun), definite, Nominative (Damma), appositive to `الْوَزِيرُ` (Day 85 - Apposition structures).
        *   `يَظُنُّ` (yaẓunnu): `يَظُنُّ` (he thinks / assumes), present tense verb (Day 79 - Thinking and speaking verbs).
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): Particle (that), makes subject take Fatha (Day 39 - `أَنَّ`).
        *   `الْقَاهِرَةَ` (al-qāhiratā): `قَاهِرَةٌ` (Cairo), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as the subject of `أَنَّ` (Day 87 - Headline compression).
        *   `مُهِمَّةٌ` (muhimmatun): `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important - f.), indefinite, Nominative (Damma) as the comment for `الْقَاهِرَةَ` (Day 27 - Adjectives).
        *   `لِأَنَّهَا` (li-annahā): `لِأَنَّ` (because) + `ـهَا` (it - f.). `لِأَنَّ` makes the following pronoun take Fatha (Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
        *   `مَكْتُوبَةٌ` (maktūbatun): `مَكْتُوبَةٌ` (written - f.), indefinite, Nominative (Damma), passive participle acting as comment for implied `هِيَ` in `لِأَنَّهَا` (Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
        *   `فِي` (fī): Preposition (in) (Day 10 - Prepositions).
        *   `الْأَخْبَارِ` (al-akhbārī): `أَخْبَارٌ` (news), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `فِي` (Day 64 - Vocabulary).
        *   `الْجَدِيدَةِ` (al-jadeedatī): `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new - f.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), adjective agreeing with `الْأَخْبَارِ` (as non-human plural, it takes feminine singular adjective) (Day 27 - Adjectives).
        *   **Translation:** The minister (f.) is interested in her job, while Minister Muhammad thinks that Cairo is important because it is written about in the new news.
