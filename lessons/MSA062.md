# Day 62 - Embedded clauses

## Lesson

Imagine you have a big sentence, like "The student reads." Now, imagine you want to add more detail to it without starting a whole new sentence. That's where embedded clauses come in! They let you "embed" (or put inside) another idea.

In Arabic, we've already learned several ways to do this, and today we'll group them under the idea of "embedded clauses" and see how they function within a larger sentence.

### 1. Relative Clauses: Adjectival Embedded Clauses (Day 31 review)

Remember `الَّذِي` (alladhī) and `الَّتِي` (allatī) for "who" or "which"? These introduce a clause that *describes* a noun, just like an adjective.

*   **Function:** They act like big adjectives, telling us more about a specific noun.
*   **Example:** `الْكِتَابُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُهُ الطَّالِبُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-kitābu alladhī yaqra'uhu aṭ-ṭālibu jadeedun.)
    *   **Main sentence:** `الْكِتَابُ جَدِيدٌ.` (The book is new.)
    *   **Embedded clause:** `الَّذِي يَقْرَأُهُ الطَّالِبُ` (which the student reads). This whole part describes `الْكِتَابُ`.

### 2. Nominal Clauses: Noun-like Embedded Clauses (Day 39 & 44 review)

These clauses act like nouns themselves, often being the "thing" that is said, known, or wanted.

*   **`أَنَّ` (anna) - "that" (from Reported Speech, Day 39)**
    *   **Function:** Introduces a clause that acts as the object of verbs like "he said" or "he knew." The subject of the `أَنَّ` clause takes Fatha.
    *   **Example:** `قَالَ الْمُدَرِّسُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Qāla al-mudarrisu anna aṭ-ṭāliba dhakiyyun.)
        *   **Main sentence:** `قَالَ الْمُدَرِّسُ` (The teacher said).
        *   **Embedded clause (the thing he said):** `أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ ذَكِيٌّ.` (that the student is smart.) This whole clause is the object of "said." Notice `الطَّالِبَ` has a Fatha.

*   **`أَنْ` (an) - "to" / "that" (from Nominalization & Modal Expressions, Day 38 & 44)**
    *   **Function:** Introduces a clause that usually acts as the object of verbs like "he wants" or "it is probable." The verb in the `أَنْ` clause becomes Subjunctive (ends in Fatha).
    *   **Example:** `تُرِيدُ هِيَ أَنْ تَكْتُبَ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Tureedu hiya an taktaba ar-risālata.)
        *   **Main sentence:** `تُرِيدُ هِيَ.` (She wants.)
        *   **Embedded clause (the thing she wants):** `أَنْ تَكْتُبَ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (to write the message.) This whole clause is the object of "wants." Notice `تَكْتُبَ` has a Fatha.

### 3. Adverbial Clauses: Adverb-like Embedded Clauses (Day 32 & 40 review)

These clauses tell us *when*, *why*, or *under what condition* something happens, just like adverbs do.

*   **`لِـ` (li-) - "in order to" (from Purpose Clauses, Day 40)**
    *   **Function:** Explains the *purpose* of the action in the main sentence. The verb in the `لِـ` clause becomes Subjunctive (ends in Fatha).
    *   **Example:** `يَذْهَبُ الطَّالِبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ لِيَدْرُسَ.` (Yadhhabu aṭ-ṭālibu ilā al-madrasati liyadrusa.)
        *   **Main sentence:** `يَذْهَبُ الطَّالِبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (The student goes to school.)
        *   **Embedded clause (why he goes):** `لِيَدْرُسَ.` (in order to study.) This explains the purpose. Notice `يَدْرُسَ` has a Fatha.

*   **`إِذَا` (idhā) - "if" (from Conditional Sentences, Day 32)**
    *   **Function:** States a condition for the main action to happen.
    *   **Example:** `إِذَا دَرَسْتَ، فَأَنْتَ نَاجِحٌ.` (Idhā darasta, fa'anta nājiḥun.)
        *   **Main sentence:** `فَأَنْتَ نَاجِحٌ.` (then you are successful.)
        *   **Embedded clause (the condition):** `إِذَا دَرَسْتَ،` (If you studied, / if you study) This sets the condition.

### Key Takeaway for Embedded Clauses:

The important thing is to recognize that when you see words like `الَّذِي`, `الَّتِي`, `أَنَّ`, `أَنْ`, `لِـ` (for purpose), or `إِذَا`, they are signaling that a new, smaller idea or sentence is coming, and it's connected to the main sentence in a specific way! These small ideas are "embedded" inside the larger thought.

## Examples

Let's see more examples of these embedded clauses:

1.  **"The teacher who writes the book is smart."** (Relative Clause)
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُ الَّذِي يَكْتُبُ الْكِتَابَ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Al-mudarrisu alladhī yaktubu al-kitāba dhakiyyun.)
        *   `الَّذِي يَكْتُبُ الْكِتَابَ` is the relative clause describing `الْمُدَرِّسُ`.

2.  **"He wants that the student understands the lesson."** (Nominal Clause with `أَنْ`)
    *   `هُوَ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَفْهَمَ الطَّالِبُ الدَّرْسَ.` (Huwa yureedu an yafhama aṭ-ṭālibu ad-darsa.)
        *   `أَنْ يَفْهَمَ الطَّالِبُ الدَّرْسَ` is the nominal clause acting as the object of `يُرِيدُ`. Notice `يَفْهَمَ` (he understands) has a Fatha.

3.  **"She said that the air is clean."** (Nominal Clause with `أَنَّ`)
    *   `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الْهَوَاءَ نَظِيفٌ.` (Qālat anna al-hawā'a naẓīfun.)
        *   `أَنَّ الْهَوَاءَ نَظِيفٌ` is the nominal clause, the object of `قَالَتْ`. Notice `الْهَوَاءَ` (the air) has a Fatha.

4.  **"I go to the university to learn."** (Purpose Clause)
    *   `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ لِأَدْرُسَ.` (Ana adhhaba ilā al-jāmi'ati li'adrusa.)
        *   `لِأَدْرُسَ` is the purpose clause. Notice `أَدْرُسَ` (I study) has a Fatha.

5.  **"If the teacher (f.) understands, then she will not fail."** (Conditional + Future Negation)
    *   `إِذَا فَهِمَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ، فَلَنْ تَرْسُبَ.` (Idhā fahimati al-mudarrisatū, falan tarsuba.)
        *   `إِذَا فَهِمَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ` is the conditional clause.
        *   `لَنْ تَرْسُبَ` is the negated future verb, `تَرْسُبَ` (she fails) is Subjunctive with Fatha because of `لَنْ`.

6.  **"It is not probable that he will send the message."** (Advanced Negation + Modal Expression)
    *   `لَيْسَ مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يُرْسِلَ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Laysa mina al-muḥtamali an yursila ar-risālata.)
        *   `أَنْ يُرْسِلَ الرِّسَالَةَ` is a nominal clause. `يُرْسِلَ` (he sends) is Subjunctive with Fatha. The whole phrase `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يُرْسِلَ الرِّسَالَةَ` acts as the predicate of `لَيْسَ`.
