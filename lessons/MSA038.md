# Day 38 - Modal expressions (maybe, probably)

## Lesson

1.  **What are Modal Expressions of Possibility?**
    *   These are words or phrases that tell us how likely something is to happen or to be true.
    *   In English, we use words like "maybe," "perhaps," "probably," "it's possible that."

2.  **Expressing "Maybe" or "Perhaps": `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā)**
    *   This is a simple and common way to say "maybe" or "perhaps."
    *   **Rule:** You usually place `رُبَّمَا` at the **beginning** of the sentence you want to make uncertain.
    *   `رُبَّمَا` does **not** change the vowels of the words that follow it.
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'rub-ba-maa', with a Shaddah on the `ب` (Baa) and a long 'aa' sound.

    *   **Example 1: "Maybe he goes to the school."**
        *   `رُبَّمَا يَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Rubbamā yadhhabu ilā al-madrasati.)
            *   *Notice*: `يَذْهَبُ` (he goes) stays with its Damma ending.

    *   **Example 2: "Maybe she writes the story."**
        *   `رُبَّمَا تَكْتُبُ الْقِصَّةَ.` (Rubbamā taktubu al-qisṣata.)
            *   *Notice*: `تَكْتُبُ` (she writes) stays with its Damma ending.

    *   **Example 3: "Maybe the book is new."** (For nominal sentences too!)
        *   `رُبَّمَا الْكِتَابُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Rubbamā al-kitābu jadeedun.)
            *   *Notice*: `الْكِتَابُ` (the book) stays with its Damma ending.

3.  **Expressing "Probably" or "It is probable that": `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ` (mina al-muḥtamali an)**
    *   This phrase is a bit longer and suggests a higher degree of probability than `رُبَّمَا`.
    *   **Rule 1: The `أَنْ` (an) Particle.** The most important thing to remember here is the word **`أَنْ` (an)**, which means "that" in this context. When `أَنْ` comes right before a **present tense verb**, it makes that verb change its ending from a Damma ( ُ ) to a **Fatha ( َ )**.
        *   Think of `أَنْ` as giving the present tense verb a little push, changing its sound!
    *   **Structure:** `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ` + [PRESENT TENSE VERB (with Fatha ending)] + [rest of sentence]
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'mi-na al-muḥ-ta-ma-li an'. `أَنْ` rhymes with "fun".

    *   **Let's compare:**
        *   Normal: `هُوَ يَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (He goes to the school.)
        *   "Probably": `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَذْهَبَ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an yadhhaba ilā al-madrasati.)
            *   *Notice*: `يَذْهَبُ` changed to `يَذْهَبَ` because of `أَنْ`.

    *   **More examples:**
        *   **"It is probable that she studies in the university."**
            *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ تَدْرُسَ فِي الْجَامِعَةِ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an tadrusa fī al-jāmi'ati.)
                *   *Notice*: `تَدْرُسُ` changed to `تَدْرُسَ`.

        *   **"It is probable that I eat the food."**
            *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ آكُلَ الطَّعَامَ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an ākula aṭ-ṭa'āma.)
                *   *Notice*: `آكُلُ` changed to `آكُلَ`.

        *   **"It is probable that they (masculine) drink the tea."**
            *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَشْرَبُوا الشَّايَ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an yashrabū ash-shāya.)
                *   *Note*: For verbs that end in `ـُونَ` for masculine plural (like `يَشْرَبُونَ`), `أَنْ` makes the `ن` (Nun) drop, so it becomes `يَشْرَبُوا`. Don't worry about this specific detail too much right now, just observe it. For most verbs, it's just Damma to Fatha.

This adds a wonderful layer of possibility and nuance to your Arabic expressions! You can now talk about things that aren't 100% certain.

## Examples

Let's put these new modal expressions into practice!

1.  **Maybe he returns from the house.**
    *   `رُبَّمَا يَرْجِعُ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ.` (Rubbamā yarji'u mina al-bayti.)

2.  **Maybe the car is big.**
    *   `رُبَّمَا السَّيَّارَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Rubbamā as-sayyāratu kabeeratun.)

3.  **It is probable that I write the story.**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ أَكْتُبَ الْقِصَّةَ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an aktuba al-qisṣata.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَكْتُبُ` becomes `أَكْتُبَ`.

4.  **It is probable that she goes to the big school.**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ تَذْهَبَ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an tadhhaba ilā al-madrasati al-kabeerati.)
        *   *Notice*: `تَذْهَبُ` becomes `تَذْهَبَ`.

5.  **Maybe you (masculine) study now, but it is probable that you will study tomorrow.**
    *   `رُبَّمَا أَنْتَ تَدْرُسُ الْآنَ، لَكِنْ مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ تَدْرُسَ غَدًا.` (Rubbamā anta tadrusu al-āna, lakin mina al-muḥtamali an tadrusa ghadan.)
        *   *Notice*: `تَدْرُسُ` for the "maybe" part, `تَدْرُسَ` for the "probable" part.

6.  **Indeed, maybe the pen is on the desk.**
    *   `إِنَّ رُبَّمَا الْقَلَمَ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Inna rubbamā al-qalama fawqa al-maktabi.)
        *   *Note*: `إِنَّ` still makes the topic (`الْقَلَمَ`) take Fatha, even with `رُبَّمَا` inside! This is getting fancy!

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   رُبَّمَا (rubbamā) - maybe, perhaps
*   مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ (mina al-muḥtamali an) - it is probable that, probably
*   أَنْ (an) - that (particle causing following present tense verb to take Fatha)
