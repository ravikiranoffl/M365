# Day 116 - Interview language and practice Part 1

## Lesson

"Interview language" refers to the specific words and phrases commonly used in formal interview settings. Whether you're applying for a job, or simply talking about one, knowing these terms is crucial. We'll start with some fundamental nouns and verbs, and then learn how to express qualities and intentions.

Let's learn some new and essential interview vocabulary!

### 1. `مُقَابَلَةٌ` (muqābalatun) - Interview

This is the noun for a formal meeting where questions are asked, especially to assess a candidate for a job.

*   **New Word:** `مُقَابَلَةٌ` (muqābalatun) - 'mu-qā-ba-la-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Interview (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمُقَابَلَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-muqābalatu muhimmatun.) - The interview is important.

### 2. `وَظِيفَةٌ` (waẓīfatun) - Job / Position / Function

This noun refers to a regular remunerated employment, or a specific duty or role.

*   **New Word:** `وَظِيفَةٌ` (waẓīfatun) - 'wa-ẓee-fa-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Job, position, function (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْوَظِيفَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-waẓīfatū jadeedatun.) - The job is new.

### 3. `شَرِكَةٌ` (sharikatun) - Company

You've seen this word in examples before, but let's officially add it to our core vocabulary as it's essential for this topic. It refers to a commercial business.

*   **New Word:** `شَرِكَةٌ` (sharikatun) - 'sha-ri-ka-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Company (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الشَّرِكَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Ash-sharikatū kabeeratun.) - The company is big.

### 4. `طَلَبَ` (ṭalaba) - He asked / requested (Past tense) / `يَطْلُبُ` (yaṭlubu) - He asks / requests (Present tense)

This verb means "to ask for" or "to request." It's crucial for talking about applying for something.

*   **New Word (Past):** `طَلَبَ` (ṭalaba) - 'ṭa-la-ba'
    *   *Meaning:* He asked, requested.
*   **New Word (Present):** `يَطْلُبُ` (yaṭlubu) - 'yaṭ-lu-bu'
    *   *Meaning:* He asks, requests / is asking, requesting.
    *   *Example (Present):* `الطَّالِبُ يَطْلُبُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yaṭlubu al-kitāba.) - The student asks for the book.

### 5. `طَلَبٌ` (ṭalabun) - Request / Application (noun)

This is the verbal noun (Masdar) of `طَلَبَ/يَطْلُبُ`, meaning "a request" or "an application."

*   **New Word:** `طَلَبٌ` (ṭalabun) - 'ṭa-la-bun'
    *   *Meaning:* Request, application (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْحُكُومَةُ تُرِيدُ طَلَبًا جَدِيدًا.` (Al-ḥukūmatu tureedu ṭalaban jadeedan.) - The government wants a new application.

### 6. `مُوَظَّفٌ` (muwaẓẓafun) - Employee

This noun refers to a person employed for wages or salary, especially at non-executive level. It's a passive participle form.

*   **New Word:** `مُوَظَّفٌ` (muwaẓẓafun) - 'mu-waẓ-ẓa-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* Employee (masculine noun). (Feminine: `مُوَظَّفَةٌ` - `muwaẓẓafatun`).
    *   *Example:* `هُوَ مُوَظَّفٌ جَدِيدٌ.` (Huwa muwaẓẓafun jadeedun.) - He is a new employee.

### 7. `عَمِلَ` (ʿamila) - He worked (Past tense) / `يَعْمَلُ` (yaʿmalu) - He works (Present tense)

You've seen this verb in many examples (like `يَعْمَلُ الْمُدِيرُ`), but let's officially include it as core vocabulary for clarity and to relate it to "job."

*   **New Word (Past):** `عَمِلَ` (ʿamila) - 'a-mi-la'
    *   *Meaning:* He worked.
*   **New Word (Present):** `يَعْمَلُ` (yaʿmalu) - 'yaʿ-ma-lu'
    *   *Meaning:* He works / is working.
    *   *Example (Present):* `الْمُوَظَّفُ يَعْمَلُ فِي الشَّرِكَةِ.` (Al-muwaẓẓafu yaʿmalu fī ash-sharikatī.) - The employee works in the company.

### 8. `خِبْرَةٌ` (khibra_tun) - Experience

This feminine noun refers to practical contact with and observation of facts or events.

*   **New Word:** `خِبْرَةٌ` (khibra_tun) - 'khib-ra-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Experience (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْخِبْرَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-khibratū muhimmatun.) - Experience is important.

### 9. `مَهَارَةٌ` (mahāratun) - Skill

This feminine noun refers to the ability to do something well.

*   **New Word:** `مَهَارَةٌ` (mahāratun) - 'ma-hā-ra-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Skill (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `لِي مَهَارَةٌ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Lī mahāratun jadeedatun.) - I have a new skill. (See `لِي` below).

### 10. `لِي` (lī) - I have / `لَهُ` (lahu) - He has / `لَهَا` (lahā) - She has, etc. (Possession with `لِـ`)

You've seen the preposition `لِـ` meaning "for" or "to." When combined with attached pronouns, it can express "to have" or "possession," especially for abstract qualities or inherent characteristics, or just simple possession.

*   **New Grammar/Phrase:** `لِـ` + pronoun for possession.
    *   `لِي` (lī) - I have (literally "to me").
    *   `لَهُ` (lahu) - He has (literally "to him").
    *   `لَهَا` (lahā) - She has (literally "to her").
    *   `لَكَ` (laka) - You (m.) have (literally "to you").
    *   `لَكِ` (laki) - You (f.) have (literally "to you").
    *   *Example:* `لِي خِبْرَةٌ فِي هَذَا الْمَجَالِ.` (Lī khibratun fī hādhā al-majāli.) - I have experience in this field. (See `مَجَالٌ` next).

### 11. `مَجَالٌ` (majālun) - Field / Area (of expertise)

This masculine noun refers to a particular branch of study or sphere of activity.

*   **New Word:** `مَجَالٌ` (majālun) - 'ma-jā-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Field, area (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْعَمَلُ فِي هَذَا الْمَجَالِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-'amalu fī hādhā al-majāli muhimun.) - Work in this field is important.

### 12. `أَفْضَلُ` (afḍalu) - Better / Best (masculine) / `فُضْلَى` (fuḍlā) - Better / Best (feminine)

These are superlative adjectives, meaning "better" or "best." You've seen similar patterns like `أَقْوَى` (stronger/strongest) and `أَكْثَرَ` (more/most) (Day 113).

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `أَفْضَلُ` (afḍalu) - 'af-ḍa-lu'
    *   *Meaning:* Better, best (masculine adjective, diptote).
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `فُضْلَى` (fuḍlā) - 'fuḍ-lā'
    *   *Meaning:* Better, best (feminine adjective, diptote).
    *   *Example (Masculine):* `هَذَا الْمَجَالُ أَفْضَلُ.` (Hādhā al-majālu afḍalu.) - This field is better/best.
    *   *Example (Feminine):* `هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةُ فُضْلَى.` (Hādhihi al-waẓīfatū fuḍlā.) - This job is better/best.

These terms provide a solid foundation for discussing jobs, applications, and qualifications in an interview context.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new interview words and phrases into sentences, using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the company wants a new employee."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ تُرِيدُ مُوَظَّفًا جَدِيدًا.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna ash-sharikatū tureedu muwaẓẓafan jadeedan.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الشَّرِكَةَ` (new word, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`). `تُرِيدُ` (known verb). `مُوَظَّفًا جَدِيدًا` (new word, Accusative direct object).

2.  **"The new employee has good experience in this field."**
    *   `لِلْمُوَظَّفِ الْجَدِيدِ خِبْرَةٌ جَيِّدَةٌ فِي هَذَا الْمَجَالِ.` (Li-l-muwaẓẓafi al-jadeedi khibratun jayyidatun fī hādhā al-majāli.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِلْمُوَظَّفِ الْجَدِيدِ` (new employee has - `لِـ` for possession, `الْمُوَظَّفِ` new, Genitive after `لِـ`). `خِبْرَةٌ جَيِّدَةٌ` (new word, Nominative subject of the sentence). `فِي هَذَا الْمَجَالِ` (in this field - `هَذَا` known, `الْمَجَالِ` new, Genitive after `فِي`).

3.  **"Why do you (m.) request this job? Because it is the best job."**
    *   `لِمَاذَا تَطْلُبُ هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةَ؟ لِأَنَّهَا الْوَظِيفَةُ الْفُضْلَى.` (Limādhā taṭlubu hādhihi al-waẓīfata? Li-annahā al-waẓīfatu al-fuḍlā.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِمَاذَا` (why, Day 79). `تَطْلُبُ` (new verb, "you ask"). `هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةَ` (this job, Accusative object). `لِأَنَّهَا` (because it is - Day 86). `الْوَظِيفَةُ الْفُضْلَى` (the best job - `الْفُضْلَى` new feminine superlative).

4.  **"Despite the fact that the interview is long, I want to show my skill."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُقَابَلَةَ طَوِيلَةٌ، أُرِيدُ أَنْ أُظْهِرَ مَهَارَتِي.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-muqābalata ṭaweelatun, ureedu an uẓhira mahāratī.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Day 107). `الْمُقَابَلَةَ طَوِيلَةٌ` (new word, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 27). `أُرِيدُ أَنْ أُظْهِرَ` (I want to show - `أُظْهِرَ` is subjunctive of `يُظْهِرُ` "he shows/reveals," Day 115). `مَهَارَتِي` (my skill - `مَهَارَةٌ` new word + `ـي` my, Day 24).

5.  **"It seems that the manager, Ali, wants to make the work easier for the employees."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْمُدِيرَ عَلِيًّا يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَجْعَلَ الْعَمَلَ أَسْهَلَ لِلْمُوَظَّفِينَ.` (Yabdū anna al-mudeera 'Aliyyan yureedu an yaj'ala al-'amala ashala li-l-muwaẓẓafeena.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (Day 80). `الْمُدِيرَ عَلِيًّا` (apposition, Accusative after `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَجْعَلَ` (wants to make - Day 98). `الْعَمَلَ أَسْهَلَ` (the work easier - `أَسْهَلُ` is masculine superlative for `سَهْلٌ` "easy", `أَفْعَلُ` pattern, it's a diptote so it takes Fatha when used this way). `لِلْمُوَظَّفِينَ` (for the employees - `الْمُوَظَّفِينَ` is plural of `مُوَظَّفٌ`, Genitive after `لِـ`, Day 36 for sound masculine plurals).

6.  **"The statistics show an increase in new jobs in the best field."**
    *   `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً فِي الْوَظَائِفِ الْجَدِيدَةِ فِي الْمَجَالِ الْأَفْضَلِ.` (Al-iḥṣā'ātū tuẓhiru ziyādatan fī al-waẓā'ifi al-jadeedati fī al-majāli al-afḍali.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `وَظَائِفُ` (waẓā'ifu) - jobs (plural of `وَظِيفَةٌ`, it's a diptote, so it takes Kasra without Tanween after `فِي`). Let's simplify and use singular `وَظِيفَةٍ` or rephrase to avoid new plural forms.
        *   **Revised 6:** **"The statistics show an increase in new job applications in the best field."**
        *   `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً فِي طَلَبَاتِ الْوَظَائِفِ الْجَدِيدَةِ فِي الْمَجَالِ الْأَفْضَلِ.` (Al-iḥṣā'ātū tuẓhiru ziyādatan fī ṭalabāti al-waẓā'ifi al-jadeedati fī al-majāli al-afḍali.)
            *   `طَلَبَاتِ` (ṭalabātī) - applications (plural of `طَلَبٌ`, feminine sound plural. Genitive after `فِي`). `الْوَظَائِفِ` is Genitive because it's the second term of Idafa with `طَلَبَاتِ`.
            *   *Notice:* `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً فِي` (Day 115). `طَلَبَاتِ الْوَظَائِفِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (applications for new jobs - complex Idafa `طَلَبَاتِ` + `الْوَظَائِفِ` + `الْجَدِيدَةِ` (adjective for `الْوَظَائِفِ`)). `فِي الْمَجَالِ الْأَفْضَلِ` (in the best field - `الْمَجَالِ` new, Genitive; `الْأَفْضَلِ` new, Genitive as adjective).

*   الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً فِي طَلَبَاتِ الْوَظَائِفِ الْجَدِيدَةِ فِي الْمَجَالِ الْأَفْضَلِ. (The statistics show an increase in new job applications in the best field.)
