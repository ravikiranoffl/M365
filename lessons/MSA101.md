# Day 101 - Government vocabulary

## Lesson

Just like in any language, understanding how to talk about government involves learning specific terms. We already know words like `وَزِيرٌ` (minister) and `رَئِيسٌ` (president), which are key figures. Today, we'll add to this by learning about structures and other important roles.

Let's learn some new and essential government vocabulary!

### 1. `حُكُومَةٌ` (ḥukūmatun) - Government

This is the general term for the governing body of a state or country.

*   **New Word:** `حُكُومَةٌ` (ḥukūmatun) - 'ḥu-kū-ma-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Government (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْحُكُومَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-ḥukūmatu jadeedatun.) - The government is new.

### 2. `وِزَارَةٌ` (wizāratun) - Ministry

A ministry is a specific department of the government, often headed by a minister. For example, `وِزَارَةُ الصِّحَّةِ` (Ministry of Health).

*   **New Word:** `وِزَارَةٌ` (wizāratun) - 'wi-zā-ra-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Ministry (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `تُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرَةُ وَزَارَةً جَدِيدَةً.` (Tureedu al-wazeeratu wizāratan jadeedatan.) - The minister (f.) wants a new ministry.

### 3. `بَرْلَمَانٌ` (barlamānun) - Parliament

This is often the legislative body in a country, where laws are made.

*   **New Word:** `بَرْلَمَانٌ` (barlamānun) - 'bar-la-mā-nun'
    *   *Meaning:* Parliament (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `أَعْلَنَ الْبَرْلَمَانُ قَانُونًا جَدِيدًا.` (A'lana al-barlamānu qānoonan jadeedan.) - The parliament announced a new law.

### 4. `دَوْلَةٌ` (dawlatun) - State / Country (formal)

While you know `بَلَدٌ` (country) from Day 78, `دَوْلَةٌ` is often used in more formal contexts to mean "state" or "sovereign country."

*   **New Word:** `دَوْلَةٌ` (dawlatun) - 'daw-la-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* State, country (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الدَّوْلَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Ad-dawlatu muhimmatun.) - The state is important.

### 5. `سَفِيرٌ` (safeerun) - Ambassador

An ambassador is a diplomat who represents his or her country abroad.

*   **New Word:** `سَفِيرٌ` (safeerun) - 'sa-fee-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Ambassador (masculine noun). (Feminine: `سَفِيرَةٌ` - `safeeratun`).
    *   *Example:* `جَاءَ السَّفِيرُ إِلَى الْقَاهِرَةِ.` (Jā'a as-safeeru ilā al-qāhirati.) - The ambassador came to Cairo.

### 6. `سِيَاسِيٌّ` (siyāsiyyun) - Politician / Political

This word can be an adjective meaning "political" or a noun meaning "politician." It is a relative adjective (nisba adjective, Day 49).

*   **New Word:** `سِيَاسِيٌّ` (siyāsiyyun) - 'see-yā-see-yyun'
    *   *Meaning:* Politician (masculine noun) / Political (masculine adjective). (Feminine: `سِيَاسِيَّةٌ` - `siyāsiyyatun`).
    *   *Example (as adjective):* `الْقَرَارُ سِيَاسِيٌّ.` (Al-qarāru siyāsiyyun.) - The decision is political.
    *   *Example (as noun):* `السِّيَاسِيُّ يَتَكَلَّمُ عَنِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (As-siyāsiyyu yatakallamu 'ani al-mushkilati.) - The politician speaks about the problem.

These words are essential for understanding news and engaging in discussions about governance.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new government words into sentences using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the new government will announce a big decision."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ سَتُعْلِنُ قَرَارًا كَبِيرًا.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-ḥukūmata al-jadeedata satu'linu qarāran kabeeran.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْحُكُومَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (new government - Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `سَتُعْلِنُ` (will announce - Day 64). `قَرَارًا كَبِيرًا` (big decision - Accusative, Day 54).

2.  **"The minister (m.) wants to make his ministry important for the state."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَجْعَلَ وِزَارَتَهُ مُهِمَّةً لِلدَّوْلَةِ.` (Yureedu al-wazeeru an yaj'ala wizāratuhu muhimmatan li-d-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَجْعَلَ` (wants to make - Day 98, Subjunctive). `وِزَارَتَهُ مُهِمَّةً` (his ministry important - both Accusative). `لِلدَّوْلَةِ` (for the state).

3.  **"Perhaps the parliament will begin by searching for a political solution."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا يَبْدَأُ الْبَرْلَمَانُ بِالْبَحْثِ عَنْ حَلٍّ سِيَاسِيٍّ.` (Rubbamā yabda'u al-barlamānu bi-l-baḥthi 'an ḥallin siyāsiyyin.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (Day 80). `يَبْدَأُ بِـ` (will begin by - Day 97). `بِالْبَحْثِ عَنْ` (by searching for - Day 97). `حَلٍّ سِيَاسِيٍّ` (political solution - Genitive after `عَنْ`).

4.  **"The ambassador (f.) believes that the economy became very important because of the joint work."**
    *   `تَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرَةُ أَنَّ الِاقْتِصَادَ أَصْبَحَ مُهِمًّا جِدًّا بِسَبَبِ الْعَمَلِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ.` (Ta'taqidu as-safeeratū anna al-iqtiṣāda aṣbaḥa muhimman jiddan bi-sababi al-'amali al-mushtaraki.)
        *   *Notice:* `السَّفِيرَةُ` (feminine ambassador). `تَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (believes that - Day 79). `الِاقْتِصَادَ أَصْبَحَ مُهِمًّا` (the economy became important - Day 98). `بِسَبَبِ` (because of - Day 86). `الْعَمَلِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ` (joint work - Genitive).

5.  **"It is known that the president, Ali, will speak about the new government's goals."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الرَّئِيسَ عَلِيًّا سَيَتَكَلَّمُ عَنْ أَهْدَافِ الْحُكُومَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna ar-ra'eeca 'Aliyyan sayatakallamu 'an ahdāfi al-ḥukūmati al-jadeedati.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `أَهْدَافٌ` (ahdāfun) - goals (plural of `هَدَفٌ`, Day 82).
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الرَّئِيسَ عَلِيًّا` (apposition, Accusative after `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `سَيَتَكَلَّمُ عَنْ` (will speak about - Day 97). `أَهْدَافِ الْحُكُومَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (goals of the new government - complex Idafa, `أَهْدَافِ` + `الْحُكُومَةِ` + `الْجَدِيدَةِ`).

6.  **"Although the minister (f.) is a politician, she wants to undertake the easy work."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَةَ سِيَاسِيَّةٌ، تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَقُومَ بِالْعَمَلِ السَّهْلِ.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-wazeerata siyāsiyyatun, tureedu an taqūma bi-l-'amali as-sahli.)
        *   *Notice:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (Day 81). `الْوَزِيرَةَ سِيَاسِيَّةٌ` (minister (f.) is a politician - Accusative for subject of `أَنَّ`, Nominative for comment). `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَقُومَ بِـ` (wants to undertake - Day 98, Subjunctive). `الْعَمَلِ السَّهْلِ` (the easy work - Genitive).
