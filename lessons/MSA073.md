# Day 73 - Complex negation patterns

## Lesson

You have a great toolkit for saying "not" in Arabic already! Now, let's learn how to use these tools in more intricate situations, adding one new negation word that acts like a "prefix" for nouns and adjectives.

### 1. Negating Nouns and Adjectives Directly with `غَيْرُ` (ghayru) - "non-, un-, other than"

Sometimes, you want to negate a *quality* or a *type* of something directly, like saying "unimportant" or "non-existent." For this, Arabic uses the word `غَيْرُ` (ghayru).

*   **New Word:** `غَيْرُ` (ghayru) - 'ghay-ru'
    *   *Meaning:* "non-", "un-", "other than." It's like a special word that sits next to another word and makes it mean the opposite, or "not that thing."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* The `غ` is like the French 'r' or gargling sound.
    *   **How it works:** `غَيْرُ` usually acts as the first part of an Idafa construction. This means the word it negates (the noun or adjective following it) will take a **Kasra** (ِ) ending.
    *   The case ending of `غَيْرُ` itself will change depending on its role in the sentence (just like any other noun). We'll see it mostly with Damma today.
    *   *Example:* `مُفِيدٌ` (useful) `--->` `غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ` (un-useful / not useful).
        *   `الْكِتَابُ غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ.` (Al-kitābu ghayru mufīdin.) - The book is not useful (literally: "The book is other-than useful").
        *   *Notice:* `غَيْرُ` has Damma because it's the comment. `مُفِيدٍ` has Kasra because it's the second term of an Idafa.
    *   *Example with noun:* `نَجَاحٌ` (success) `--->` `غَيْرُ نَجَاحٍ` (non-success / failure). (Often, `رَسَبَ` and `رَاسِبٌ` are used for failure, but this shows the `غَيْرُ` pattern).
        *   `هَذَا عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ نَاجِحٍ.` (Hādhā 'amalun ghayru nājiḥin.) - This is unsuccessful work. (Literally: "This is work other-than successful.") `نَاجِحٍ` is Kasra, matching `غَيْرُ` in being part of the Idafa structure, and `غَيْرُ` has Damma as it describes `عَمَلٌ`.

### 2. Negating the Predicate of an `أَنَّ` (anna) Clause

You know `أَنَّ` introduces a clause (a mini-sentence) that states a fact, like "He said that the house is big." (`قَالَ أَنَّ الْبَيْتَ كَبِيرٌ.`). What if you want to say, "He said that the house is **not** big"?

*   **Rule:** You use `لَيْسَ` (or its appropriate form) *inside* the `أَنَّ` clause, following all the rules of `لَيْسَ`!
    *   The noun after `أَنَّ` still takes Fatha.
    *   The noun *after* `لَيْسَ` (its subject) takes Damma.
    *   The predicate of `لَيْسَ` takes Fatha.
*   *Example:* `قَالَ أَنَّ الْبَيْتَ لَيْسَ كَبِيرًا.` (Qāla anna al-bayta laysa kabeeran.)
    *   *Literal breakdown:* He said that the house is not big.
    *   *Notice:* `الْبَيْتَ` has Fatha (due to `أَنَّ`). `لَيْسَ` (he is not, matches `الْبَيْتَ` in gender/number). `كَبِيرًا` has Fatha (predicate of `لَيْسَ`).

### 3. Negating the Action in an `أَنْ` (an) Clause (Purpose or Desire)

You know `أَنْ` introduces an action that is desired or intended, like "I want to go" (`أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَذْهَبَ.`). What if you want to say, "I want **not to go**"?

*   **Rule:** You use `لَا` directly before the present tense verb in the `أَنْ` clause. The verb will still remain in the Subjunctive mood (ending in Fatha).
*   *Example:* `أُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا أَذْهَبَ.` (Ureedu an lā adhhaba.)
    *   *Literal breakdown:* I want that not I go.
    *   *Notice:* `أَنْ لَا` is used. `أَذْهَبَ` still takes Fatha because `أَنْ` is still working on it.

### 4. Negating Passive Verbs

You just learned about passive verbs on Day 72. How do you say "It was **not** done" or "It will **not** be done"?

*   **Rule:** You use `لَمْ` (for past negation) or `لَنْ` (for future negation) directly before the passive present tense verb.
    *   Remember, `لَمْ` makes the verb Jussive (Sukoon, or Nūn-drop, or weak letter drop).
    *   Remember, `لَنْ` makes the verb Subjunctive (Fatha, or Nūn-drop).
*   *Example (Past Passive Negation):* `لَمْ يُكْتَبِ الْكِتَابُ.` (Lam yuktabi al-kitābu.)
    *   *Literal breakdown:* Not it-is-written the book. (Meaning: The book was not written.)
    *   *Notice:* `لَمْ` + passive present `يُكْتَبُ` (it is written) becomes `يُكْتَبْ` (Jussive Sukoon, here Kasra for meeting `الـ`). `الْكِتَابُ` is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` (new subject), taking Damma.
*   *Example (Future Passive Negation):* `لَنْ تُحَلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةُ.` (Lan tuḥalla al-mushkilatu.)
    *   *Literal breakdown:* Will not it-is-solved the problem. (Meaning: The problem will not be solved.)
    *   *Notice:* `لَنْ` + passive present `تُحَلُّ` (it is solved - feminine for `الْمُشْكِلَةُ`) becomes `تُحَلَّ` (Subjunctive Fatha). `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`, taking Damma.

These patterns allow you to express more complex and nuanced negative statements in Arabic. Keep practicing each one, and you'll see how they build on the foundational negation you've already learned!

## Examples

Let's see these complex negation patterns in action!

1.  **"The house is non-big."** (Meaning: The house is not big, using `غَيْرُ`)
    *   `الْبَيْتُ غَيْرُ كَبِيرٍ.` (Al-baytu ghayru kabeerin.)
        *   *Notice:* `غَيْرُ` is the comment, taking Damma. `كَبِيرٍ` takes Kasra as the second term of the Idafa.

2.  **"She said that the student is not successful."** (Negating `أَنَّ` clause predicate)
    *   `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَةَ لَيْسَتْ نَاجِحَةً.` (Qālat anna aṭ-ṭālibata laysat nājiḥatan.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ الطَّالِبَةَ` (that the student - f.) with Fatha. `لَيْسَتْ` (she is not) matches `الطَّالِبَةَ`. `نَاجِحَةً` (successful - f.) takes Fatha (predicate of `لَيْسَتْ`).

3.  **"We want not to fail in the exam."** (Negating `أَنْ` clause action)
    *   `نُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا نَرْسُبَ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Nureedu an lā narsuba fī al-imtiḥāni.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَنْ لَا نَرْسُبَ` (not to fail). `نَرْسُبَ` (we fail) takes Fatha because of `أَنْ`.

4.  **"The message was not sent by the company."** (Negating passive verb, past)
    *   `لَمْ تُرْسَلِ الرِّسَالَةُ مِنَ الشَّرِكَةِ.` (Lam tursalī ar-risālatu mina ash-sharikatī.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَمْ` + passive present `تُرْسَلُ` (it is sent - feminine for `الرِّسَالَةُ`) becomes `تُرْسَلْ` (Jussive Sukoon, here Kasra for meeting `الـ`). `الرِّسَالَةُ` is `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`. `مِنَ الشَّرِكَةِ` (by the company).

5.  **"It will not be used in the new meeting."** (Negating passive verb, future)
    *   `لَنْ يُسْتَخْدَمَ فِي الِاجْتِمَاعِ الْجَدِيدِ.` (Lan yustakhdama fī al-ijtimā'i al-jadeedi.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَنْ` + passive present `يُسْتَخْدَمُ` (it is used) becomes `يُسْتَخْدَمَ` (Subjunctive Fatha).

6.  **"It is clear that the solution is non-possible."** (Meaning: not possible)
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الْحَلَّ غَيْرُ مُمْكِنٍ.` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna al-ḥalla ghayru mumkinin.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `مُمْكِنٌ` (mumkinun) - possible (masculine adjective).
        *   *Notice:* `أَنَّ الْحَلَّ` (that the solution) with Fatha. `غَيْرُ مُمْكِنٍ` is the comment for `الْحَلَّ`, taking Damma (for `غَيْرُ`) and Kasra (for `مُمْكِنٍ`).

7.  **"The news affirms that the judge's decision is not just."**
    *   `تُؤَكِّدُ الْأَخْبَارُ أَنَّ قَرَارَ الْقَاضِي لَيْسَ عَدْلًا.` (Tu'akkidu al-akhbāru anna qarāra al-qāḍī laysa 'adlan.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `قَرَارٌ` (qarārun) - decision (masculine noun). `عَدْلٌ` (adlun) - justice (used here as an adjective "just").
        *   *Notice:* `تُؤَكِّدُ الْأَخْبَارُ` (the news affirms). `أَنَّ قَرَارَ الْقَاضِي` (that the decision of the judge) with `قَرَارَ` taking Fatha and being part of an Idafa. `لَيْسَ عَدْلًا` (it is not just) as the negation of the embedded nominal sentence.

Now, let's practice these complex negation patterns!

1.  How do you say "non-important" using `غَيْرُ`?
2.  How do you say "I want not to eat" (`أَكُلُ` - I eat)?
3.  How would you say "The lesson was not understood"? (`يُفْهَمُ` - it is understood, passive present).
4.  **Translate to Arabic:** "This is an unsuccessful project." (`هَذَا`, `مَشْرُوعٌ` - project, `نَاجِحٌ`). *Use `غَيْرُ`*
5.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The teacher (m.) believes that the student (f.) is not smart." (`مُدَرِّسٌ`, `يَعْتَقِدُ`, `أَنَّ`, `طَالِبَةٌ`, `ذَكِيَّةٌ`). *Use `لَيْسَتْ`*
6.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The minister will not be seen today." (`وَزِيرٌ`, `يُشَاهَدُ` - passive present of `شَاهَدَ` - he sees, meaning "he is seen", `الْيَوْمَ`). *Use `لَنْ`*
7.  **Translate to Arabic:** "We know that it is not necessary to go now." (`نَعْلَمُ`, `أَنَّهُ`, `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَذْهَبَ`, `الْآنَ` - now). *Hint: Negate `يَجِبُ أَنْ` with `لَيْسَ`*
    *   `نَعْلَمُ أَنَّهُ لَيْسَ مِنَ الْوَاجِبِ أَنْ نَذْهَبَ الْآنَ.` (We know that it is not among what is necessary that we go now.) This uses `لَيْسَ مِنَ الْوَاجِبِ أَنْ` which means "it is not obligatory to". Let's simplify and use `لَيْسَ مِنَ الْمُهِمِّ أَنْ` (it is not important to).
    *   **Revised Practice 7:** **Translate to Arabic:** "We know that it is not important to go now." (`نَعْلَمُ`, `أَنَّهُ`, `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ نَذْهَبَ`, `الْآنَ`). *Use `لَيْسَ` to negate `مُهِمٌّ`.*
8.  **Translate to Arabic:** "The company wants not to send the message." (`شَرِكَةٌ`, `تُرِيدُ`, `تُرْسِلُ`, `رِسَالَةٌ`). *Use `أَنْ لَا`*
9.  **Translate to Arabic:** "It is clear that the old computer was not used." (`مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ`, `حَاسُوبٌ`, `قَدِيمٌ`, `اُسْتُخْدِمَ`). *Use `لَمْ`*
10. **Translate to Arabic:** "The president hopes not to announce the bad news." (`رَئِيسٌ`, `يَأْمُلُ`, `يُعْلِنُ`, `أَخْبَارٌ`, `سَيِّئَةٌ` - bad, feminine plural). *Use `أَنْ لَا`*
