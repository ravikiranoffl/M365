## Lesson

1.  **The "He" Form is Our Starting Point**
    *   Remember from yesterday that the basic three-letter past tense verb, like `كَتَبَ` (he wrote), is our starting point. All other past tense forms are built by adding special endings to this "he" form.
    *   Think of it like adding different sleeves to the same shirt to make it fit different people!

2.  **The Past Tense Verb for "I" (أَنَا)**
    *   To say **"I wrote"** or **"I studied"**, we take the "he" form of the verb and add a special ending: **`ـتُ`**.
    *   Here's how it works:
        *   Take `كَتَبَ` (kataba - he wrote).
        *   The last letter of the root (ب) will get a Sukoon ( ْ ) to connect smoothly.
        *   Then, we add **`تُ`** (tū).
        *   So, `كَتَبَ` becomes `كَتَبْتُ` (katabtu) - **I wrote.**
            *   *Pronunciation hint*: The `تُ` sounds like the 'too' in 'tattoo'.
        *   Let's try with `دَرَسَ` (darasa - he studied):
            *   It becomes `دَرَسْتُ` (darastu) - **I studied.**

3.  **The Past Tense Verb for "You" (Masculine Singular - أَنْتَ)**
    *   To say **"You (a man) wrote"** or **"You (a man) studied"**, we use a slightly different ending: **`ـتَ`**.
    *   Here's how it works:
        *   Take `كَتَبَ` (kataba - he wrote).
        *   The last letter of the root (ب) will get a Sukoon ( ْ ).
        *   Then, we add **`تَ`** (ta).
        *   So, `كَتَبَ` becomes `كَتَبْتَ` (katabta) - **You (m) wrote.**
            *   *Pronunciation hint*: The `تَ` sounds like the 'ta' in 'tar'.
        *   Let's try with `دَرَسَ` (darasa - he studied):
            *   It becomes `دَرَسْتَ` (darasta) - **You (m) studied.**

4.  **The Past Tense Verb for "You" (Feminine Singular - أَنْتِ)**
    *   To say **"You (a woman) wrote"** or **"You (a woman) studied"**, we use another ending: **`ـتِ`**.
    *   Here's how it works:
        *   Take `كَتَبَ` (kataba - he wrote).
        *   The last letter of the root (ب) will get a Sukoon ( ْ ).
        *   Then, we add **`تِ`** (ti).
        *   So, `كَتَبَ` becomes `كَتَبْتِ` (katabti) - **You (f) wrote.**
            *   *Pronunciation hint*: The `تِ` sounds like the 'tee' in 'kitty'.
        *   Let's try with `دَرَسَ` (darasa - he studied):
            *   It becomes `دَرَسْتِ` (darasti) - **You (f) studied.**

5.  **The Past Tense Verb for "We" (نَحْنُ)**
    *   To say **"We wrote"** or **"We studied"**, we use the ending: **`ـنَا`**.
    *   Here's how it works:
        *   Take `كَتَبَ` (kataba - he wrote).
        *   The last letter of the root (ب) will get a Sukoon ( ْ ).
        *   Then, we add **`نَا`** (nā) - a long 'aa' sound.
        *   So, `كَتَبَ` becomes `كَتَبْنَا` (katabnā) - **We wrote.**
            *   *Pronunciation hint*: The `نَا` sounds like the 'nah' in 'banana'.
        *   Let's try with `دَرَسَ` (darasa - he studied):
            *   It becomes `دَرَسْنَا` (darasnā) - **We studied.**

Notice how the ending always tells us "who" did the action! The root letters stay the same, but the ending changes.

## Examples

Let's put these new verb forms into practice with the pronouns you learned on Day 7, and some nouns you already know!

1.  **I wrote the book.**
    *   `أَنَا كَتَبْتُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Ana katabtu al-kitaaba.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنَا` (I) matches `كَتَبْتُ` (I wrote). `الْكِتَابَ` (the book) is the object of the verb.

2.  **You (masculine) studied in the school.**
    *   `أَنْتَ دَرَسْتَ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Anta darasta fī al-madrasati.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنْتَ` (You-m) matches `دَرَسْتَ` (You-m studied). `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (in the school) uses our preposition rule.

3.  **You (feminine) wrote my name.**
    *   `أَنْتِ كَتَبْتِ اِسْمِي.` (Anti katabti ismī.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنْتِ` (You-f) matches `كَتَبْتِ` (You-f wrote). `اِسْمِي` (my name) uses the attached pronoun.

4.  **We studied the new book.**
    *   `نَحْنُ دَرَسْنَا الْكِتَابَ الْجَدِيدَ.` (Nahnu darasnā al-kitaaba al-jadeeda.)
        *   *Notice*: `نَحْنُ` (We) matches `دَرَسْنَا` (We studied). `الْكِتَابَ الْجَدِيدَ` (the new book) is a definite noun with a definite adjective, as learned on Day 11.

5.  **I wrote my name in my book.**
    *   `أَنَا كَتَبْتُ اِسْمِي فِي كِتَابِي.` (Ana katabtu ismī fī kitaabī.)
        *   *Notice*: This sentence uses a lot of what you've learned! Independent pronoun, conjugated verb, noun with attached pronoun, preposition, and another noun with an attached pronoun! You're building complex sentences!

You've just learned how to express actions in the past for "I," "You," and "We"! This is a massive leap in your Arabic communication skills and lets you talk about a lot more everyday situations.
