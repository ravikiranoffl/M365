# Day 94 - Intensive listening to Arabic news (Transcription Day 4)

## Lesson

Today, your mission is to tackle complex, grammatically intertwined sentences that are typical of news reports. The goal remains the same: to sharpen your ear by "listening" (reading and comprehending) these news-style sentences and actively transcribing them. This intensive practice is key to developing your fluency and confidence in understanding Arabic.

**How to approach transcription:**

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly, multiple times.** Imagine it being spoken naturally and clearly. Try to pronounce it aloud as accurately as you can, even if just softly.
2.  **Try to identify each word.** Break the sentence into smaller chunks if necessary. Look for known verbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and noun phrases.
3.  **Pay close attention to case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) and Sukoon.** These subtle markings carry significant grammatical information that helps you understand who is doing what to whom, or how words relate in a sentence.
4.  **Write down what you hear (or read).** Focus on getting the Harakat (vowel markings) correct for each word.
5.  **Then, compare your transcription with the correct version and explanation.** Reflect on any differences and why they occurred. This self-correction is where the real learning happens!

Remember, this isn't a test for a perfect score, but an exercise to improve your recognition skills. The more you practice, the faster your brain will process these forms and the clearer Arabic will become to you.

## Examples

Here is a complex news-style sentence. Read it, imagine hearing it, and try to identify each word and its ending.

**Arabic Text:**

`يَعْتَقِدُ الْوَزِيرُ الْفَاهِمُ عُمَرُ أَنَّ هَدَفَهُ الرَّئِيسِيَّ أَنْ يَدْعُوَ إِلَى السَّلَامِ، لِذَلِكَ سَيَزُورُ الْقَاهِرَةَ حَتَّى يُعْلِنَ خُطَّةً مُشْتَرَكَةً.`

*(Take your time to read and identify the words and their endings. When you are ready, continue below for the breakdown.)*

---

**Breakdown and Explanation:**

The sentence is: `يَعْتَقِدُ الْوَزِيرُ الْفَاهِمُ عُمَرُ أَنَّ هَدَفَهُ الرَّئِيسِيَّ أَنْ يَدْعُوَ إِلَى السَّلَامِ، لِذَلِكَ سَيَزُورُ الْقَاهِرَةَ حَتَّى يُعْلِنَ خُطَّةً مُشْتَرَكَةً.`

Let's break it down word by word, identifying each one and its grammatical features:

1.  **`يَعْتَقِدُ` (ya'taqidū):**
    *   **Word:** `يَعْتَقِدُ` (he believes).
    *   **Type:** Present tense verb (Day 64 - Thinking and Speaking Verbs).
    *   **Meaning:** He believes.

2.  **`الْوَزِيرُ` (al-wazeerū):**
    *   **Word:** `وَزِيرٌ` (minister - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Nominative (Damma - `ـُ`) as the subject of `يَعْتَقِدُ`.
    *   **Meaning:** the minister.

3.  **`الْفَاهِمُ` (al-fāhimū):**
    *   **Word:** `فَاهِمٌ` (understanding - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Nominative (Damma - `ـُ`), active participle (Day 88 - Participial modifiers) acting as an adjective agreeing with `الْوَزِيرُ`.
    *   **Meaning:** the understanding.

4.  **`عُمَرُ` (ʿUmaru):**
    *   **Word:** `عُمَرُ` (Omar - masculine proper noun).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (proper noun).
    *   **Case:** Nominative (Damma - `ـُ`) because it's in apposition to `الْوَزِيرُ` (Day 85 - Apposition structures). (Note: `عُمَرُ` is a diptote, so it takes Damma, Fatha, or Fatha, not Kasra in the genitive).
    *   **Meaning:** Omar.

5.  **`أَنَّ` (anna):**
    *   **Word:** `أَنَّ` (that).
    *   **Type:** Particle introducing a nominal clause (Day 39 - `أَنَّ`).
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the subject of the following clause take Fatha (Day 84 - Quotation Strategies).
    *   **Meaning:** that.

6.  **`هَدَفَهُ` (hadafahū):**
    *   **Word:** `هَدَفٌ` (goal) + `ـهُ` (his).
    *   **Grammar:** `هَدَفَ` takes Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause (Day 82 - Interview language). `ـهُ` is the attached pronoun.
    *   **Meaning:** his goal.

7.  **`الرَّئِيسِيَّ` (ar-ra'eesiyyā):**
    *   **Word:** `الرَّئِيسِيُّ` (main / primary - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`), adjective agreeing with `هَدَفَهُ` (Day 83 - Core Vocabulary).
    *   **Meaning:** the main.

8.  **`أَنْ` (an):**
    *   **Word:** `أَنْ` (to / that).
    *   **Type:** Particle introducing a subjunctive verb (Day 60 - Subjunctive mood).
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the following present tense verb take Fatha (Subjunctive mood).
    *   **Meaning:** to.

9.  **`يَدْعُوَ` (yad'uwā):**
    *   **Word:** `يَدْعُو` (he calls).
    *   **Conjugation:** Present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha - `ـَ`) because of `أَنْ` (Day 64 - Speech acts).
    *   **Meaning:** call.

10. **`إِلَى` (ilā):**
    *   **Word:** `إِلَى` (to / towards).
    *   **Type:** Preposition.
    *   **Meaning:** for.

11. **`السَّلَامِ` (as-salāmi):**
    *   **Word:** `سَلَامٌ` (peace).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Genitive (Kasra - `ـِ`) because it follows the preposition `إِلَى`.
    *   **Meaning:** peace.

12. **`لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika):**
    *   **Word:** `لِذَلِكَ` (therefore).
    *   **Type:** Discourse marker / Cause/effect connector (Day 69 - Persuasive language, Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
    *   **Meaning:** therefore.

13. **`سَيَزُورُ` (sayazūru):**
    *   **Word:** `يَزُورُ` (he visits).
    *   **Conjugation:** Future tense (`سَـ` prefix) (Day 22 - Future tense). Present tense verb, Nominative (Damma). (Day 87 - Headline compression - `زَارَ/يَزُورُ`).
    *   **Meaning:** he will visit.

14. **`الْقَاهِرَةَ` (al-qāhiratā):**
    *   **Word:** `قَاهِرَةٌ` (Cairo).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the direct object of `يَزُورُ` (Day 87).
    *   **Meaning:** Cairo.

15. **`حَتَّى` (ḥattā):**
    *   **Word:** `حَتَّى` (in order to / so that).
    *   **Type:** Conjunctive particle indicating purpose (Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
    *   **Grammar:** Makes the following present tense verb take Fatha (Subjunctive mood).
    *   **Meaning:** in order to.

16. **`يُعْلِنَ` (yu'linā):**
    *   **Word:** `يُعْلِنُ` (he announces).
    *   **Conjugation:** Present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha - `ـَ`) because of `حَتَّى` (Day 64 - Speech acts).
    *   **Meaning:** announce.

17. **`خُطَّةً` (khuṭṭatan):**
    *   **Word:** `خُطَّةٌ` (plan).
    *   **Definiteness:** Indefinite (no `الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًا`) as the direct object of `يُعْلِنَ` (Day 79 - Core Vocabulary).
    *   **Meaning:** a plan.

18. **`مُشْتَرَكَةً` (mushtarakatān):**
    *   **Word:** `مُشْتَرَكَةٌ` (joint - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Indefinite.
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًـَ`), participial adjective agreeing with `خُطَّةً` (Day 67 - Diplomatic phrasing, Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
    *   **Meaning:** joint.

**Full Sentence Meaning:** The understanding Minister Omar believes that his main goal is to call for peace, therefore he will visit Cairo in order to announce a joint plan.

## Practice

1.  **Arabic Text:** `رُبَّمَا تُرِيدُ الشَّرِكَةُ أَنْ تَخْرُجَ مِنَ السُّوقِ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ الْمَحْلُولَةِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā): Adverb (perhaps / maybe) (Day 80 - Hedging language).
        *   `تُرِيدُ` (tūreedū): `تُرِيدُ` (she wants), present tense verb (Day 44 - Wanting verbs).
        *   `الشَّرِكَةُ` (ash-sharikatū): `شَرِكَةٌ` (company - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as subject.
        *   `أَنْ` (an): Particle (to / that), makes verb take Fatha (Subjunctive) (Day 60 - Subjunctive mood).
        *   `تَخْرُجَ` (takhrujā): `تَخْرُجُ` (she exits), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `أَنْ` (Day 78 - Motion verbs).
        *   `مِنَ` (mina): Preposition `مِنْ` (from), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 10 - Prepositions).
        *   `السُّوقِ` (as-sūqi): `سُوقٌ` (market), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `مِنَ` (Day 51 - Vocabulary).
        *   `بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi): Prepositional phrase (because of), followed by Genitive (Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
        *   `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (al-mushkilatī): `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `بِسَبَبِ` (Day 50 - Vocabulary).
        *   `الْمَحْلُولَةِ` (al-maḥloolatī): `مَحْلُولَةٌ` (solved - f.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), passive participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
        *   **Translation:** Perhaps the company wants to exit the market because of the solved problem.

2.  **Arabic Text:** `قَالَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ فَاطِمَةُ لِلطَّالِبِ الْكَاتِبِ: "اِكْتُبِ الرِّسَالَةَ جَيِّدًا."`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `قَالَتِ` (qālatī): `قَالَ` (he said) + `ـتْ` (she, feminine marker for past tense), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 39 - `قَالَ`).
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ` (al-mudarrisatū): `مُدَرِّسَةٌ` (teacher - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject of `قَالَتِ`.
        *   `فَاطِمَةُ` (Fāṭimatū): `فَاطِمَةُ` (Fatima - proper noun), definite, Nominative (Damma), appositive to `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ` (Day 85 - Apposition structures). (Note: `فَاطِمَةُ` is a diptote).
        *   `لِلطَّالِبِ` (li-ṭ-ṭālibi): `لِـ` (to/for - preposition) + `الطَّالِبُ` (student - m.). `الطَّالِبِ` takes Genitive (Kasra) after `لِـ` (Day 17 - Prepositions, Day 39 - `لِـ`).
        *   `الْكَاتِبِ` (al-kātibi): `كَاتِبٌ` (writing - m.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), active participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الطَّالِبِ` (Day 88 - Participial modifiers).
        *   `اِكْتُبِ` (iktubi): `اِكْتُبْ` (write! - m.s. imperative) (Day 45 - Imperative verbs), takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons.
        *   `الرِّسَالَةَ` (ar-risālatā): `رِسَالَةٌ` (message), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as the direct object of `اِكْتُبِ` (Day 45 - Vocabulary).
        *   `جَيِّدًا` (jayyidan): Adverb (well), takes Fatha (Day 59 - Adverbs).
        *   **Translation:** The teacher, Fatima, said to the writing student: "Write the message well." (This is a direct quote, Day 84 - Quotation strategies).

3.  **Arabic Text:** `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْعَمَلَ مُهِمٌّ جِدًّا، يَظُنُّ الطَّالِبُ أَنَّهُ لَنْ يَنْجَحَ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ الصَّعْبَةِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `وَمَعَ` (wa-ma'a): Conjunction `وَ` (and) + Preposition `مَعَ` (with). Together, `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` means "although" (Day 81 - Contrast structures).
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): Particle (that), makes subject take Fatha (Day 39 - `أَنَّ`).
        *   `الْعَمَلَ` (al-'amalā): `عَمَلٌ` (work), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause (Day 29 - Vocabulary).
        *   `مُهِمٌّ` (muhimun): `مُهِمٌّ` (important), indefinite, Nominative (Damma) as the comment for `الْعَمَلَ` (Day 27 - Adjectives).
        *   `جِدًّا` (jiddan): Adverb (very), takes Fatha (Day 27 - Adverbs).
        *   `يَظُنُّ` (yaẓunnu): `يَظُنُّ` (he thinks / assumes), present tense verb (Day 79 - Thinking and speaking verbs).
        *   `الطَّالِبُ` (aṭ-ṭālibū): `طَالِبٌ` (student), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject of `يَظُنُّ`.
        *   `أَنَّهُ` (annahū): `أَنَّ` (that) + `ـهُ` (he / it). `ـهُ` takes Fatha because of `أَنَّ` (Day 84 - Quotation strategies).
        *   `لَنْ` (lan): Negation particle for future tense (Day 60 - Subjunctive mood).
        *   `يَنْجَحَ` (yanjaḥā): `يَنْجَحُ` (he succeeds), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `لَنْ` (Day 58 - Vocabulary).
        *   `بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi): Prepositional phrase (because of), followed by Genitive (Day 86 - Cause/effect connectors).
        *   `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (al-mushkilatī): `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `بِسَبَبِ` (Day 50 - Vocabulary).
        *   `الصَّعْبَةِ` (aṣ-ṣa'batī): `صَعْبَةٌ` (difficult - f.), definite, Genitive (Kasra), adjective agreeing with `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (Day 88 - Core Vocabulary).
        *   **Translation:** Although the work is very important, the student thinks that he will not succeed because of the difficult problem.
