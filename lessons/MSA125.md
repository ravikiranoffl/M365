# Day 125 - Fluency: Argumentation structures

## Lesson

"Argumentation structures" means learning to organize your ideas in Arabic to present a clear, logical, and often persuasive point of view. You already possess an incredible toolkit for this, including your stance markers and advanced connectors. Today, we'll explicitly link these tools to specific functions within an argument.

Think of it as having different "argumentative moves" you can make:

### 1. Stating a Claim or Main Point

These phrases help you introduce your main idea or what you believe to be true.

*   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna) - It is certain that... (Day 99)
*   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna) - It is known that... (Day 99)
*   `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ` (Lā shakka anna) - There is no doubt that... (Day 99)
*   `يَعْتَقِدُ ... أَنَّ` (Ya'taqidu ... anna) - ... believes that... (Day 79)
*   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (Yabdū anna) - It seems that... (Day 80)
*   **New Argumentative Phrase:** `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna) - It is clear that...
    *   *Explanation:* This phrase uses `وَاضِحٌ` (clear, Day 98) in the same structure as other "It is..." stance markers (Day 99). It's a powerful way to state a claim you consider obvious.

### 2. Adding Support or Elaboration

Once you've made a claim, you often need to back it up or add more details.

*   `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Alāwatan 'alā dhālika) - Moreover, in addition to that... (Day 107)
*   `خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ` (Khāṣṣatan wa-anna) - Especially since... (Day 108)

### 3. Presenting a Counter-Argument or Contrasting View

A good argument often considers other perspectives or acknowledges opposing facts.

*   `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (Min jihatin ukhrā) - On the other hand... (Day 108)
*   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna) - Despite the fact that... (Day 107)
*   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (Wa-ma'a anna) - Although... (Day 108 practice)
*   `وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (Wa-fī al-waqti nafsihi) - At the same time (can imply contrast or parallel action) (Day 108)

### 4. Drawing a Conclusion or Consequence

These phrases help you summarize your argument or state what logically follows from the presented facts.

*   `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika) - And accordingly... (Day 107)
*   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (Fī ḍaw'i dhālika) - In light of that... (Day 108)

### 5. Expressing Sentiment or Evaluation

Sometimes, you want to convey your feelings about a situation within your argument.

*   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi) - Unfortunately... (Day 99)
*   `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (Mina al-ghareebi anna) - It is strange that... (Day 99)

**How to build an argument:**

You can combine these structures to create a flowing, logical argument. For example:
1.  **Start with a claim:** `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الْبِيئَةَ تَتَطَلَّبُ حِمَايَةً.` (It is clear that the environment requires protection.)
2.  **Add support:** `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، يُظْهِرُ التَّقْرِيرُ زِيَادَةً فِي التَّلَوُّثِ.` (Moreover, the report shows an increase in pollution.)
3.  **Introduce a counter-argument/nuance:** `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، تَعْمَلُ الْحُكُومَةُ عَلَى تَحْسِينِ الْمَوَارِدِ.` (On the other hand, the government is working to improve resources.)
4.  **Draw a conclusion:** `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ أَفْضَلَ لِلْأَرْضِ.` (In light of that, everyone wants the best for the Earth.)

This systematic approach will allow you to articulate complex arguments with clarity and impact in Modern Standard Arabic.

## Examples

Let's look at some examples that weave together these argumentation structures. Try to identify the function of each connector and stance marker!

1.  **Claim + Support + Conclusion:**
    `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الْمُنَاخَ يَتَغَيَّرُ بِشَكْلٍ سَرِيعٍ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُظْهِرُ الْبَيَانَاتُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ زِيَادَةً فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ تَقْلِيلَ التَّلَوُّثِ.`
    (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna al-munākha yataghayyaru bi-shaklin saree'in. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, tuẓhiru al-bayānātu al-ʿilmiyyatu ziyādatan fī talawwuthi al-hawā'i. Wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika, yureedu al-khubarā'u taqleela at-talawwuthi.)
    *   *Translation:* It is clear that the climate is changing rapidly. Moreover, the scientific data shows an increase in air pollution. And accordingly, the experts want the reduction of pollution.
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Starts with a strong claim (`مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ`), adds supporting evidence (`عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ`), and then presents a logical, policy-oriented conclusion (`وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ`).

2.  **Claim + Contrast + Justification + Evaluation:**
    `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ تُرِيدُ النَّجَاحَ لِشَعْبِهَا. مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّهَا قَدَّمَتْ مُقْتَرَحَاتٍ جَدِيدَةٍ، لَمْ تَقْبَلْ الشَّرِكَةُ الْأَجْنَبِيَّةُ الِاسْتِثْمَارَ. لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، هَذَا يُمَثِّلُ مُشْكِلَةً كَبِيرَةً.`
    (Lā shakka anna al-ḥukūmata tureedu an-najāḥa li-sha'bihā. Min jihatin ukhrā, bi-r-raghmi min annahā qaddamat muqtarahātin jadeedatin, lam taqbal ash-sharikatū al-ajnabiyyatu al-istihtmāra. Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, hādhā yumaththilu mushkilatan kabeeratan.)
    *   *Translation:* There is no doubt that the government wants success for its people. On the other hand, despite the fact that it presented new proposals, the foreign company did not accept the investment. Unfortunately, this represents a big problem.
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Presents a positive initial claim (`لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ`), immediately counters it with a problem (`مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى`), explains the problem with a concession (`بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّهَا`), and concludes with a negative evaluation (`لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ`).

3.  **Belief + Elaboration + Conclusion:**
    `يَعْتَقِدُ الْقَاضِي الْعَالِمُ أَنَّ حِمَايَةَ حُقُوقِ الْإِنْسَانِ مُهِمَّةٌ فِي كُلِّ الْأَحْوَالِ. خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ الْجَرَائِمَ مُتَزَايِدَةٌ بِشَكْلٍ مَلْحُوظٍ. فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ تَطْبِيقَ الْعَدَالَةِ بِشَكْلٍ سَرِيعٍ.`
    (Ya'taqidu al-qāḍī al-ʿālimu anna ḥimāyata ḥuqūqi al-insāni muhimmatun fī kulli al-aḥwāli. Khāṣṣatan wa-anna al-jarā'ima mutazāyidatun bi-shaklin malḥūẓin. Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, yureedu al-jamee'u taṭbeeqa al-'adālati bi-shaklin saree'in.)
    *   *Translation:* The knowing judge believes that the protection of human rights is important in all cases. Especially since crimes are noticeably increasing. In light of that, everyone wants the swift application of justice.
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Begins with a strong belief (`يَعْتَقِدُ ... أَنَّ`), justifies it with a cause (`خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ`), and then draws a policy recommendation (`فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ`).

These examples demonstrate how to create nuanced and impactful arguments by strategically deploying your learned structures.
