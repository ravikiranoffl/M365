# Day 134 - Fluency: Translation exercises

## Lesson

"Fluency: Translation exercises" is a continuous and crucial practice for consolidating your advanced Arabic skills. You've become proficient in constructing complex sentences, expressing nuanced meanings, and reporting on diverse topics. Now, the challenge is to seamlessly and precisely translate from English to Arabic, ensuring every grammatical rule, every case ending, and every word choice is meticulously correct and contextually appropriate.

Think of yourself as a highly skilled artisan, now tasked with crafting exquisite pieces by carefully selecting and combining all the specialized materials and techniques you've acquired. This exercise will rigorously test your ability to:

1.  **Recall and apply diverse vocabulary:** Select the most appropriate words from all the specialized categories you've studied, including government, economy, environment, culture, science, legal, interview language, and the newer reporting fields.
2.  **Master grammatical structures:** Accurately implement `أَنَّ` and `أَنْ` clauses, subjunctive verbs, light verb constructions, verb + preposition collocations, Idafa constructions, and correctly handle plural forms (human/non-human, sound/broken, diptote).
3.  **Utilize connectors and stance markers:** Strategically place advanced connectors (`عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ`, `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ`, `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ`, `لِذَلِكَ`, etc.) and stance markers (`مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ`, `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ`, `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ`, `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ`, etc.) to ensure the translated text flows logically, maintains coherence, and conveys the intended tone and emphasis.
4.  **Demonstrate morphological understanding:** Use the correct forms of verbs, Masdars, active, and passive participles derived from roots.
5.  **Apply editing skills:** Pay meticulous attention to details like noun case endings (Nominative, Accusative, Genitive), adjective agreement (especially with non-human plurals and diptotes), and verb-subject agreement.

Each sentence is a comprehensive puzzle, requiring you to carefully consider how to best express the English meaning using your entire advanced Arabic toolkit. Remember to take your time, think through the grammar, and write your translations with full Harakat.

## Examples

Let's work through a few examples together. Read the English sentence, try to translate it in your mind (or aloud!), and then compare your version with my Arabic translation and the breakdown.

1.  **English Sentence:** "It cannot be denied that the reduction of air pollution is a global goal. And the evidence for that is that the scientific reports indicate a big impact on health."
    *   **Arabic Translation:** `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ تَقْلِيلَ تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ هَدَفٌ عَالَمِيٌّ. وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ التَّقَارِيرَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ تُشِيرُ إِلَى تَأْثِيرٍ كَبِيرٍ عَلَى الصِّحَّةِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ` (It cannot be denied that - New argumentation phrase, Day 132).
        *   `تَقْلِيلَ تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ` (the reduction of air pollution - Idafa, `تَقْلِيلَ` Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 124; `تَلَوُّثِ` Genitive, Day 124; `الْهَوَاءِ` Genitive, Day 124).
        *   `هَدَفٌ عَالَمِيٌّ` (a global goal - `هَدَفٌ` Nominative predicate, Day 117; `عَالَمِيٌّ` adjective, Day 106).
        *   `وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ` (And the evidence for that is that - New argumentation phrase, Day 132).
        *   `التَّقَارِيرَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ` (the scientific reports - `التَّقَارِيرَ` Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, plural of `تَقْرِيرٌ` (Day 115), broken plural diptote; `الْعِلْمِيَّةَ` feminine singular adjective, Day 123).
        *   `تُشِيرُ إِلَى تَأْثِيرٍ كَبِيرٍ عَلَى الصِّحَّةِ` (indicate a big impact on health - `تُشِيرُ إِلَى` (she indicates to) feminine verb for non-human plural `التَّقَارِيرَ`, Day 123; `تَأْثِيرٍ كَبِيرٍ` Genitive after `إِلَى`, Day 122; `عَلَى الصِّحَّةِ` (on health) `الصِّحَّةِ` Genitive, Day 126).

2.  **English Sentence:** "Despite the fact that old cultural traditions have a strong influence on the young generation, many people are interested in modern arts."
    *   **Arabic Translation:** `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ التَّقَالِيدَ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ الْقَدِيمَةَ لَهَا تَأْثِيرٌ قَوِيٌّ عَلَى الْجِيلِ الصَّغِيرِ، يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ النَّاسِ بِالْفُنُونِ الْحَدِيثَةِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `التَّقَالِيدَ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ الْقَدِيمَةَ` (old cultural traditions - `التَّقَالِيدَ` Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 122; `الثَّقَافِيَّةَ الْقَدِيمَةَ` feminine singular adjectives for non-human plural, Day 122/54).
        *   `لَهَا تَأْثِيرٌ قَوِيٌّ عَلَى الْجِيلِ الصَّغِيرِ` (have a strong influence on the young generation - `لَهَا` (they have); `تَأْثِيرٌ قَوِيٌّ` (strong influence) Nominative, Day 122/99; `عَلَى الْجِيلِ الصَّغِيرِ` (on the young generation) `الْجِيلِ الصَّغِيرِ` Genitive, Day 129).
        *   `يَهْتَمُّ الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ النَّاسِ بِالْفُنُونِ الْحَدِيثَةِ` (many people are interested in modern arts - `يَهْتَمُّ بِـ` (is interested in) verb + preposition, Day 122; `الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ النَّاسِ` (many people) Nominative subject, Day 110; `الْفُنُونِ الْحَدِيثَةِ` (modern arts) Genitive after `بِـ`, Day 129).

3.  **English Sentence:** "It is strange that the government did not announce plans for the protection of environmental resources. Therefore, many challenges remain."
    *   **Arabic Translation:** `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ لَمْ تُعْلِنْ عَنْ خُطَطٍ لِحِمَايَةِ الْمَوَارِدِ الْبِيئِيَّةِ. لِذَلِكَ، تَبْقَى الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ التَّحَدِّيَاتِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (It is strange that - Stance marker, Day 99).
        *   `الْحُكُومَةَ لَمْ تُعْلِنْ عَنْ` (the government did not announce - `الْحُكُومَةَ` Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 101; `لَمْ تُعْلِنْ عَنْ` (did not announce about) negation + verb, Day 106).
        *   `خُطَطٍ لِحِمَايَةِ الْمَوَارِدِ الْبِيئِيَّةِ` (plans for the protection of environmental resources - `خُطَطٍ` Genitive after `عَنْ`, Day 113; `لِحِمَايَةِ` (for protection) preposition + Masdar, Day 124; `الْمَوَارِدِ الْبِيئِيَّةِ` (environmental resources) Idafa + adjective, Day 124).
        *   `لِذَلِكَ` (Therefore - New argumentation word, Day 132).
        *   `تَبْقَى الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ التَّحَدِّيَاتِ` (many challenges remain - `تَبْقَى` (she remains) feminine verb; `الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ التَّحَدِّيَاتِ` (many challenges) Nominative subject, Day 110/105).

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   التَّقَارِيرُ (at-taqāreeru) - the reports (masculine plural noun, broken plural, diptote, e.g., in Accusative/Genitive indefinite: `تَقَارِيرَ`)
*   تَبْقَى (tabqā) - she remains (present tense verb)
*   إِنْسَانِيَّةٌ (insāniyyatun) - humanity (feminine noun)
*   زِرَاعِيٌّ (zirāʿiyyun) - agricultural (masculine adjective)
*   إِنْتَاجٌ (intāj_un) - production (masculine Masdar/noun)
*   وِزَارَةُ الْبِيئَةِ (wizāratu al-bee'ati) - Ministry of Environment (feminine noun phrase, Idafa)
*   شَبَابٌ (shabābun) - young people, youth (masculine noun)
*   تَحْسِينٌ (taḥseenun) - improvement (masculine Masdar/noun)
