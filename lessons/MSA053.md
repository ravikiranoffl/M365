# Day 53 - Weekly reading practice

## Lesson

Today, your main task is to **read a short passage in Arabic**. This is a chance to see how all the grammar rules and vocabulary words you've learned combine to form meaningful sentences, just like in real Arabic texts.

**How to approach this reading practice:**

1.  **Read Slowly:** Don't rush! Take your time with each word.
2.  **Focus on Harakat:** Use the small vowel marks (Fatha, Kasra, Damma, Sukoon, Shaddah) to guide your pronunciation.
3.  **Identify Familiar Words and Patterns:** As you read, try to recognize words you know. Can you spot verbs, nouns, prepositions, or specific grammatical structures like Idafa or participles?
4.  **Try to Understand the General Meaning:** After reading a sentence, pause and try to understand what it means before looking at the translation.
5.  **Don't worry about perfection:** It's okay if you don't understand every single word at first glance. The goal is to practice and build your confidence!

## Examples

**Passage: "News and Understanding"**

1.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `الطَّالِبُ الْجَدِيدُ يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The new student reads the news every morning.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `الطَّالِبُ الْجَدِيدُ` (Aṭ-ṭālibu al-jadeedu): "The new student." Here, `الطَّالِبُ` (student) is the definite masculine singular noun, and `الْجَدِيدُ` (new) is its definite masculine singular adjective, matching in definiteness, gender, and case (Damma).
        *   `يَقْرَأُ` (yaqra'u): "He reads." This is a present tense verb (Day 45). The subject is the student.
        *   `الْأَخْبَارَ` (al-akhbāra): "The news." This is a non-human broken plural (Day 45) and the direct object of `يَقْرَأُ`, so it takes a Fatha.
        *   `كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ` (kulla ṣabāḥin): "Every morning." This is an adverbial phrase for time (Day 27).

2.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `الْأَخْبَارُ عَنِ الدَّوْلَةِ وَ سِيَاسَتِهَا وَ اقْتِصَادِهَا.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The news is about the state, its politics, and its economy.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `الْأَخْبَارُ` (al-akhbāru): "The news." This is the subject (topic) of the nominal sentence.
        *   `عَنِ` ('ani): "About." This is a preposition (Day 48). It became `عَنِ` instead of `عَنْ` because it's followed by `الـ` (the definite article).
        *   `الدَّوْلَةِ` (ad-dawlati): "The state." This noun (Day 48) takes a Kasra because it follows the preposition `عَنِ`.
        *   `وَ` (wa): "And" (Day 28).
        *   `سِيَاسَتِهَا` (siyāsatihā): "Its politics" or "politics of it." This is an Idafa construct (Day 25)! `سِيَاسَةِ` (politics - Day 48) is the first noun, taking Kasra because it's linked to `الدَّوْلَةِ` by `عَنِ`. `هَا` (-hā) is the feminine pronoun (it/her) referring to `الدَّوْلَةِ`.
        *   `وَ اقْتِصَادِهَا` (wa iqtiṣādihā): "And its economy." Another Idafa construct with `اقْتِصَادِ` (economy - Day 49) and the pronoun `هَا`.

3.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْوَزِيرُ فِي اجْتِمَاعٍ مُهِمٍّ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    It is probable that the minister is in an important meeting.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ` (mina al-muḥtamali an): "It is probable that" (Day 38).
        *   `يَكُونَ` (yakūna): "He is/will be." This is the present tense verb for "to be," and it takes a Fatha because it follows `أَنْ` (Day 38).
        *   `الْوَزِيرُ` (al-wazeeru): "The minister" (Day 48). This is the subject of `يَكُونَ`.
        *   `فِي اجْتِمَاعٍ مُهِمٍّ` (fī ijtimā'in muhimun): "In an important meeting." `فِي` is a preposition. `اجْتِمَاعٍ` (meeting - Day 50) is indefinite and takes Tanween Kasra. `مُهِمٍّ` (important - Day 43) is its adjective, matching in indefiniteness, gender (masculine), and case (Tanween Kasra).

4.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `قَالَ الرَّئِيسُ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ تُرِيدُ حَلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The president said that the government wants to solve the problem.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `قَالَ الرَّئِيسُ` (Qāla ar-ra'eecu): "The president said" (Day 39, Day 48).
        *   `أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ` (anna al-ḥukūmata): "That the government." `أَنَّ` (Day 39) makes `الْحُكُومَةَ` (government - Day 48) take a Fatha.
        *   `تُرِيدُ` (tureedu): "She wants" (Day 44). The subject is `الْحُكُومَةَ` (feminine), so `تُرِيدُ` is used.
        *   `حَلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (ḥalla al-mushkilati): "Solving of the problem" or "to solve the problem." This is a nominalized phrase acting as the object of `تُرِيدُ`. `حَلَّ` (solving/solution - Masdar from Day 52) is the first noun of the Idafa, taking Fatha because it's the object of `تُرِيدُ`. `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (the problem - Day 50) is the second noun of the Idafa, taking Kasra.

5.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `يَطْلُبُ الشَّعْبُ عَدْلاً وَ اتِّفَاقاً كَبِيرًا.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The people seek justice and a big agreement.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `يَطْلُبُ` (yaṭlubu): "He seeks/asks for" (Day 51).
        *   `الشَّعْبُ` (ash-sha'bu): "The people" (Day 48). This is the subject of `يَطْلُبُ` (treated as masculine singular).
        *   `عَدْلاً` ('adlan): "Justice" (Day 51). This is the direct object, taking Tanween Fatha.
        *   `وَ` (wa): "And" (Day 28).
        *   `اتِّفَاقاً كَبِيرًا` (ittifāqan kabeeran): "A big agreement." `اتِّفَاقاً` (agreement - Day 50) is another direct object, taking Tanween Fatha. `كَبِيرًا` (big - Day 3) is its adjective, matching in indefiniteness, gender (masculine), and case (Tanween Fatha).

6.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `الدِّرَاسَةُ وَ الْكِتَابَةُ مُفِيدَةٌ لِفَهْمِ الْقَانُونِ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    Studying and writing are useful for understanding the law.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `الدِّرَاسَةُ` (ad-dirāsatū): "Studying" (Masdar from Day 43). This is the first subject.
        *   `وَ الْكِتَابَةُ` (wa al-kitābatū): "And writing" (Masdar from Day 43). This is the second subject.
        *   `مُفِيدَةٌ` (mufīdatun): "Useful" (Day 43). This is the comment/adjective. Even though there are two subjects (`الدِّرَاسَةُ` and `الْكِتَابَةُ`), both are feminine, and when multiple non-human things are subjects, the comment often takes feminine singular form, hence `مُفِيدَةٌ`.
        *   `لِفَهْمِ الْقَانُونِ` (li-fahmi al-qānūni): "For understanding the law." `لِـ` (Day 10) is a preposition, meaning "for." `فَهْمِ` (understanding - Masdar from Day 52) is the first noun of an Idafa, taking Kasra after `لِـ`. `الْقَانُونِ` (the law - Day 51) is the second noun of the Idafa, taking Kasra.
