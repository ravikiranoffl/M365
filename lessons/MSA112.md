# Day 112 - Morphology practice Part 2

## Lesson

Let's continue to explore how words are derived from their fundamental three-letter roots in **Form I**. Remember, the three-letter root carries the core meaning, and different patterns create related verbs, nouns, and adjectives.

We'll take two more roots that you've already encountered in vocabulary and trace their common Form I derivations. Pay close attention to the vowels in the past and present tense verbs, and the structure of the Masdar; you'll notice they aren't always exactly the same as `فَعَلَ - يَفْعُلُ - فِعَالَةٌ` from yesterday, but they still belong to Form I.

### 1. Root `ع ل م` (A-L-M) - related to knowing, learning, knowledge

This root is very common and gives us words related to knowing. You already know `يَعْلَمُ` (he knows) from Day 79 and `عَالِمٌ` (knowing/scholar) from Day 98's examples.

*   **Past Tense Verb (فِعْلٌ مَاضٍ):**
    *   `عَلِمَ` (ʿalima) - He knew. (Notice the Kasra on the middle letter, following the `فَعِلَ` pattern).
*   **Present Tense Verb (فِعْلٌ مُضَارِعٌ):**
    *   `يَعْلَمُ` (yaʿlamu) - He knows / is knowing. (Notice the Fatha on the middle letter, following the `يَفْعَلُ` pattern).
*   **Active Participle (اسْمُ فَاعِلٍ):**
    *   `عَالِمٌ` (ʿālimun) - Knowing (one who knows) / Scholar. (Follows `فَاعِلٌ` pattern).
*   **Passive Participle (اسْمُ مَفْعُولٍ):**
    *   `مَعْلُومٌ` (maʿlūmun) - Known (one being known). (Follows `مَفْعُولٌ` pattern).
*   **Verbal Noun / Masdar (مَصْدَرٌ):** This Masdar is `فِعْلٌ` pattern, similar to `دَرْسٌ` (lesson), but here it means "knowledge."
    *   `عِلْمٌ` (ʿilmun) - Knowledge / Science. (This follows a common pattern `فِعْلٌ`).

### 2. Root `ح ف ظ` (H-F-Z) - related to protecting, memorizing, keeping

This root gives us words related to keeping something safe or keeping it in mind. You already know `تَحْفَظُ` (she protects) from Day 102.

*   **Past Tense Verb:**
    *   `حَفِظَ` (ḥafiẓa) - He protected / memorized. (Again, Kasra on the middle letter, `فَعِلَ` pattern).
*   **Present Tense Verb:**
    *   `يَحْفَظُ` (yaḥfaẓu) - He protects / memorizes. (Again, Fatha on the middle letter, `يَفْعَلُ` pattern).
*   **Active Participle:**
    *   `حَافِظٌ` (ḥāfiẓun) - Protector / Memorizer. (Follows `فَاعِلٌ` pattern).
*   **Passive Participle:**
    *   `مَحْفُوظٌ` (maḥfūẓun) - Protected / Memorized. (Follows `مَفْعُولٌ` pattern).
*   **Verbal Noun / Masdar:** This Masdar is also a `فِعْلٌ` pattern.
    *   `حِفْظٌ` (ḥifẓun) - Protection / Memorization. (This follows a common pattern `فِعْلٌ`).

**Key takeaway for today:**
Even within **Form I**, the vowels of the present tense verb and the Masdar can vary, but the overall patterns for past tense, active participle, and passive participle are very consistent. Knowing these common patterns helps you quickly identify and understand new words from familiar roots!

## Examples

Let's see these morphological derivations in sentences, combining them with the grammar and vocabulary you've already mastered.

1.  **"He knew that the knowledge is important for everyone."**
    *   `عَلِمَ أَنَّ الْعِلْمَ مُهِمٌّ لِلْجَمِيعِ.` (ʿAlima anna al-ʿilma muhimmun li-l-jamee'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `عَلِمَ` (new past tense verb). `أَنَّ` clause (Day 39). `الْعِلْمَ` (new Masdar, Accusative after `أَنَّ`).

2.  **"The knowing judge wants to protect human rights."**
    *   `الْقَاضِي الْعَالِمُ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَحْفَظَ حُقُوقَ الْإِنْسَانِ.` (Al-qāḍī al-ʿālimu yureedu an yaḥfaẓa ḥuqūqa al-insāni.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْعَالِمُ` (active participle, apposition to `الْقَاضِي`, Day 102, 88). `يَحْفَظَ` (new present tense verb, Subjunctive after `أَنْ`, Day 60). `حُقُوقَ الْإِنْسَانِ` (human rights, Idafa, Day 102).

3.  **"Protection of the state is important. Moreover, it is a great duty."**
    *   `حِفْظُ الدَّوْلَةِ مُهِمٌّ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، هُوَ وَاجِبٌ عَظِيمٌ.` (Ḥifẓu ad-dawlatu muhimun. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, huwa wājibun ʿaẓīmun.)
        *   *Notice:* `حِفْظُ الدَّوْلَةِ` (new Masdar as first term of Idafa, Day 101 `الدَّوْلَةِ`). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (advanced connector, Day 107). `وَاجِبٌ عَظِيمٌ` (great duty, Day 99, 102).

4.  **"It is certain that the protected decision is known."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْقَرَارَ الْمَحْفُوظَ مَعْلُومٌ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-qarāra al-maḥfūẓa maʿlūmun.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (stance marker, Day 99). `الْقَرَارَ الْمَحْفُوظَ` (the protected decision, `الْقَرَارَ` is Accusative as subject of `أَنَّ`, `الْمَحْفُوظَ` (new passive participle) is its adjective). `مَعْلُومٌ` (new passive participle, Nominative comment for `الْقَرَارَ`).

5.  **"Despite the fact that the problem is old, the minister (m.) knows the solution."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ قَدِيمَةٌ، يَعْلَمُ الْوَزِيرُ الْحَلَّ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-mushkilata qadeematun, yaʿlamu al-wazeeru al-ḥalla.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (advanced connector, Day 107). `يَعْلَمُ` (known present tense verb). `الْوَزِيرُ` (Day 50). `الْحَلَّ` (Day 97).
