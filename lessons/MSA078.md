# Day 78 - Motion verbs

## Lesson

Motion verbs are exactly what they sound like: verbs that tell you about movement! Just like in English, you need different verbs to describe different kinds of movement. We will learn a few new, very common motion verbs today and practice how to use them with the places you already know.

### 1. `ذَهَبَ` / `يَذْهَبُ` - To Go (Review)

You already know this verb from Day 38! It's one of the most basic motion verbs.
*   **Root:** `ذ ه ب` (dh-h-b)
*   **Past Tense (he went):** `ذَهَبَ` (dhahaba) - 'dha-ha-ba'
*   **Present Tense (he goes):** `يَذْهَبُ` (yadhhabu) - 'yadh-ha-bu'
*   **Present Tense (she/you-m.s. go):** `تَذْهَبُ` (tadhhabu) - 'tadh-ha-bu'
*   **Present Tense (I go):** `أَذْهَبُ` (adhhabu) - 'adh-ha-bu'
*   **Present Tense (we go):** `نَذْهَبُ` (nadhhabu) - 'nadh-ha-bu'
*   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `ذَهَابٌ` (dhahābun) - going, departure (masculine noun).
*   **Common Preposition:** `إِلَى` (ilā - to, towards).
    *   *Example:* `يَذْهَبُ الطَّالِبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Yadhhabu aṭ-ṭālibu ilā al-madrasati.) - The student goes to the school.

### 2. `جَاءَ` / `يَجِيءُ` - To Come

This verb is the opposite of "to go"! You've seen the past tense `جَاءَ` before in relative clauses (Day 63).

*   **Root:** `ج ي ء` (j-y-')
*   **Past Tense (he came):** `جَاءَ` (jā'a) - 'jaa-a'
    *   *Example:* `جَاءَ الْوَزِيرُ إِلَى الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Jā'a al-wazeeru ilā al-maktabi.) - The minister came to the office.
*   **Present Tense (he comes):** `يَجِيءُ` (yajī'u) - 'ya-jee-u'
*   **Present Tense (she/you-m.s. come):** `تَجِيءُ` (tajī'u) - 'ta-jee-u'
*   **Present Tense (I come):** `أَجِيءُ` (ajī'u) - 'a-jee-u'
*   **Present Tense (we come):** `نَجِيءُ` (najī'u) - 'na-jee-u'
*   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `مَجِيءٌ` (majī'un) - coming (masculine noun).
*   **Common Preposition:** `إِلَى` (ilā - to, towards).
    *   *Example:* `تَجِيءُ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ.` (Tajī'u al-mudarrisatū ilā al-bayti.) - The teacher (f.) comes to the house.

### 3. `عَادَ` / `يَعُودُ` - To Return

This verb means to go back to a place you were before.

*   **Root:** `ع و د` (a-w-d) - Notice the middle `و` (w). This kind of verb changes its middle sound in different tenses.
*   **Past Tense (he returned):** `عَادَ` (ʿāda) - 'aa-da' (The `و` becomes an `ا` in the past tense here).
    *   *Example:* `عَادَ الطَّالِبُ مِنَ الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (ʿĀda aṭ-ṭālibu mina al-madrasati.) - The student returned from the school.
*   **Present Tense (he returns):** `يَعُودُ` (yaʿūdu) - 'ya-oo-du' (The `و` shows up as `ُو` in the present tense here).
*   **Present Tense (she/you-m.s. return):** `تَعُودُ` (taʿūdu) - 'ta-oo-du'
*   **Present Tense (I return):** `أَعُودُ` (aʿūdu) - 'a-oo-du'
*   **Present Tense (we return):** `نَعُودُ` (naʿūdu) - 'na-oo-du'
*   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `عَوْدَةٌ` (ʿawdatun) - return (feminine noun).
*   **Common Prepositions:** `إِلَى` (to) when returning *to* a place, and `مِنْ` (from) when returning *from* a place.
    *   *Example:* `نَعُودُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ.` (Naʿūdu ilā al-bayti.) - We return to the house.

### 4. `دَخَلَ` / `يَدْخُلُ` - To Enter

This verb describes movement *into* a place.

*   **Root:** `د خ ل` (d-kh-l)
*   **Past Tense (he entered):** `دَخَلَ` (dakhala) - 'da-kha-la'
    *   *Example:* `دَخَلَ الرَّجُلُ الْمَكْتَبَ.` (Dakhala ar-rajulu al-maktaba.) - The man entered the office. (Notice: often `دَخَلَ` takes its object directly without `إِلَى` when referring to a specific place).
*   **Present Tense (he enters):** `يَدْخُلُ` (yadkhulu) - 'yad-khu-lu'
*   **Present Tense (she/you-m.s. enter):** `تَدْخُلُ` (tadkhulu) - 'tad-khu-lu'
*   **Present Tense (I enter):** `أَدْخُلُ` (adkhulu) - 'ad-khu-lu'
*   **Present Tense (we enter):** `نَدْخُلُ` (nadkhulu) - 'nad-khu-lu'
*   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `دُخُولٌ` (dukhūlun) - entering (masculine noun).
*   **Common Usage:** Can take an object directly (like `دَخَلَ الْبَيْتَ` - he entered the house) or use `إِلَى` (like `دَخَلَ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ` - he entered into the house). We'll mostly use it directly taking the object for now.
    *   *Example:* `تَدْخُلُ الْمَرْأَةُ السُّوقَ.` (Tadkhulu al-mar'atu as-sūqa.) - The woman enters the market.

### 5. `خَرَجَ` / `يَخْرُجُ` - To Exit / To Go Out

This verb describes movement *out of* a place. It's the opposite of "to enter."

*   **Root:** `خ ر ج` (kh-r-j)
*   **Past Tense (he exited):** `خَرَجَ` (kharaja) - 'kha-ra-ja'
    *   *Example:* `خَرَجَ الْوَلَدُ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ.` (Kharaja al-waladu mina al-bayti.) - The boy exited from the house.
*   **Present Tense (he exits):** `يَخْرُجُ` (yakhruju) - 'yakh-ru-ju'
*   **Present Tense (she/you-m.s. exit):** `تَخْرُجُ` (takhruju) - 'takh-ru-ju'
*   **Present Tense (I exit):** `أَخْرُجُ` (akhruju) - 'akh-ru-ju'
*   **Present Tense (we exit):** `نَخْرُجُ` (nakhruju) - 'nakh-ru-ju'
*   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `خُرُوجٌ` (khurūjun) - exiting, going out (masculine noun).
*   **Common Preposition:** `مِنْ` (min - from).
    *   *Example:* `يَخْرُجُ الْوَزِيرُ مِنَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Yakhruju al-wazeeru mina al-maktabi.) - The minister exits from the office.

These verbs are incredibly useful for describing everyday activities and building more dynamic sentences. Remember to pay attention to the prepositions that usually go with them!

## Examples

Let's use these new motion verbs in sentences, combining them with grammar you've already mastered!

1.  **"The new teacher (f.) came to the school."**
    *   `جَاءَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Jā'ati al-mudarrisatū al-jadeedatū ilā al-madrasati.)
        *   *Notice:* `جَاءَتْ` (she came) with a `ـتِ` for feminine. `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` is the subject. `إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (to the school).

2.  **"He will return from the city tomorrow."**
    *   `سَيَعُودُ مِنَ الْمَدِينَةِ غَدًا.` (Say'a'ūdu mina al-madeenati ghadan.)
        *   *Notice:* `سَـ` for future tense (Day 22). `يَعُودُ` (he returns). `مِنَ الْمَدِينَةِ` (from the city). `غَدًا` (tomorrow - Day 27).

3.  **"The student wants to enter the library."**
    *   `يُرِيدُ الطَّالِبُ أَنْ يَدْخُلَ الْمَكْتَبَةَ.` (Yureedu aṭ-ṭālibu an yadkhula al-maktabata.)
        *   *New word:* `مَكْتَبَةٌ` (maktabatun) - library (feminine noun).
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ أَنْ` (he wants to - Day 44). `يَدْخُلَ` (he enters) takes Fatha (Subjunctive mood) because of `أَنْ`. `الْمَكْتَبَةَ` (the library) is the direct object.

4.  **"We do not exit from the house today."**
    *   `لَا نَخْرُجُ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ الْيَوْمَ.` (Lā nakhruju mina al-bayti al-yawma.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَا` for present tense negation (Day 21). `نَخْرُجُ` (we exit). `مِنَ الْبَيْتِ` (from the house). `الْيَوْمَ` (today - Day 27).

5.  **"It is necessary to go to the market now."**
    *   `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَذْهَبَ إِلَى السُّوقِ الْآنَ.` (Yajibu an nadhhab ila as-sūqi al-āna.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَجِبُ أَنْ` (it is necessary to - Day 67). `نَذْهَبَ` (we go) takes Fatha (Subjunctive). `إِلَى السُّوقِ` (to the market - Day 51). `الْآنَ` (now - Day 44).

6.  **"The old man returned to his home."**
    *   `عَادَ الرَّجُلُ الْقَدِيمُ إِلَى بَيْتِهِ.` (ʿĀda ar-rajulu al-qadeemu ilā baytihi.)
        *   *Notice:* `عَادَ` (he returned). `الرَّجُلُ الْقَدِيمُ` (the old man). `إِلَى بَيْتِهِ` (to his house - Idafa with pronoun `ـهِ`, Day 7).

7.  **"The minister (f.) said that she wants to return to her country."**
    *   `قَالَتِ الْوَزِيرَةُ أَنَّهَا تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَعُودَ إِلَى بَلَدِهَا.` (Qālati al-wazeeratū annahā tureedu an ta'ūda ilā baladihā.)
        *   *New word:* `بَلَدٌ` (baladun) - country (masculine noun).
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَتِ` (she said - Day 39). `أَنَّهَا` (that she - Day 64). `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَعُودَ` (she wants to return - `تَعُودَ` is Subjunctive). `إِلَى بَلَدِهَا` (to her country - Idafa with pronoun `ـهَا`).
