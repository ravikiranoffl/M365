# Day 61 - Advanced negation

## Lesson

Imagine you want to say "The house is big." In Arabic, that's `الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-baytu kabeerun.). Now, what if you want to say "The house is **not** big."?

While `لَا` can sometimes be used to negate "X is Y" sentences, there is a much more common and proper way to do it using a special word called **`لَيْسَ` (laysa)**.

### 1. Negating "X is Y" Sentences with `لَيْسَ` (laysa) - "is not"

`لَيْسَ` (laysa) is a unique word in Arabic. Think of it as a special "is not" word that helps you negate sentences where you are describing a noun (like "the house is big"). It changes its form to match who or what "is not."

Here are the important rules for using `لَيْسَ`:

*   **Rule 1: `لَيْسَ` always comes first.** It starts the "not" part of your sentence.
*   **Rule 2: The Subject (the "X" part) stays in the Nominative (Damma).** The noun that "is not" something comes right after `لَيْسَ` (or its changed form), and it keeps its regular Damma ending (or sound masculine plural ending, etc.).
*   **Rule 3: The Predicate (the "Y" part) takes a Fatha!** This is the **most important new rule** for today! The word that describes *what the subject is not* (the "Y" part) will now end with a **Fatha** (َ). If it's an indefinite word (without `الـ`), it will take a **Tanween Fatha** (ً).
    *   *Example:* `الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ.` (The house is big.) becomes `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرًا.` (The house is not big.) See how `كَبِيرٌ` changed to `كَبِيرًا`?

Here are some common forms of `لَيْسَ` for different pronouns:

*   **`لَيْسَ` (laysa) - He is not**
    *   Used for masculine singular subjects (like "he," "the boy," "the computer").
    *   *Example:* `لَيْسَ هُوَ طَالِبًا.` (He is not a student.)
*   **`لَيْسَتْ` (laysat) - She is not**
    *   Used for feminine singular subjects (like "she," "the girl," "the school").
    *   *Example:* `لَيْسَتْ هِيَ طَالِبَةً.` (She is not a student.)
*   **`لَسْتُ` (lastu) - I am not**
    *   *Example:* `لَسْتُ مُدَرِّسًا.` (I am not a teacher.)
*   **`لَسْتَ` (lasta) - You (m.s.) are not**
    *   *Example:* `لَسْتَ ذَكِيًّا.` (You (m.s.) are not smart.)
*   **`لَسْتِ` (lasti) - You (f.s.) are not**
    *   *Example:* `لَسْتِ مُتَوَسِّطَةً.` (You (f.s.) are not average.)
*   **`لَسْنَا` (lasnā) - We are not**
    *   *Example:* `لَسْنَا صِغَارًا.` (We are not small.)
*   **`لَيْسُوا` (laysū) - They (m.p.) are not**
    *   *Example:* `لَيْسُوا مُدَرِّسِينَ.` (They are not teachers.) (Remember, for sound masculine plural predicate, it ends in `ـِينَ` in the Fatha/Kasra case.)

*   **Rule 4 (Optional for Emphasis): Add `بِـ` (bi-) - "with" before the predicate.**
    *   Sometimes, to add a bit of emphasis, Arabic speakers will put the preposition `بِـ` (bi-) before the predicate of `لَيْسَ`. When you do this, the predicate will take a **Kasra** (or Tanween Kasra if indefinite) instead of a Fatha.
    *   *Example:* `لَيْسَ الْكِتَابُ مُفِيدًا.` (The book is not useful.) becomes `لَيْسَ الْكِتَابُ بِمُفِيدٍ.` (The book is *not* useful at all.)

### 2. Quick Review: Other Negation Words (`مَا`, `لَا`, `لَمْ`, `لَنْ`)

Let's quickly remember the other important negation words we've learned:

*   **`مَا` (mā) - "did not" (for past actions)**
    *   Used directly before a **past tense verb**.
    *   *Example:* `مَا كَتَبَ.` (Mā kataba.) - He did not write.
*   **`لَا` (lā) - "does not" (for general present actions)**
    *   Used directly before a **present tense verb**. The verb stays in the Indicative mood (Damma ending).
    *   *Example:* `لَا يَكْتُبُ.` (Lā yaktubu.) - He does not write.
*   **`لَنْ` (lan) - "will not" / "never" (for future actions)**
    *   Used before a **present tense verb**. It forces the verb into the **Subjunctive mood** (Fatha ending, or Nūn drops).
    *   *Example:* `لَنْ يَكْتُبَ.` (Lan yaktuba.) - He will not write.
*   **`لَمْ` (lam) - "did not" (for past actions, using present tense verb)**
    *   Used before a **present tense verb**. It forces the verb into the **Jussive mood** (Sukoon ending, or Nūn/weak letter drops). This is for talking about the past.
    *   *Example:* `لَمْ يَكْتُبْ.` (Lam yaktub.) - He did not write.
*   **`لَا` (lā) - "do not" (for prohibition / negative commands)**
    *   Used before a **present tense verb** to tell someone *not* to do something. It forces the verb into the **Jussive mood** (Sukoon ending, or Nūn/weak letter drops).
    *   *Example:* `لَا تَكْتُبْ!` (Lā taktūb!) - Do not write! (to a masculine singular 'you').

Phew! That's a lot of "not"s! But each one has a special job. `لَيْسَ` is your new best friend for saying "X is not Y."

## Examples

Let's see these negation words in action!

1.  **"The house is not big."**
    *   `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرًا.` (Laysa al-baytu kabeeran.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْبَيْتُ` (the house) keeps its Damma. `كَبِيرًا` (big) takes Tanween Fatha.

2.  **"The new school is not small."**
    *   `لَيْسَتِ الْمَدْرَسَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ صَغِيرَةً.` (Laysati al-madrasatu al-jadeedatu ṣagheeratan.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَيْسَتْ` because `الْمَدْرَسَةُ` (the school) is feminine. `الْمَدْرَسَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` (the new school) keeps Damma. `صَغِيرَةً` (small) takes Tanween Fatha and is feminine to match.

3.  **"I am not a smart student."**
    *   `لَسْتُ طَالِبًا ذَكِيًّا.` (Lastu ṭāliban dhakiyyan.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَسْتُ` for "I am not." `طَالِبًا ذَكِيًّا` (a smart student) takes Tanween Fatha for both noun and adjective.

4.  **"They (m.p.) are not in the university."**
    *   `لَيْسُوا فِي الْجَامِعَةِ.` (Laysū fī al-jāmi'ati.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَيْسُوا` for "they (m.p.) are not." The predicate here is a prepositional phrase, so `الْجَامِعَةِ` takes Kasra after `فِي`.

5.  **"The lesson is not useful." (with emphasis)**
    *   `لَيْسَ الدَّرْسُ بِمُفِيدٍ.` (Laysa ad-darsu bi-mufīdin.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِـ` is added before `مُفِيدٍ`, making it take Tanween Kasra.

6.  **"The minister did not understand the politics."** (Past negation with `مَا`)
    *   `مَا فَهِمَ الْوَزِيرُ السِّيَاسَةَ.` (Mā fahima al-wazeeru as-siyāsata.)
        *   *Notice:* `فَهِمَ` is past tense (Day 48), so `مَا` is used.

7.  **"The company does not send messages."** (General present negation with `لَا`)
    *   `لَا تُرْسِلُ الشَّرِكَةُ الرَّسَائِلَ.` (Lā tursilu ash-sharikatū ar-rasā'ila.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرْسِلُ` is present tense (Day 55), `لَا` is used, and `تُرْسِلُ` keeps its Damma. (`الرَّسَائِلَ` is plural of `رِسَالَةٌ`, meaning messages, takes Fatha as object).

8.  **"The president will not come to the meeting."** (Future negation with `لَنْ`)
    *   `لَنْ يَأْتِيَ الرَّئِيسُ إِلَى الِاجْتِمَاعِ.` (Lan ya'tiya ar-ra'eecu ilā al-ijtimā'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَأْتِي` (he comes - Day 56) becomes `يَأْتِيَ` (Subjunctive with Fatha) because of `لَنْ`.

9.  **"The judge did not solve the problem."** (Past negation with `لَمْ`)
    *   `لَمْ يَحُلَّ الْقَاضِي الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Lam yaḥulla al-qāḍī al-mushkilata.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَحُلُّ` (he solves - Day 50) becomes `يَحُلَّ` (Jussive with Fatha because it's a geminated verb, acting like it dropped a Sukoon on the last letter which is doubled). This is a tricky specific case for geminated verbs, the key is it's *not* Damma.

10. **"Do not use the phone!"** (Prohibition with `لَا`)
    *   `لَا تَسْتَخْدِمِ الْهَاتِفَ!` (Lā tastakhdimi al-hātifa!)
        *   *Notice:* `تَسْتَخْدِمُ` (you use - Day 55) becomes `تَسْتَخْدِمِ` (Jussive with Sukoon, changed to Kasra due to `الـ` after it).
