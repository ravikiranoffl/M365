# Day 118 - Editing skills in Arabic Part 1

## Lesson

"Editing skills" in Arabic, especially at your stage, means carefully checking your sentences for common grammatical errors. It's about becoming your own grammar detective! Even native speakers proofread their writing, so this is a lifelong skill.

Today, we will focus on three major areas where mistakes often happen:

### 1. Noun Case Endings (الإِعْرَابُ - Al-Iʿrābu)

This is perhaps the most frequent area of error. Arabic nouns change their last vowel (their case ending) depending on their role in the sentence. Forgetting or mixing these up can change the meaning or make the sentence grammatically incorrect.

*   **Nominative (مَرْفُوعٌ - Marfūʿun):** The subject of a verbal sentence or the subject/predicate of a nominal sentence. Ends in `ـُ` or `ـٌ` (or `ـَا` for sound masculine plurals like `ـُونَ`, or `ـَاتُ` for sound feminine plurals).
    *   *Example:* `الْقَاضِي كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-qāḍī kabeerun.) - The judge is big. (`الْقَاضِي` is Nominative).
*   **Accusative (مَنْصُوبٌ - Manṣūbun):** The direct object of a verb, the subject of `أَنَّ` or `إِنَّ`, or the predicate of `أَصْبَحَ` or `جَعَلَ`. Ends in `ـَ` or `ـًـَ` (or `ـِينَ` for sound masculine plurals, or `ـَاتِ` for sound feminine plurals).
    *   *Example:* `رَأَيْتُ الْقَاضِيَ.` (Ra'aytu al-qāḍiya.) - I saw the judge. (`الْقَاضِيَ` is Accusative).
    *   *Example:* `أَنَّ الْقَاضِيَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Anna al-qāḍiya muhimun.) - That the judge is important. (`الْقَاضِيَ` is Accusative as subject of `أَنَّ`).
*   **Genitive (مَجْرُورٌ - Majrūrun):** After a preposition, or as the second term in an Idafa construction. Ends in `ـِ` or `ـٍ` (or `ـِينَ` for sound masculine plurals, or `ـَاتِ` for sound feminine plurals).
    *   *Example:* `تَكَلَّمْتُ عَنِ الْقَاضِي.` (Takallamtu 'ani al-qāḍī.) - I spoke about the judge. (`الْقَاضِي` is Genitive).
    *   *Example:* `بَيْتُ الْقَاضِي.` (Baytu al-qāḍī.) - The judge's house. (`الْقَاضِي` is Genitive).

### 2. Adjective Agreement

Adjectives must match the noun they describe in four ways: **Gender** (masculine/feminine), **Number** (singular/dual/plural), **Definiteness** (definite/indefinite), and **Case** (Nominative/Accusative/Genitive).

*   **Common Error:** Forgetting that **non-human plural nouns (like `الْبَيَانَاتُ` - data, `الْأَرْقَامُ` - numbers, `الْعَلَاقَاتُ` - relations) take a feminine singular adjective.**
    *   *Incorrect Example:* `الْعَلَاقَاتُ كَبِيرُونَ.` (Al-'alāqātu kabeeroona.) - (Relations are big - trying to use masculine plural adjective).
    *   *Correct Example:* `الْعَلَاقَاتُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-'alāqātu kabeeratun.) - The relations are big. (Feminine singular adjective for a non-human plural noun).

### 3. Verb-Subject Agreement

Verbs must agree with their subject in gender and number.

*   **Singular Subject:** The verb comes *before* the subject and is singular (masculine or feminine).
    *   *Example:* `يَذْهَبُ الرَّجُلُ.` (Yadhhabu ar-rajulu.) - The man goes.
    *   *Example:* `تَذْهَبُ الْمَرْأَةُ.` (Tadhhabu al-mar'atu.) - The woman goes.
*   **Plural Subject (Humans):** If the verb comes *before* the human plural subject, it's still singular. If it comes *after* the subject, it becomes plural.
    *   *Example:* `يَذْهَبُ الرِّجَالُ.` (Yadhhabu ar-rijālu.) - The men go.
    *   *Example:* `الرِّجَالُ يَذْهَبُونَ.` (Ar-rijālu yadhhaboona.) - The men go.
*   **Plural Subject (Non-Humans):** If the verb comes *before* a non-human plural subject, it is always **feminine singular**. If it comes *after*, it is also **feminine singular**. This is like the adjective rule!
    *   *Example:* `تُظْهِرُ الْبَيَانَاتُ.` (Tuẓhiru al-bayānātu.) - The data shows. (`تُظْهِرُ` is feminine singular).
    *   *Example:* `الْبَيَانَاتُ تُظْهِرُ.` (Al-bayānātu tuẓhiru.) - The data shows.

Being mindful of these three areas will significantly improve your writing accuracy. Let's practice finding and fixing these kinds of errors!

## Examples

Below are sentences with common errors. We'll identify the mistake and then show the correct version with an explanation.

1.  **Original (Incorrect):** `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةُ تُرِيدُ النَّجَاحَ.`
    *   **Error:** Case ending for `الْحُكُومَةُ`. It is the subject of `أَنَّ`.
    *   **Corrected:** `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ تُرِيدُ النَّجَاحَ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-ḥukūmata tureedu an-najāḥa.)
    *   **Explanation:** The word `الْحُكُومَةَ` must be in the Accusative case (with a Fatha) because it is the subject of `أَنَّ` (Day 39).

2.  **Original (Incorrect):** `الْأَرْقَامُ جَدِيدُونَ.`
    *   **Error:** Adjective agreement for `جَدِيدُونَ`. `الْأَرْقَامُ` (numbers) is a non-human plural.
    *   **Corrected:** `الْأَرْقَامُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-arqāmu jadeedatun.)
    *   **Explanation:** `الْأَرْقَامُ` (numbers) is a non-human plural noun, so its adjective `جَدِيدَةٌ` must be feminine singular (Day 27).

3.  **Original (Incorrect):** `يَعْمَلُ الْمُوَظَّفِينَ فِي الشَّرِكَةِ.`
    *   **Error:** Case ending for `الْمُوَظَّفِينَ`. It is the subject of the verb `يَعْمَلُ`.
    *   **Corrected:** `يَعْمَلُ الْمُوَظَّفُونَ فِي الشَّرِكَةِ.` (Yaʿmalu al-muwaẓẓafoona fī ash-sharikatī.)
    *   **Explanation:** `الْمُوَظَّفُونَ` (employees) is the subject of the verb `يَعْمَلُ`, so it must be in the Nominative case, which for sound masculine plurals is `ـُونَ` (Day 36).

4.  **Original (Incorrect):** `قَدَّمَتْ الْمُوَظَّفُ الطَّلَبَ.`
    *   **Error:** Verb-subject agreement. The verb `قَدَّمَتْ` is feminine, but `الْمُوَظَّفُ` (employee) is masculine.
    *   **Corrected:** `قَدَّمَ الْمُوَظَّفُ الطَّلَبَ.` (Qaddama al-muwaẓẓafu aṭ-ṭalaba.)
    *   **Explanation:** The verb `قَدَّمَ` (he submitted) must agree with its masculine singular subject `الْمُوَظَّفُ` (the employee) (Day 59).

5.  **Original (Incorrect):** `لِي خِبْرَةٌ في هَذَا الْمَجَالُ.`
    *   **Error:** Case ending for `الْمَجَالُ`. It follows the preposition `فِي`.
    *   **Corrected:** `لِي خِبْرَةٌ فِي هَذَا الْمَجَالِ.` (Lī khibratun fī hādhā al-majāli.)
    *   **Explanation:** The word `الْمَجَالِ` (field) must be in the Genitive case (with a Kasra) because it follows the preposition `فِي` (Day 17).

6.  **Original (Incorrect):** `أَصْبَحَتْ الْعَلَاقَاتُ مُهِمٌّ.`
    *   **Error:** Adjective agreement for `مُهِمٌّ`. `الْعَلَاقَاتُ` is a non-human plural subject, requiring a feminine singular predicate. Also, the predicate of `أَصْبَحَ` should be Accusative.
    *   **Corrected:** `أَصْبَحَتِ الْعَلَاقَاتُ مُهِمَّةً.` (Aṣbaḥati al-'alāqātu muhimmatan.)
    *   **Explanation:** `الْعَلَاقَاتُ` (relations) is a non-human plural, so its predicate `مُهِمَّةً` must be feminine singular. Also, `مُهِمَّةً` is the predicate of `أَصْبَحَتْ` (became), so it must be in the Accusative case (Day 98).

**Answer Key for Practice:**

1.  **Original (Incorrect):** `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحُلُّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.`
    *   **Error:** The verb `تَحُلُّ` should be in the Subjunctive mood after `أَنْ`.
    *   **Corrected:** `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحُلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Tureedu al-ḥukūmatu an taḥulla al-mushkilata.)

2.  **Original (Incorrect):** `الشَّرِكَةُ تَقْبَلُ طَلَبٌ الْوَظِيفَةِ.`
    *   **Error:** `طَلَبٌ` is the direct object of `تَقْبَلُ` and should be Accusative.
    *   **Corrected:** `الشَّرِكَةُ تَقْبَلُ طَلَبَ الْوَظِيفَةِ.` (Ash-sharikatū taqbalu ṭalaba al-waẓīfati.)

3.  **Original (Incorrect):** `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ نَقْصٌ كَبِيرٌ.`
    *   **Error:** `نَقْصٌ` is the direct object of `تُظْهِرُ` and should be Accusative. `كَبِيرٌ` as its adjective should also be Accusative.
    *   **Corrected:** `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ نَقْصًا كَبِيرًا.` (Al-iḥṣā'ātū tuẓhiru naqṣan kabeeran.)

4.  **Original (Incorrect):** `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُقَابَلَةَ صَعْبَةٌ، الْمُوَظَّفُونَ سَيَنْجَحُونَ.`
    *   **Error:** The comma (`,`) indicates a new clause. `الْمُوَظَّفُونَ` is the subject of the new verbal sentence. The verb comes *before* the subject when the subject is explicit and plural.
    *   **Corrected:** `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُقَابَلَةَ صَعْبَةٌ، سَيَنْجَحُ الْمُوَظَّفُونَ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-muqābalata ṣa'batun, sayanjahu al-muwaẓẓafoona.)
        *   *(Alternatively, if the verb must come after the subject, the sentence structure needs to be `الْمُوَظَّفُونَ سَيَنْجَحُونَ` as in the original, but the teacher's intention here might be to enforce the VSO order for singular verb with plural subject)*. Given the lesson on verb-subject agreement, `سَيَنْجَحُ الْمُوَظَّفُونَ` is the more consistent application for a verb preceding a plural human subject.

5.  **Original (Incorrect):** `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يُقَدِّمُ الْمُحَامِيَةُ خِبْرَةً جَيِّدَةً.`
    *   **Error:** `يُقَدِّمُ` (he submits) is masculine, but its subject `الْمُحَامِيَةُ` (the lawyer - f.) is feminine.
    *   **Corrected:** `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، تُقَدِّمُ الْمُحَامِيَةُ خِبْرَةً جَيِّدَةً.` (Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, tuqaddimu al-muḥāmiyatū khibratan jayyidatan.)

6.  **Original (Incorrect):** `أَصْبَحَتْ الْمُتَطَلَّبَاتُ سَهْلٌ.`
    *   **Error:** `الْمُتَطَلَّبَاتُ` (requirements) is a non-human plural, so its predicate `سَهْلٌ` should be feminine singular (`سَهْلَةٌ`). Also, the predicate of `أَصْبَحَتْ` should be Accusative (`سَهْلَةً`).
    *   **Corrected:** `أَصْبَحَتِ الْمُتَطَلَّبَاتُ سَهْلَةً.` (Aṣbaḥati al-mutaṭallabātū sahlatan.)

7.  **Original (Incorrect):** `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرُ سَيُعْلِنُ عَنِ التَّقْرِيرَ.`
    *   **Error:** `الْوَزِيرُ` is the subject of `أَنَّ`, so it should be Accusative. `التَّقْرِيرَ` follows `عَنْ` and should be Genitive.
    *   **Corrected:** `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ سَيُعْلِنُ عَنِ التَّقْرِيرِ.` (Lā shakka anna al-wazeera sayu'linu 'ani at-taqrīri.)

8.  **Original (Incorrect):** `لَهَا مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ جَيِّدُونَ.`
    *   **Error:** `مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ` (qualifications) is a non-human plural, so its adjective `جَيِّدُونَ` should be feminine singular.
    *   **Corrected:** `لَهَا مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ جَيِّدَةٌ.` (Lahā mu'ahhilātun jayyidatun.)

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   إِعْرَابٌ (iʿrābun) - noun case endings, inflection (masculine noun)
*   مَرْفُوعٌ (marfūʿun) - Nominative case (masculine adjective)
*   مَنْصُوبٌ (manṣūbun) - Accusative case (masculine adjective)
*   مَجْرُورٌ (majrūrun) - Genitive case (masculine adjective)
*   تَحُلَّ (taḥulla) - she solves (present tense verb, subjunctive)
*   طَلَبَ (ṭalaba) - request, application (masculine noun) - *re-emphasized in this context for case endings*
*   نَقْصًا (naqṣan) - decrease, lack (masculine Masdar/noun, Accusative)
*   كَبِيرًا (kabeeran) - big, large (masculine adjective, Accusative)
*   سَيَنْجَحُ (sayanjahu) - he will succeed (present tense verb)
*   تُقَدِّمُ (tuqaddimu) - she submits, presents, offers (present tense verb)
*   سَهْلَةً (sahlatun) - easy (feminine adjective, Accusative)
*   الْوَزِيرَ (al-wazeera) - the minister (masculine noun, Accusative)
*   التَّقْرِيرِ (at-taqrīri) - the report (masculine noun, Genitive)
*   جَيِّدَةٌ (jayyidatun) - good (feminine adjective)
