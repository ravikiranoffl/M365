# Day 90 - Intensive listening week start

## Lesson

Today, our goal is to practice comprehensive understanding by "listening" (reading and comprehending) a short news summary. Imagine you are hearing this report on the radio or watching it on TV. Your task is to:

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly and carefully**, trying to understand the overall meaning without immediately looking at the English translation.
2.  **Identify familiar vocabulary and grammatical structures.** Can you spot the motion verbs, the hedging, the cause/effect connectors?
3.  **After your attempt, read the English translation.**
4.  **Then, go through the detailed breakdown** to see how all the pieces fit together.

Don't worry if you don't catch every single detail on your first read. The goal is to improve your recognition and comprehension over time. This is what "intensive listening" is all about!

## Examples

Here is a short news summary. Read it multiple times, as if you're listening, and try to grasp its meaning.

**Arabic Text:**

`أَخْبَارٌ مُهِمَّةٌ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ.`

`يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ الْقَارِئَ عُمَرَ جَاءَ إِلَى الْقَاهِرَةِ أَمْسِ بِسَبَبِ مُشْكِلَةٍ مَفْهُومَةٍ. هُوَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ هَدَفَهُ الرَّئِيسِيَّ أَنْ يَدْعُوَ إِلَى عَمَلٍ مُشْتَرَكٍ.`

`قَالَ الْوَزِيرُ عُمَرُ: "يَجِبُ أَنْ نَسْأَلَ الْجَمِيعَ حَتَّى نَفْهَمَ الْحَلَّ." وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ، يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْخُطَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مَحْلُولَةٌ. رُبَّمَا يَكُونُ الْعَمَلُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ مُهِمًّا جِدًّا لِلْبَلَدِ.`

---

*(Take your time to read and understand the Arabic. When you are ready, continue below for the translation and explanation.)*

---

**English Translation:**

**Important News from Cairo.**

It seems that the reading Minister Omar came to Cairo yesterday because of an understood problem. He thinks that his main goal is to call for joint work.

Minister Omar said: "It is necessary that we ask everyone so that we understand the solution." Although the problem is big, it seems that the new plan is solved. Perhaps the work in Cairo will be very important for the country.

---

**Detailed Breakdown and Explanation:**

1.  **`أَخْبَارٌ مُهِمَّةٌ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ.`**
    *   **Translation:** Important News from Cairo.
    *   **Explanation:** This is a headline (Day 87). `أَخْبَارٌ` (news) is the topic, `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important - feminine singular, agreeing with `أَخْبَارٌ` as non-human plural) is the comment. `مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ` (from Cairo) is a prepositional phrase, with `الْقَاهِرَةِ` (Cairo - Day 87) taking Kasra.

2.  **`يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ الْقَارِئَ عُمَرَ جَاءَ إِلَى الْقَاهِرَةِ أَمْسِ بِسَبَبِ مُشْكِلَةٍ مَفْهُومَةٍ.`**
    *   **Translation:** It seems that the reading Minister Omar came to Cairo yesterday because of an understood problem.
    *   **Explanation:**
        *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that - Hedging Language, Day 80) introduces an embedded clause.
        *   `الْوَزِيرَ الْقَارِئَ عُمَرَ` (the reading Minister Omar) is an example of **Apposition** (Day 85). `الْوَزِيرَ` is the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause, so it takes Fatha. `الْقَارِئَ` (reading - Active Participle, Day 88) is an adjective for `الْوَزِيرَ`, taking Fatha and agreeing. `عُمَرَ` (Omar - a diptote proper noun, Day 85) is the appositive, also taking Fatha.
        *   `جَاءَ إِلَى الْقَاهِرَةِ أَمْسِ` (he came to Cairo yesterday - Motion Verb `جَاءَ`, Day 78; `إِلَى` preposition; `الْقَاهِرَةِ` takes Kasra; `أَمْسِ` adverb of time).
        *   `بِسَبَبِ مُشْكِلَةٍ مَفْهُومَةٍ` (because of an understood problem - Cause/Effect Connector `بِسَبَبِ`, Day 86). `مُشْكِلَةٍ` (problem) takes Kasra after `بِسَبَبِ`. `مَفْهُومَةٍ` (understood - Passive Participle, Day 88) is an adjective agreeing with `مُشْكِلَةٍ` in feminine gender and Kasra case.

3.  **`هُوَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ هَدَفَهُ الرَّئِيسِيَّ أَنْ يَدْعُوَ إِلَى عَمَلٍ مُشْتَرَكٍ.`**
    *   **Translation:** He thinks that his main goal is to call for joint work.
    *   **Explanation:**
        *   `هُوَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ` (he thinks that - Thinking Verb `يَظُنُّ`, Day 79; `أَنَّ` for indirect quotation, Day 84).
        *   `هَدَفَهُ الرَّئِيسِيَّ` (his main goal). `هَدَفَ` (goal - Day 82) is the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause, taking Fatha, with attached pronoun `ـهُ`. `الرَّئِيسِيَّ` (main - Day 83) is an adjective agreeing with `هَدَفَهُ` (implied definite, Fatha).
        *   `أَنْ يَدْعُوَ إِلَى عَمَلٍ مُشْتَرَكٍ` (to call for joint work). `أَنْ` (to - Day 60) makes `يَدْعُوَ` (he calls - Subjunctive, Speech Act, Day 64) take Fatha. `عَمَلٍ مُشْتَرَكٍ` (joint work - `عَمَلٌ` takes Kasra after `إِلَى`, `مُشْتَرَكٍ` is adjective agreeing, Day 67).

4.  **`قَالَ الْوَزِيرُ عُمَرُ: "يَجِبُ أَنْ نَسْأَلَ الْجَمِيعَ حَتَّى نَفْهَمَ الْحَلَّ."`**
    *   **Translation:** Minister Omar said: "It is necessary that we ask everyone so that we understand the solution."
    *   **Explanation:**
        *   `قَالَ الْوَزِيرُ عُمَرُ` (Minister Omar said - Direct Quotation, Day 84; Apposition, Day 85). `الْوَزِيرُ` and `عُمَرُ` are in Nominative case.
        *   `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَسْأَلَ الْجَمِيعَ` (it is necessary that we ask everyone - Diplomatic Phrasing `يَجِبُ أَنْ`, Day 67; Thinking/Speaking Verb `نَسْأَلَ` (we ask - Subjunctive, Day 79); `الْجَمِيعَ` (everyone) is the direct object).
        *   `حَتَّى نَفْهَمَ الْحَلَّ` (so that we understand the solution - Cause/Effect Connector `حَتَّى`, Day 86, making `نَفْهَمَ` (we understand - Subjunctive, Day 45) take Fatha). `الْحَلَّ` (the solution) is the direct object.

5.  **`وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ، يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْخُطَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مَحْلُولَةٌ.`**
    *   **Translation:** Although the problem is big, it seems that the new plan is solved.
    *   **Explanation:**
        *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ` (although the problem is big - Contrast Structure `وَمَعَ أَنَّ`, Day 81). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` is subject of `أَنَّ`, takes Fatha. `كَبِيرَةٌ` is its comment.
        *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْخُطَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مَحْلُولَةٌ` (it seems that the new plan is solved - Hedging Language `يَبْدُو أَنَّ`, Day 80). `الْخُطَّةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (the new plan) takes Fatha (subject of `أَنَّ`). `مَحْلُولَةٌ` (solved - Passive Participle, Day 88) is the comment, feminine agreeing with `الْخُطَّةَ`.

6.  **`رُبَّمَا يَكُونُ الْعَمَلُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ مُهِمًّا جِدًّا لِلْبَلَدِ.`**
    *   **Translation:** Perhaps the work in Cairo will be very important for the country.
    *   **Explanation:**
        *   `رُبَّمَا` (perhaps - Hedging Language, Day 80).
        *   `يَكُونُ الْعَمَلُ فِي الْقَاهِرَةِ مُهِمًّا` (the work in Cairo will be important - `كَانَ` in present tense, Day 40). `الْعَمَلُ` is the subject of `يَكُونُ`, taking Damma. `مُهِمًّا` (important) is the predicate of `يَكُونُ`, taking Fatha.
        *   `جِدًّا` (very - Day 27). `لِلْبَلَدِ` (for the country - `لِـ` preposition, `بَلَدٌ` country, Day 78).

Excellent work following along with that complex passage! You can see how all the pieces of grammar and vocabulary you've learned fit together in a cohesive text. This is what truly understanding Arabic looks like!
