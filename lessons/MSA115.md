# Day 115 - Data description techniques

## Lesson

"Data description techniques" involves learning the specific Arabic words and phrases used to talk about facts and figures. Imagine you're reading a news report about the economy, or a government announcement about changes. These words will help you understand and convey quantitative information.

Let's learn some new and essential vocabulary for describing data!

### 1. `بَيَانَاتٌ` (bayānātun) - Data

This is the general term for facts and statistics collected together for reference or analysis. It is a feminine plural noun.

*   **New Word:** `بَيَانَاتٌ` (bayānātun) - 'ba-yā-nā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Data (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْبَيَانَاتُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-bayānātu jadeedatun.) - The data is new. (Remember non-human plurals take feminine singular adjectives, Day 27).

### 2. `رَقْمٌ` (raqmun) / `أَرْقَامٌ` (arqāmun) - Number / Numbers

`رَقْمٌ` is the singular for a number, and `أَرْقَامٌ` is its plural.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `رَقْمٌ` (raqmun) - 'raq-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Number (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `أَرْقَامٌ` (arqāmun) - 'ar-qā-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Numbers (masculine plural, broken plural, takes feminine singular adjective for its adjectives).
    *   *Example:* `الْأَرْقَامُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-arqāmu muhimmatun.) - The numbers are important.

### 3. `إِحْصَاءٌ` (iḥṣā'un) / `إِحْصَاءَاتٌ` (iḥṣā'ātun) - Statistic / Statistics

`إِحْصَاءٌ` refers to a single statistic or enumeration, while `إِحْصَاءَاتٌ` is its plural.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `إِحْصَاءٌ` (iḥṣā'un) - 'iḥ-ṣā-un'
    *   *Meaning:* Statistic (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `إِحْصَاءَاتٌ` (iḥṣā'ātun) - 'iḥ-ṣā-ā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Statistics (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-iḥṣā'ātu jadeedatun.) - The statistics are new.

### 4. `نِسْبَةٌ مِئَوِيَّةٌ` (nisbatun mi'awiyyatun) - Percentage

This is a specific phrase for "percentage." `نِسْبَةٌ` means "ratio" or "proportion," and `مِئَوِيَّةٌ` means "percentile" or "per hundred," functioning as an adjective for `نِسْبَةٌ`.

*   **New Phrase:** `نِسْبَةٌ مِئَوِيَّةٌ` (nisbatun mi'awiyyatun) - 'nis-ba-tun mi'-a-wee-yya-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Percentage (feminine noun + feminine adjective).
    *   *Example:* `النِّسْبَةُ الْمِئَوِيَّةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (An-nisbatū al-mi'awiyyatū kabeeratun.) - The percentage is big.

### 5. `زِيَادَةٌ` (ziyādatun) - Increase (noun) / `يَزْدَادُ` (yazdādu) - He increases (verb)

These words describe an upward trend or growth. `زِيَادَةٌ` is a Masdar (verbal noun). `يَزْدَادُ` is an intransitive verb (meaning "to increase" by itself, without an object).

*   **New Word (Noun):** `زِيَادَةٌ` (ziyādatun) - 'zee-yā-da-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Increase (feminine Masdar/noun).
*   **New Word (Verb):** `يَزْدَادُ` (yazdādu) - 'yaz-dā-du' (masculine) / `تَزْدَادُ` (tazdādu) - 'taz-dā-du' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she increases / is increasing (present tense verb).
    *   *Example (Noun):* `هُنَاكَ زِيَادَةٌ فِي الِاسْتِثْمَارِ.` (Hunāka ziyādatun fī al-istihtmāri.) - There is an increase in investment. (`هُنَاكَ` - there is/are, Day 69).
    *   *Example (Verb):* `الِاسْتِثْمَارُ يَزْدَادُ.` (Al-istihtmāru yazdādu.) - Investment is increasing.

### 6. `نَقْصٌ` (naqṣun) - Decrease (noun) / `يَنْقُصُ` (yanquṣu) - He decreases (verb)

These words describe a downward trend or reduction. `نَقْصٌ` is a Masdar (verbal noun). `يَنْقُصُ` is an intransitive verb.

*   **New Word (Noun):** `نَقْصٌ` (naqṣun) - 'naq-ṣun'
    *   *Meaning:* Decrease, lack (masculine Masdar/noun).
*   **New Word (Verb):** `يَنْقُصُ` (yanquṣu) - 'yan-qu-ṣu' (masculine) / `تَنْقُصُ` (tanquṣu) - 'tan-qu-ṣu' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she decreases / is decreasing (present tense verb).
    *   *Example (Noun):* `هُنَاكَ نَقْصٌ فِي الْمَالِ.` (Hunāka naqṣun fī al-māli.) - There is a decrease in money.
    *   *Example (Verb):* `الْمَالُ يَنْقُصُ.` (Al-mālu yanquṣu.) - The money is decreasing.

### 7. `تَقْرِيرٌ` (taqrīrun) - Report

This noun refers to a detailed account or statement, often official.

*   **New Word:** `تَقْرِيرٌ` (taqrīrun) - 'taq-ree-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Report (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْحُكُومَةُ تُرِيدُ تَقْرِيرًا جَدِيدًا.` (Al-ḥukūmatu tureedu taqrīran jadeedan.) - The government wants a new report.

### 8. `تَحْلِيلٌ` (taḥleelun) - Analysis / `يُحَلِّلُ` (yuḥallilu) - He analyzes (verb)

`تَحْلِيلٌ` is the Masdar for "analysis." `يُحَلِّلُ` is the present tense verb form (from Form II in morphology, which we'll cover later, but you can learn the verb directly now).

*   **New Word (Noun):** `تَحْلِيلٌ` (taḥleelun) - 'taḥ-lee-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Analysis (masculine Masdar/noun).
*   **New Word (Verb):** `يُحَلِّلُ` (yuḥallilu) - 'yu-ḥal-li-lu' (masculine) / `تُحَلِّلُ` (tuḥallilu) - 'tu-ḥal-li-lu' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she analyzes / is analyzing (present tense verb).
    *   *Example (Noun):* `تَحْلِيلُ الْبَيَانَاتِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Taḥleelu al-bayānāti muhimmun.) - Data analysis is important.
    *   *Example (Verb):* `الْوَزِيرُ يُحَلِّلُ الْأَرْقَامَ.` (Al-wazeeru yuḥallilu al-arqāma.) - The minister analyzes the numbers.

### 9. `يُظْهِرُ` (yuẓhiru) - He shows / reveals

This verb is very useful for introducing data or findings from a report.

*   **New Word (Verb):** `يُظْهِرُ` (yuẓhiru) - 'yuẓ-hi-ru' (masculine) / `تُظْهِرُ` (tuẓhiru) - 'tuẓ-hi-ru' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she shows, reveals (present tense verb).
    *   *Example:* `الْبَيَانَاتُ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً.` (Al-bayānātu tuẓhiru ziyādatan kabeeratan.) - The data shows a big increase.

These terms are crucial for discussing information, trends, and reports in Arabic.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new data description words into sentences, using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the new data shows an increase in investment."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْبَيَانَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةَ تُظْهِرُ زِيَادَةً فِي الِاسْتِثْمَارِ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-bayānāti al-jadeedata tuẓhiru ziyādatan fī al-istihtmāri.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الْبَيَانَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, feminine plural and adjective, Day 115). `تُظْهِرُ` (new verb, Day 115). `زِيَادَةً` (new Masdar, Accusative object, Day 115). `فِي الِاسْتِثْمَارِ` (Day 103).

2.  **"The report analyzes the numbers. Moreover, the statistics show a big decrease."**
    *   `التَّقْرِيرُ يُحَلِّلُ الْأَرْقَامَ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ نَقْصًا كَبِيرًا.` (At-taqrīru yuḥallilu al-arqāma. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, al-iḥṣā'ātu tuẓhiru naqṣan kabeeran.)
        *   *Notice:* `التَّقْرِيرُ يُحَلِّلُ الْأَرْقَامَ` (`التَّقْرِيرُ` new, `يُحَلِّلُ` new, `الْأَرْقَامَ` new, Day 115). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ تُظْهِرُ نَقْصًا كَبِيرًا` (`الْإِحْصَاءَاتُ` new, `نَقْصًا` new, Day 115).

3.  **"Despite the fact that the percentage is small, the trade will increase."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ صَغِيرَةٌ، سَتَزْدَادُ التِّجَارَةُ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna an-nisbata al-mi'awiyyata ṣagheeratun, satazdādu at-tijāratu.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Day 107). `النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ صَغِيرَةٌ` (`النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ` new, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 115). `سَتَزْدَادُ التِّجَارَةُ` (`تَزْدَادُ` new, future tense `سَـ`, Day 115; `التِّجَارَةُ` Day 103).

4.  **"In light of that, the government wants to make the decrease in violence clear for everyone."**
    *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ نَقْصَ الْعُنْفِ وَاضِحًا لِلْجَمِيعِ.` (Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, tureedu al-ḥukūmatu an taj'ala naqṣa al-'unfi wāḍiḥan li-l-jamee'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (Day 108). `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ` (Day 98, 101). `نَقْصَ الْعُنْفِ` (Idafa, `نَقْصَ` new, Accusative first object of `تَجْعَلَ`, Day 115; `الْعُنْفِ` Day 105). `وَاضِحًا لِلْجَمِيعِ` (Day 98).

5.  **"It seems that the lawyer (f.) believes that the statistics are not enough for a complete analysis."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْمُحَامِيَةَ تَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْإِحْصَاءَاتِ لَيْسَتْ كَافِيَةً لِتَحْلِيلٍ كَامِلٍ.` (Yabdū anna al-muḥāmiyata ta'taqidu anna al-iḥṣā'āti laysat kāfiyatan li-taḥleelin kāmilin.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (Day 80). `الْمُحَامِيَةَ تَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (Day 102, 79). `الْإِحْصَاءَاتِ` (new, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 115). `لَيْسَتْ كَافِيَةً` (is not enough, feminine verb for `إِحْصَاءَاتِ` (non-human plural), `كَافِيَةً` Accusative predicate, Day 108, 113). `لِتَحْلِيلٍ كَامِلٍ` (`تَحْلِيلٍ` new Masdar, Genitive after `لِـ`, `كَامِلٍ` Day 113, Day 115).

These examples show how you can combine new vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences about data.
