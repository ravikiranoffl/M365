# Day 91 - Intensive listening to Arabic news (Transcription Day 1)

## Lesson

Transcription is the process of writing down what you hear. For language learners, this is an incredibly powerful exercise. It forces you to:
*   Pay close attention to every sound.
*   Recognize individual words in a stream of speech.
*   Identify grammatical endings (like Damma, Fatha, Kasra) which are often subtle but crucial.
*   Connect sounds to the Arabic letters and words you know.

Today, we'll start with short sentences, typical of news headlines or brief statements. Imagine you're hearing these spoken clearly. Your task will be to "listen" (read slowly and carefully), and then try to "transcribe" (write down) the words you identify.

**How to approach transcription:**

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly, multiple times.** Imagine it being spoken.
2.  **Try to identify each word.** Break the sentence into smaller chunks if necessary.
3.  **Pay attention to case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) and Sukoon.** These are often the hardest to catch but are essential for correct grammar.
4.  **Write down what you hear.** Don't worry about perfection on the first try. It's a learning process!
5.  **Then, compare your transcription with the correct version.**

This exercise is not about perfect spelling from memory (we'll provide the correct text), but about training your ear to segment speech and recognize the forms you've been learning. Since we're reading text, we're effectively simulating the "listening" part by internalizing the pronunciation as you read.

Let's begin with an example!

## Examples

Here is a short news-style sentence. Read it, imagine hearing it, and try to identify each word and its ending.

**Arabic Text:**

`الرَّئِيسُ فِي الْمَكْتَبِ الْآنَ.`

*(Take your time to read and identify the words and their endings. When you are ready, continue below for the breakdown.)*

---

**Breakdown and Explanation:**

The sentence is: `الرَّئِيسُ فِي الْمَكْتَبِ الْآنَ.`

Let's break it down word by word, identifying each one and its grammatical features:

1.  **`الرَّئِيسُ` (ar-ra'eecu):**
    *   **Word:** `رَئِيسٌ` (president).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Nominative (Damma - `ـُ`) because it's the subject/topic of the sentence.
    *   **Meaning:** The president.

2.  **`فِي` (fī):**
    *   **Word:** `فِي` (in).
    *   **Type:** Preposition.
    *   **Meaning:** in.

3.  **`الْمَكْتَبِ` (al-maktabi):**
    *   **Word:** `مَكْتَبٌ` (office).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Genitive (Kasra - `ـِ`) because it follows the preposition `فِي`.
    *   **Meaning:** the office.

4.  **`الْآنَ` (al-āna):**
    *   **Word:** `الْآنَ` (now).
    *   **Type:** Adverb of time.
    *   **Case:** Always takes Fatha (`ـَ`).
    *   **Meaning:** now.

**Full Sentence Meaning:** The president is in the office now.
*   *Grammar Connection:* This is a nominal sentence. In news headlines or short statements, `كَانَ` (was/is) is often omitted (Day 87), and it's understood that the president *is* in the office.

## Practice

For each practice item below, read the Arabic sentence multiple times, imagine hearing it, then try to identify each word and its exact case ending. Write it down, and then check your answer!

1.  **Arabic Text:** `عَادَتِ الْوَزِيرَةُ مِنْ مِصْرَ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `عَادَتِ` (ʿādatī): `عَادَ` (returned) + `ـتْ` (she, feminine marker) + Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 78).
        *   `الْوَزِيرَةُ` (al-wazeeratū): `وَزِيرَةٌ` (minister - f.), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject.
        *   `مِنْ` (min): Preposition (from).
        *   `مِصْرَ` (Miṣra): `مِصْرٌ` (Egypt), takes Fatha as a diptote, in Genitive case after `مِنْ` (Day 87).
        *   **Translation:** The minister (f.) returned from Egypt.

2.  **Arabic Text:** `إِعْلَانُ الْخُطَّةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `إِعْلَانُ` (i'lānū): Masdar (`إِعْلَانٌ` - announcement), Nominative (Damma) as the first term of an Idafa (Day 87).
        *   `الْخُطَّةِ` (al-khuṭṭatī): `خُطَّةٌ` (plan), definite, Genitive (Kasra) as the second term of the Idafa (Day 87).
        *   `الْجَدِيدَةِ` (al-jadeedatī): `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new), definite, Genitive (Kasra), adjective agreeing with `الْخُطَّةِ` (Day 27).
        *   **Translation:** Announcement of the new plan. (Headline style, Day 87).

3.  **Arabic Text:** `يَسْأَلُ الطَّالِبُ الْمُدَرِّسَ عَنِ الدَّرْسِ.`
    *   *(Read and transcribe here)*
    *   ---
    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `يَسْأَلُ` (yas'alū): `يَسْأَلُ` (he asks), present tense verb (Day 79).
        *   `الطَّالِبُ` (aṭ-ṭālibū): `طَالِبٌ` (student), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject.
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسَ` (al-mudarrisā): `مُدَرِّسٌ` (teacher), definite, Accusative (Fatha) as the direct object (Day 79).
        *   `عَنِ` (ʿanī): `عَنْ` (about), preposition, takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons (Day 79).
        *   `الدَّرْسِ` (ad-darsi): `دَرْسٌ` (lesson), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `عَنِ`.
        *   **Translation:** The student asks the teacher about the lesson.
