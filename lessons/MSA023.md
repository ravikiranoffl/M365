# Day 23 - Plurals Part 1: Regular Masculine and Feminine Plurals

## Lesson

1.  **What are Plurals?**
    *   A **singular** noun is for one item (like "a book").
    *   A **plural** noun is for more than one item (like "books").
    *   In English, we usually add "-s" or "-es" (e.g., "book" -> "books," "car" -> "cars").
    *   Arabic has a few ways to make plurals. Today, we'll learn the two "regular" ways, which are often called **Sound Plurals** because their singular form stays mostly "sound" (unchanged) when you make them plural, you just add an ending.

2.  **The Sound Masculine Plural (الْجَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرُ السَّالِمُ)**
    *   This plural is used for **masculine nouns that refer to people or rational beings**.
    *   To form it, we take the singular masculine noun and add a special ending: **`ـُونَ` (ūna)**.
    *   This `ـُونَ` ending is used when the noun is in the **nominative case** (meaning it's the subject of a sentence or just a standalone noun).
    *   **Rule:** Take the singular masculine noun, keep its ending vowel as a Damma, then add `ـُونَ`.
    *   **Example 1: Teacher (masculine)**
        *   Singular: `مُدَرِّسٌ` (mudarrisun) - a teacher
        *   Plural: `مُدَرِّسُونَ` (mudarrisūna) - teachers
            *   *Notice*: The `ـسُ` of `مُدَرِّسُ` combines with `ـونَ` to make `مُدَرِّسُونَ`.
    *   **Example 2: Student (masculine)**
        *   Singular: `طَالِبٌ` (taalibun) - a student
        *   Plural: `طَالِبُونَ` (taalibūna) - students
            *   *Notice*: The `ـبُ` of `طَالِبُ` combines with `ـونَ` to make `طَالِبُونَ`.

3.  **The Sound Feminine Plural (الْجَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثُ السَّالِمُ)**
    *   This plural is used for **feminine nouns** (which usually end with `تَاء مَرْبُوطَة ة` in their singular form).
    *   To form it, we do two things:
        1.  We **drop** the `تَاء مَرْبُوطَة (ة)` from the end of the singular noun.
        2.  We **add** a special ending: **`ـَاتٌ` (ātun)**.
    *   This `ـَاتٌ` ending is used when the noun is in the **nominative case** (meaning it's the subject of a sentence or just a standalone noun).
    *   **Rule:** Drop `ة`, then add `ـَاتٌ`.
    *   **Example 1: Teacher (feminine)**
        *   Singular: `مُدَرِّسَةٌ` (mudarrisatun) - a teacher
        *   Plural: `مُدَرِّسَاتٌ` (mudarrisātun) - teachers
            *   *Notice*: `ةٌ` is dropped, `ـَاتٌ` is added.
    *   **Example 2: Student (feminine)**
        *   Singular: `طَالِبَةٌ` (taalibatun) - a student
        *   Plural: `طَالِبَاتٌ` (taalibātun) - students
            *   *Notice*: `ةٌ` is dropped, `ـَاتٌ` is added.
    *   **Example 3: Car**
        *   Singular: `سَيَّارَةٌ` (sayyaaratun) - a car
        *   Plural: `سَيَّارَاتٌ` (sayyaaraatun) - cars
    *   **Example 4: School**
        *   Singular: `مَدْرَسَةٌ` (madrasatun) - a school
        *   Plural: `مَدْرَسَاتٌ` (madrasaatun) - schools

4.  **Definiteness with Plurals**
    *   The rules for definiteness (using `الْـ` for "the" or `التَّنْوِينُ` for "a/an") apply to plural nouns just as they do to singular nouns.
    *   **Indefinite Plural:**
        *   `مُدَرِّسُونَ` (teachers)
        *   `مُدَرِّسَاتٌ` (teachers)
    *   **Definite Plural:** Add `الْـ` to the beginning, and remove the Tanween for feminine plurals, or keep the `ـُونَ` for masculine plurals.
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسُونَ` (al-mudarrisūna) - the teachers (masculine)
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسَاتُ` (al-mudarrisātu) - the teachers (feminine)
            *   *Notice*: `الْـ` makes the `ـَاتٌ` change to `ـَاتُ`.
        *   `السَّيَّارَاتُ` (as-sayyaaraatu) - the cars (Remember Sun Letters for س!)

5.  **Adjectives with Plural Nouns**
    *   Remember our "Four Agreements" for adjectives from Day 11? They still apply! An adjective describing a plural noun must **also be plural** and match in gender, definiteness, and case.
    *   **Masculine Plural Adjectives:** Take the `ـُونَ` ending.
        *   Singular: `جَدِيدٌ` (jadeedun) - new (m)
        *   Plural: `جَدِيدُونَ` (jadeedūna) - new (m.pl)
    *   **Feminine Plural Adjectives:** Take the `ـَاتٌ` ending.
        *   Singular: `جَدِيدَةٌ` (jadeedatun) - new (f)
        *   Plural: `جَدِيدَاتٌ` (jadeedātun) - new (f.pl)

This is a powerful step! You can now talk about groups of people and things in Arabic!

## Examples

Let's put these new plural forms into practice!

**1. Singular to Plural Conversion:**
*   Singular: `طَالِبٌ` (a student - m)
    *   Plural: `طَالِبُونَ` (students - m)
*   Singular: `مَدْرَسَةٌ` (a school - f)
    *   Plural: `مَدْرَسَاتٌ` (schools - f)
*   Singular: `غُرْفَةٌ` (a room - f)
    *   Plural: `غُرْفَاتٌ` (rooms - f)

**2. Indefinite Plural Nouns with Adjectives:**
*   **New teachers (masculine).**
    *   `مُدَرِّسُونَ جَدِيدُونَ.` (mudarrisūna jadeedūna.)
        *   *Notice*: Both noun and adjective are indefinite masculine plural, with `ـُونَ`.
*   **Big cars.**
    *   `سَيَّارَاتٌ كَبِيرَاتٌ.` (sayyaaraatun kabeerātun.)
        *   *Notice*: Both noun and adjective are indefinite feminine plural, with `ـَاتٌ`.

**3. Definite Plural Nouns with Adjectives:**
*   **The new students (masculine).**
    *   `الطَّالِبُونَ الْجَدِيدُونَ.` (at-taalibūna al-jadeedūna.)
        *   *Notice*: Both noun and adjective are definite masculine plural, with `الْـ` and `ـُونَ`.
*   **The small schools.**
    *   `الْمَدْرَسَاتُ الصَّغِيرَاتُ.` (al-madrasātu as-sagheerātu.)
        *   *Notice*: Both noun and adjective are definite feminine plural, with `الْـ` and `ـَاتُ`.

**4. Plurals in Nominal Sentences:**
*   **They (masculine) are new teachers.**
    *   `هُمْ مُدَرِّسُونَ جَدِيدُونَ.` (Hum mudarrisūna jadeedūna.)
        *   *Notice*: `هُمْ` is the subject. `مُدَرِّسُونَ جَدِيدُونَ` is the indefinite plural predicate.
*   **These are beautiful cars.**
    *   `هَذِهِ سَيَّارَاتٌ جَمِيلَاتٌ.` (Hādhihi sayyaaraatun jameelātun.)
        *   *Note*: For non-human plural nouns, `هَذِهِ` (this/these - feminine singular) is often used for "these." We'll talk more about this later. For now, just learn this as a fixed phrase. `سَيَّارَاتٌ جَمِيلَاتٌ` is the indefinite plural predicate.
*   **The students (feminine) are in the new rooms.**
    *   `الطَّالِبَاتُ فِي الْغُرْفَاتِ الْجَدِيدَاتِ.` (At-taalibātu fī al-ghurfaati al-jadeedāti.)
        *   *Notice*: `الطَّالِبَاتُ` is the definite feminine plural subject. `فِي` is the preposition, causing `الْغُرْفَاتِ الْجَدِيدَاتِ` to end in Kasra (since `ـَاتٌ` changes to `ـَاتِ` after a preposition or when definite).

You can now form plural nouns and describe them with adjectives! This adds so much more detail to your Arabic sentences.
