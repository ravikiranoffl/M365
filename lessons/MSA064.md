# Day 64 - Speech acts in formal Arabic

## Lesson

In every language, we use words not just to describe things, but to *perform actions*. When you say "I promise," you are performing the act of promising. When you say "I declare you husband and wife," you are performing the act of marrying. In formal Arabic, there are specific verbs and phrases that clearly signal these "speech acts."

We'll focus on a few very common formal phrases today:

### 1. Affirming or Emphasizing (`يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ` - he affirms that...)

When you want to state something strongly or confirm its truth, you use phrases that mean "he affirms" or "he stresses."

*   **New Verb: "To Affirm / To Emphasize"**
    *   **Root:** `أ ك د` (a-k-d)
    *   **Present Tense (he affirms):** `يُؤَكِّدُ` (yu'akkidu) - 'yu-ak-ki-du'.
    *   **Present Tense (she affirms / you-m.s. affirm):** `تُؤَكِّدُ` (tu'akkidu) - 'tu-ak-ki-du'.
    *   **Present Tense (I affirm):** `أُؤَكِّدُ` (u'akkidu) - 'u-ak-ki-du'.
    *   **Present Tense (we affirm):** `نُؤَكِّدُ` (nu'akkidu) - 'nu-ak-ki-du'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `تَأْكِيدٌ` (ta'keedun) - affirmation, emphasis (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb is almost always followed by `أَنَّ` (anna - that), which you know from Reported Speech (Day 39). Remember, `أَنَّ` makes the noun after it take a Fatha.
    *   *Example:* `هُوَ يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ السَّلَامَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Huwa yu'akkidu anna as-salāma muhimun.) - He affirms that peace is important.

### 2. Announcing or Declaring (`يُعْلِنُ أَنَّ` - he announces that...)

When you want to formally make something known, you use a verb meaning "to announce."

*   **New Verb: "To Announce / To Declare"**
    *   **Root:** `ع ل ن` (a-l-n)
    *   **Present Tense (he announces):** `يُعْلِنُ` (yu'linu) - 'yu'-li-nu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she announces / you-m.s. announce):** `تُعْلِنُ` (tu'linu) - 'tu'-li-nu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I announce):** `أُعْلِنُ` (u'linu) - 'u'-li-nu'.
    *   **Present Tense (we announce):** `نُعْلِنُ` (nu'linu) - 'nu'-li-nu'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `إِعْلَانٌ` (i'lānun) - announcement (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** Also typically followed by `أَنَّ`.
    *   *Example:* `الْوَزِيرُ يُعْلِنُ أَنَّ اتِّفَاقًا جَدِيدًا مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-wazeeru yu'linu anna ittifāqan jadeedan muhimun.) - The minister announces that a new agreement is important. (Note: `اتِّفَاقًا جَدِيدًا` are indefinite, so they take Tanween Fatha).

### 3. Calling for or Advocating (`يَدْعُو إِلَى` - he calls for...)

When a person or organization formally urges action or supports a cause, they "call for" it. This verb is often used in political and diplomatic contexts.

*   **New Verb: "To Call for / To Invite to"**
    *   **Root:** `د ع و` (d-'-w) - This is a weak verb.
    *   **Present Tense (he calls for):** `يَدْعُو` (yad'ū) - 'yad-u'. (Notice the silent Alif that carries the Damma).
    *   **Present Tense (she calls for / you-m.s. call for):** `تَدْعُو` (tad'ū) - 'tad-u'.
    *   **Present Tense (I call for):** `أَدْعُو` (ad'ū) - 'ad-u'.
    *   **Present Tense (we call for):** `نَدْعُو` (nad'ū) - 'nad-u'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `دَعْوَةٌ` (da'watun) - call, invitation (feminine noun).
    *   **How to use:** This verb is always followed by the preposition `إِلَى` (ilā - to, towards) to mean "calls for" or "invites to." Remember `إِلَى` makes the next noun take a Kasra.
    *   *Example:* `الرَّئِيسُ يَدْعُو إِلَى السَّلَامِ.` (Ar-ra'eecu yad'ū ilā as-salāmi.) - The president calls for peace.

### 4. Expressing Belief (`يَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` - he believes that...)

To express a firm opinion or conviction, you use the verb "to believe."

*   **New Verb: "To Believe"**
    *   **Root:** `ع ق د` (a-q-d)
    *   **Present Tense (he believes):** `يَعْتَقِدُ` (ya'taqidu) - 'ya'-ta-qi-du'.
    *   **Present Tense (she believes / you-m.s. believe):** `تَعْتَقِدُ` (ta'taqidu) - 'ta'-ta-qi-du'.
    *   **Present Tense (I believe):** `أَعْتَقِدُ` (a'taqidu) - 'a'-ta-qi-du'.
    *   **Present Tense (we believe):** `نَعْتَقِدُ` (na'taqidu) - 'na'-ta-qi-du'.
    *   **Verbal Noun (Masdar):** `اعْتِقَادٌ` (i'tiqādun) - belief (masculine noun).
    *   **How to use:** Typically followed by `أَنَّ`.
    *   *Example:* `نَحْنُ نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْعَدْلَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Naḥnu na'taqidu anna al-'adla muhimun.) - We believe that justice is important.

### 5. Stating Common Knowledge (`مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` - it is known that...)

This is a very formal way to introduce a fact that is widely accepted or generally understood.

*   **Phrase:** `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (mina al-ma'rūfi anna) - 'mi-na al-ma'-roo-fi an-na'.
    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `مِنَ` (mina): "From" (Day 10). Here it links to the idea of origin.
        *   `الْمَعْرُوفِ` (al-ma'rūfi): "The known (thing)." This is a passive participle (Day 42) acting as a noun, taking Kasra because of `مِنَ`.
        *   `أَنَّ` (anna): "That" (Day 39).
    *   **How to use:** This phrase simply starts a sentence to introduce a known fact. It is always followed by `أَنَّ`, making the subject of the embedded clause take a Fatha.
    *   *Example:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الصِّحَّةَ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Mina al-ma'rūfi anna aṣ-ṣiḥḥata muhimmatun.) - It is known that health is important.

These phrases are like special tools in your Arabic toolbox for making very clear, formal statements and requests. They are essential for reading and understanding sophisticated Arabic.

## Examples

Let's combine these new speech act phrases with the grammar and vocabulary you've already learned!

1.  **"The president affirms that the new agreement is important."**
    *   `الرَّئِيسُ يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ الْجَدِيدَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Ar-ra'eecu yu'akkidu anna al-ittifāqa al-jadeeda muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُؤَكِّدُ` is 'he affirms'. `أَنَّ` makes `الِاتِّفَاقَ الْجَدِيدَ` (the new agreement) take Fatha.

2.  **"The company announces that it will protect the environment."**
    *   `الشَّرِكَةُ تُعْلِنُ أَنَّهَا سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Ash-sharikatū tu'linu annahā sa-taḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُعْلِنُ` is 'she announces'. `أَنَّهَا` (annahā) is `أَنَّ` + `هَا` (it/her, referring to the company). `سَتَحْفَظُ` (she will protect - Day 56, future tense Day 22).

3.  **"The minister calls for understanding the law."**
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يَدْعُو إِلَى فَهْمِ الْقَانُونِ.` (Al-wazeeru yad'ū ilā fahmi al-qānūni.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَدْعُو إِلَى` is 'he calls for'. `فَهْمِ الْقَانُونِ` (understanding of the law) is an Idafa (Day 52, 51) taking Kasra after `إِلَى`.

4.  **"We believe that the student will succeed in the exam."**
    *   `نَحْنُ نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ سَيَنْجَحُ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Naḥnu na'taqidu anna aṭ-ṭāliba sa-yanjaḥu fī al-imtiḥāni.)
        *   *Notice:* `نَعْتَقِدُ` is 'we believe'. `أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ` (that the student) makes `الطَّالِبَ` take Fatha. `سَيَنْجَحُ` (he will succeed - Day 58, future tense).

5.  **"It is known that pollution is a big problem."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ التَّلَوُّثَ مُشْكِلَةٌ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Mina al-ma'rūfi anna at-talawwutha mushkilatun kabeeratun.)
        *   *Notice:* `التَّلَوُّثَ` (the pollution) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`. `مُشْكِلَةٌ كَبِيرَةٌ` (a big problem) is its comment, feminine singular.

6.  **"The judge affirms that justice is important for the people."**
    *   `الْقَاضِي يُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ الْعَدْلَ مُهِمٌّ لِلشَّعْبِ.` (Al-qāḍī yu'akkidu anna al-'adla muhimun li-sh-sha'bi.)
        *   `يُؤَكِّدُ` (he affirms). `الْعَدْلَ` (justice - Day 51) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`.

7.  **"They (m.p.) announce that they will not fail."**
    *   `هُمْ يُعْلِنُونَ أَنَّهُمْ لَنْ يَرْسُبُوا.` (Hum yu'linoona annahum lan yarsuboo.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُعْلِنُونَ` is the plural form of `يُعْلِنُ`. `أَنَّهُمْ` is `أَنَّ` + `هُمْ` (they). `لَنْ يَرْسُبُوا` uses `لَنْ` (will not - Day 60) which makes the verb `يَرْسُبُونَ` (they fail - Day 58) drop its `ن` and become `يَرْسُبُوا`.

8.  **"I call for protecting my health."**
    *   `أَنَا أَدْعُو إِلَى حِفْظِ صِحَّتِي.` (Ana ad'ū ilā ḥifẓi ṣiḥḥatī.)
        *   *New Masdar:* `حِفْظٌ` (ḥifẓun) - protection (masculine Masdar for `حَفِظَ` - to protect, Day 56).
        *   *Notice:* `أَدْعُو` (I call). `حِفْظِ صِحَّتِي` (protection of my health) is a chained Idafa acting as the object of `إِلَى`, so `حِفْظِ` takes Kasra.
