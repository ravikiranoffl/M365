# Day 76 - Error correction practice

## Lesson

Today, I will provide you with several Arabic sentences that contain a mistake. Your task is to:
1.  **Read the sentence** carefully.
2.  **Identify the error**.
3.  **Provide the correct version** of the sentence.
4.  **Explain why it was wrong** and what rule applies.

We will focus on common mistakes related to the grammar points we've covered recently, especially case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra), verb moods (Subjunctive, Jussive), and agreement rules.

Let's start!

## Examples

Let's try a few examples together first, so you can see how it works.

**Example 1: Identifying a `لَيْسَ` (laysa) error**

*   **Incorrect Arabic:** `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Laysa al-baytu kabeerun.)
*   **Question:** What is the error in this sentence, and how should it be corrected?

*(Pause for your answer)*

---

**Corrected Arabic:** `لَيْسَ الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرًا.` (Laysa al-baytu kabeeran.)
*   **Explanation:** The error was in the case ending of `كَبِيرٌ`. When `لَيْسَ` negates an "X is Y" sentence, the predicate (the `Y` part, here `كَبِيرٌ`) **must take a Fatha** (َ). This rule was taught on Day 61.

**Example 2: Identifying an `أَنَّ` (anna) error**

*   **Incorrect Arabic:** `أَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبُ نَاجِحٌ.` (A'lamu anna aṭ-ṭālibu nājiḥun.)
*   **Question:** What is the error in this sentence, and how should it be corrected?

*(Pause for your answer)*

---

**Corrected Arabic:** `أَعْلَمُ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ نَاجِحٌ.` (A'lamu anna aṭ-ṭāliba nājiḥun.)
*   **Explanation:** The error was in the case ending of `الطَّالِبُ`. When `أَنَّ` (that) introduces a nominal clause, the subject of that clause (here `الطَّالِبُ`) **must take a Fatha** (َ). This rule was taught on Day 39 and reinforced on Day 62 and 68.

**Example 3: Identifying a Subjunctive Mood error**

*   **Incorrect Arabic:** `تُرِيدُ هِيَ أَنْ تَكْتُبُ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Tureedu hiya an taktubu ar-risālata.)
*   **Question:** What is the error in this sentence, and how should it be corrected?

*(Pause for your answer)*

---

**Corrected Arabic:** `تُرِيدُ هِيَ أَنْ تَكْتُبَ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Tureedu hiya an taktaba ar-risālata.)
*   **Explanation:** The error was in the verb ending of `تَكْتُبُ`. When `أَنْ` (to/that) precedes a present tense verb, it forces the verb into the **Subjunctive mood**, meaning it must end with a **Fatha** (َ). This rule was taught on Day 60.

## Practice

For each practice item, identify the error, correct the sentence, and explain the rule.

1.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `الْكِتَابُ غَيْرُ مُفِيدٌ.` (Al-kitābu ghayru mufīdun.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `الْكِتَابُ غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ.` (Al-kitābu ghayru mufīdin.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Tanween Damma on `مُفِيدٌ`. `غَيْرُ` (non-, un-) usually acts as the first part of an Idafa construction. Therefore, the word it negates (`مُفِيدٌ`) must take a **Kasra** (ِ) ending. This rule was taught on Day 73.

2.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `لَمْ يَكْتُبُ الطَّالِبُ.` (Lam yaktubu aṭ-ṭālibu.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `لَمْ يَكْتُبِ الطَّالِبُ.` (Lam yaktubi aṭ-ṭālibu.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Damma on `يَكْتُبُ`. When `لَمْ` (did not) precedes a present tense verb, it forces the verb into the **Jussive mood**, meaning it must end with a **Sukoon** (ْ). In this case, the Sukoon on `يَكْتُبْ` changes to a Kasra (`يَكْتُبِ`) because it is followed by `الـ` (al-). This rule was taught on Day 60.

3.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `بِخُصُوصِ السَّلَامُ، نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ.` (Bi-khuṣūṣi as-salāmu, na'mulu an na'mala.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `بِخُصُوصِ السَّلَامِ، نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ.` (Bi-khuṣūṣi as-salāmi, na'mulu an na'mala.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Damma on `السَّلَامُ`. The phrase `بِخُصُوصِ` (regarding/concerning) acts like a preposition, so the noun following it (`السَّلَامِ`) must take a **Kasra** (ِ). This rule was taught on Day 67.

4.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `فُهِمَ الدَّرْسَ.` (Fuhima ad-darsa.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `فُهِمَ الدَّرْسُ.` (Fuhima ad-darsu.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Fatha on `الدَّرْسَ`. In a passive sentence, the new subject (`نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`, here `الدَّرْسُ`) replaces the original subject and takes a **Damma** (ُ). This rule was taught on Day 72.

5.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `يَشْرَحُ الرَّئِيسُ أَنَّ السِّيَاسَةَ جَدِيدَةٌ مُشْتَرَكٌ.` (Yashraḥu ar-ra'eecu anna as-siyāsata jadeedatun mushtarakun.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `يَشْرَحُ الرَّئِيسُ أَنَّ السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مُشْتَرَكَةٌ.` (Yashraḥu ar-ra'eecu anna as-siyāsata al-jadeedata mushtarakatun.)
    *   **Explanation:** There were two errors here:
        1.  `جَدِيدَةٌ` should be definite and match the case of `السِّيَاسَةَ` if it's an adjective for it. Since `السِّيَاسَةَ` is definite with Fatha, its adjective should be `الْجَدِيدَةَ`. (Day 12)
        2.  `مُشْتَرَكٌ` is masculine, but it is the comment for `السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (the new policy), which is feminine. Therefore, the adjective must agree in gender and be `مُشْتَرَكَةٌ`. This rule was taught on Day 12 and reinforced with `مُشْتَرَكٌ` on Day 67.

6.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `لَنْ تَحُلُّ الْمُشْكِلَةُ.` (Lan taḥullu al-mushkilatu.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `لَنْ تُحَلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةُ.` (Lan tuḥalla al-mushkilatu.)
    *   **Explanation:** There were two errors:
        1.  The verb `تَحُلُّ` (she solves) should be passive because `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` (it is the one being solved). The passive present form is `تُحَلُّ`. (Day 72)
        2.  `لَنْ` (will not) makes the verb Subjunctive. For a geminated verb like `تُحَلُّ`, the Subjunctive form is `تُحَلَّ` (taking a Fatha). This rule was taught on Day 60.

7.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Yajibu an naḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Yajibu an naḥfaẓa al-bī'ata.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Damma on `نَحْفَظُ`. When `أَنْ` (to/that) precedes a present tense verb (as it does after `يَجِبُ`), it forces the verb into the **Subjunctive mood**, meaning it must end with a **Fatha** (َ). This rule was taught on Day 60 and applied with `يَجِبُ أَنْ` on Day 67.

8.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `الْيَوْمُ تُرْسِلُ الشَّرِكَةُ رِسَالَةً.` (Al-yawmu tursilu ash-sharikatū risālatan.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `الْيَوْمَ تُرْسِلُ الشَّرِكَةُ رِسَالَةً.` (Al-yawma tursilu ash-sharikatū risālatan.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Damma on `الْيَوْمُ`. When an adverb of time (like `الْيَوْمَ` - today) is used in a sentence, it typically takes a **Fatha** (َ). When placed at the beginning for emphasis (as intended here, Day 74), it retains its adverbial Fatha.

9.  **Incorrect Arabic:** `نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْعَمَلُ الْمُشْتَرَكُ مُفِيدٌ.` (Na'taqidu anna al-'amalu al-mushtaraka mufīdun.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `نَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ الْعَمَلَ الْمُشْتَرَكَ مُفِيدٌ.` (Na'taqidu anna al-'amala al-mushtaraka mufīdun.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Damma on `الْعَمَلُ`. As the subject of an `أَنَّ` clause, `الْعَمَلُ` must take a **Fatha** (َ). Its adjective `الْمُشْتَرَكُ` must also take Fatha to agree in case, so `الْمُشْتَرَكَ`. This rule was taught on Day 39 and reinforced on Day 62 and 68.

10. **Incorrect Arabic:** `قَالَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ أَنَّهَا تُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا تَرْسُبُ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Qālati aṭ-ṭālibatū annahā tureedu an lā tarsubu fī al-imtiḥāni.)
    *   **Your Answer & Explanation:**

    ---

    **Corrected Arabic:** `قَالَتِ الطَّالِبَةُ أَنَّهَا تُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا تَرْسُبَ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Qālati aṭ-ṭālibatū annahā tureedu an lā tarsuba fī al-imtiḥāni.)
    *   **Explanation:** The error was the Damma on `تَرْسُبُ`. Even when `لَا` (not) is used to negate an action within an `أَنْ` clause, the verb still remains in the **Subjunctive mood** because of `أَنْ`, meaning it must end with a **Fatha** (َ). This rule was taught on Day 73.
