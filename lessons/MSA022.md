## Lesson

1.  **What is the Future Tense?**
    *   The Future Tense describes actions that **will happen** at some point in the future.
    *   In English, we often use "will" (e.g., "He will write") or "is going to" (e.g., "He is going to write").
    *   In Arabic, we have two main ways to show the future tense, and both are added to the **present tense verb forms** you just learned!

2.  **The Two Ways to Form the Future Tense**
    *   Arabic uses two small words to turn a present tense verb into a future tense verb:
        *   **سَـ (sa-)**: This is a **prefix** (it sticks to the beginning of the verb).
        *   **سَوْفَ (sawfa)**: This is a **separate word** that comes before the verb.
    *   The good news is that when you add `سَـ` or `سَوْفَ`, the **present tense verb itself does NOT change its form or its endings!** You just attach or place the future marker.

3.  **Using `سَـ` (sa-) - The Near Future Prefix**
    *   **Meaning:** "will" (often implies a relatively near future, but can be general).
    *   **How to use it:** Attach `سَـ` directly to the beginning of the present tense verb.
    *   **Structure:** `سَـ` + [Present Tense Verb (conjugated as usual)]
    *   **Examples with `يَكْتُبُ` (he writes/is writing) and `تَدْرُسِينَ` (you-f study/are studying):**
        *   `يَكْتُبُ` (yaktubu) - He writes.
        *   `سَيَكْتُبُ` (sayaktubu) - **He will write.**
            *   *Notice*: The `سَـ` is simply added to `يَكْتُبُ`.
        *   `تَدْرُسِينَ` (tadrusīna) - You (f) study.
        *   `سَتَدْرُسِينَ` (satadrusīna) - **You (f) will study.**
            *   *Notice*: The `سَـ` is simply added to `تَدْرُسِينَ`.

4.  **Using `سَوْفَ` (sawfa) - The Distant Future Word**
    *   **Meaning:** "will" (often implies a more distant future, but can also be general).
    *   **How to use it:** Place `سَوْفَ` as a separate word before the present tense verb.
    *   **Structure:** `سَوْفَ` + [Present Tense Verb (conjugated as usual)]
    *   **Examples with `نَكْتُبُ` (we write/are writing) and `أَدْرُسُ` (I study/am studying):**
        *   `نَكْتُبُ` (naktubu) - We write.
        *   `سَوْفَ نَكْتُبُ` (sawfa naktubu) - **We will write.**
            *   *Notice*: `سَوْفَ` is a separate word.
        *   `أَدْرُسُ` (adrusu) - I study.
        *   `سَوْفَ أَدْرُسُ` (sawfa adrusu) - **I will study.**
            *   *Notice*: `سَوْفَ` is a separate word.

5.  **`سَـ` vs. `سَوْفَ`**
    *   While `سَـ` often implies a "near future" and `سَوْفَ` a "distant future," for beginners, you can generally use them interchangeably without issues. Both clearly mark the future tense. Just remember one is a prefix (`سَـ`) and one is a separate word (`سَوْفَ`).

You've just learned two incredibly simple ways to make any present tense action into a future tense action! This is a powerful new tool in your Arabic toolkit.

## Examples

Let's put these new future tense markers into practice with different pronouns and verbs.

**Using `سَـ` (sa-):**

1.  **He will write.**
    *   `هُوَ سَيَكْتُبُ.` (Huwa sayaktubu.)
2.  **She will study.**
    *   `هِيَ سَتَدْرُسُ.` (Hiya satadrusu.)
3.  **They (masculine) will write.**
    *   `هُمْ سَيَكْتُبُونَ.` (Hum sayaktubūna.)
4.  **I will study the book.**
    *   `أَنَا سَأَدْرُسُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Ana sa'adrusu al-kitaaba.)
5.  **You (masculine) will write your name.**
    *   `أَنْتَ سَتَكْتُبُ اِسْمَكَ.` (Anta sataktubu ismaka.)

**Using `سَوْفَ` (sawfa):**

1.  **He will write.**
    *   `هُوَ سَوْفَ يَكْتُبُ.` (Huwa sawfa yaktubu.)
2.  **She will study.**
    *   `هِيَ سَوْفَ تَدْرُسُ.` (Hiya sawfa tadrusu.)
3.  **They (masculine) will write.**
    *   `هُمْ سَوْفَ يَكْتُبُونَ.` (Hum sawfa yaktubūna.)
4.  **I will study the book.**
    *   `أَنَا سَوْفَ أَدْرُسُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Ana sawfa adrusu al-kitaaba.)
5.  **You (feminine) will write my name.**
    *   `أَنْتِ سَوْفَ تَكْتُبِينَ اِسْمِي.` (Anti sawfa taktubīna ismī.)
6.  **We will study in the new school.**
    *   `نَحْنُ سَوْفَ نَدْرُسُ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Nahnu sawfa nadrusu fī al-madrasati al-jadeedati.)

You can now express actions in the future! This opens up a whole new world of conversation possibilities.
