Today is another very important day! Now that you know how to say what *did* happen or what *is* happening, we're going to learn how to say what **did NOT happen** or what **is NOT happening**. This is called **negation**, and we'll learn how to negate both the past and present tense verbs!

## Lesson

1.  **Negating the Past Tense (What did NOT happen)**
    *   To say that an action **did not happen** in the past, Arabic uses a special word: **مَا (mā)**.
    *   You place `مَا` **before** the conjugated past tense verb.
    *   The verb itself **does not change its form** when you add `مَا` to negate it.
    *   **Structure:** `مَا` + [Past Tense Verb (conjugated as usual)]
    *   **Example: "He wrote" vs. "He did not write"**
        *   `هُوَ كَتَبَ.` (Huwa kataba.) - He wrote.
        *   `هُوَ مَا كَتَبَ.` (Huwa mā kataba.) - He did not write.
    *   **Example: "She studied" vs. "She did not study"**
        *   `هِيَ دَرَسَتْ.` (Hiya darasat.) - She studied.
        *   `هِيَ مَا دَرَسَتْ.` (Hiya mā darasat.) - She did not study.
    *   **Example: "I wrote" vs. "I did not write"**
        *   `أَنَا كَتَبْتُ.` (Ana katabtu.) - I wrote.
        *   `أَنَا مَا كَتَبْتُ.` (Ana mā katabtu.) - I did not write.

2.  **Negating the Present Tense (What is NOT happening / What does NOT happen)**
    *   To say that an action **is not happening** now, or **does not happen** regularly, Arabic uses a different special word: **لَا (lā)**.
    *   You place `لَا` **before** the conjugated present tense verb.
    *   Just like with the past tense, the present tense verb itself **does not change its form** when you add `لَا` to negate it for a beginner.
    *   **Structure:** `لَا` + [Present Tense Verb (conjugated as usual)]
    *   **Example: "He writes" vs. "He does not write"**
        *   `هُوَ يَكْتُبُ.` (Huwa yaktubu.) - He writes / He is writing.
        *   `هُوَ لَا يَكْتُبُ.` (Huwa lā yaktubu.) - He does not write / He is not writing.
    *   **Example: "She studies" vs. "She does not study"**
        *   `هِيَ تَدْرُسُ.` (Hiya tadrusu.) - She studies / She is studying.
        *   `هِيَ لَا تَدْرُسُ.` (Hiya lā tadrusu.) - She does not study / She is not studying.
    *   **Example: "I write" vs. "I do not write"**
        *   `أَنَا أَكْتُبُ.` (Ana aktubu.) - I write / I am writing.
        *   `أَنَا لَا أَكْتُبُ.` (Ana lā aktubu.) - I do not write / I am not writing.

You've just learned two incredibly useful words that let you express the opposite of an action! Remember `مَا` for past tense and `لَا` for present tense.

## Examples

Let's put these new negation words into practice with sentences using the verbs and pronouns you've learned.

**Negating Past Tense with مَا:**

1.  **They did not write.** (`هُمْ كَتَبُوا.` -> `هُمْ مَا كَتَبُوا.`)
    *   `هُمْ مَا كَتَبُوا.` (Hum mā katabū.)
2.  **You (masculine) did not study the book.** (`أَنْتَ دَرَسْتَ الْكِتَابَ.` -> `أَنْتَ مَا دَرَسْتَ الْكِتَابَ.`)
    *   `أَنْتَ مَا دَرَسْتَ الْكِتَابَ.` (Anta mā darasta al-kitaaba.)
3.  **We did not write our names.** (`نَحْنُ كَتَبْنَا أَسْمَاءَنَا.` (assuming `أَسْمَاءَنَا` is plural for names, or `اِسْمَنَا` for our name singular, let's use `اِسْمَنَا`) `نَحْنُ كَتَبْنَا اِسْمَنَا.` -> `نَحْنُ مَا كَتَبْنَا اِسْمَنَا.`)
    *   `نَحْنُ مَا كَتَبْنَا اِسْمَنَا.` (Nahnu mā katabnā ismanā.)
        *   *(Note: `اِسْمَنَا` (our name) is `اِسْمٌ` + `ـنَا`. For simplicity, let's stick to verbs only and use `اِسْمِي` (my name) or `اِسْمُكَ` (your name) as objects for writing, or just the verb itself.)*
    *   Let's rephrase: **We did not write.**
        *   `نَحْنُ مَا كَتَبْنَا.` (Nahnu mā katabnā.)
4.  **The teacher (feminine) did not study in the school.** (`الْمُدَرِّسَةُ دَرَسَتْ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` -> `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ مَا دَرَسَتْ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.`)
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ مَا دَرَسَتْ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Al-mudarrisatu mā darasat fī al-madrasati.)

**Negating Present Tense with لَا:**

1.  **They do not study.** (`هُمْ يَدْرُسُونَ.` -> `هُمْ لَا يَدْرُسُونَ.`)
    *   `هُمْ لَا يَدْرُسُونَ.` (Hum lā yadrusūna.)
2.  **You (feminine) do not write in the book.** (`أَنْتِ تَكْتُبِينَ فِي الْكِتَابِ.` -> `أَنْتِ لَا تَكْتُبِينَ فِي الْكِتَابِ.`)
    *   `أَنْتِ لَا تَكْتُبِينَ فِي الْكِتَابِ.` (Anti lā taktubīna fī al-kitaabi.)
3.  **I do not study the new car.** (`أَنَا أَدْرُسُ السَّيَّارَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ.` -> `أَنَا لَا أَدْرُسُ السَّيَّارَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ.`)
    *   `أَنَا لَا أَدْرُسُ السَّيَّارَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ.` (Ana lā adrusu as-sayyaarata al-jadeedata.)
4.  **He does not write his name.** (`هُوَ يَكْتُبُ اِسْمَهُ.` -> `هُوَ لَا يَكْتُبُ اِسْمَهُ.`)
    *   `هُوَ لَا يَكْتُبُ اِسْمَهُ.` (Huwa lā yaktubu ismahu.)
        *   *(Note: `اِسْمَهُ` (his name) is `اِسْمٌ` + `ـهُ`.)*

You can now express both affirmative and negative actions in both the past and present tenses! This is a massive step towards versatile communication in Arabic.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   مَا (mā) - not (used for past tense negation)
*   لَا (lā) - not (used for present tense negation)
