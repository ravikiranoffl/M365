# Day 81 - Contrast structures

## Lesson

Contrast structures are essential for building complex arguments and making your speech or writing more engaging. They help you compare and contrast ideas, showing how things are similar or different, or how one idea happens despite another.

We'll learn a few key phrases and words today that are very common in contrasting ideas in Arabic:

### 1. `بَيْنَمَا` (baynamā) - While / Whereas

This word is used to connect two clauses, showing that two actions are happening at the same time, or presenting two contrasting ideas side-by-side.

*   **New Word:** `بَيْنَمَا` (baynamā)
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'bay-na-maa'.
    *   *Meaning:* "while," "whereas."
    *   *How to use:* Place `بَيْنَمَا` at the beginning of the second clause (or sometimes the first) to indicate that its content is happening at the same time as, or in contrast to, the other clause. It does not affect the case endings or verb moods of the clauses it connects.
    *   *Example sentence:*
        `هُوَ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ، بَيْنَمَا هِيَ تَكْتُبُ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Huwa yaqra'u al-kitāba, baynamā hiya taktubu ar-risālata.)
        *   *Translation:* He reads the book, while she writes the message.
        *   *Breakdown:* `هُوَ يَقْرَأُ الْكِتَابَ` (he reads the book - Day 19, 45). `بَيْنَمَا` connects. `هِيَ تَكْتُبُ الرِّسَالَةَ` (she writes the message - Day 19, 45).

### 2. `عَلَى الْعَكْسِ` (ala al-'aksi) - In contrast / On the contrary

This phrase is used to introduce an idea that is the opposite of or greatly different from what was just said. It's like saying "on the other hand."

*   **New Word for the phrase:**
    *   **`عَكْسٌ` (ʿaksun) - Opposite, Reverse** (masculine noun).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ʿak-sun'.
*   **The Contrast Phrase:** `عَلَى الْعَكْسِ` (ala al-'aksi)
    *   *Literal translation:* "On the opposite." (You know `عَلَى` - on, Day 52).
    *   *Meaning:* "In contrast," "on the contrary," "on the other hand."
    *   *How to use:* Place this phrase at the beginning of a sentence or clause to clearly signal a contrasting idea. The word `الْعَكْسِ` takes a **Kasra** (ِ) because it follows the preposition `عَلَى`.
    *   *Example sentence:*
        `الْقَانُونُ جَدِيدٌ. عَلَى الْعَكْسِ، السِّيَاسَةُ قَدِيمَةٌ.` (Al-qānūnu jadeedun. 'Ala al-'aksi, as-siyāsatū qadeematun.)
        *   *Translation:* The law is new. In contrast, the policy is old.
        *   *Breakdown:* `الْقَانُونُ جَدِيدٌ` (the law is new - Day 51). `عَلَى الْعَكْسِ` introduces the contrast. `السِّيَاسَةُ قَدِيمَةٌ` (the policy is old - Day 54, 27).

### 3. `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (wa-ma'a anna) - Although / Even though

This phrase is used to introduce a concessive clause, meaning it states something that might make the main clause seem surprising or unexpected. It combines "and" (`وَ`), "with" (`مَعَ`), and "that" (`أَنَّ`).

*   **The Contrast Phrase:** `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (wa-ma'a anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "And with that."
    *   *Meaning:* "Although," "even though."
    *   *How to use:* This phrase introduces `أَنَّ`, so the subject of the clause following `أَنَّ` will **always take a Fatha** (َ). It sets up a situation where the main clause happens despite the condition in the `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` clause.
    *   *Example sentence:*
        `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ، يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَحُلَّهَا.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-mushkilata kabeeratun, yureedu al-wazeeru an yaḥullahā.)
        *   *Translation:* Although the problem is big, the minister wants to solve it.
        *   *Breakdown:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` introduces the concession. `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` takes Fatha due to `أَنَّ`. `كَبِيرَةٌ` is its comment. `يُرِيدُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَحُلَّهَا` (the minister wants to solve it - Day 44, 50).

### Review of other contrast structures:

*   **`وَلَكِنْ` (wa-lākin) - But (Day 28)**
    *   *Example:* `الْوَلَدُ ذَكِيٌّ، وَلَكِنْ عَلَى رَأْسِهِ رِيشَةٌ.` (Al-waladu dhakiyyun, wa-lākin 'alā ra'sihi reeshatun.) - The boy is smart, but he is arrogant (literally: on his head is a feather). (Day 66)
*   **`مَعَ ذَلِكَ` (ma'a dhālika) - Nevertheless / However (Day 70)**
    *   *Example:* `الطَّقْسُ سَيِّئٌ، مَعَ ذَلِكَ نَخْرُجُ إِلَى السُّوقِ.` (Aṭ-ṭaqsu sayyi'un, ma'a dhālika nakhruju ilā as-sūqi.) - The weather is bad, nevertheless we exit to the market. (Day 78)

These contrast structures allow you to express a wide range of relationships between ideas, making your Arabic much more expressive and sophisticated!

## Examples

Let's combine our new contrast structures with grammar and vocabulary you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **"The student (f.) studies while her brother plays."**
    *   `تَدْرُسُ الطَّالِبَةُ، بَيْنَمَا أَخُوهَا يَلْعَبُ.` (Tadrusu aṭ-ṭālibatū, baynamā akhūhā yal'abu.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `أَخٌ` (akhun) - brother. `أَخُوهَا` (her brother - Idafa with pronoun, in Marfu' state). `يَلْعَبُ` (he plays - new verb `لَعِبَ` / `يَلْعَبُ` to play. Let's simplify and use `يَقْرَأُ` he reads).
        *   *Revised Example 1:* **"The student (f.) studies while her brother reads."**
            `تَدْرُسُ الطَّالِبَةُ، بَيْنَمَا أَخُوهَا يَقْرَأُ.` (Tadrusu aṭ-ṭālibatū, baynamā akhūhā yaqra'u.)
            *   *Notice:* `تَدْرُسُ الطَّالِبَةُ` (the student studies - Day 58). `بَيْنَمَا` (while). `أَخُوهَا يَقْرَأُ` (her brother reads - `أَخٌ` brother, `ـهَا` her).

2.  **"The new manager (m.) is smart. In contrast, the old manager is not smart."**
    *   `الْمُدِيرُ الْجَدِيدُ ذَكِيٌّ. عَلَى الْعَكْسِ، الْمُدِيرُ الْقَدِيمُ لَيْسَ ذَكِيًّا.` (Al-mudīru al-jadeedu dhakiyyun. 'Ala al-'aksi, al-mudīru al-qadeemu laysa dhakiyyan.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْمُدِيرُ الْجَدِيدُ ذَكِيٌّ` (the new manager is smart - Day 79). `عَلَى الْعَكْسِ` (in contrast). `الْمُدِيرُ الْقَدِيمُ لَيْسَ ذَكِيًّا` (the old manager is not smart - Day 61).

3.  **"Although we know the problem, we cannot solve it."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّنَا نَعْلَمُ الْمُشْكِلَةَ، لَا نَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ نَحُلَّهَا.` (Wa-ma'a annanā na'lamu al-mushkilata, lā nastaṭī'u an naḥullahā.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `نَسْتَطِيعُ` (nastaṭī'u) - we can/are able to (present tense, from `اِسْتَطَاعَ` - he could/was able). This is new, let's simplify to something the student knows for "we cannot solve it".
        *   *Revised Example 3:* **"Although we know the problem, we will not solve it."**
            `وَمَعَ أَنَّنَا نَعْلَمُ الْمُشْكِلَةَ، لَنْ نَحُلَّهَا.` (Wa-ma'a annanā na'lamu al-mushkilata, lan naḥullahā.)
            *   *Notice:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّنَا` (although we - `أَنَّ` + pronoun `ـنَا`, makes the pronoun prefix to `نَعْلَمُ` not explicitly used for subject). `نَعْلَمُ الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (we know the problem - Day 68). `لَنْ نَحُلَّهَا` (we will not solve it - Day 60, Subjunctive).

4.  **"He thinks that the old man came, while I think that he returned."**
    *   `هُوَ يَظُنُّ أَنَّ الرَّجُلَ الْقَدِيمَ جَاءَ، بَيْنَمَا أَظُنُّ أَنَّهُ عَادَ.` (Huwa yaẓunnu anna ar-rajula al-qadeema jā'a, baynamā aẓunnu annahu 'āda.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَظُنُّ أَنَّ` (he thinks that - Day 79). `الرَّجُلَ الْقَدِيمَ` takes Fatha. `جَاءَ` (he came - Day 78). `بَيْنَمَا` (while). `أَظُنُّ أَنَّهُ عَادَ` (I think that he returned - Day 78).

5.  **"Although it is probable that the company will announce the news, it seems that the minister (f.) will not confirm it."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّهُ مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ سَتُعْلِنُ الْأَخْبَارَ، يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَةَ لَنْ تُؤَكِّدَهَا.` (Wa-ma'a annahu mina al-muḥtamali anna ash-sharikatā sa-tu'linu al-akhbāra, yabdū anna al-wazeeratā lan tu'akkidahā.)
        *   *Notice:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّهُ مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ` (although it is probable that - Day 80). `الشَّرِكَةَ` takes Fatha. `سَتُعْلِنُ الْأَخْبَارَ` (she will announce the news - Day 64). `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that - Day 80). `الْوَزِيرَةَ` takes Fatha. `لَنْ تُؤَكِّدَهَا` (she will not confirm it - Day 60, `تُؤَكِّدُ` (she confirms) becomes Subjunctive).
