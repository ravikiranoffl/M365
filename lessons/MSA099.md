# Day 99 - Stance markers

## Lesson

Stance markers are like signposts for your listener or reader. They immediately tell them how you feel about the information you're presenting. Do you think it's definitely true? Is it a well-known fact? Are you happy or unhappy about it? These markers help you express your "stance" or viewpoint.

You know that `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ` (it is probable that) introduces a possibility. Now, let's learn how to express stronger opinions!

### 1. `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (mina al-mu'akkadi anna) - It is certain that

This phrase is used to express strong certainty about a fact. It's the opposite of `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ`!

*   **New Word:** `مُؤَكَّدٌ` (mu'akkadun) - 'moo-ak-ka-dun'
    *   *Meaning:* certain, confirmed (masculine adjective / passive participle, similar to `مَحْلُولٌ` - solved, Day 88).
*   **The Phrase:** `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (mina al-mu'akkadi anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "From the certain that."
    *   *How to use:* Just like `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ`, it introduces an `أَنَّ` clause. This means the subject of the clause immediately following `أَنَّ` will take a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   *Example:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الدَّرْسَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna ad-darsa muhimun.)
        *   *Translation:* It is certain that the lesson is important.
        *   *Breakdown:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ` (from the certain) takes Kasra due to `مِنْ`. `أَنَّ` (that) makes `الدَّرْسَ` (the lesson) take Fatha. `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is its comment.

### 2. `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (mina al-ma'roofi anna) - It is known that

This phrase is used to introduce information that is generally accepted as true or widely known. It's related to the verb `يَعْلَمُ` (he knows, Day 79).

*   **New Word:** `مَعْرُوفٌ` (ma'roofun) - 'ma'-roo-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* known (masculine adjective / passive participle).
*   **The Phrase:** `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (mina al-ma'roofi anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "From the known that."
    *   *How to use:* It introduces an `أَنَّ` clause, so the subject of the clause immediately following `أَنَّ` will take a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   *Example:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الشَّمْسَ حَارَّةٌ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna ash-shamsa ḥārratun.)
        *   *Translation:* It is known that the sun is hot.
        *   *Breakdown:* `الشَّمْسَ` (the sun) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`. `حَارَّةٌ` (hot - feminine, Day 98) is its comment.

### 3. `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ` (lā shakka anna) - There is no doubt that

This is another strong way to express absolute certainty. `شَكٌّ` means "doubt."

*   **New Word:** `شَكٌّ` (shakkun) - 'shak-kun'
    *   *Meaning:* doubt (masculine noun).
*   **The Phrase:** `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ` (lā shakka anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "No doubt that."
    *   *How to use:* `لَا` here is a special negation that makes the word after it (if indefinite and masculine singular, like `شَكَّ`) take a **Fatha** (َ) without tanween. Then it introduces an `أَنَّ` clause, so the subject of that clause will also take a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   *Example:* `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الْخُطَّةَ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Lā shakka anna al-khuṭṭata jadeedatun.)
        *   *Translation:* There is no doubt that the plan is new.
        *   *Breakdown:* `شَكَّ` takes Fatha. `أَنَّ` makes `الْخُطَّةَ` (the plan) take Fatha. `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new) is its comment.

### 4. `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ` (li-ḥusni al-ḥaẓẓi) - Fortunately

This phrase expresses a positive sentiment – something happened by good luck.

*   **New Word:** `حَظٌّ` (ḥaẓẓun) - 'ḥaẓ-ẓun'
    *   *Meaning:* luck (masculine noun).
*   **New Word:** `حُسْنٌ` (ḥusnun) - 'ḥus-nun'
    *   *Meaning:* goodness (masculine noun, often used in Idafa constructions).
*   **The Phrase:** `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ` (li-ḥusni al-ḥaẓẓi)
    *   *Literal translation:* "For the goodness of the luck."
    *   *How to use:* It's a preposition (`لِـ`) followed by an Idafa construction (`حُسْنِ الْحَظِّ`). Both words in the Idafa take the **Genitive case** (Kasra `ـِ`). It can start a sentence or be placed within it.
    *   *Example:* `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ، عَادَ الْمُدِيرُ إِلَى الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Li-ḥusni al-ḥaẓẓi, 'āda al-mudeeru ilā al-maktabi.)
        *   *Translation:* Fortunately, the manager returned to the office.
        *   *Breakdown:* `لِـ` (for), `حُسْنِ` (goodness - Genitive, first term of Idafa), `الْحَظِّ` (the luck - Genitive, second term of Idafa). `عَادَ` (he returned - Day 78).

### 5. `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi) - Unfortunately

This phrase expresses a negative sentiment – something happened by bad luck. It's the opposite of `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ`!

*   **New Word:** `سُوءٌ` (sū'un) - 'soo-un'
    *   *Meaning:* badness (masculine noun, often used in Idafa constructions).
*   **The Phrase:** `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi)
    *   *Literal translation:* "For the badness of the luck."
    *   *How to use:* Similar to `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ`, it's `لِـ` + an Idafa construction (`سُوءِ الْحَظِّ`). Both words in the Idafa take the **Genitive case** (Kasra `ـِ`).
    *   *Example:* `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، لَمْ يَنْجَحِ الطَّالِبُ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, lam yanjahi aṭ-ṭālibu fī al-imtiḥāni.)
        *   *Translation:* Unfortunately, the student did not succeed in the exam.
        *   *Breakdown:* `لِـ`, `سُوءِ`, `الْحَظِّ` (all Genitive). `لَمْ يَنْجَحِ` (he did not succeed - Jussive, Day 60).

### 6. `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (mina al-ghareebi anna) - It is strange that

This phrase expresses surprise or that something is unusual.

*   **New Word:** `غَرِيبٌ` (ghareebun) - 'gha-ree-bun'
    *   *Meaning:* strange, foreign (masculine adjective).
*   **The Phrase:** `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (mina al-ghareebi anna)
    *   *Literal translation:* "From the strange that."
    *   *How to use:* Similar to `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنَّ` and `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ`, it introduces an `أَنَّ` clause, making the subject of that clause take a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   *Example:* `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّهُ عَادَ مُبَكِّرًا.` (Mina al-ghareebi annahu 'āda mubakkiran.)
        *   *Translation:* It is strange that he returned early.
        *   *Breakdown:* `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ` (from the strange - Kasra due to `مِنْ`). `أَنَّهُ` (that he - `ـهُ` takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`). `عَادَ مُبَكِّرًا` (he returned early - Day 78, 86).

These stance markers are powerful tools for conveying your perspective and adding richness to your Arabic expressions!

## Examples

Let's see these stance markers in more complex sentences, combining them with grammar you've already mastered.

1.  **"It is certain that the problem is difficult, but it will be solved."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ صَعْبَةٌ، وَلَكِنَّهَا سَتُحَلُّ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-mushkilata ṣa'batun, wa-lakinnahā satuhallu.)
        *   *Notice:* `وَلَكِنَّهَا` (but it - Day 61). `سَتُحَلُّ` (it will be solved - passive, Day 72, 50).

2.  **"It is known that the minister, Muhammad, will undertake the project."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ مُحَمَّدًا سَيَقُومُ بِالْمَشْرُوعِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna al-wazeera Muḥammadan sayaqūmu bi-l-mashroo'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْوَزِيرَ مُحَمَّدًا` (apposition, Accusative after `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `سَيَقُومُ بِالْمَشْرُوعِ` (will undertake the project - light verb, Day 98).

3.  **"There is no doubt that the company wants profit, although the economy is not good."**
    *   `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ تُرِيدُ الرِّبْحَ، وَمَعَ أَنَّ الِاقْتِصَادَ غَيْرُ جَيِّدٍ.` (Lā shakka anna ash-sharikatā tureedu ar-ribḥa, wa-ma'a anna al-iqtiṣāda ghayru jayyidin.)
        *   *Notice:* `الرِّبْحَ` (profit - Day 89). `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (although - Day 81). `الِاقْتِصَادَ غَيْرُ جَيِّدٍ` (economy is not good - Day 89, 73).

4.  **"Fortunately, the doctor, Fatima, became interested in the new medicine."**
    *   `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ، أَصْبَحَتِ الطَّبِيبَةُ فَاطِمَةُ مُهْتَمَّةً بِالدَّوَاءِ الْجَدِيدِ.` (Li-ḥusni al-ḥaẓẓi, aṣbaḥati aṭ-ṭabeebatū Fāṭimatū muhtammatan bi-d-dawā'i al-jadeedi.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَصْبَحَتِ` (she became - Day 98). `الطَّبِيبَةُ فَاطِمَةُ` (apposition, Nominative, Day 85). `مُهْتَمَّةً بِـ` (interested in - Accusative predicate of `أَصْبَحَتْ`, Day 82, 88). `الدَّوَاءِ الْجَدِيدِ` (new medicine - Genitive after `بِـ`, Day 97).

5.  **"Unfortunately, the weather became hot, so everyone wants to exit the city."**
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، أَصْبَحَ الطَّقْسُ حَارًّا، فَيُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنْ يَخْرُجُوا مِنَ الْمَدِينَةِ.` (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, aṣbaḥa aṭ-ṭaqsu ḥārran, fa-yureedu al-jamee'u an yakhrujoo mina al-madeenati.)
        *   *Notice:* `أَصْبَحَ الطَّقْسُ حَارًّا` (the weather became hot - Day 98). `فَـ` (so - Day 86). `يُرِيدُ الْجَمِيعُ أَنْ يَخْرُجُوا` (everyone wants to exit - Subjunctive Nūn-drop, Day 60).

6.  **"It is strange that the president, Ali, made the easy problem difficult."**
    *   `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ الرَّئِيسَ عَلِيًّا جَعَلَ الْمُشْكِلَةَ السَّهْلَةَ صَعْبَةً.` (Mina al-ghareebi anna ar-ra'eeca 'Aliyyan ja'ala al-mushkilata as-sahlatā ṣa'batan.)
        *   *Notice:* `الرَّئِيسَ عَلِيًّا` (apposition, Accusative after `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `جَعَلَ` (he made - Day 98). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ السَّهْلَةَ` (the easy problem - Accusative first object). `صَعْبَةً` (difficult - Accusative second object).
