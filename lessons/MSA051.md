# Day 51 - Legal vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns and a new verb related to law and justice. Pay close attention to the gender of each noun, as this will affect how adjectives describe them and how they're used in sentences!

1.  **`قَانُونٌ` (qānūn) - Law**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It means a rule or a system of rules.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'qaa-noo-nun'.
    *   Example: `الْقَانُونُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-qānūnu muhimun.) - The law is important. (Using `مُهِمٌّ` - important, from Day 43).

2.  **`عَدْلٌ` ('adl) - Justice**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It refers to fairness or the concept of justice.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'a'-dlun'.
    *   Example: `الْعَدْلُ جَمِيلٌ.` (Al-'adlu jameelun.) - Justice is beautiful. (Using `جَمِيلٌ` - beautiful, from Day 3).

3.  **`مَحْكَمَةٌ` (maḥkamatun) - Court (of law)**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun. This is the place where legal cases are heard.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'maḥ-ka-ma-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمَحْكَمَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-maḥkamatū kabeeratun.) - The court is big. (Using `كَبِيرَةٌ` - big, from Day 3).

4.  **`شُرْطَةٌ` (shurṭatun) - Police (force)**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun. It refers to the police as an institution or force.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'shur-ṭa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الشُّرْطَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Ash-shurṭatū jadeedatun.) - The police are new. (Referring to the police as an institution, using `جَدِيدَةٌ` - new, from Day 3).

5.  **`سِجْنٌ` (sijnun) - Prison**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It's a place where criminals are kept.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'sij-nun'.
    *   Example: `السِّجْنُ كَبِيرٌ.` (As-sijnū kabeerun.) - The prison is big.

6.  **`جَرِيمَةٌ` (jarīmatun) - Crime**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun. It refers to an illegal act.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ja-ree-ma-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْجَرِيمَةُ صَغِيرَةٌ.` (Al-jarīmatū ṣagheeratun.) - The crime is small. (Using `صَغِيرَةٌ` - small, from Day 3).

7.  **`حَقٌّ` (ḥaqq) - Right (as in a legal or human right)**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It means something a person is morally or legally entitled to.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ḥaq-qun'.
    *   Example: `الْحَقُّ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-ḥaqqu muhimun.) - The right is important.

8.  **`قَاضٍ` (qāḍin) - Judge**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It means a public official who hears and decides cases in a court of law.
    *   *Special Note for Beginners:* When you see `قَاضٍ` (qāḍin), it means "a judge" (masculine singular, indefinite, in the nominative or genitive case). It has a special ending where the `ي` is often dropped and replaced with Tanween Kasra ( ٍ ) in this form. If it's definite, it becomes `الْقَاضِي` (al-qāḍī), with the `ي` present. For today, we will primarily use the definite form `الْقَاضِي` to simplify.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'qaa-ḍin' (indefinite), 'al-qaa-ḍee' (definite).
    *   Example: `الْقَاضِي ذَكِيٌّ.` (Al-qāḍī dhakiyyun.) - The judge is smart. (Using `ذَكِيٌّ` - smart, from Day 37).

9.  **New Verb: "To Seek / To Ask For"**
    *   This verb is very useful for saying someone wants or requests something.
    *   **Root:** `ط ل ب` (ṭ-l-b)
    *   **Past Tense (he sought/asked for):** `طَلَبَ` (ṭalaba) - 'ṭa-la-ba'.
    *   **Present Tense (he seeks/asks for):** `يَطْلُبُ` (yaṭlubu) - 'yaṭ-lu-bu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I seek/ask for):** `أَطْلُبُ` (aṭlubu) - 'aṭ-lu-bu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she seeks/asks for / you-m.s. seek/ask for):** `تَطْلُبُ` (taṭlubu) - 'taṭ-lu-bu'.
    *   Example: `هُوَ يَطْلُبُ الْعَدْلَ.` (Huwa yaṭlubu al-'adla.) - He seeks justice. (`الْعَدْلَ` takes Fatha as it's the direct object).

You've just learned a strong set of words to begin discussing legal topics! Remember to use them with their correct genders and case endings.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **The law of the state is important.** (Idafa construct + adjective)
    *   `قَانُونُ الدَّوْلَةِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Qānūnū ad-dawlati muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَانُونُ` (law of) is masculine, so `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is masculine. `الدَّوْلَةِ` (the state) is from Day 48.

2.  **The judge seeks justice.** (New noun and verb)
    *   `الْقَاضِي يَطْلُبُ الْعَدْلَ.` (Al-qāḍī yaṭlubu al-'adla.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْعَدْلَ` (justice) takes a Fatha because it is the direct object of the verb `يَطْلُبُ`.

3.  **Indeed, the police are in the big court.** (Emphasis particle + fronted comment)
    *   `إِنَّ فِي الْمَحْكَمَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ الشُّرْطَةَ.` (Inna fī al-maḥkamati al-kabeerati ash-shurṭata.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` (Day 37) makes `الشُّرْطَةَ` (the police) take a Fatha. `فِي الْمَحْكَمَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ` is the fronted comment (`الْمَحْكَمَةِ` takes Kasra after `فِي`, and `الْكَبِيرَةِ` is its adjective, matching in gender, definiteness, and case).

4.  **The people want to understand the law.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ` + verb)
    *   `الشَّعْبُ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَفْهَمَ الْقَانُونَ.` (Ash-sha'bu yureedu an yafhama al-qānūna.)
        *   *Notice:* `الشَّعْبُ` (the people - Day 48) `يُرِيدُ` (he wants - Day 44). `أَنْ يَفْهَمَ` (to understand - `يَفْهَمُ` from Day 48, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `الْقَانُونَ` (the law) takes Fatha as the object.

5.  **She said that the crime is big.** (Reported speech)
    *   `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الْجَرِيمَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Qālat anna al-jarīmata kabeeratun.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَتْ` (she said - Day 39). `أَنَّ` makes `الْجَرِيمَةَ` (the crime) take Fatha. `كَبِيرَةٌ` (big) is the feminine comment matching `الْجَرِيمَةَ`.

6.  **I go to the court to seek justice.** (Purpose clause + new verb)
    *   `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَحْكَمَةِ لِأَطْلُبَ الْعَدْلَ.` (Ana adhhaba ilā al-maḥkamati li'aṭluba al-'adla.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِأَطْلُبَ` (in order for me to seek) where `أَطْلُبُ` takes Fatha because of `لِـ` (Day 40). `الْعَدْلَ` takes Fatha as object.

7.  **It is probable that the minister wants to solve the problem of the law.** (Modal expression + Idafa)
    *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يُرِيدَ الْوَزِيرُ أَنْ يَحُلَّ مُشْكِلَةَ الْقَانُونِ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an yureeda al-wazeeru an yaḥulla mushkilata al-qānūni.)
        *   *Notice:* This is a complex sentence! `يُرِيدَ` takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`. Then "to solve" (`أَنْ يَحُلَّ`) is the object of "wants," also taking Fatha. `مُشْكِلَةَ الْقَانُونِ` (the problem of the law) is an Idafa, the object of `يَحُلَّ`. `مُشْكِلَةَ` takes Fatha, `الْقَانُونِ` takes Kasra. (`الْوَزِيرُ` is 'the minister' from Day 48, `مُشْكِلَةٌ` and `حَلَّ` from Day 50).

8.  **This is an important right.**
    *   `هَذَا حَقٌّ مُهِمٌّ.` (Hādhā ḥaqqun muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `حَقٌّ` is masculine, so `مُهِمٌّ` is masculine.

9.  **The problem is in the prison.**
    *   `الْمُشْكِلَةُ فِي السِّجْنِ.` (Al-mushkilatū fī as-sijni.)
