# Day 69 - Persuasive language

## Lesson

Persuasive language is all about making your arguments stronger and your calls to action more impactful. It helps you present information in a way that encourages others to agree with you or take a specific course of action.

We'll learn some key phrases and words today that are very common in persuasive discourse in Arabic:

### 1. Connecting Cause and Effect (`لِذَلِكَ`) - Therefore / Thus

When you want to show a clear consequence or logical conclusion, this word is essential.

*   **New word:** `لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika) - 'li-dhaa-li-ka'
    *   *Let's break it down:* It combines `لِـ` (li-) "for" (Day 10) and `ذَلِكَ` (dhālika) "that" (Day 11). So, literally "for that."
    *   *How to use:* It acts like "therefore" or "thus" in English, connecting a previous statement to a conclusion or consequence.
    *   *Example:* `الْبِيئَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ، لِذَلِكَ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَحْفَظَهَا.` (Al-bī'atu muhimmatun, li-dhālika yajibu an naḥfaẓahā.) - The environment is important, therefore it is necessary to protect it. (`ـهَا` is 'it' referring to `الْبِيئَةَ`, Day 7).

### 2. Stating Clear Facts (`مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ`) - It is clear that...

This phrase is used to introduce information as an undeniable, self-evident truth, which strengthens your argument.

*   **New phrase:** `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (mina al-wāḍiḥi anna) - 'mi-na al-waa-ḍi-ḥi an-na'
    *   *New word for the idiom:* `وَاضِحٌ` (wāḍiḥun) - Clear (masculine adjective). (Feminine: `وَاضِحَةٌ` - wāḍiḥatun). This is an active participle, like `كَاتِبٌ` (writer, Day 42).
    *   *Let's break it down:* `مِنَ` (from - Day 10) + `الْوَاضِحِ` (the clear one - active participle acting as a noun, takes Kasra after `مِنَ`) + `أَنَّ` (that - Day 39).
    *   *How to use:* It's similar in structure to `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (it is known that, Day 64). It always introduces `أَنَّ`, so the subject of the clause will take a Fatha.
    *   *Example:* `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الزِّيَادَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna az-ziyādata kabeeratun.) - It is clear that the increase is big.

### 3. Emphasizing Importance of an Action (`مُهِمٌّ أَنْ`) - It is important to/that...

While `يَجِبُ أَنْ` (it is necessary to, Day 67) implies a strong obligation, `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ` presents something as highly valuable or significant to do.

*   **New phrase:** `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ` (muhimun an) - 'mu-hi-mun an'
    *   *Let's break it down:* `مُهِمٌّ` (important - masculine adjective, Day 43) + `أَنْ` (to/that - Day 38).
    *   *How to use:* This introduces an action, so the verb following `أَنْ` will be in the Subjunctive mood (ending in Fatha). It's a nominal sentence where `مُهِمٌّ` is the predicate, and the `أَنْ` clause is the implied subject.
    *   *Example:* `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ نَفْهَمَ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Muhimun an nafham al-mushkilata.) - It is important to understand the problem. (`نَفْهَمَ` takes Fatha because of `أَنْ`).

### 4. Stating a Goal or Objective (`لِتَحْقِيقِ`) - In order to achieve / to realize...

To motivate action, you often need to articulate the positive outcome. This phrase helps you state what you aim to achieve.

*   **New phrase:** `لِتَحْقِيقِ` (li-taḥqīqi) - 'li-taḥ-qee-qi'
    *   *New word:* `تَحْقِيقٌ` (taḥqīqun) - Achievement, realization (masculine noun / Masdar).
    *   *Let's break it down:* `لِـ` (in order to - Day 40) + `تَحْقِيقِ` (achievement/realization - Masdar, takes Kasra after `لِـ`). This phrase typically forms an Idafa construct with the goal.
    *   *How to use:* It is usually followed by a noun in the Kasra case, forming an Idafa to specify *what* is being achieved.
    *   *Example:* `نَعْمَلُ لِتَحْقِيقِ السَّلَامِ.` (Na'malu li-taḥqīqi as-salāmi.) - We work to achieve peace.

These phrases are excellent for building persuasive arguments, whether you're speaking or writing in formal Arabic. They add structure and emphasis to your points.

## Examples

Let's combine our new persuasive language with grammar and vocabulary you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **"The statistics are important, therefore it is necessary to study them."**
    *   `الْإِحْصَاءُ مُهِمٌّ، لِذَلِكَ يَجِبُ أَنْ نَدْرُسَهُ.` (Al-iḥṣā'u muhimun, li-dhālika yajibu an nadrusahu.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْإِحْصَاءُ مُهِمٌّ` (statistics is important - Day 54, 43). `لِذَلِكَ` (therefore). `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَدْرُسَهُ` (it is necessary to study it - Day 67, `نَدْرُسَهُ` (we study it) takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`).

2.  **"It is clear that the new company will protect the environment."**
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Mina al-wāḍiḥi anna ash-sharikatū al-jadeedata sa-taḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (it is clear that). `الشَّرِكَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (the new company) takes Fatha after `أَنَّ`. `سَتَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ` (she will protect the environment - Day 56, future tense).

3.  **"It is important to work hand in hand for justice."**
    *   `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ يَدًا بِيَدٍ لِلْعَدْلِ.` (Muhimun an na'mala yadan bi-yadin li-l-'adli.)
        *   *Notice:* `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ` (it is important to). `نَعْمَلَ` (we work) takes Fatha. `يَدًا بِيَدٍ` (hand in hand - Day 66). `لِلْعَدْلِ` (for justice - Day 51).

4.  **"The minister calls for a big meeting to achieve the agreement."**
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يَدْعُو إِلَى اجْتِمَاعٍ كَبِيرٍ لِتَحْقِيقِ الِاتِّفَاقِ.` (Al-wazeeru yad'ū ilā ijtimā'in kabeerin li-taḥqīqi al-ittifāqi.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَدْعُو إِلَى` (he calls for - Day 64). `اجْتِمَاعٍ كَبِيرٍ` (a big meeting - Day 50). `لِتَحْقِيقِ` (to achieve). `الِاتِّفَاقِ` (the agreement - Day 50) takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa.

5.  **"We affirm that the president believes that peace is useful, therefore he calls for it."**
    *   `نُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ الرَّئِيسَ يَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ السَّلَامَ مُفِيدٌ، لِذَلِكَ يَدْعُو إِلَيْهِ.` (Nu'akkidu anna ar-ra'eeca ya'taqidu anna as-salāma mufīdun, li-dhālika yad'ū ilayhi.)
        *   *Notice:* This sentence combines many elements! `نُؤَكِّدُ أَنَّ` (we affirm that - Day 64). `الرَّئِيسَ` (the president) takes Fatha. `يَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (he believes that - Day 64). `السَّلَامَ` (peace) takes Fatha. `مُفِيدٌ` (useful - Day 43). `لِذَلِكَ` (therefore). `يَدْعُو إِلَيْهِ` (he calls for it - `إِلَى` + `ـهُ` for 'it').

6.  **"It is known that the numbers show a big increase, therefore it is important to understand this problem."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ الْأَرْقَامَ تُشِيرُ إِلَى زِيَادَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ، لِذَلِكَ مُهِمٌّ أَنْ نَفْهَمَ هَذِهِ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Mina al-ma'rūfi anna al-arqāma tusheeru ilā ziyādatin kabeeratin, li-dhālika muhimun an nafham hadhihi al-mushkilata.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (it is known that - Day 64). `الْأَرْقَامَ` (the numbers) takes Fatha. `تُشِيرُ إِلَى زِيَادَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ` (indicate a big increase - Day 54). `لِذَلِكَ` (therefore). `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ نَفْهَمَ` (it is important to understand). `هَذِهِ الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (this problem - `هَذِهِ` (this) + definite noun, Day 11, takes Fatha as object).
