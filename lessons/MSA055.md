# Day 55 - Technology vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns and verbs related to technology. Remember to pay attention to the gender of each noun, as this will affect how adjectives describe them!

1.  **`حَاسُوبٌ` (ḥāsūbun) - Computer**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It literally means "calculator" or "reckoner."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ḥaa-soo-bun'.
    *   Example: `الْحَاسُوبُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-ḥāsūbu jadeedun.) - The computer is new. (Using `جَدِيدٌ` - new, from Day 3).

2.  **`هَاتِفٌ` (hātifun) - Phone**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It means "caller" or "phone."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'haa-ti-fun'.
    *   Example: `الْهَاتِفُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-hātifu kabeerun.) - The phone is big. (Using `كَبِيرٌ` - big, from Day 3).

3.  **`إِنْتَرْنِت` (intarnit) - Internet**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun, often used as a direct loanword from English.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'in-tar-nit'.
    *   Example: `الْإِنْتَرْنِتُ مُفِيدٌ.` (Al-intarnitu mufīdun.) - The Internet is useful. (Using `مُفِيدٌ` - useful, from Day 43).

4.  **`شَاشَةٌ` (shāshatun) - Screen**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'shaa-sha-tun'.
    *   Example: `الشَّاشَةُ صَغِيرَةٌ.` (Ash-shāshatū ṣagheeratun.) - The screen is small. (Using `صَغِيرَةٌ` - small, from Day 3).

5.  **`مَوْقِعٌ` (mawqi'un) - Website, Site, Location**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. While it can mean any "site" or "location," in a technology context, it often refers to a "website."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'maw-qi-'un'.
    *   Example: `الْمَوْقِعُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-mawqi'u muhimun.) - The website is important. (Using `مُهِمٌّ` - important, from Day 43).

6.  **`مَعْلُومَاتٌ` (ma'lūmātun) - Information**
    *   This is a **feminine sound plural** noun (ending in `ـَاتٌ`). Remember from Day 24, non-human broken and sound plurals are treated as **feminine singular** for adjectives!
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ma'-loo-maa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمَعْلُومَاتُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-ma'lūmātū jadeedatun.) - The information is new. (Notice `جَدِيدَةٌ` is feminine singular).

7.  **`رِسَالَةٌ` (risālatun) - Message**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ri-saa-la-tun'.
    *   Example: `الرِّسَالَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Ar-risālatū kabeeratun.) - The message is big.

8.  **New Verb: "To Use"**
    *   **Root:** `خ د م` (kh-d-m) - often associated with service.
    *   **Past Tense (he used):** `اسْتَخْدَمَ` (istakhdama) - 'is-takh-da-ma'.
    *   **Present Tense (he uses):** `يَسْتَخْدِمُ` (yastakhdimu) - 'yas-takh-di-mu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I use):** `أُسْتَخْدِمُ` (ustakhdimu) - 'us-takh-di-mu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she uses / you-m.s. use):** `تَسْتَخْدِمُ` (tastakhdimu) - 'tas-takh-di-mu'.
    *   Example: `هُوَ يَسْتَخْدِمُ الْهَاتِفَ.` (Huwa yastakhdimu al-hātifa.) - He uses the phone. (`الْهَاتِفَ` takes Fatha as the direct object).

9.  **New Verb: "To Send"**
    *   **Root:** `ر س ل` (r-s-l) - associated with sending.
    *   **Past Tense (he sent):** `أَرْسَلَ` (arsala) - 'ar-sa-la'.
    *   **Present Tense (he sends):** `يُرْسِلُ` (yursilu) - 'yur-si-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I send):** `أُرْسِلُ` (ursilu) - 'ur-si-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she sends / you-m.s. send):** `تُرْسِلُ` (tursilu) - 'tur-si-lu'.
    *   Example: `هِيَ تُرْسِلُ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Hiya tursilu ar-risālata.) - She sends the message. (`الرِّسَالَةَ` takes Fatha as the direct object).

10. **New Verb: "To Take / To Receive"**
    *   **Root:** `أ خ ذ` (a-kh-dh) - associated with taking.
    *   **Past Tense (he took / received):** `أَخَذَ` (akhadha) - 'a-kha-dha'.
    *   **Present Tense (he takes / receives):** `يَأْخُذُ` (ya'khudhu) - 'ya'-khu-dhu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I take / receive):** `آخُذُ` (ākhudhu) - 'aa-khu-dhu'. (Remember the long 'aa' sound with Hamza and Alif: `آ`).
    *   **Present Tense (she takes / receives / you-m.s. take / receive):** `تَأْخُذُ` (ta'khudhu) - 'ta'-khu-dhu'.
    *   Example: `أَنَا آخُذُ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ.` (Ana ākhudhu al-ma'lūmāti.) - I receive the information. (`الْمَعْلُومَاتِ` takes Kasra as a direct object because it's a sound feminine plural, similar to Day 23's rules for objects of `ـَات` plurals).

You've just learned a fantastic set of words to start discussing technology! Remember to use them with their correct genders and case endings.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **The new computer is big.** (Nominal sentence + adjectives)
    *   `الْحَاسُوبُ الْجَدِيدُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-ḥāsūbu al-jadeedu kabeerun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْحَاسُوبُ` (computer) is masculine, so `الْجَدِيدُ` (new) and `كَبِيرٌ` (big) are also masculine.

2.  **I use the phone now.** (New verb + time adverb)
    *   `أُسْتَخْدِمُ الْهَاتِفَ الْآنَ.` (Ustakhdimu al-hātifa al-āna.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْهَاتِفَ` (the phone) takes Fatha as it's the direct object of `أُسْتَخْدِمُ`. `الْآنَ` (now) is an adverb (Day 27).

3.  **She wants to send the message.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تُرْسِلَ الرِّسَالَةَ.` (Tureedu an tursila ar-risālata.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرِيدُ` (she wants - Day 44). `أَنْ تُرْسِلَ` (to send) with the verb `تُرْسِلُ` (she sends) getting a Fatha ending because of `أَنْ` (Day 38, 44). `الرِّسَالَةَ` (the message) takes Fatha as the object.

4.  **He reads new information about the internet.** (New non-human plural + adjective + preposition)
    *   `هُوَ يَقْرَأُ مَعْلُومَاتٍ جَدِيدَةً عَنِ الْإِنْتَرْنِتِ.` (Huwa yaqra'u ma'lūmātin jadeedatan 'ani al-intarniti.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَقْرَأُ` (he reads - Day 45). `مَعْلُومَاتٍ` (information) is indefinite, a sound feminine plural, so it takes Tanween Kasra for direct object. `جَدِيدَةً` (new) is its adjective, feminine singular, taking Tanween Fatha. `عَنِ` (about - Day 48) is a preposition, so `الْإِنْتَرْنِتِ` takes Kasra.

5.  **The small screen is on the computer.** (Nominal sentence + preposition)
    *   `الشَّاشَةُ الصَّغِيرَةُ عَلَى الْحَاسُوبِ.` (Ash-shāshatū aṣ-ṣagheeratū 'alā al-ḥāsūbi.)
        *   *Notice:* `الشَّاشَةُ` (screen) is feminine, so `الصَّغِيرَةُ` (small) is feminine. `عَلَى` (on - Day 52) is a preposition, so `الْحَاسُوبِ` (the computer) takes Kasra.

6.  **The student receives the message from the website.** (New verb + prepositions)
    *   `الطَّالِبُ يَأْخُذُ الرِّسَالَةَ مِنَ الْمَوْقِعِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu ya'khudhu ar-risālata mina al-mawqi'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَأْخُذُ` (he receives). `الرِّسَالَةَ` takes Fatha as object. `مِنَ` (from - Day 10) is a preposition, so `الْمَوْقِعِ` (the website) takes Kasra.

7.  **Perhaps the minister will use the new phone.** (Modal expression + future tense)
    *   `رُبَّمَا سَيَسْتَخْدِمُ الْوَزِيرُ الْهَاتِفَ الْجَدِيدَ.` (Rubbamā sa-yastakhdimu al-wazeeru al-hātifa al-jadeeda.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (maybe - Day 38). `سَيَسْتَخْدِمُ` (he will use - `سَـ` for future, Day 22). `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister - Day 48) is the subject. `الْهَاتِفَ الْجَدِيدَ` is the object with its adjective, both taking Fatha.

8.  **Indeed, understanding information is important.** (Emphasis particle + Masdar in Idafa)
    *   `إِنَّ فَهْمَ الْمَعْلُومَاتِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Inna fahma al-ma'lūmāti muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` (Day 37) makes `فَهْمَ` (understanding - Masdar from Day 52) take Fatha as the first noun in the Idafa. `الْمَعْلُومَاتِ` takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa. `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is the masculine comment matching `فَهْمَ`.
