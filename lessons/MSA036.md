# Day 36 - Topic/comment sentence structure

## Lesson

1.  **The Basic "Topic" and "Comment" (Nominal Sentence Review)**
    *   Remember our "X is Y" sentences, like "The book is new"? In Arabic grammar, we have special names for "X" and "Y" in these sentences.
    *   The "X" part, which is the main subject or what we are talking about, is called the **Topic** or **Subject**. In Arabic, its name is **`الْمُبْتَدَأُ` (al-mubtada'u)**.
        *   The `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` usually comes first.
        *   It is usually **definite** (has `الْـ`) or is a pronoun (like `هُوَ`).
        *   It usually ends with a Damma ( ُ ).
    *   The "Y" part, which tells us something *about* the topic, is called the **Comment** or **Predicate**. In Arabic, its name is **`الْخَبَرُ` (al-khabaru)**.
        *   The `الْخَبَرُ` usually comes second.
        *   If it's a single noun or adjective, it's usually **indefinite** and ends with a Damma ( ُ ) (or Tanween Damma ٌ ).
        *   It can also be a **prepositional phrase** (like `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ` - in the school) or an **adverb of place/time** (like `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ` - on the desk, or `هُنَا` - here). These types of comments often end in a Kasra ( ِ ) or a Fatha ( َ ), depending on the words in the phrase.

    *   **Example 1: "The book is new."**
        *   `الْكِتَابُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-kitābu jadeedun.)
        *   `الْكِتَابُ` is the `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` (Topic).
        *   `جَدِيدٌ` is the `الْخَبَرُ` (Comment).

    *   **Example 2: "He is a student."**
        *   `هُوَ طَالِبٌ.` (Huwa taalibun.)
        *   `هُوَ` is the `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` (Topic).
        *   `طَالِبٌ` is the `الْخَبَرُ` (Comment).

    *   **Example 3: "The student is in the school."** (Here the comment is a prepositional phrase)
        *   `الطَّالِبُ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu fī al-madrasati.)
        *   `الطَّالِبُ` is the `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` (Topic).
        *   `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ` is the `الْخَبَرُ` (Comment). This type of comment is called a **Prepositional Phrase** in English, and in Arabic, it's `جَارٌّ وَ مَجْرُورٌ` (jārrūn wa majrūrūn).

    *   **Example 4: "The pen is on the desk."** (Here the comment is an adverb of place)
        *   `الْقَلَمُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Al-qalamu fawqa al-maktabi.)
        *   `الْقَلَمُ` is the `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` (Topic).
        *   `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ` is the `الْخَبَرُ` (Comment). This type of comment is an **Adverb** (of place), called `ظَرْفٌ` (ẓarfūn) in Arabic.

2.  **When the Comment Comes First: The "Fronted Comment" Structure**
    *   Now for the new part! In Arabic, it is very common, especially when the `الْخَبَرُ` (Comment) is a **prepositional phrase** (`جَارٌّ وَ مَجْرُورٌ`) or an **adverb of place/time** (`ظَرْفٌ`), for the comment to come *before* the topic.
    *   When the `الْخَبَرُ` comes first, it's called a **Fronted Comment** (`خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ` - khabarun muqaddamun).
    *   When the `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` comes after, it's called a **Delayed Topic** (`مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ` - mubtada'un mu'akhkharun).
    *   This structure often emphasizes the location or time, or simply sounds more natural in many contexts.
    *   The good news is that the grammatical rules for each word (like Kasra after `فِي` or `فَوْقَ`, or Damma for the topic) do **not** change. Only their order changes!

    *   **Structure:** `[خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ (Fronted Comment)] + [مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ (Delayed Topic)]`

    *   **Let's convert our previous examples:**

        *   From: `الطَّالِبُ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (The student is in the school.)
        *   To: `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ الطَّالِبُ.` (Fī al-madrasati aṭ-ṭālibu.)
            *   **Meaning:** In the school *is* the student. (Emphasis on "in the school").

        *   From: `الْقَلَمُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (The pen is on the desk.)
        *   To: `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ الْقَلَمُ.` (Fawqa al-maktabi al-qalamu.)
            *   **Meaning:** On the desk *is* the pen. (Emphasis on "on the desk").

        *   From: `الْكُتُبُ هُنَاكَ.` (The books are there.)
        *   To: `هُنَاكَ الْكُتُبُ.` (Hunāka al-kutubu.)
            *   **Meaning:** There *are* the books. (Emphasis on "there").
            *   *Notice*: `الْكُتُبُ` (books) is a non-human broken plural. Its status as `مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ` doesn't change its Damma ending.

    *   **Important Note for Beginners:** For now, focus on definite topics (those with `الْـ`). When the topic is definite, this "fronted comment" structure is a very common *alternative* way to say things. You'll notice both forms used frequently in Arabic.

You've just learned a very common and important sentence structure in Arabic! This will help you understand and construct more natural-sounding sentences.

## Examples

Let's practice both the standard and fronted topic/comment structures.

**1. Standard Order (Topic first, Comment second):**

*   **The car is beautiful.**
    *   `السَّيَّارَةُ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (As-sayyāratu jameelatun.)
*   **The teacher (masculine) is here.**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُ هُنَا.` (Al-mudarrisu hunā.)
*   **My book is in the room.**
    *   `كِتَابِي فِي الْغُرْفَةِ.` (Kitābī fī al-ghurfati.)
*   **The big house is there.**
    *   `الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ هُنَاكَ.` (Al-baytu al-kabeeru hunāka.)
*   **The new student (feminine) is at the university.**
    *   `الطَّالِبَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ فِي الْجَامِعَةِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibatu al-jadeedatu fī al-jāmi'ati.)

**2. Fronted Comment Order (Comment first, Topic second):**

*   **In the school is the student (masculine).**
    *   `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ الطَّالِبُ.` (Fī al-madrasati aṭ-ṭālibu.)
        *   `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ` is the `خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ`. `الطَّالِبُ` is the `مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ`.
*   **Under the car is the book.**
    *   `تَحْتَ السَّيَّارَةِ الْكِتَابُ.` (Taḥta as-sayyārati al-kitābu.)
        *   `تَحْتَ السَّيَّارَةِ` is the `خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ`. `الْكِتَابُ` is the `مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ`.
*   **Here are the pens.** (`أَقْلَامٌ` is plural for pen from Day 24)
    *   `هُنَا الْأَقْلَامُ.` (Hunā al-aqlāmu.)
        *   `هُنَا` is the `خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ`. `الْأَقْلَامُ` is the `مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ`.
*   **On the big desk is the small book.**
    *   `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ الْكَبِيرِ الْكِتَابُ الصَّغِيرُ.` (Fawqa al-maktabi al-kabeeri al-kitābu aṣ-ṣagheeru.)
        *   `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ الْكَبِيرِ` is the `خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ`. `الْكِتَابُ الصَّغِيرُ` is the `مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ`.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   الْمُبْتَدَأُ (al-mubtada'u) - Topic, Subject (of a nominal sentence)
*   الْخَبَرُ (al-khabaru) - Comment, Predicate (of a nominal sentence)
*   جَارٌّ وَ مَجْرُورٌ (jārrūn wa majrūrūn) - Prepositional Phrase
*   ظَرْفٌ (ẓarfūn) - Adverb (of time or place)
*   خَبَرٌ مُقَدَّمٌ (khabarun muqaddamun) - Fronted Comment
*   مُبْتَدَأٌ مُؤَخَّرٌ (mubtada'un mu'akhkharun) - Delayed Topic
*   كُرْسِيٌّ (kursiyyun) - chair
