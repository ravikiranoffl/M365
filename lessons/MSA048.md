# Day 48 - Politics vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we're going to learn key nouns related to politics. Remember to pay attention to the gender of each noun, as this will affect how adjectives describe them!

1.  **`سِيَاسَةٌ` (siyāsatun) - Politics**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'see-yaa-sa-tun'.
    *   Example: `السِّيَاسَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (As-siyāsatū muhimmatun.) - Politics is important.

2.  **`حُكُومَةٌ` (ḥukūmatun) - Government**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ḥu-koo-ma-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْحُكُومَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-ḥukūmatu jadeedatun.) - The government is new.

3.  **`دَوْلَةٌ` (dawlatun) - State, Country**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'daw-la-tun'.
    *   Example: `الدَّوْلَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Ad-dawlatu kabeeratun.) - The state/country is big.

4.  **`شَعْبٌ` (sha'bun) - People, Nation**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'sha'b-un'.
    *   Example: `الشَّعْبُ يُرِيدُ.` (Ash-sha'bu yureedu.) - The people want. (We learned `يُرِيدُ` - he wants, on Day 44, it can be used for 'people' as a collective singular).

5.  **`رَئِيسٌ` (ra'īsun) - President, Head**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. You might recognize the `فَاعِلٌ` pattern from Active Participles (Day 42)! This word means "the one who heads."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ra-ee-sun'.
    *   Example: `الرَّئِيسُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Ar-ra'īsu dhakiyyun.) - The president is smart.

6.  **`وَزِيرٌ` (wazīrun) - Minister**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'wa-zee-run'.
    *   Example: `الْوَزِيرُ فِي الْمَدِينَةِ.` (Al-wazīru fī al-madeenati.) - The minister is in the city.

7.  **`بَرْلَمَانٌ` (barlamānun) - Parliament**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'bar-la-maan-un'.
    *   Example: `الْبَرْلَمَانُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-barlamānu muhimun.) - The parliament is important.

8.  **New Verb: "To Understand"**
    *   To make your politics vocabulary even more useful, let's learn a new verb:
    *   **Root:** `ف ه م` (f-h-m)
    *   **Past Tense (he understood):** `فَهِمَ` (fahima) - 'fa-hi-ma'
    *   **Present Tense (he understands):** `يَفْهَمُ` (yafhamu) - 'yaf-ha-mu'
    *   **Present Tense (I understand):** `أَفْهَمُ` (afhamu) - 'af-ha-mu'
    *   **Present Tense (she understands / you-m.s. understand):** `تَفْهَمُ` (tafhamu) - 'taf-ha-mu'
    *   Example: `أَفْهَمُ السِّيَاسَةَ.` (Afhamu as-siyāsata.) - I understand politics.

Now you have a solid set of words to start talking about politics! Remember to practice them with their correct genders and case endings.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned.

1.  **The government of the state is new.** (Idafa construct)
    *   `حُكُومَةُ الدَّوْلَةِ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Ḥukūmatu ad-dawlati jadeedatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `حُكُومَةُ` (government) is feminine, so `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new) is also feminine.

2.  **The president understands politics.**
    *   `الرَّئِيسُ يَفْهَمُ السِّيَاسَةَ.` (Ar-ra'īsu yafhamu as-siyāsata.)
        *   *Notice:* `السِّيَاسَةَ` (politics) takes a Fatha because it is the direct object of the verb `يَفْهَمُ`.

3.  **Indeed, the minister is in the parliament.** (Emphasis particle)
    *   `إِنَّ الْوَزِيرَ فِي الْبَرْلَمَانِ.` (Inna al-wazeera fī al-barlamāni.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْوَزِيرَ` (the minister) takes a Fatha because of `إِنَّ` (Day 37).

4.  **The people want to understand politics.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `الشَّعْبُ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَفْهَمَ السِّيَاسَةَ.` (Ash-sha'bu yureedu an yafhama as-siyāsata.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَفْهَمَ` (to understand) takes a Fatha because of `أَنْ` (Day 44).

5.  **She said that the government is important.** (Reported speech)
    *   `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Qālat anna al-ḥukūmata muhimmatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْحُكُومَةَ` (the government) takes a Fatha because of `أَنَّ` (Day 39). `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important) is feminine to match `الْحُكُومَةُ`.

6.  **I read the news about politics.** (News collocations)
    *   `أَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ عَنِ السِّيَاسَةِ.` (Aqra'u al-akhbāra 'ani as-siyāsati.)
        *   *New word*: `عَنْ` ('an) - about, from (a preposition). `عَنِ` is `عَنْ` + `الْـ` (similar to `مِنَ`). The noun after a preposition takes Kasra.
        *   *Notice:* `الْأَخْبَارَ` (the news) takes Fatha as object. `السِّيَاسَةِ` (politics) takes Kasra after `عَنِ`.

7.  **Maybe the president is here to understand the parliament.** (Modal expression + Purpose clause)
    *   `رُبَّمَا الرَّئِيسُ هُنَا لِيَفْهَمَ الْبَرْلَمَانَ.` (Rubbamā ar-ra'īsu hunā li-yafhamu al-barlamāna.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَفْهَمَ` (to understand) takes Fatha because of `لِـ` (Day 40).
