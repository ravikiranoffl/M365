# Day 58 - Education vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns, adjectives, and verbs related to education. Pay close attention to the gender of each noun and adjective, as this will affect how they are used in sentences!

1.  **`مَدْرَسَةٌ` (madrasatun) - School**
    *   You learned this word on Day 10! It's a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mad-ra-sa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمَدْرَسَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-madrasatū jadeedatun.) - The school is new.

2.  **`جَامِعَةٌ` (jāmi'atun) - University**
    *   You learned this word on Day 25! It's a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'jaa-mi-a-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْجَامِعَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-jāmi'atū kabeeratun.) - The university is big.

3.  **`طَالِبٌ` (ṭālibun) - Student (masculine)**
    *   You learned this word on Day 6! It's a **masculine** noun, also an active participle from `طَلَبَ` (to seek).
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ṭaa-li-bun'.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu dhakiyyun.) - The student is smart.

4.  **`طَالِبَةٌ` (ṭālibatun) - Student (feminine)**
    *   You learned this word on Day 6! It's a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ṭaa-li-ba-tun'.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibatū jadeedatun.) - The student (f.) is new.

5.  **`مُدَرِّسٌ` (mudarrisun) - Teacher (masculine)**
    *   You learned this word on Day 11! It's a **masculine** noun, also an active participle from `دَرَّسَ` (to teach).
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mu-dar-ri-sun'.
    *   Example: `الْمُدَرِّسُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-mudarrisu muhimun.) - The teacher is important.

6.  **`مُدَرِّسَةٌ` (mudarrisatun) - Teacher (feminine)**
    *   You learned this word on Day 11! It's a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mu-dar-ri-sa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ نَظِيفَةٌ.` (Al-mudarrisatū naẓīfatun.) - The teacher (f.) is clean. (Using `نَظِيفَةٌ` - clean, feminine from Day 56).

7.  **`فَصْلٌ` (faṣlun) - Classroom / Class (as in a group of students)**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'faṣ-lun'.
    *   Example: `الْفَصْلُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-faṣlu kabeerun.) - The classroom is big.

8.  **`دَرْسٌ` (darsun) - Lesson / Study**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It is related to the verb `دَرَسَ` (he studied) and the Masdar `دِرَاسَةٌ` (studying) you learned on Day 43. `دَرْسٌ` is the specific thing being taught or studied.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'dar-sun'.
    *   Example: `الدَّرْسُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Ad-darsu muhimun.) - The lesson is important.

9.  **`امْتِحَانٌ` (imtiḥānun) - Exam / Test**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'im-ti-ḥaa-nun'.
    *   Example: `الِامْتِحَانُ صَغِيرٌ.` (Al-imtiḥānu ṣagheerun.) - The exam is small (easy).

10. **`نَاجِحٌ` (nājiḥun) / `نَاجِحَةٌ` (nājiḥatun) - Successful**
    *   This is an adjective, also an active participle from `نَجَحَ` (to succeed). `نَاجِحٌ` for masculine singular, `نَاجِحَةٌ` for feminine singular.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'naa-ji-ḥun' / 'naa-ji-ḥa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ نَاجِحٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu nājiḥun.) - The student is successful.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبَةُ نَاجِحَةٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibatū nājiḥatun.) - The student (f.) is successful.

11. **`رَاسِبٌ` (rāsibun) / `رَاسِبَةٌ` (rāsibatun) - Failing / Unsuccessful**
    *   This is an adjective, also an active participle from `رَسَبَ` (to fail). `رَاسِبٌ` for masculine singular, `رَاسِبَةٌ` for feminine singular.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'raa-si-bun' / 'raa-si-ba-tun'.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ رَاسِبٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu rāsibun.) - The student is failing.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبَةُ رَاسِبَةٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibatū rāsibatun.) - The student (f.) is failing.

12. **New Verb: "To Succeed"**
    *   **Root:** `ن ج ح` (n-j-ḥ)
    *   **Past Tense (he succeeded):** `نَجَحَ` (najaḥa) - 'na-ja-ḥa'.
    *   **Present Tense (he succeeds):** `يَنْجَحُ` (yanjaḥu) - 'yan-ja-ḥu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I succeed):** `أَنْجَحُ` (anjaḥu) - 'an-ja-ḥu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she succeeds / you-m.s. succeed):** `تَنْجَحُ` (tanjaḥu) - 'tan-ja-ḥu'.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ يَنْجَحُ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yanjahu.) - The student succeeds.

13. **New Verb: "To Fail"**
    *   **Root:** `ر س ب` (r-s-b)
    *   **Past Tense (he failed):** `رَسَبَ` (rasaba) - 'ra-sa-ba'.
    *   **Present Tense (he fails):** `يَرْسُبُ` (yarsubu) - 'yar-su-bu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I fail):** `أَرْسُبُ` (arsubu) - 'ar-su-bu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she fails / you-m.s. fail):** `تَرْسُبُ` (tarsubu) - 'tar-su-bu'.
    *   Example: `الطَّالِبُ يَرْسُبُ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yarsubu.) - The student fails.

You now have a solid set of words to begin discussing education! Remember to pay attention to the gender and case endings of these words, especially when they combine with other grammar rules you know.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **The new student (f.) is in the big classroom.** (Nominal sentence + adjectives)
    *   `الطَّالِبَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ فِي الْفَصْلِ الْكَبِيرِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibatū al-jadeedatū fī al-faṣli al-kabeeri.)
        *   *Notice:* `الطَّالِبَةُ` (student) is feminine, so `الْجَدِيدَةُ` (new) is feminine. `الْفَصْلِ الْكَبِيرِ` (the big classroom) is a prepositional phrase, with `الْفَصْلِ` taking Kasra after `فِي`, and its adjective `الْكَبِيرِ` matching.

2.  **The teacher (m.) wants to study the lesson.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَدْرُسَ الدَّرْسَ.` (Al-mudarrisu yureedu an yadrusa ad-darsa.)
        *   *Notice:* `يُرِيدُ` (he wants - Day 44). `أَنْ يَدْرُسَ` (to study - `يَدْرُسُ` from Day 19, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `الدَّرْسَ` (the lesson) takes Fatha as the object.

3.  **Indeed, the exam is certainly important.** (Emphasis particles `إِنَّ` and `لَـ`)
    *   `إِنَّ الِامْتِحَانَ لَمُهِمٌّ.` (Inna al-imtiḥāna la-muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` (Day 37) makes `الِامْتِحَانَ` (the exam) take Fatha. `لَمُهِمٌّ` (certainly important) has `لَـ` attached, and is masculine to match `الِامْتِحَانَ`.

4.  **The successful student (m.) will go to the university.** (Future tense + adjective)
    *   `الطَّالِبُ النَّاجِحُ سَيَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu an-nājiḥu sa-yadhhabu ilā al-jāmi'ati.)
        *   *Notice:* `الطَّالِبُ النَّاجِحُ` (the successful student) is the subject with its adjective. `سَيَذْهَبُ` (he will go - Day 22) is the future tense verb. `إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ` (to the university) is a prepositional phrase.

5.  **She said that the failing student (f.) is sad.** (`حَزِينَةٌ` (ḥazīnatun) = sad, feminine.)
    *   `قَالَتْ أَنَّ الطَّالِبَةَ الرَّاسِبَةَ حَزِينَةٌ.` (Qālat anna aṭ-ṭālibata ar-rāsibata ḥazīnatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَتْ` (she said - Day 39). `أَنَّ` makes `الطَّالِبَةَ الرَّاسِبَةَ` (the failing student) take Fatha. `حَزِينَةٌ` (sad) is the feminine comment.

6.  **I go to the school to succeed in the lesson.** (Purpose clause + new verb)
    *   `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ لِأَنْجَحَ فِي الدَّرْسِ.` (Ana adhhaba ilā al-madrasati li'anjaḥa fī ad-darsi.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِأَنْجَحَ` (in order for me to succeed) where `أَنْجَحُ` takes Fatha because of `لِـ` (Day 40). `فِي الدَّرْسِ` (in the lesson) is a prepositional phrase.

7.  **Perhaps the minister will understand the importance of education.** (Modal expression + Idafa chain)
    *   `رُبَّمَا سَيَفْهَمُ الْوَزِيرُ أَهَمِّيَّةَ التَّعْلِيمِ.` (Rubbamā sa-yafhamu al-wazeeru ahammiyata at-ta'leemi.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `أَهَمِّيَّةٌ` (ahammiyyatun) - importance (feminine noun). `تَعْلِيمٌ` (ta'leemun) - education (masculine noun, Masdar). These are more advanced, so I will simplify this example for a beginner. Let's use `دِرَاسَةٌ` (studying) for education.
    *   *Revised Example 7:* **Perhaps the minister will understand the importance of studying.**
        *   `رُبَّمَا سَيَفْهَمُ الْوَزِيرُ أَهَمِّيَّةَ الدِّرَاسَةِ.` (Rubbamā sa-yafhamu al-wazeeru ahammiyata ad-dirāsati.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (maybe - Day 38). `سَيَفْهَمُ` (he will understand - Day 48, Day 22 for future). `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister - Day 48) is the subject. `أَهَمِّيَّةَ الدِّرَاسَةِ` is an Idafa. `أَهَمِّيَّةَ` (importance of) takes Fatha as the object of `يَفْهَمُ`. `الدِّرَاسَةِ` (studying - Masdar from Day 43) takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa.

8.  **The teacher (f.) understands the lesson.**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ تَفْهَمُ الدَّرْسَ.` (Al-mudarrisatū tafhamu ad-darsa.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ` (the teacher - f.) is feminine, so `تَفْهَمُ` (she understands - Day 48) is used. `الدَّرْسَ` (the lesson) is the object.
