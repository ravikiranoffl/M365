Today, we're going to learn about **Verb + preposition collocations**! This is a fancy way of saying that in Arabic, many verbs like to hang out with specific prepositions, and when they do, they create a special meaning together. It's like how in English you "listen *to*" music or "wait *for*" someone – the preposition is a fixed partner with the verb.

# Day 97 - Verb + preposition collocations

## Lesson

In Arabic, just like in English, verbs often require a specific preposition to complete their meaning or to express a particular nuance. Sometimes, the choice of preposition changes the meaning of the verb entirely! These fixed pairings of a verb and a preposition are called **collocations**, and they are very important to learn.

There isn't always a direct logical translation from English. For example, where English might use "to ask *about*", Arabic also uses a preposition that means "about". But sometimes, where English doesn't use a preposition, Arabic does, or it uses a different one. The best way to learn these is by memorizing them as a pair.

Let's look at some common and useful verb + preposition collocations:

### 1. `سَأَلَ / يَسْأَلُ عَنْ` - To ask about

You already know `سَأَلَ / يَسْأَلُ` (he asked / he asks) from Day 79. When you want to specify *what* someone is asking about, you use the preposition `عَنْ` (about).

*   **New collocation:** `سَأَلَ عَنْ` (sa'ala 'an) / `يَسْأَلُ عَنْ` (yas'alu 'an)
*   **Meaning:** to ask about.
*   *How to use:* `عَنْ` is a preposition, so the noun that follows it will be in the **Genitive case** (taking Kasra).
*   *Example:* `يَسْأَلُ الطَّالِبُ عَنِ الدَّرْسِ.` (Yas'alu aṭ-ṭālibu 'ani ad-darsi.)
    *   *Translation:* The student asks about the lesson.
    *   *Breakdown:* `يَسْأَلُ` (he asks). `الطَّالِبُ` (the student - subject, Damma). `عَنِ` (about - takes Kasra to avoid two Sukoons). `الدَّرْسِ` (the lesson - Genitive, Kasra).

### 2. `بَحَثَ / يَبْحَثُ عَنْ` - To search for / To look for

This is a very common verb for finding things. When you want to say you are "searching *for*" something, you use the preposition `عَنْ`.

*   **New Verb:** `بَحَثَ` (baḥatha) - 'ba-ḥa-tha' (he searched, past tense).
*   **New Verb:** `يَبْحَثُ` (yabḥathu) - 'yab-ḥa-thu' (he searches, present tense).
*   **New collocation:** `بَحَثَ عَنْ` (baḥatha 'an) / `يَبْحَثُ عَنْ` (yabḥathu 'an)
*   **Meaning:** to search for / to look for.
*   *How to use:* `عَنْ` is a preposition, so the noun that follows it will be in the **Genitive case** (taking Kasra).
*   *Example:* `يَبْحَثُ الْمُدِيرُ عَنِ الْكِتَابِ.` (Yabḥathu al-mudeeru 'ani al-kitābi.)
    *   *Translation:* The manager searches for the book.
    *   *Breakdown:* `يَبْحَثُ` (he searches). `الْمُدِيرُ` (the manager - subject, Damma). `عَنِ` (for). `الْكِتَابِ` (the book - Genitive, Kasra).

### 3. `تَكَلَّمَ / يَتَكَلَّمُ عَنْ` - To speak about

You know `تَكَلَّمَ / يَتَكَلَّمُ` (he spoke / he speaks) from Day 59. When you want to say you are "speaking *about*" a topic, you use the preposition `عَنْ`.

*   **New collocation:** `تَكَلَّمَ عَنْ` (takallama 'an) / `يَتَكَلَّمُ عَنْ` (yatakallamu 'an)
*   **Meaning:** to speak about.
*   *How to use:* `عَنْ` is a preposition, so the noun that follows it will be in the **Genitive case** (taking Kasra).
*   *Example:* `تَكَلَّمَ الْوَزِيرُ عَنِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ.` (Takallama al-wazeeru 'ani al-mushkilati.)
    *   *Translation:* The minister spoke about the problem.
    *   *Breakdown:* `تَكَلَّمَ` (he spoke). `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister - subject, Damma). `عَنِ` (about). `الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (the problem - Genitive, Kasra).

### 4. `رَغِبَ / يَرْغَبُ فِي` - To desire / To want / To be interested in

This verb expresses desire or interest. In Arabic, it takes the preposition `فِي` (in) to mean "to desire/want" something.

*   **New Verb:** `رَغِبَ` (raghiba) - 'ra-ghi-ba' (he desired, past tense).
*   **New Verb:** `يَرْغَبُ` (yarghabu) - 'yar-gha-bu' (he desires, present tense).
*   **New collocation:** `رَغِبَ فِي` (raghiba fī) / `يَرْغَبُ فِي` (yarghabu fī)
*   **Meaning:** to desire / to want (something) / to be interested in.
*   *How to use:* `فِي` is a preposition, so the noun that follows it will be in the **Genitive case** (taking Kasra).
*   *Example:* `يَرْغَبُ الْجَمِيعُ فِي السَّلَامِ.` (Yarghabu al-jamee'u fī as-salāmi.)
    *   *Translation:* Everyone desires peace.
    *   *Breakdown:* `يَرْغَبُ` (he desires). `الْجَمِيعُ` (everyone - subject, Damma). `فِي` (in/for). `السَّلَامِ` (peace - Genitive, Kasra).

### 5. `بَدَأَ / يَبْدَأُ بِـ` - To start with / To begin by

This verb means "to start" or "to begin." When you want to specify *what* you start with or *how* you begin, you often use the preposition `بِـ` (with / by means of).

*   **New Verb:** `بَدَأَ` (bada'a) - 'ba-da-a' (he started, past tense).
*   **New Verb:** `يَبْدَأُ` (yabda'u) - 'yab-da-u' (he starts, present tense).
*   **New collocation:** `بَدَأَ بِـ` (bada'a bi-) / `يَبْدَأُ بِـ` (yabda'u bi-)
*   **Meaning:** to start with / to begin by.
*   *How to use:* `بِـ` is a preposition, so the noun that follows it will be in the **Genitive case** (taking Kasra).
*   *Example:* `بَدَأَ الطَّالِبُ بِقِرَاءَةِ الْكِتَابِ.` (Bada'a aṭ-ṭālibu bi-qirā'ati al-kitābi.)
    *   *Translation:* The student started by reading the book.
    *   *Breakdown:* `بَدَأَ` (he started). `الطَّالِبُ` (the student - subject, Damma). `بِـ` (by/with). `قِرَاءَةِ` (reading - Masdar/verbal noun, Genitive, because it follows `بِـ`. This is the first term of an Idafa). `الْكِتَابِ` (the book - Genitive, second term of Idafa).

Learning these collocations will make your Arabic sound much more natural and precise!

## Examples

Let's see these verb + preposition collocations in more complex sentences, combining them with grammar you've already mastered.

1.  **"The minister (f.) searched for a solution to the difficult problem."**
    *   `بَحَثَتِ الْوَزِيرَةُ عَنْ حَلٍّ لِلْمُشْكِلَةِ الصَّعْبَةِ.` (Baḥathati al-wazeeratu 'an ḥallin li-l-mushkilati aṣ-ṣa'bati.)
        *   *Notice:* `بَحَثَتِ` (she searched - past tense). `الْوَزِيرَةُ` (subject). `عَنْ حَلٍّ` (for a solution - `حَلٌّ` is a new noun meaning "solution," but it was implicitly used in Day 90 translation. I'll add it to core vocab for today). `لِلْمُشْكِلَةِ الصَّعْبَةِ` (for the difficult problem).

2.  **"Everyone wants to ask about the new policy."**
    *   `يَرْغَبُ الْجَمِيعُ فِي أَنْ يَسْأَلُوا عَنِ السِّيَاسَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Yarghabu al-jamee'u fī an yas'aloo 'ani as-siyāsati al-jadeedati.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَرْغَبُ الْجَمِيعُ فِي` (everyone desires in/wants). `أَنْ يَسْأَلُوا` (that they ask - Subjunctive with Nūn-drop, Day 60). `عَنِ السِّيَاسَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (about the new policy).

3.  **"The understanding manager (m.) began by announcing the joint project."**
    *   `بَدَأَ الْمُدِيرُ الْفَاهِمُ بِإِعْلَانِ الْمَشْرُوعِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ.` (Bada'a al-mudeeru al-fāhimu bi-i'lāni al-mashroo'i al-mushtaraki.)
        *   *Notice:* `بَدَأَ` (he started). `الْمُدِيرُ الْفَاهِمُ` (the understanding manager - apposition with active participle, Day 85, 88). `بِإِعْلَانِ` (by announcing - `إِعْلَانٌ` is a Masdar, Genitive after `بِـ`, Day 64, 86). `الْمَشْرُوعِ الْمُشْتَرَكِ` (the joint project - Idafa, Day 67, 79).

4.  **"It seems that the student, Omar, desires to speak about the written message."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ الطَّالِبَ عُمَرَ يَرْغَبُ فِي أَنْ يَتَكَلَّمَ عَنِ الرِّسَالَةِ الْمَكْتُوبَةِ.` (Yabdū anna aṭ-ṭāliba 'Umarā yarghabu fī an yatakallama 'ani ar-risālati al-maktūbati.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (hedging, Day 80). `الطَّالِبَ عُمَرَ` (apposition, Fatha due to `أَنَّ`, Day 85). `يَرْغَبُ فِي أَنْ يَتَكَلَّمَ` (desires to speak - `يَتَكَلَّمَ` is Subjunctive due to `أَنْ`, Day 59). `عَنِ الرِّسَالَةِ الْمَكْتُوبَةِ` (about the written message - passive participle as adjective, Day 88).

5.  **"Although the minister (m.) returned from Cairo, he is still searching for the lost solution."**
    *   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ عَادَ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ، فَهُوَ يَبْحَثُ عَنِ الْحَلِّ الْمَفْقُودِ.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-wazeera 'āda mina al-qāhirati, fa-huwa yabḥathu 'ani al-ḥalli al-mafqoodi.)
        *   *New word for explanation:* `مَفْقُودٌ` (mafqoodun) - lost (masculine passive participle). This is a new word for "lost", I should use a known one. Let's rephrase this.
        *   **Revised Example 5:** **"Although the minister (m.) returned from Cairo, he is still searching for the understood problem's solution."** (using 'understood problem' as context, and `حَلٌّ` for solution).
            `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ عَادَ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ، فَهُوَ يَبْحَثُ عَنْ حَلِّ الْمُشْكِلَةِ الْمَفْهُومَةِ.` (Wa-ma'a anna al-wazeera 'āda mina al-qāhirati, fa-huwa yabḥathu 'an ḥalli al-mushkilati al-mafhūmati.)
            *   *Notice:* `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (contrast, Day 81). `الْوَزِيرَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `عَادَ مِنَ الْقَاهِرَةِ` (motion verb, Day 78, 87). `فَـ هُوَ يَبْحَثُ` (so he searches). `عَنْ حَلِّ الْمُشْكِلَةِ الْمَفْهُومَةِ` (for the solution of the understood problem - Idafa with passive participle adjective, Day 88).
