# Day 54 - Statistics language

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns, adjectives, and a verb that will help you understand and discuss statistics in Arabic. Pay close attention to the gender of each noun, as this will affect how adjectives describe them!

1.  **`إِحْصَاءٌ` (iḥṣā'un) - Statistics / Census**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It refers to the field of statistics or a count of a population.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ih-ṣaa-un'.
    *   Example: `الْإِحْصَاءُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-iḥṣā'u muhimun.) - Statistics is important. (Using `مُهِمٌّ` - important, from Day 43).

2.  **`رَقْمٌ` (raqmun) - Number / Figure**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It means a single numerical value.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'raq-mun'.
    *   Its **broken plural** is `أَرْقَامٌ` (arqāmun) - numbers / figures. (Remember the rule for non-human broken plurals with adjectives from Day 24 and Day 45!)
    *   Example: `الرَّقْمُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Ar-raqmu kabeerun.) - The number is big. (Using `كَبِيرٌ` - big, from Day 3).
    *   Example: `الْأَرْقَامُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-arqāmu jadeedatun.) - The numbers are new. (Note: `جَدِيدَةٌ` is feminine singular to match `أَرْقَامٌ`).

3.  **`زِيَادَةٌ` (ziyādatun) - Increase**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun. It's a verbal noun (Masdar) from the verb "to increase."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'zee-yaa-da-tun'.
    *   Example: `الزِّيَادَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Az-ziyādatu muhimmatun.) - The increase is important. (Using `مُهِمَّةٌ` - important, feminine from Day 43).

4.  **`نَقْصٌ` (naqṣun) - Decrease / Shortage / Lack**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It's a verbal noun (Masdar) from the verb "to decrease."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'naq-ṣun'.
    *   Example: `النَّقْصُ صَغِيرٌ.` (An-naqṣu ṣagheerun.) - The decrease is small. (Using `صَغِيرٌ` - small, from Day 3).

5.  **`نِسْبَةٌ مِئَوِيَّةٌ` (nisbatun mi'awiyyatun) - Percentage**
    *   This is a compound term. `نِسْبَةٌ` (nisbatun) means "ratio" or "rate" (feminine noun). `مِئَوِيَّةٌ` (mi'awiyyatun) is an adjective meaning "percentage-related" (feminine adjective). Together, they mean "percentage."
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'nis-ba-tun mi'-a-wiy-ya-tun'.
    *   Example: `النِّسْبَةُ الْمِئَوِيَّةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (An-nisbatū al-mi'awiyyatū kabeeratun.) - The percentage is big.

6.  **`مُتَوَسِّطٌ` (mutawassiṭun) / `مُتَوَسِّطَةٌ` (mutawassiṭatun) - Average**
    *   This word can be used as an adjective or implicitly as a noun for "the average." `مُتَوَسِّطٌ` (masculine singular), `مُتَوَسِّطَةٌ` (feminine singular).
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mu-ta-was-si-ṭun' / 'mu-ta-was-si-ṭa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الرَّقْمُ مُتَوَسِّطٌ.` (Ar-raqmu mutawassiṭun.) - The number is average.
    *   Example: `الْمُتَوَسِّطُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-mutawassiṭu muhimun.) - The average is important. (Here `الْمُتَوَسِّطُ` acts as a noun).

7.  **New Verb: "To Indicate / To Show"**
    *   This verb is very useful for describing what statistics or figures do.
    *   **Root:** `ش و ر` (sh-w-r), but often seen in form IV (Day 16 reminder: roots can form different verb patterns).
    *   **Past Tense (he indicated):** `أَشَارَ` (ashāra) - 'a-shaa-ra'.
    *   **Present Tense (he indicates):** `يُشِيرُ` (yusheeru) - 'yu-shee-ru'.
    *   **Present Tense (I indicate):** `أُشِيرُ` (usheeru) - 'u-shee-ru'.
    *   **Present Tense (she indicates / you-m.s. indicate):** `تُشِيرُ` (tusheeru) - 'tu-shee-ru'.
    *   This verb almost always takes the preposition `إِلَى` (ilā) - to, towards (Day 10). So, "he indicates to" is `يُشِيرُ إِلَى`.
    *   Example: `الْإِحْصَاءُ يُشِيرُ إِلَى الزِّيَادَةِ.` (Al-iḥṣā'u yusheeru ilā az-ziyādati.) - The statistics indicate the increase. (`الزِّيَادَةِ` takes Kasra after `إِلَى`).

You now have a foundational vocabulary to begin discussing statistical information! Remember to pay attention to the gender and case endings of these words, especially when they combine with other grammar rules you know.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **The statistics are important for the state.** (Nominal sentence + preposition)
    *   `الْإِحْصَاءُ مُهِمٌّ لِلدَّوْلَةِ.` (Al-iḥṣā'u muhimun li-d-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْإِحْصَاءُ` (statistics) is masculine, so `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is masculine. `لِلدَّوْلَةِ` (for the state) uses the preposition `لِـ` (Day 10), so `الدَّوْلَةِ` (state - Day 48) takes Kasra.

2.  **The numbers show the big increase.** (New verb + direct object Idafa)
    *   `الْأَرْقَامُ تُشِيرُ إِلَى الزِّيَادَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ.` (Al-arqāmu tusheeru ilā az-ziyādati al-kabeerati.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْأَرْقَامُ` (the numbers) is a non-human broken plural, so `تُشِيرُ` (they indicate) uses the feminine singular form of the verb (Day 24, for `هِيَ`). `إِلَى الزِّيَادَةِ الْكَبِيرَةِ` (to the big increase) is a prepositional phrase, with `الزِّيَادَةِ` taking Kasra, and its adjective `الْكَبِيرَةِ` matching in definiteness, gender (feminine), and case (Kasra).

3.  **Indeed, the percentage is certainly small.** (Emphasis particles `إِنَّ` and `لَـ`)
    *   `إِنَّ النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ لَصَغِيرَةٌ.` (Inna an-nisbata al-mi'awiyyata la-ṣagheeratun.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` (Day 37) makes `النِّسْبَةَ الْمِئَوِيَّةَ` take Fatha. `لَصَغِيرَةٌ` (certainly small) has `لَـ` attached, and is feminine to match `النِّسْبَةُ الْمِئَوِيَّةُ`.

4.  **He said that the average is important for the economy.** (Reported speech + `أَنَّ`)
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْمُتَوَسِّطَ مُهِمٌّ لِلِاقْتِصَادِ.` (Qāla anna al-mutawassiṭa muhimun li-l-iqtiṣādi.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَ` (he said - Day 39). `أَنَّ` makes `الْمُتَوَسِّطَ` (the average) take Fatha. `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is the comment. `لِلِاقْتِصَادِ` (for the economy - Day 49).

5.  **Understanding the numbers is useful for the minister.** (Masdar in Idafa)
    *   `فَهْمُ الْأَرْقَامِ مُفِيدٌ لِلْوَزِيرِ.` (Fahmu al-arqāmi mufīdun li-l-wazeeri.)
        *   *Notice:* `فَهْمُ` (understanding - Masdar from Day 52) is the first noun of the Idafa, masculine, so `مُفِيدٌ` (useful - Day 43) is masculine. `الْأَرْقَامِ` is the second noun of the Idafa, taking Kasra. `لِلْوَزِيرِ` (for the minister - Day 48).

6.  **I want to understand the increase and the decrease in the state.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَفْهَمَ الزِّيَادَةَ وَ النَّقْصَ فِي الدَّوْلَةِ.` (Ureedu an afhama az-ziyādata wa an-naqṣa fī ad-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `أُرِيدُ` (I want - Day 44). `أَنْ أَفْهَمَ` (to understand - `أَفْهَمُ` from Day 48, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `الزِّيَادَةَ` (the increase) and `النَّقْصَ` (the decrease) are both direct objects, taking Fatha. `فِي الدَّوْلَةِ` (in the state).

7.  **Maybe the minister will seek a solution to the problem of decrease.** (Modal expression + purpose clause implicitly + Idafa chain)
    *   `رُبَّمَا يَطْلُبُ الْوَزِيرُ حَلَّ مُشْكِلَةِ النَّقْصِ.` (Rubbamā yaṭlubu al-wazeeru ḥalla mushkilati an-naqṣi.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا` (maybe - Day 38). `يَطْلُبُ` (he seeks - Day 51). `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister - Day 48) is the subject. `حَلَّ مُشْكِلَةِ النَّقْصِ` (solution to the problem of decrease) is a chained Idafa. `حَلَّ` (solution - Masdar from Day 52) takes Fatha as it's the object of `يَطْلُبُ`. `مُشْكِلَةِ` (problem of - Day 50) takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa. `النَّقْصِ` (decrease) takes Kasra as the third noun in the Idafa.

*   مُتَوَسِّطٌ (mutawassiṭun) - average (masculine adjective/noun)
*   مُتَوَسِّطَةٌ (mutawassiṭatun) - average (feminine adjective)
*   أَشَارَ (ashāra) - he indicated / showed (past tense verb)

*   تُشِيرُ (tusheeru) - she indicates / shows / you (m.s.) indicate / show (present tense verb)
