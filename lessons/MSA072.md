# Day 72 - Advanced passive structures

## Lesson

In English, we say "The boy wrote the message" (active voice) or "The message **was written**" (passive voice). In the passive voice, the original *object* of the sentence becomes the *new subject*. Arabic also has a passive voice, and it's formed by changing the vowel sounds (Harakat) within the verb itself!

### 1. The Passive Verb: `الْفِعْلُ الْمَبْنِيُّ لِلْمَجْهُولِ` (al-fi'lu al-mabniyyu lil-majhoōli)

This long Arabic phrase means "the verb built for the unknown" (or "passive verb"). When we make a verb passive, its sounds change.

### 2. The New Subject: `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` (nā'ibu al-fā'ili)

In a passive sentence, the word that receives the action (what *was* the object) becomes the new subject. We call this the **"deputy of the doer"** or `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`. This `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` always takes a **Damma** (ُ) at the end, just like a normal subject. The passive verb must also agree with its `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` in gender and number.

### 3. Forming the Passive Voice for Different Verb Forms

Let's look at how verbs change to become passive in both past and present tenses, focusing on common verb patterns (Forms) you've encountered.

#### A. Form I Verbs (Simple 3-letter verbs)

*   **Past Tense:**
    *   **Rule:** The first letter takes Damma (ُ), and the letter before the last takes Kasra (ِ). All other letters are as they were.
    *   **Pattern:** `فَعَلَ` (fa'ala) `--->` `فُعِلَ` (fu'ila) - "he did" `--->` "it was done"
    *   **Example 1: `كَتَبَ` (kataba - he wrote)**
        *   Passive Past: `كُتِبَ` (kutiba) - "it was written"
        *   Example: `كُتِبَتِ الرِّسَالَةُ.` (Kutibatī ar-risālatu.) - The message was written. (Notice the `ـَتْ` for feminine `الرِّسَالَةُ`).
    *   **Example 2: `فَهِمَ` (fahima - he understood)**
        *   Passive Past: `فُهِمَ` (fuhima) - "it was understood"
        *   Example: `فُهِمَ الدَّرْسُ.` (Fuhima ad-darsu.) - The lesson was understood.

*   **Present Tense:**
    *   **Rule:** The first letter (the `يـ` or `تـ` or `أَ` or `نَـ` prefix) takes Damma (ُ), and the letter before the last takes Fatha (َ). All other letters are as they were.
    *   **Pattern:** `يَفْعَلُ` (yaf'alu) `--->` `يُفْعَلُ` (yuf'alu) - "he does" `--->` "it is being done / will be done"
    *   **Example 1: `يَكْتُبُ` (yaktubu - he writes)**
        *   Passive Present: `يُكْتَبُ` (yuktabu) - "it is written" / "it will be written"
        *   Example: `تُكْتَبُ الرِّسَالَةُ.` (Tuktabu ar-risālatu.) - The message is written. (Notice the `تُـ` for feminine `الرِّسَالَةُ`).
    *   **Example 2: `يَفْهَمُ` (yafhamu - he understands)**
        *   Passive Present: `يُفْهَمُ` (yufhamu) - "it is understood"
        *   Example: `يُفْهَمُ الدَّرْسُ.` (Yufhamu ad-darsu.) - The lesson is understood.

#### B. Other Common Verb Forms

The same Damma-on-first-letter and specific-vowel-before-last-letter rules apply to other verb forms you've seen.

*   **Form II (e.g., `دَرَّسَ` - he taught, `يُدَرِّسُ` - he teaches)**
    *   Passive Past: `دُرِّسَ` (durrisa) - "it was taught" (Damma on first, Kasra on second-to-last, Shaddah remains)
    *   Passive Present: `يُدَرَّسُ` (yudarrasu) - "it is taught" (Damma on prefix, Fatha on second-to-last, Shaddah remains)
    *   *Example:* `دُرِّسَ الدَّرْسُ.` (Durrisa ad-darsu.) - The lesson was taught.
    *   *Example:* `تُدَرَّسُ اللُّغَةُ.` (Tudarrasu al-lughatu.) - The language is taught. (`اللُّغَةُ` - language, Day 51).

*   **Form IV (e.g., `أَرْسَلَ` - he sent, `يُرْسِلُ` - he sends)**
    *   Passive Past: `أُرْسِلَ` (ursila) - "it was sent" (Damma on first, Kasra on second-to-last)
    *   Passive Present: `يُرْسَلُ` (yursalu) - "it is sent" (Damma on prefix, Fatha on second-to-last)
    *   *Example:* `أُرْسِلَتِ الرِّسَالَةُ.` (Ursilatī ar-risālatu.) - The message was sent.
    *   *Example:* `تُرْسَلُ الْمَعْلُومَاتُ.` (Tursalu al-ma'lūmātu.) - The information is sent. (`الْمَعْلُومَاتُ` - information, Day 55).

*   **Form X (e.g., `اِسْتَخْدَمَ` - he used, `يَسْتَخْدِمُ` - he uses)**
    *   Passive Past: `اُسْتُخْدِمَ` (ustukhdima) - "it was used" (Damma on first two, Kasra on second-to-last)
    *   Passive Present: `يُسْتَخْدَمُ` (yustakhdamu) - "it is used" (Damma on prefix, Fatha on second-to-last)
    *   *Example:* `اُسْتُخْدِمَ الْحَاسُوبُ.` (Ustukhdima al-ḥāsūbu.) - The computer was used.
    *   *Example:* `يُسْتَخْدَمُ الْمَاءُ.` (Yustakhdamu al-mā'u.) - The water is used.

### 4. Specifying the Doer (Optional - "by...")

If you *do* want to mention who performed the action in a passive sentence, you use `مِنْ قِبَلِ` (min qibali) which means "by" or "by means of." The noun following `قِبَلِ` takes a Kasra.
*   *Example:* `كُتِبَتِ الرِّسَالَةُ مِنَ الْوَلَدِ.` (Kutibatī ar-risālatu mina al-waladi.) - The message was written **by the boy**.

This is a very important concept in Arabic, especially in news and formal language, where often the agent is not mentioned or is less important than the action itself.

## Examples

Let's see more examples of these advanced passive structures!

1.  **"The new law was understood."**
    *   `فُهِمَ الْقَانُونُ الْجَدِيدُ.` (Fuhima al-qānūnu al-jadeedu.)
        *   *Notice:* `فُهِمَ` is the passive past of `فَهِمَ`. `الْقَانُونُ الْجَدِيدُ` (the new law) is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`, taking Damma.

2.  **"The problem is solved."**
    *   `تُحَلُّ الْمُشْكِلَةُ.` (Tuḥallu al-mushkilatu.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُحَلُّ` is the passive present of `يَحُلُّ` (he solves - Day 50), which is a geminated verb. The Damma on the prefix and the Damma on the last letter (due to the doubling) makes it passive. `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` (the problem) is feminine, so the verb starts with `تُـ`.

3.  **"The sick woman was seen by the doctor (f.)."**
    *   `شُوهِدَتِ الْمَرْأَةُ الْمَرِيضَةُ مِنَ الطَّبِيبَةِ.` (Shoohidatī al-mar'atu al-mareeḍatu mina aṭ-ṭabeebati.)
        *   *New passive verb:* `شُوهِدَ` (shoohida) - "he was seen" (passive of `شَاهَدَ` - he saw/watched, Form III).
        *   *Notice:* `شُوهِدَتْ` for the feminine `الْمَرْأَةُ الْمَرِيضَةُ`. `مِنَ الطَّبِيبَةِ` (by the doctor - f.).

4.  **"The information is explained."**
    *   `تُشْرَحُ الْمَعْلُومَاتُ.` (Tushraḥu al-ma'lūmātu.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُشْرَحُ` is the passive present of `تَشْرَحُ` (she explains - Day 68). `الْمَعْلُومَاتُ` (the information) is a sound feminine plural, which takes Damma as `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`. The verb takes `تُـ` because `الْمَعْلُومَاتُ` is treated as feminine singular for verb agreement.

5.  **"It is believed that the agreement is important."**
    *   `يُعْتَقَدُ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Yu'taqadu anna al-ittifāqa muhimun.)
        *   *New passive verb:* `يُعْتَقَدُ` (yu'taqadu) - "it is believed" (passive of `يَعْتَقِدُ` - he believes, Form VIII).
        *   *Notice:* This is an impersonal passive. The `أَنَّ` clause acts as the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` in meaning.

6.  **"The environment will be protected."**
    *   `سَتُحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةُ.` (Satuḥfaẓu al-bī'atu.)
        *   *Notice:* `سَـ` (will - Day 22) + `تُحْفَظُ` (it is protected - passive present of `تَحْفَظُ` - she protects, Day 56). `الْبِيئَةُ` (the environment) is feminine.

7.  **"The computer was not used."**
    *   `لَمْ يُسْتَخْدَمِ الْحَاسُوبُ.` (Lam yustakhdami al-ḥāsūbu.)
        *   *Notice:* `لَمْ` (did not - Day 60) makes the passive present verb `يُسْتَخْدَمُ` (it is used) become Jussive `يُسْتَخْدَمْ`. Here, it takes Kasra `يُسْتَخْدَمِ` because of the definite article `الـ` following it. `الْحَاسُوبُ` (the computer) is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`.

8.  **"The student (m.) was taught the lesson."**
    *   `دُرِّسَ الطَّالِبُ الدَّرْسَ.` (Durrisa aṭ-ṭālibu ad-darsa.)
        *   *Notice:* This example is tricky. `الطَّالِبُ` (the student) is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`. The verb `دَرَّسَ` (to teach) takes two objects in the active (he taught the student the lesson). In the passive, one object becomes the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` and the other remains as an object (taking Fatha). We will stick to simpler passive structures for now, where there is only one clear `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` and no second object.

    *   *Revised Example 8 (Simpler):* **"The lesson was taught."**
        *   `دُرِّسَ الدَّرْسُ.` (Durrisa ad-darsu.)
        *   *Notice:* `دُرِّسَ` is the passive past of `دَرَّسَ` (he taught). `الدَّرْسُ` (the lesson) is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`.
