# Day 37 - Emphasis particles

## Lesson

1.  **What are Emphasis Particles?**
    *   Emphasis particles are small words or prefixes that we add to sentences to make them stronger, more certain, or to confirm a fact.
    *   Think of them as saying "Indeed," "Certainly," or "Verily" in English.

2.  **Introducing `إِنَّ` (inna) - "Indeed," "Certainly," "Verily"**
    *   This is the most common and important emphasis particle.
    *   **Rule 1: Placement.** `إِنَّ` always comes at the **beginning of a nominal sentence** (an "X is Y" sentence, where X is the `الْمُبْتَدَأُ` - Topic, and Y is the `الْخَبَرُ` - Comment).
    *   **Rule 2: Changing the Topic's Vowel!** This is the crucial part! `إِنَّ` acts like a powerful magnet that pulls down the vowel of the **Topic** (`الْمُبْتَدَأُ`) to a **Fatha ( َ )**.
        *   So, if the topic usually has a Damma ( ُ ) at the end, after `إِنَّ` it will have a Fatha ( َ ).
        *   The Topic after `إِنَّ` is no longer called `الْمُبْتَدَأُ`; it's now called `اسْمُ إِنَّ` (ismu inna - the name of inna).
    *   **Rule 3: The Comment Stays the Same.** The **Comment** (`الْخَبَرُ`) keeps its usual Damma ( ُ ) or Tanween Damma ( ٌ ) at the end. It's now called `خَبَرُ إِنَّ` (khabaru inna - the news of inna).

    *   **Let's see the change:**
        *   Normal Sentence: `الْكِتَابُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-kitābu jadeedun.) - The book is new.
            *   (`الْكِتَابُ` is Topic, Damma. `جَدِيدٌ` is Comment, Tanween Damma.)
        *   With `إِنَّ`: `إِنَّ الْكِتَابَ جَدِيدٌ.` (Inna al-kitāba jadeedun.) - Indeed, the book is new.
            *   *Notice*: `الْكِتَابَ` now has a Fatha ( َ ) because of `إِنَّ`. `جَدِيدٌ` still has Tanween Damma.

    *   **Using `إِنَّ` with Pronouns:**
        *   You've learned `لِأَنَّهُ` (because he/it is) and `لِأَنَّهَا` (because she/it is) on Day 28. This is similar. `إِنَّ` combines with independent pronouns to form attached pronouns that take the Fatha case.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `هُوَ` (he/it is) = `إِنَّهُ` (innahu) - Indeed, he is / it is.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `هِيَ` (she/it is) = `إِنَّهَا` (innahā) - Indeed, she is / it is.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `أَنْتَ` (you-m) = `إِنَّكَ` (innaka) - Indeed, you (m) are.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `أَنْتِ` (you-f) = `إِنَّكِ` (innaki) - Indeed, you (f) are.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `أَنَا` (I am) = `إِنِّي` (innī) - Indeed, I am. (or `إِنَّنِي`)
        *   `إِنَّ` + `نَحْنُ` (we are) = `إِنَّنَا` (innanā) - Indeed, we are.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `هُمْ` (they-m) = `إِنَّهُمْ` (innahum) - Indeed, they (m) are.
        *   `إِنَّ` + `هُنَّ` (they-f) = `إِنَّهُنَّ` (innahunna) - Indeed, they (f) are.

3.  **Introducing `لَـ` (la-) - An Additional Emphatic Prefix**
    *   Sometimes, after `إِنَّ`, Arabic speakers want to add *even more* emphasis!
    *   You can add a prefix `لَـ` (la-) to the **Comment** (`الْخَبَرُ`) of the sentence.
    *   This `لَـ` simply means "certainly" or "indeed" and does **not** change the vowel ending of the comment. It just attaches to it.
    *   **Structure:** `إِنَّ` + `اسْمُ إِنَّ (Fatha)` + `لَـ الْخَبَرُ (Damma/Tanween Damma)`

    *   **Let's combine `إِنَّ` and `لَـ`:**
        *   Normal Sentence: `الْكِتَابُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-kitābu jadeedun.) - The book is new.
        *   With `إِنَّ`: `إِنَّ الْكِتَابَ جَدِيدٌ.` (Inna al-kitāba jadeedun.) - Indeed, the book is new.
        *   With `إِنَّ` and `لَـ`: `إِنَّ الْكِتَابَ لَجَدِيدٌ.` (Inna al-kitāba lajadeedun.) - Indeed, the book is certainly new!
            *   *Notice*: `الْكِتَابَ` has Fatha, `لَجَدِيدٌ` has Tanween Damma, just with `لَـ` added.

This is a powerful way to add nuance and strength to your statements!

## Examples

Let's put `إِنَّ` and `لَـ` into practice with sentences you already know!

**1. Using `إِنَّ` with a noun topic:**

*   **Original:** `الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu dhakiyyun.) - The student is smart.
*   **Emphatic:** `إِنَّ الطَّالِبَ ذَكِيٌّ.` (Inna aṭ-ṭāliba dhakiyyun.) - Indeed, the student is smart.
    *   *Notice*: `الطَّالِبَ` (the student) now has Fatha.

*   **Original:** `الْمَدْرَسَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-madrasatu kabeeratun.) - The school is big.
*   **Emphatic:** `إِنَّ الْمَدْرَسَةَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Inna al-madrasata kabeeratun.) - Indeed, the school is big.
    *   *Notice*: `الْمَدْرَسَةَ` (the school) now has Fatha.

*   **Original:** `الْقَلَمُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Al-qalamu fawqa al-maktabi.) - The pen is on the desk.
*   **Emphatic:** `إِنَّ الْقَلَمَ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Inna al-qalama fawqa al-maktabi.) - Indeed, the pen is on the desk.
    *   *Notice*: `الْقَلَمَ` (the pen) now has Fatha. The comment `فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ` does not change.

**2. Using `إِنَّ` with pronouns:**

*   **Original:** `هُوَ مُدَرِّسٌ.` (Huwa mudarrisun.) - He is a teacher.
*   **Emphatic:** `إِنَّهُ مُدَرِّسٌ.` (Innahu mudarrisun.) - Indeed, he is a teacher.
    *   *Notice*: `إِنَّ` combines with `هُوَ` to become `إِنَّهُ`.

*   **Original:** `هِيَ طَالِبَةٌ.` (Hiya taalibatun.) - She is a student.
*   **Emphatic:** `إِنَّهَا طَالِبَةٌ.` (Innahā taalibatun.) - Indeed, she is a student.
    *   *Notice*: `إِنَّ` combines with `هِيَ` to become `إِنَّهَا`.

*   **Original:** `أَنْتَ جَدِيدٌ.` (Anta jadeedun.) - You (m) are new.
*   **Emphatic:** `إِنَّكَ جَدِيدٌ.` (Innaka jadeedun.) - Indeed, you (m) are new.

**3. Using `إِنَّ` and `لَـ` together:**

*   **Original:** `الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-baytu kabeerun.) - The house is big.
*   **Strongly Emphatic:** `إِنَّ الْبَيْتَ لَكَبِيرٌ.` (Inna al-bayta lakabeerun.) - Indeed, the house is certainly big!
    *   *Notice*: `الْبَيْتَ` has Fatha, `لَكَبِيرٌ` has Tanween Damma with `لَـ` attached.

*   **Original:** `هِيَ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Hiya jameelatun.) - She is beautiful.
*   **Strongly Emphatic:** `إِنَّهَا لَجَمِيلَةٌ.` (Innahā lajameelatun.) - Indeed, she is certainly beautiful!
    *   *Notice*: `إِنَّهَا` for the pronoun, and `لَجَمِيلَةٌ` for the comment.

This is a powerful addition to your Arabic toolkit! You can now express certainty and emphasis in your statements.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   إِنَّ (inna) - indeed, certainly, verily (emphasis particle)
*   اسْمُ إِنَّ (ismu inna) - the topic of `إِنَّ` (takes Fatha)
*   خَبَرُ إِنَّ (khabaru inna) - the comment of `إِنَّ` (keeps Damma)
*   إِنَّهُ (innahu) - indeed, he/it is
*   إِنَّهَا (innahā) - indeed, she/it is
*   إِنَّكَ (innaka) - indeed, you (m) are
*   إِنَّكِ (innaki) - indeed, you (f) are
*   إِنِّي (innī) - indeed, I am
*   إِنَّنَا (innanā) - indeed, we are
*   إِنَّهُمْ (innahum) - indeed, they (m) are
*   إِنَّهُنَّ (innahunna) - indeed, they (f) are
*   لَـ (la-) - certainly (emphatic prefix for the comment)
