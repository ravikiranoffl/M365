# Day 56 - Environment vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns and a new verb related to the environment. Pay close attention to the gender of each noun, as this will affect how adjectives describe them!

1.  **`بِيئَةٌ` (bī'atun) - Environment**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'bee-a-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْبِيئَةُ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Al-bī'atu jameelatun.) - The environment is beautiful. (Using `جَمِيلَةٌ` - beautiful, from Day 3).

2.  **`شَجَرَةٌ` (shajaratun) - Tree**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'sha-ja-ra-tun'.
    *   Its **broken plural** is `أَشْجَارٌ` (ashjārun) - trees. Remember from Day 24, non-human broken plurals are treated as **feminine singular** for adjectives!
    *   Example: `الشَّجَرَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Ash-shajaratu kabeeratun.) - The tree is big. (Using `كَبِيرَةٌ` - big, from Day 3).
    *   Example: `الْأَشْجَارُ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Al-ashjāru jameelatun.) - The trees are beautiful. (Notice `جَمِيلَةٌ` is feminine singular).

3.  **`مَاءٌ` (mā'un) - Water**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. You've known this word for a while, but it's essential for talking about the environment!
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'maa-un'.
    *   Example: `الْمَاءُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-mā'u muhimun.) - Water is important. (Using `مُهِمٌّ` - important, from Day 43).

4.  **`هَوَاءٌ` (hawā'un) - Air**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ha-waa-un'.
    *   Example: `الْهَوَاءُ نَظِيفٌ.` (Al-hawā'u naẓīfun.) - The air is clean. (New adjective below!)

5.  **`أَرْضٌ` (arḍun) - Earth, Land**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ar-ḍun'.
    *   Example: `الْأَرْضُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-arḍu kabeeratun.) - The earth is big.

6.  **`بَحْرٌ` (baḥrun) - Sea**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'baḥ-run'.
    *   Example: `الْبَحْرُ جَمِيلٌ.` (Al-baḥru jameelun.) - The sea is beautiful.

7.  **`نَهْرٌ` (nahrun) - River**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'nah-run'.
    *   Example: `النَّهْرُ صَغِيرٌ.` (An-nahru ṣagheerun.) - The river is small. (Using `صَغِيرٌ` - small, from Day 3).

8.  **`جَبَلٌ` (jabalun) - Mountain**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ja-ba-lun'.
    *   Its **broken plural** is `جِبَالٌ` (jibālun) - mountains. (Also treated as feminine singular for adjectives.)
    *   Example: `الْجَبَلُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-jabalū kabeerun.) - The mountain is big.
    *   Example: `الْجِبَالُ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Al-jibālu jameelatun.) - The mountains are beautiful.

9.  **`غَابَةٌ` (ghābatun) - Forest**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ghaa-ba-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْغَابَةُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-ghābatū kabeeratun.) - The forest is big.

10. **`تَلَوُّثٌ` (talawwuthun) - Pollution**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. It's a verbal noun (Masdar).
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ta-law-wu-thun'.
    *   Example: `التَّلَوُّثُ كَبِيرٌ.` (At-talawwuthu kabeerun.) - The pollution is big.

11. **New Adjective: `نَظِيفٌ` (naẓīfun) / `نَظِيفَةٌ` (naẓīfatun) - Clean**
    *   `نَظِيفٌ` for masculine singular, `نَظِيفَةٌ` for feminine singular.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'na-ẓee-fun' / 'na-ẓee-fa-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمَاءُ نَظِيفٌ.` (Al-mā'u naẓīfun.) - The water is clean.
    *   Example: `الْبِيئَةُ نَظِيفَةٌ.` (Al-bī'atū naẓīfatun.) - The environment is clean.

12. **New Verb: "To Protect"**
    *   **Root:** `ح ف ظ` (ḥ-f-ẓ)
    *   **Past Tense (he protected):** `حَفِظَ` (ḥafiẓa) - 'ḥa-fi-ẓa'.
    *   **Present Tense (he protects):** `يَحْفَظُ` (yaḥfaẓu) - 'yaḥ-fa-ẓu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I protect):** `أَحْفَظُ` (aḥfaẓu) - 'aḥ-fa-ẓu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she protects / you-m.s. protect):** `تَحْفَظُ` (taḥfaẓu) - 'taḥ-fa-ẓu'.
    *   Example: `هُوَ يَحْفَظُ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Huwa yaḥfaẓu al-bī'ata.) - He protects the environment. (`الْبِيئَةَ` takes Fatha as the direct object).

You've just learned a comprehensive set of words to start discussing the environment! Remember to use them with their correct genders and case endings.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **The clean air is important.** (Nominal sentence + adjectives)
    *   `الْهَوَاءُ النَّظِيفُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-hawā'u an-naẓīfu muhimun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْهَوَاءُ` (air) is masculine, so `النَّظِيفُ` (clean) and `مُهِمٌّ` (important) are also masculine.

2.  **I protect the big trees.** (New verb + non-human plural object)
    *   `أَنَا أَحْفَظُ الْأَشْجَارَ الْكَبِيرَةَ.` (Ana aḥfaẓu al-ashjāra al-kabeerata.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْأَشْجَارَ` (the trees) is a non-human broken plural object, taking Fatha. Its adjective `الْكَبِيرَةَ` (the big) is feminine singular, also taking Fatha to match.

3.  **She wants to protect the beautiful sea.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `هِيَ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَحْفَظَ الْبَحْرَ الْجَمِيلَ.` (Hiya tureedu an taḥfaẓa al-baḥra al-jameela.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرِيدُ` (she wants - Day 44). `أَنْ تَحْفَظَ` (to protect) with the verb `تَحْفَظُ` getting a Fatha ending because of `أَنْ` (Day 38, 44). `الْبَحْرَ الْجَمِيلَ` (the beautiful sea) is the object with its adjective, both taking Fatha.

4.  **The pollution of the river is big.** (Idafa construct)
    *   `تَلَوُّثُ النَّهْرِ كَبِيرٌ.` (Talawwuthu an-nahri kabeerun.)
        *   *Notice:* `تَلَوُّثُ` (pollution of) is the first noun of the Idafa, masculine, so `كَبِيرٌ` (big) is masculine. `النَّهْرِ` (the river) is the second noun, taking Kasra.

5.  **Indeed, understanding the environment is important for the people.** (Emphasis particle + Masdar in Idafa)
    *   `إِنَّ فَهْمَ الْبِيئَةِ مُهِمٌّ لِلشَّعْبِ.` (Inna fahma al-bī'ati muhimun li-sh-sha'bi.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّ` (Day 37) makes `فَهْمَ` (understanding - Masdar from Day 52) take Fatha as the first noun in the Idafa. `الْبِيئَةِ` takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa. `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is the masculine comment matching `فَهْمَ`. `لِلشَّعْبِ` (for the people - Day 48).

6.  **He said that the mountains are beautiful.** (Reported speech + `أَنَّ`)
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْجِبَالَ جَمِيلَةٌ.` (Qāla anna al-jibāla jameelatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَ` (he said - Day 39). `أَنَّ` makes `الْجِبَالَ` (the mountains) take Fatha. `جَمِيلَةٌ` (beautiful) is the feminine singular comment matching `الْجِبَالَ` (as it's a non-human broken plural).

7.  **The minister comes to the forest to protect the land.** (Purpose clause)
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يَأْتِي إِلَى الْغَابَةِ لِيَحْفَظَ الْأَرْضَ.` (Al-wazeeru ya'tī ilā al-ghābati liyaḥfaẓa al-arḍa.)
        *   *New verb*: `يَأْتِي` (ya'tī) - he comes (from Day 29).
        *   *Notice:* `لِيَحْفَظَ` (in order for him to protect) where `يَحْفَظُ` takes Fatha because of `لِـ` (Day 40). `الْأَرْضَ` (the land) takes Fatha as object.

8.  **Maybe the new company will solve the problem of water pollution.** (Modal expression + future + Idafa chain)
    *   `رُبَّمَا سَتَحُلُّ الشَّرِكَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ مُشْكِلَةَ تَلَوُّثِ الْمَاءِ.` (Rubbamā sa-taḥullu ash-sharikatū al-jadeedatū mushkilata talawwuthi al-mā'i.)
        *   *Notice:* `سَتَحُلُّ` (she will solve - Day 50, Day 22 for future). `الشَّرِكَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` (the new company - Day 49, 3) is the feminine subject. `مُشْكِلَةَ تَلَوُّثِ الْمَاءِ` (problem of pollution of the water) is a chained Idafa acting as the object of `تَحُلُّ`. `مُشْكِلَةَ` takes Fatha, `تَلَوُّثِ` takes Kasra, `الْمَاءِ` takes Kasra.
