Today, we're going to learn about another very useful type of pronoun: **Attached Pronouns**. These are tiny little parts that stick right onto the end of a noun to show **possession**. Think of them as the Arabic way to say "my," "your," "his," "her," and so on!

## Lesson

1.  **What are Attached Pronouns for Possession?**
    *   In English, we say "my book," "your pen," "his car." The words "my," "your," "his" show that something belongs to someone.
    *   In Arabic, we don't use separate words like "my" or "your." Instead, we add a special small ending directly to the noun. This ending is called an **Attached Pronoun**.
    *   These attached pronouns are like sticky notes that tell you who owns the noun.

2.  **How Attached Pronouns Work**
    *   Attached pronouns **always attach to the end of a noun**.
    *   When a noun has an attached pronoun, it automatically becomes **definite**. This means it **cannot** have `الْـ` (the definite article "the") at the beginning, nor can it have `التَّنْوِينُ` (the indefinite "a" or "an" markers) at the end. The attached pronoun *itself* makes the word definite.

3.  **Meet the Attached Pronouns (Possession)**

    Let's learn these important little endings:

    *   **ـِي (-ī)**: Means **"my"** (for both masculine and feminine).
        *   Example: `كِتَابٌ` (a book) becomes `كِتَابِي` (my book).

    *   **ـكَ (-ka)**: Means **"your"** (singular, for one boy/man).
        *   Example: `قَلَمٌ` (a pen) becomes `قَلَمُكَ` (your pen).

    *   **ـكِ (-ki)**: Means **"your"** (singular, for one girl/woman).
        *   Example: `كِتَابٌ` (a book) becomes `كِتَابُكِ` (your book).

    *   **ـهُ (-hu)**: Means **"his"** (for one boy/man).
        *   Example: `قَلَمٌ` (a pen) becomes `قَلَمُهُ` (his pen).
        *   *Note*: The vowel on the هُ sometimes changes to هِ if the letter before it has a Kasra or a long `ي`. We'll stick to هُ for now!

    *   **ـهَا (-hā)**: Means **"her"** (for one girl/woman).
        *   Example: `كِتَابٌ` (a book) becomes `كِتَابُهَا` (her book).

    *   **ـنَا (-nā)**: Means **"our"** (for both masculine and feminine groups).
        *   Example: `قَلَمٌ` (a pen) becomes `قَلَمُنَا` (our pen).

    *   **ـهُمْ (-hum)**: Means **"their"** (for groups of boys/men, or mixed groups).
        *   Example: `كِتَابٌ` (a book) becomes `كِتَابُهُمْ` (their book).

    *   **ـهُنَّ (-hunna)**: Means **"their"** (for groups of girls/women only).
        *   Example: `قَلَمٌ` (a pen) becomes `قَلَمُهُنَّ` (their pen).

4.  **Special Rule for تَاء مَرْبُوطَة (Taa Marbuta)**
    *   Remember our special letter **تَاء مَرْبُوطَة (ة / ـة)** that makes nouns feminine?
    *   When an attached pronoun is added to a noun ending in **تَاء مَرْبُوطَة**, the **تَاء مَرْبُوطَة** changes its shape into an open **تَاء (ـتْ)** before the pronoun attaches. It's like it opens up to let the pronoun join!
    *   Example: `مَدْرَسَةٌ` (a school) -> `مَدْرَسَتِي` (my school). Notice `سَةٌ` becomes `سَتِـ` before the `ي`.

## Examples

Let's use some words you know and add these attached pronouns to them!

**Using the Masculine Noun: كِتَابٌ (kitaabun) = book**

1.  My book: `كِتَابِي` (kitaabī)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـِي`
2.  Your (m) book: `كِتَابُكَ` (kitaabuka)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـكَ`
3.  Your (f) book: `كِتَابُكِ` (kitaabuki)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـكِ`
4.  His book: `كِتَابُهُ` (kitaabuhu)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـهُ`
5.  Her book: `كِتَابُهَا` (kitaabuhā)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـهَا`
6.  Our book: `كِتَابُنَا` (kitaabunā)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـنَا`
7.  Their (m/mixed) book: `كِتَابُهُمْ` (kitaabuhum)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـهُمْ`
8.  Their (f) book: `كِتَابُهُنَّ` (kitaabuhunna)
    *   `كِتَاب` + `ـهُنَّ`

**Using the Feminine Noun: مَدْرَسَةٌ (madrasatun) = school**
*Remember the تَاء مَرْبُوطَة changes to open تَاء!*

1.  My school: `مَدْرَسَتِي` (madrasatī)
    *   `مَدْرَسَة` becomes `مَدْرَسَتِـ` + `ـِي`
2.  Your (m) school: `مَدْرَسَتُكَ` (madrasatuka)
    *   `مَدْرَسَة` becomes `مَدْرَسَتُـ` + `ـكَ`
3.  Your (f) school: `مَدْرَسَتُكِ` (madrasatuki)
    *   `مَدْرَسَة` becomes `مَدْرَسَتِـ` + `ـكِ`
4.  His school: `مَدْرَسَتُهُ` (madrasatuhu)
    *   `مَدْرَسَة` becomes `مَدْرَسَتُـ` + `ـهُ`
5.  Her school: `مَدْرَسَتُهَا` (madrasatuhā)
    *   `مَدْرَسَة` becomes `مَدْرَسَتُـ` + `ـهَا`

You can now say "my school" and "your book"! This is a huge step in making your Arabic sentences more personal!
