# Day 39 - Reported speech

## Lesson

1.  **What is Reported Speech?**
    *   Imagine your friend Ahmad tells you: "I am hungry."
    *   If you tell someone else about it, you can say: "Ahmad said, 'I am hungry.'" (This is **Direct Speech** - using his exact words).
    *   Or, you can say: "Ahmad said **that he is** hungry." (This is **Reported Speech** - telling what he said without using his exact words).
    *   Today, we'll learn how to form these "said that he is..." sentences in Arabic.

2.  **The Verbs for "Said": `قَالَ` and `قَالَتْ`**
    *   To start reported speech, we need a verb that means "he said" or "she said."
    *   **`قَالَ` (qāla):** He said. (This is a past tense verb, for a masculine singular subject).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'qaa-la'.
    *   **`قَالَتْ` (qālat):** She said. (This is a past tense verb, for a feminine singular subject).
        *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'qaa-lat'.
    *   These are both from the root `ق و ل` (q-w-l).

3.  **The Crucial "That" Particle: `أَنَّ` (anna)**
    *   Just like in English, where we use "that" to connect "he said" to the reported statement ("He said **that** he is smart"), Arabic uses a special particle: **`أَنَّ` (anna)**.
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'an-na', with a Shaddah on the `ن` (Nun).
    *   **Very Important Rule!** `أَنَّ` behaves almost exactly like `إِنَّ` (from Day 37) and `لِأَنَّ` (from Day 28):
        *   It makes the **noun** that immediately follows it take a **Fatha ( َ )** at the end.
        *   If a **pronoun** immediately follows `أَنَّ`, it attaches to `أَنَّ` and takes the Fatha form.
            *   `أَنَّ` + `هُوَ` (he) = **`أَنَّهُ` (annahu)** - that he is / that it is
            *   `أَنَّ` + `هِيَ` (she) = **`أَنَّهَا` (annahā)** - that she is / that it is
            *   `أَنَّ` + `أَنْتَ` (you-m) = **`أَنَّكَ` (annaka)** - that you (m) are
            *   `أَنَّ` + `أَنْتِ` (you-f) = **`أَنَّكِ` (annaki)** - that you (f) are
            *   `أَنَّ` + `أَنَا` (I) = **`أَنَّنِي` (annanī)** or **`أَنِّي` (annī)** - that I am
            *   `أَنَّ` + `نَحْنُ` (we) = **`أَنَّنَا` (annanā)** - that we are
            *   `أَنَّ` + `هُمْ` (they-m) = **`أَنَّهُمْ` (annahum)** - that they (m) are
            *   `أَنَّ` + `هُنَّ` (they-f) = **`أَنَّهُنَّ` (annahunna)** - that they (f) are

4.  **Putting it Together: The Structure of Reported Speech**
    *   The basic structure is:
        **`[Speaker] + قَالَ/قَالَتْ + أَنَّ + [reported statement]`**
    *   The "reported statement" part will have its subject (a noun or an attached pronoun like `أَنَّهُ`) followed by its comment or verb.
    *   **A Key Challenge: Pronoun Shifts!** When you report what someone said, the pronouns usually change.
        *   If Ahmad says "I am a student," when *you* report it, you say "Ahmad said that **he** is a student." (`أَنَا` becomes `أَنَّهُ`).
        *   If Fatima says "My book is new," when *you* report it, you say "Fatima said that **her** book is new." (`كِتَابِي` becomes `كِتَابَهَا`).

Let's look at examples to make this clearer. We'll show the original (direct) speech first, then the reported speech.

## Examples

Let's practice reporting different kinds of statements!

**1. Reporting a simple "X is Y" sentence (Nominal Sentence):**

*   **Direct Speech (Ahmad says):** "I am a student." (`أَنَا طَالِبٌ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Ahmad):** "Ahmad said that he is a student."
    *   `قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَنَّهُ طَالِبٌ.` (Qāla Aḥmadu annahu ṭālibun.)
        *   *Notice*: `قَالَ` (he said) for Ahmad. `أَنَّهُ` (that he is) for "I am." `طَالِبٌ` (student) remains the comment.

*   **Direct Speech (Fatima says):** "My car is new." (`سَيَّارَتِي جَدِيدَةٌ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Fatima):** "Fatima said that her car is new."
    *   `قَالَتْ فَاطِمَةُ أَنَّ سَيَّارَتَهَا جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Qālat Fāṭimatu anna sayyāratahā jadeedatun.)
        *   *Notice*: `قَالَتْ` (she said) for Fatima. `أَنَّ` makes `سَيَّارَةَ` take Fatha (`سَيَّارَتَهَا`). The `ـهَا` (her) refers back to Fatima. `جَدِيدَةٌ` (new) is the comment.

*   **Direct Speech (Ahmad says to you (f)):** "You are smart." (`أَنْتِ ذَكِيَّةٌ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You (f) say about Ahmad):** "Ahmad said that I am smart."
    *   `قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَنَّنِي ذَكِيَّةٌ.` (Qāla Aḥmadu annanī dhakiyyatun.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنَّنِي` (that I am) for "you (f) are." `ذَكِيَّةٌ` (smart) remains the comment.

**2. Reporting a sentence with a verb:**

*   **Direct Speech (Ahmad says):** "I study every day." (`أَنَا أَدْرُسُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Ahmad):** "Ahmad said that he studies every day."
    *   `قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَنَّهُ يَدْرُسُ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ.` (Qāla Aḥmadu annahu yadrusu kulla yawmin.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنَّهُ` (that he) for `أَنَا`. `يَدْرُسُ` (he studies) for `أَدْرُسُ` (I study).

*   **Direct Speech (Fatima says):** "I wrote the story." (`أَنَا كَتَبْتُ الْقِصَّةَ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Fatima):** "Fatima said that she wrote the story."
    *   `قَالَتْ فَاطِمَةُ أَنَّهَا كَتَبَتِ الْقِصَّةَ.` (Qālat Fāṭimatu annahā katabati al-qisṣata.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنَّهَا` (that she) for `أَنَا`. `كَتَبَتِ` (she wrote) for `كَتَبْتُ` (I wrote). (`الْقِصَّةَ` takes Fatha as it's the object).

*   **Direct Speech (Ahmad says):** "The pen is on the desk." (`الْقَلَمُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Ahmad):** "Ahmad said that the pen is on the desk."
    *   `قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَنَّ الْقَلَمَ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Qāla Aḥmadu anna al-qalama fawqa al-maktabi.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنَّ` makes `الْقَلَمَ` take Fatha. No pronoun shift needed for "the pen."

**3. Reporting with negation:**

*   **Direct Speech (Fatima says):** "I am not eating now." (`أَنَا لَا آكُلُ الْآنَ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Fatima):** "Fatima said that she is not eating now."
    *   `قَالَتْ فَاطِمَةُ أَنَّهَا لَا تَأْكُلُ الْآنَ.` (Qālat Fāṭimatu annahā lā ta'kulu al-āna.)

**4. Reporting with emphasis (from Day 37):**

*   **Direct Speech (Ahmad says):** "Indeed, the book is big." (`إِنَّ الْكِتَابَ كَبِيرٌ.`)
*   **Reported Speech (You say about Ahmad):** "Ahmad said that the book is big." (Here, `أَنَّ` replaces `إِنَّ` and the meaning is generally the same - the emphasis is carried over).
    *   `قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَنَّ الْكِتَابَ كَبِيرٌ.` (Qāla Aḥmadu anna al-kitāba kabeerun.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَنَّ` already makes `الْكِتَابَ` take Fatha, so the form looks very similar to the original emphatic statement.

Reported speech is a big step because it involves combining many rules you've learned: verb conjugation, pronoun reference, the Fatha rule after `أَنَّ`, and nominal sentence structure. Don't worry if it takes time to get used to the pronoun shifts!
