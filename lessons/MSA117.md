# Day 117 - Interview language and practice Part 2

## Lesson

Continuing our exploration of "interview language," today we'll focus on verbs and nouns that describe actions like submitting applications, qualifications, and company objectives. These words are essential for a professional discussion about employment.

Let's learn some new and essential interview vocabulary!

### 1. `قَدَّمَ` (qaddama) - He submitted / presented / offered (Past tense) / `يُقَدِّمُ` (yuqaddimu) - He submits / presents / offers (Present tense)

This verb is very versatile. It means "to submit" (an application), "to present" (an idea), or "to offer" (a job). It's a Form II verb in morphology (which we'll study more later), so the present tense has a `ـُـ` vowel on the prefix `يُـ` and a `ـِـ` vowel on the middle radical.

*   **New Word (Past):** `قَدَّمَ` (qaddama) - 'qad-da-ma'
    *   *Meaning:* He submitted, presented, offered.
*   **New Word (Present):** `يُقَدِّمُ` (yuqaddimu) - 'yu-qad-di-mu' (masculine) / `تُقَدِّمُ` (tuqaddimu) - 'tu-qad-di-mu' (feminine)
    *   *Meaning:* He/she submits, presents, offers / is submitting, presenting, offering.
    *   *Example (Present):* `الْمُوَظَّفُ يُقَدِّمُ الطَّلَبَ.` (Al-muwaẓẓafu yuqaddimu aṭ-ṭalaba.) - The employee submits the application.

### 2. `تَقْدِيمٌ` (taqdeemun) - Submission / Presentation / Offering (Masdar)

This is the verbal noun (Masdar) of `قَدَّمَ/يُقَدِّمُ`, meaning "the act of submitting," "presentation," or "an offering."

*   **New Word:** `تَقْدِيمٌ` (taqdeemun) - 'taq-dee-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Submission, presentation, offering (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `تَقْدِيمُ الطَّلَبِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Taqdeemu aṭ-ṭalabi muhimun.) - The submission of the application is important.

### 3. `مُؤَهِّلٌ` (mu'ahhilun) - Qualification (Singular) / `مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ` (mu'ahhilātun) - Qualifications (Plural)

`مُؤَهِّلٌ` refers to a specific skill or achievement that makes someone suitable for a job. It's often used in its sound feminine plural form `مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ` when referring to a person's "qualifications."

*   **New Word (Singular):** `مُؤَهِّلٌ` (mu'ahhilun) - 'mu-ah-hi-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Qualification (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ` (mu'ahhilātun) - 'mu-ah-hi-lā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Qualifications (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example (Plural):* `لِي مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ جَيِّدَةٌ.` (Lī mu'ahhilātun jayyidatun.) - I have good qualifications. (Remember non-human plurals take feminine singular adjectives, Day 27).

### 4. `مُتَطَلَّبٌ` (mutaṭallabun) - Requirement (Singular) / `مُتَطَلَّبَاتٌ` (mutaṭallabātun) - Requirements (Plural)

`مُتَطَلَّبٌ` refers to something that is required or necessary. It is also commonly used in its feminine plural form `مُتَطَلَّبَاتٌ` for "requirements." You saw the verb `يَتَطَلَّبُ` (he requires) in the Day 113 example article.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `مُتَطَلَّبٌ` (mutaṭallabun) - 'mu-ta-ṭal-la-bun'
    *   *Meaning:* Requirement (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `مُتَطَلَّبَاتٌ` (mutaṭallabātun) - 'mu-ta-ṭal-la-bā-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Requirements (feminine plural noun).
    *   *Example (Plural):* `لِلْوَظِيفَةِ مُتَطَلَّبَاتٌ صَعْبَةٌ.` (Li-l-waẓīfati mutaṭallabātun ṣa'batun.) - The job has difficult requirements.

### 5. `تَوْظِيفٌ` (tawẓeefun) - Hiring / Employment (Masdar)

This is the verbal noun for "hiring" or "employing" someone. It comes from the same root as `وَظِيفَةٌ` (job) and `مُوَظَّفٌ` (employee).

*   **New Word:** `تَوْظِيفٌ` (tawẓeefun) - 'taw-ẓee-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* Hiring, employment (masculine Masdar/noun).
    *   *Example:* `تَوْظِيفُ الْمُوَظَّفِينَ الْجُدُدِ مُهِمٌّ.` (Tawẓeefu al-muwaẓẓafeena al-jududi muhimun.) - The hiring of new employees is important. (`الْجُدُدِ` is plural of `جَدِيدٌ` for `الموظفين`).

### 6. `قِسْمٌ` (qismun) - Department

This noun refers to a distinct part of an organization, such as a company or government ministry.

*   **New Word:** `قِسْمٌ` (qismun) - 'qis-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Department (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْقِسْمُ جَدِيدٌ.` (Al-qismu jadeedun.) - The department is new.

### 7. `هَدَفٌ` (hadafun) - Goal / Objective

This noun refers to an aim or desired result.

*   **New Word:** `هَدَفٌ` (hadafun) - 'ha-da-fun'
    *   *Meaning:* Goal, objective (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `لِلشَّرِكَةِ أَهْدَافٌ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Li-sh-sharikatī ahdāfun kabeeratun.) - The company has big goals. (`أَهْدَافٌ` (ahdāfun) is the plural of `هَدَفٌ`, it is a broken plural and takes a feminine singular adjective).

### 8. `نَجَحَ` (najaḥa) - He succeeded (Past tense) / `يَنْجَحُ` (yanjaḥu) - He succeeds (Present tense)

This verb means "to achieve success." You already know the Masdar `نَجَاحٌ` (success, Day 107).

*   **New Word (Past):** `نَجَحَ` (najaḥa) - 'na-ja-ḥa'
    *   *Meaning:* He succeeded.
*   **New Word (Present):** `يَنْجَحُ` (yanjaḥu) - 'yan-ja-ḥu'
    *   *Meaning:* He succeeds / is succeeding.
    *   *Example (Present):* `الْمُوَظَّفُ يَنْجَحُ فِي عَمَلِهِ.` (Al-muwaẓẓafu yanjahu fī 'amalihi.) - The employee succeeds in his work.

### 9. `قَبِلَ` (qabila) - He accepted (Past tense) / `يَقْبَلُ` (yaqbalu) - He accepts (Present tense)

This verb means "to take or receive something offered," or "to agree to something." You saw `يَقْبَلُ` in an example on Day 110.

*   **New Word (Past):** `قَبِلَ` (qabila) - 'qa-bi-la'
    *   *Meaning:* He accepted.
*   **New Word (Present):** `يَقْبَلُ` (yaqbalu) - 'yaq-ba-lu'
    *   *Meaning:* He accepts / is accepting.
    *   *Example (Present):* `الشَّرِكَةُ تَقْبَلُ الطَّلَبَ.` (Ash-sharikatū taqbalu aṭ-ṭalaba.) - The company accepts the application.

These terms are crucial for discussing the application and hiring process, as well as qualifications and objectives in an interview setting.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new interview words and phrases into sentences, using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that the company wants to offer a new job."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تُقَدِّمَ وَظِيفَةً جَدِيدَةً.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna ash-sharikatū tureedu an tuqaddima waẓīfatan jadeedatan.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الشَّرِكَةَ` (Day 116, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`). `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تُقَدِّمَ` (wants to offer - `تُقَدِّمَ` is new verb in Subjunctive, Day 60, Day 117). `وَظِيفَةً جَدِيدَةً` (new job - Day 116, Accusative object).

2.  **"He submitted the application, and accordingly, the company accepted it."**
    *   `قَدَّمَ الطَّلَبَ، وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، قَبِلَتْهُ الشَّرِكَةُ.` (Qaddama aṭ-ṭalaba, wa-binā'an 'alā dhālika, qabilathu ash-sharikatū.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَدَّمَ` (new past tense verb, Day 117). `الطَّلَبَ` (Day 116, Accusative object). `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `قَبِلَتْهُ الشَّرِكَةُ` (`قَبِلَتْ` new past tense verb, `ـهُ` attached pronoun "it," `الشَّرِكَةُ` subject, Day 117).

3.  **"The hiring of employees is a big goal for the new department."**
    *   `تَوْظِيفُ الْمُوَظَّفِينَ هَدَفٌ كَبِيرٌ لِلْقِسْمِ الْجَدِيدِ.` (Tawẓeefu al-muwaẓẓafeena hadafun kabeerun li-l-qismi al-jadeedi.)
        *   *Notice:* `تَوْظِيفُ الْمُوَظَّفِينَ` (Idafa, `تَوْظِيفُ` new Masdar, Day 117; `الْمُوَظَّفِينَ` Genitive plural, Day 116). `هَدَفٌ كَبِيرٌ` (new word `هَدَفٌ` + `كَبِيرٌ` (Day 54), Nominative predicate). `لِلْقِسْمِ الْجَدِيدِ` (for the new department - `لِـ` preposition, `الْقِسْمِ` new, Genitive, Day 117).

4.  **"Despite the fact that the requirements are difficult, I want to succeed in this job."**
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْمُتَطَلَّبَاتِ صَعْبَةٌ، أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَنْجَحَ فِي هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ.` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna al-mutaṭallabāti ṣa'batun, ureedu an anjaḥa fī hādhihi al-waẓīfati.)
        *   *Notice:* `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Day 107). `الْمُتَطَلَّبَاتِ صَعْبَةٌ` (new word, Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, feminine plural and adjective, Day 117). `أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَنْجَحَ` (I want to succeed - `أَنْجَحَ` is new verb in Subjunctive, Day 60, Day 117). `فِي هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ` (in this job - Day 116).

5.  **"The employee has good qualifications. Moreover, he wants to achieve the company's goals."**
    *   `لِلْمُوَظَّفِ مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ جَيِّدَةٌ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، يُرِيدُ أَنْ يُحَقِّقَ أَهْدَافَ الشَّرِكَةِ.` (Li-l-muwaẓẓafi mu'ahhilātun jayyidatun. 'Alāwatan 'alā dhālika, yureedu an yuḥaqqiqa ahdāfa ash-sharikatī.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِلْمُوَظَّفِ مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ جَيِّدَةٌ` (Day 116 for `لِـ` possession, `الْمُوَظَّفِ` Day 116, `مُؤَهِّلَاتٌ` new, Day 117). `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Day 107). `يُرِيدُ أَنْ يُحَقِّقَ` (wants to achieve - `يُحَقِّقَ` is subjunctive of "he achieves," a Form II verb we've seen in Masdar form `تَحْقِيقٌ` Day 113. Let's make sure it's clear). `أَهْدَافَ الشَّرِكَةِ` (the company's goals - `أَهْدَافَ` new plural, Accusative object; `الشَّرِكَةِ` Day 116, Idafa, Day 117).

These examples show how you can combine new vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences about job interviews.
