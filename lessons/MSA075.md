# Day 75 - Translation practice

## Lesson

Today, we're going to solidify your understanding by practicing translation from English into Modern Standard Arabic. This requires you to recall vocabulary, apply grammatical rules (like verb moods, case endings, and agreements), choose the correct negation particle, and even consider word order for emphasis.

**How this will work:**

1.  I will give you an English sentence.
2.  You should try to translate it into Arabic, paying close attention to all the rules you've learned. Think about:
    *   Is it a nominal or verbal sentence?
    *   What tense is the verb? What mood should it be in?
    *   Are there any special particles like `أَنَّ`, `أَنْ`, `لَنْ`, `لَمْ`, `لَيْسَ` that will change the case endings?
    *   Are there any idioms, diplomatic phrases, or discourse markers you can use?
    *   Is there a specific word you want to emphasize by putting it first?
    *   Don't forget noun-adjective agreement, Idafa rules, and pronouns!
3.  After you've tried your translation, I will reveal the correct Arabic translation (with Harakat for pronunciation) and explain the key grammatical points.

Don't worry if you don't get every sentence perfect right away. The goal is to practice, learn from your attempts, and strengthen your command of Arabic!

## Examples

Let's try a few examples together first to warm up.

**Example 1: Guided Practice**

*   **English:** "He wants to protect the environment."

*(Take a moment to translate this sentence into Arabic in your mind or on paper.)*

**Arabic Translation:**
`هُوَ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَحْفَظَ الْبِيئَةَ.` (Huwa yureedu an yaḥfaẓa al-bī'ata.)

*   **Explanation:**
    *   `هُوَ يُرِيدُ` (he wants) is a verbal sentence.
    *   `أَنْ` (to) introduces a purpose/desire, making the verb that follows (`يَحْفَظُ` - he protects) take a **Fatha** (َ) at the end, becoming `يَحْفَظَ` (Subjunctive mood).
    *   `الْبِيئَةَ` (the environment) is the direct object, taking a **Fatha** (َ).

**Example 2: Guided Practice**

*   **English:** "The problem was not solved."

*(Take a moment to translate this sentence into Arabic in your mind or on paper.)*

**Arabic Translation:**
`لَمْ تُحَلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةُ.` (Lam tuḥalla al-mushkilatu.)

*   **Explanation:**
    *   This sentence uses the passive voice ("was solved"). The active verb for "he solves" is `يَحُلُّ`. Its passive present is `يُحَلُّ` (or `تُحَلُّ` for feminine `الْمُشْكِلَةُ`).
    *   `لَمْ` (did not) negates a past action using a present tense verb, making the verb Jussive. For a geminated verb like `تُحَلُّ`, the Jussive form is `تُحَلَّ` (taking a Fatha due to the gemination, not a Sukoon or Nūn-drop).
    *   `الْمُشْكِلَةُ` (the problem) is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ` (new subject of the passive verb), so it takes a **Damma** (ُ).

**Example 3: Guided Practice**

*   **English:** "The company believes that the agreement is important."

*(Take a moment to translate this sentence into Arabic in your mind or on paper.)*

**Arabic Translation:**
`تَعْتَقِدُ الشَّرِكَةُ أَنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ مُهِمٌّ.` (Ta'taqidu ash-sharikatū anna al-ittifāqa muhimun.)

*   **Explanation:**
    *   `تَعْتَقِدُ الشَّرِكَةُ` (the company believes) is the main verbal sentence. `تَعْتَقِدُ` is feminine for `الشَّرِكَةُ`.
    *   `أَنَّ` (that) introduces the embedded clause.
    *   `الِاتِّفَاقَ` (the agreement) is the subject of the `أَنَّ` clause, so it takes a **Fatha** (َ).
    *   `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is the comment for `الِاتِّفَاقَ`, taking a **Damma** (ٌ).

**Example 4: Guided Practice**

*   **English:** "In conclusion, we hope to work hand in hand."

*(Take a moment to translate this sentence into Arabic in your mind or on paper.)*

**Arabic Translation:**
`فِي الْخِتَامِ، نَأْمُلُ أَنْ نَعْمَلَ يَدًا بِيَدٍ.` (Fī al-khitāmi, na'mulu an na'mala yadan bi-yadin.)

*   **Explanation:**
    *   `فِي الْخِتَامِ` (in conclusion) is a discourse marker. `الْخِتَامِ` takes Kasra after `فِي`.
    *   `نَأْمُلُ` (we hope).
    *   `أَنْ` (to) introduces the action, making `نَعْمَلُ` (we work) take a **Fatha** (َ) (Subjunctive mood).
    *   `يَدًا بِيَدٍ` (hand in hand) is an idiom.

## Practice

For each practice item, translate the English sentence into Modern Standard Arabic.

1.  **English:** "The new computer is not useful."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `الْحَاسُوبُ الْجَدِيدُ غَيْرُ مُفِيدٍ.` (Al-ḥāsūbu al-jadeedu ghayru mufīdin.)
    *   **Explanation:** `الْحَاسُوبُ الْجَدِيدُ` (the new computer) is the Topic. `غَيْرُ` (non-/un-) is the Comment, taking Damma. `مُفِيدٍ` (useful) takes Kasra because it's the second part of the Idafa with `غَيْرُ`.

2.  **English:** "It is necessary to understand the big problem."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `يَجِبُ أَنْ نَفْهَمَ الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْكَبِيرَةَ.` (Yajibu an nafham al-mushkilata al-kabeerata.)
    *   **Explanation:** `يَجِبُ أَنْ` (it is necessary to). `نَفْهَمَ` (we understand) takes Fatha (Subjunctive) after `أَنْ`. `الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْكَبِيرَةَ` (the big problem) is the direct object, so both noun and adjective take Fatha.

3.  **English:** "The minister announces that the law is clear."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `يُعْلِنُ الْوَزِيرُ أَنَّ الْقَانُونَ وَاضِحٌ.` (Yu'linu al-wazeeru anna al-qānūna wāḍiḥun.)
    *   **Explanation:** `يُعْلِنُ الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister announces). `أَنَّ` (that) introduces the clause. `الْقَانُونَ` (the law) takes Fatha as the subject of `أَنَّ`. `وَاضِحٌ` (clear) is its Comment, taking Damma.

4.  **English:** "They (m.p.) will not come to the meeting."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `لَنْ يَأْتُوا إِلَى الِاجْتِمَاعِ.` (Lan ya'tū ilā al-ijtimā'i.)
    *   **Explanation:** `لَنْ` (will not) makes the verb Subjunctive. `يَأْتُونَ` (they come) becomes `يَأْتُوا` (dropping the Nūn). `إِلَى الِاجْتِمَاعِ` (to the meeting) is a prepositional phrase, with `الِاجْتِمَاعِ` taking Kasra.

5.  **English:** "I know that the health of the people is important."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `أَعْلَمُ أَنَّ صِحَّةَ الشَّعْبِ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (A'lamu anna ṣiḥḥata ash-sha'bi muhimmatun.)
    *   **Explanation:** `أَعْلَمُ` (I know). `أَنَّ` (that) introduces the clause. `صِحَّةَ الشَّعْبِ` (the health of the people) is an Idafa. `صِحَّةَ` takes Fatha as the subject of `أَنَّ`, and `الشَّعْبِ` takes Kasra (Idafa rule). `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important) is the Comment, matching feminine `صِحَّةَ`.

6.  **English:** "The message was sent by the successful student."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `أُرْسِلَتِ الرِّسَالَةُ مِنَ الطَّالِبِ النَّاجِحِ.` (Ursilatī ar-risālatu mina aṭ-ṭālibi an-nājiḥi.)
    *   **Explanation:** `أُرْسِلَتْ` is the passive past verb for "it was sent" (feminine for `الرِّسَالَةُ`). `الرِّسَالَةُ` (the message) is the `نَائِبُ الْفَاعِلِ`, taking Damma. `مِنَ` (from/by) introduces the agent. `الطَّالِبِ النَّاجِحِ` (the successful student) takes Kasra after `مِنَ`, and the adjective matches.

7.  **English:** "She wants not to fail in the exam."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `تُرِيدُ أَنْ لَا تَرْسُبَ فِي الِامْتِحَانِ.` (Tureedu an lā tarsuba fī al-imtiḥāni.)
    *   **Explanation:** `تُرِيدُ` (she wants). `أَنْ لَا` (not to). `تَرْسُبَ` (she fails) takes Fatha (Subjunctive) after `أَنْ`. `فِي الِامْتِحَانِ` (in the exam).

8.  **English:** "Regarding the economy, it is important to achieve growth."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `بِخُصُوصِ الِاقْتِصَادِ، مُهِمٌّ أَنْ نُحَقِّقَ النُّمُوَّ.` (Bi-khuṣūṣi al-iqtiṣādi, muhimun an nuḥaqqiqa an-numuwwa.)
    *   **Explanation:** `بِخُصُوصِ الِاقْتِصَادِ` (regarding the economy). `الِاقْتِصَادِ` takes Kasra after `بِخُصُوصِ`. `مُهِمٌّ أَنْ` (it is important to). `نُحَقِّقَ` (we achieve) takes Fatha (Subjunctive) after `أَنْ`. `النُّمُوَّ` (the growth) is the direct object, taking Fatha.

9.  **English:** "The president explains that the new policy is joint."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `يَشْرَحُ الرَّئِيسُ أَنَّ السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ مُشْتَرَكَةٌ.` (Yashraḥu ar-ra'eecu anna as-siyāsata al-jadeedata mushtarakatun.)
    *   **Explanation:** `يَشْرَحُ الرَّئِيسُ` (the president explains). `أَنَّ` (that) introduces the clause. `السِّيَاسَةَ الْجَدِيدَةَ` (the new policy) takes Fatha as the subject of `أَنَّ`. `مُشْتَرَكَةٌ` (joint) is the Comment, matching feminine `السِّيَاسَةَ`.

10. **English:** "The information is old, nevertheless it is useful."

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `الْمَعْلُومَاتُ قَدِيمَةٌ، مَعَ ذَلِكَ هِيَ مُفِيدَةٌ.` (Al-ma'lūmātu qadeematun, ma'a dhālika hiya mufīdatun.)
    *   **Explanation:** `الْمَعْلُومَاتُ قَدِيمَةٌ` (the information is old). `الْمَعْلُومَاتُ` is treated as feminine singular for adjectives. `مَعَ ذَلِكَ` (nevertheless) is a discourse marker. `هِيَ مُفِيدَةٌ` (it is useful) is a new nominal sentence.

11. **English:** "Do not use the phone!" (to a masculine singular 'you')

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `لَا تَسْتَخْدِمِ الْهَاتِفَ!` (Lā tastakhdimi al-hātifa!)
    *   **Explanation:** `لَا` (do not) is for prohibition, making the verb Jussive. `تَسْتَخْدِمُ` (you use) becomes `تَسْتَخْدِمْ` (Jussive Sukoon), which changes to Kasra `تَسْتَخْدِمِ` before `الـ`. `الْهَاتِفَ` (the phone) is the direct object.

12. **English:** "Every day, the teacher reads the news." (Emphasize "Every day")

    *(Your Translation:)*

    ---

    **Arabic Translation:**
    `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ يَقْرَأُ الْمُدَرِّسُ الْأَخْبَارَ.` (Kulla yawmin yaqra'u al-mudarrisu al-akhbāra.)
    *   **Explanation:** `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ` (every day) is an adverbial phrase of time. By placing it at the beginning, it is emphasized. `يَقْرَأُ الْمُدَرِّسُ الْأَخْبَارَ` (the teacher reads the news) follows in normal verb-subject-object order.
