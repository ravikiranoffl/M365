# Day 82 - Interview language

## Lesson

Interview language is all about presenting yourself, your qualifications, and your motivations clearly and effectively. It often involves formal greetings, discussing your background, and expressing your future aspirations.

We'll learn a few key words and phrases today that are very common in interview settings.

### 1. Introducing Yourself (Review & New)

You already know how to say "My name is..." This is a basic but important start.

*   **`اِسْمِي...` (ismī...)** - My name is... (Day 2)
    *   *Example:* `اِسْمِي مُحَمَّدٌ.` (Ismī Muḥammadun.) - My name is Muhammad.

### 2. "Tell me about yourself (m.s.)" (`حَدِّثْنِي عَنْ نَفْسِكَ.`)

This is a very common opening question in an interview.

*   **New Verb: "To Tell / To Narrate"**
    *   **Root:** `ح د ث` (ḥ-d-th)
    *   **Past Tense (he told):** `حَدَّثَ` (ḥaddatha) - 'ḥad-da-tha'. (This is a Form II verb, meaning it often implies causing something or doing something more intensely, like causing someone to hear something by telling).
    *   **Present Tense (he tells):** `يُحَدِّثُ` (yuḥaddithu) - 'yu-ḥad-di-thu'.
    *   **Imperative (Tell! - m.s.):** `حَدِّثْ!` (ḥaddith!) - 'ḥad-dith!'.
    *   **New Noun: "Self / Soul"**
        *   **`نَفْسٌ` (nafsun)** - 'naf-sun' (feminine noun).
    *   **How to use:** When someone asks you to "tell me about yourself," they'll use this phrase. `حَدِّثْنِي` combines the imperative `حَدِّثْ` (tell!) with the attached pronoun `ـنِي` (me). `عَنْ نَفْسِكَ` means "about your self."
    *   *Example phrase:* `حَدِّثْنِي عَنْ نَفْسِكَ.` (Ḥaddithnī 'an nafsika.) - Tell me about yourself (to a masculine singular person).
        *   *If asking a feminine singular person:* `حَدِّثِينِي عَنْ نَفْسِكِ.` (Ḥaddithīnī 'an nafsiki.) - Tell me about yourself.

### 3. Talking About Experience (`خِبْرَةٌ` & `لَدَيَّ`)

One of the most important things in an interview is to talk about what you have learned and done.

*   **New Noun: "Experience"**
    *   **`خِبْرَةٌ` (khibratun)** - 'khib-ra-tun' (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `لَدَيَّ خِبْرَةٌ.` (Ladayya khibratun.) - I have experience.
*   **New Phrase: "I have" (for possessions/experience)**
    *   **`لَدَيَّ` (ladayya)** - 'la-day-ya'.
    *   *How to use:* You know `لِي` for "I have" (Day 16). `لَدَيَّ` is another way to say "I have," often used for things like skills, experiences, or abstract possessions, and it's very common in formal settings like interviews.
    *   *Example phrase:* `لَدَيَّ خِبْرَةٌ فِي الْعَمَلِ.` (Ladayya khibratun fī al-'amali.) - I have experience in work.
        *   *Notice:* `فِي الْعَمَلِ` (in the work - Day 10, 29).

### 4. Expressing Interest (`مُهْتَمٌّ`)

Showing your interest in the job or company is key.

*   **New Adjective: "Interested"**
    *   **`مُهْتَمٌّ` (muhtammun)** - 'muh-tam-mun' (masculine singular).
    *   **`مُهْتَمَّةٌ` (muhtammatun)** - 'muh-tam-ma-tun' (feminine singular).
    *   *How to use:* This adjective always takes the preposition `بِـ` (bi-) - in/with to mean "interested *in* something." It must agree in gender with the speaker.
    *   *Example phrase (m.s. speaker):* `أَنَا مُهْتَمٌّ بِهَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ.` (Ana muhtammun bi-hādhihi al-waẓīfati.) - I am interested in this job.
        *   **New Noun: "Job / Position"**
            *   **`وَظِيفَةٌ` (waẓīfatun)** - 'wa-ẓee-fa-tun' (feminine noun).
        *   *Notice:* `بِـ` (in) makes `هَذِهِ` (this) take Kasra, and `الْوَظِيفَةِ` (the job) also takes Kasra as an adjective to `هَذِهِ` (Day 11, 12).

### 5. Stating Goals and Aspirations (`هَدَفٌ`)

Interviewers want to know your future plans.

*   **New Noun: "Goal / Aim"**
    *   **`هَدَفٌ` (hadafun)** - 'ha-da-fun' (masculine noun).
    *   *Example phrase:* `هَدَفِي أَنْ أَعْمَلَ بِجِدٍّ.` (Hadafī an a'mala bi-jiddin.) - My goal is to work hard.
        *   *Notice:* `هَدَفِي` (my goal - Idafa with pronoun `ـي`, Day 7). `أَنْ أَعْمَلَ` (to work - `أَنْ` makes `أَعْمَلَ` (I work) Subjunctive, Day 60). `بِجِدٍّ` (hard/seriously - Day 59).
    *   You can also combine this with `آمُلُ أَنْ...` (I hope to..., Day 67).
    *   *Example:* `آمُلُ أَنْ أُحَقِّقَ هَدَفِي.` (Āmulū an uḥaqqiqa hadafī.) - I hope to achieve my goal. (`أُحَقِّقَ` - I achieve, from `حَقَّقَ` he achieved, Form II verb, Subjunctive because of `أَنْ`).

### 6. Expressing Gratitude (`شُكْرًا لَكَ.`)

Always end on a polite note.

*   **`شُكْرًا لَكَ.` (shukran laka.)** - Thank you (to a masculine singular person). (Day 2, 7)
    *   *For a feminine singular person:* `شُكْرًا لَكِ.` (shukran laki.)
    *   *For a group:* `شُكْرًا لَكُمْ.` (shukran lakum.)

These phrases will significantly help you navigate an interview situation in Arabic with confidence!

## Examples

Let's combine our new interview language with grammar and vocabulary you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **"Tell me about yourself (m.s.), please."**
    *   `حَدِّثْنِي عَنْ نَفْسِكَ، مِنْ فَضْلِكَ.` (Ḥaddithnī 'an nafsika, min faḍlika.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنْ فَضْلِكَ` (please - Day 31).

2.  **"I have good experience in the field of education."**
    *   `لَدَيَّ خِبْرَةٌ جَيِّدَةٌ فِي مَجَالِ التَّعْلِيمِ.` (Ladayya khibratun jayyidatun fī majāli at-ta'leemi.)
        *   *New word:* `مَجَالٌ` (majālun) - field, area (masculine noun). `تَعْلِيمٌ` (ta'leemun) - education (Masdar of `عَلَّمَ` - he taught, Form II).
        *   *Notice:* `خِبْرَةٌ جَيِّدَةٌ` (good experience) - adjective `جَيِّدَةٌ` agrees with `خِبْرَةٌ`. `مَجَالِ التَّعْلِيمِ` (field of education) is an Idafa.

3.  **"I am interested in this new company."** (spoken by a man)
    *   `أَنَا مُهْتَمٌّ بِهَذِهِ الشَّرِكَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Ana muhtammun bi-hādhihi ash-sharikatī al-jadeedati.)
        *   *Notice:* `مُهْتَمٌّ` (interested) is masculine. `بِـ` (in) makes `هَذِهِ الشَّرِكَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` (this new company) take Kasra. `الشَّرِكَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` is definite with Kasra.

4.  **"My goal is to achieve success in this job."**
    *   `هَدَفِي أَنْ أُحَقِّقَ النَّجَاحَ فِي هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ.` (Hadafī an uḥaqqiqa an-najāḥa fī hādhihi al-waẓīfati.)
        *   *Notice:* `هَدَفِي` (my goal). `أَنْ أُحَقِّقَ` (to achieve - `أُحَقِّقُ` I achieve, Subjunctive, Day 58). `النَّجَاحَ` (the success) is the object. `فِي هَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ` (in this job) - `الْوَظِيفَةِ` takes Kasra.

5.  **"Why are you (m.s.) interested in this job?"**
    *   `لِمَاذَا أَنْتَ مُهْتَمٌّ بِهَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ؟` (Limādhā anta muhtammun bi-hādhihi al-waẓīfati?)
        *   *Notice:* `لِمَاذَا` (why - Day 28). `أَنْتَ مُهْتَمٌّ` (you are interested). `بِهَذِهِ الْوَظِيفَةِ` (in this job).

6.  **"I hope to work with you (m.p.) hand in hand."**
    *   `آمُلُ أَنْ أَعْمَلَ مَعَكُمْ يَدًا بِيَدٍ.` (Āmulū an a'mala ma'akum yadan bi-yadin.)
        *   *Notice:* `آمُلُ أَنْ أَعْمَلَ` (I hope to work - Day 67, Subjunctive). `مَعَكُمْ` (with you all - `مَعَ` (with) + `ـكُمْ` (you plural), Day 27, 7). `يَدًا بِيَدٍ` (hand in hand - Day 66).

7.  **"It seems that you (f.s.) have excellent experience."**
    *   `يَبْدُو أَنَّ لَدَيْكِ خِبْرَةً مُمْتَازَةً.` (Yabdū anna ladayki khibratan mumtāzatan.)
        *   *New word:* `مُمْتَازٌ` (mumtāzun) - excellent (masculine adjective). `مُمْتَازَةٌ` (feminine).
        *   *Notice:* `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that - Day 80). `لَدَيْكِ` (you (f.s.) have - `لَدَى` + `ـكِ`). `خِبْرَةً مُمْتَازَةً` (excellent experience) takes Fatha because it's the nominal subject after `لَدَيْكِ` in an `أَنَّ` clause construction (`أَنَّ` + `لَدَيْكِ` + noun is considered `أَنَّ` + subject + predicate). This is a more advanced structure for now, but `خِبْرَةً` takes Fatha here.
