## Lesson

Today, we are starting with the Arabic alphabet. This is the foundation of reading and writing Arabic, so it's a very important day!

1.  **The Arabic Alphabet**
    *   The Arabic language has 28 letters.
    *   It is written from right to left. This is a bit different from English, so remember to always start reading from the right side of the page and move towards the left.

2.  **Letters and Sounds**
    *   Every letter has a sound, just like in English. We will learn each letter's name and its primary sound today.

3.  **The Short Vowels (Harakat)**
    *   In Arabic, we use special marks called Harakat to show short vowel sounds. Think of them like tiny helpers that tell us how to pronounce a letter.
    *   There are three main short vowels:
        *   **Fatha ( َ )**: This mark is placed **above** a letter and makes a short "a" sound, like the 'a' in 'cat'.
        *   **Damma ( ُ )**: This mark is placed **above** a letter and makes a short "u" sound, like the 'u' in 'put'.
        *   **Kasra ( ِ )**: This mark is placed **below** a letter and makes a short "i" sound, like the 'i' in 'sit'.

Now, let's meet all 28 letters of the Arabic alphabet! We will learn their names, their sounds, and how they sound with each of the three short vowels.

## Examples

We will go through each letter one by one. Pay close attention to how each letter looks and its sound.

1.  **أَلِف (Alif)**: It is often silent or represents a short "a" sound.
    *   أَ : (A as in 'apple')
    *   أُ : (U as in 'put')
    *   إِ : (I as in 'ink') - *Note: When Alif has a Kasra, a tiny 'hamza' mark appears below it.*

2.  **بَاء (Ba)**: Sounds like 'b' in 'bat'.
    *   بَ : (Ba as in 'bat')
    *   بُ : (Bu as in 'book')
    *   بِ : (Bi as in 'bit')

3.  **تَاء (Ta)**: Sounds like 't' in 'tap'.
    *   تَ : (Ta as in 'tap')
    *   تُ : (Tu as in 'tune')
    *   تِ : (Ti as in 'tick')

4.  **ثَاء (Tha)**: Sounds like 'th' in 'think'.
    *   ثَ : (Tha as in 'thin')
    *   ثُ : (Thu as in 'thumb')
    *   ثِ : (Thi as in 'thick')

5.  **جِيم (Jim)**: Sounds like 'j' in 'jump'.
    *   جَ : (Ja as in 'jam')
    *   جُ : (Ju as in 'jug')
    *   جِ : (Ji as in 'jig')

6.  **حَاء (Ha)**: A strong, breathy 'h' sound from the back of the throat, not found in English.
    *   حَ : (Ha, breathy 'h')
    *   حُ : (Hu, breathy 'h')
    *   حِ : (Hi, breathy 'h')

7.  **خَاء (Kha)**: A guttural 'kh' sound, like 'ch' in Scottish 'loch' or German 'Bach'.
    *   خَ : (Kha, guttural 'kh')
    *   خُ : (Khu, guttural 'kh')
    *   خِ : (Khi, guttural 'kh')

8.  **دَال (Dal)**: Sounds like 'd' in 'dog'.
    *   دَ : (Da as in 'dad')
    *   دُ : (Du as in 'dunk')
    *   دِ : (Di as in 'dig')

9.  **ذَال (Dhal)**: Sounds like 'th' in 'that'.
    *   ذَ : (Dha as in 'this')
    *   ذُ : (Dhu as in 'them')
    *   ذِ : (Dhi as in 'then')

10. **رَاء (Ra)**: A rolled or trilled 'r', like in Spanish 'pero'.
    *   رَ : (Ra, rolled 'r')
    *   رُ : (Ru, rolled 'r')
    *   رِ : (Ri, rolled 'r')

11. **زَاي (Zay)**: Sounds like 'z' in 'zebra'.
    *   زَ : (Za as in 'zap')
    *   زُ : (Zu as in 'zoom')
    *   زِ : (Zi as in 'zip')

12. **سِين (Seen)**: Sounds like 's' in 'sun'.
    *   سَ : (Sa as in 'sat')
    *   سُ : (Su as in 'sum')
    *   سِ : (Si as in 'sit')

13. **شِين (Sheen)**: Sounds like 'sh' in 'she'.
    *   شَ : (Sha as in 'shape')
    *   شُ : (Shu as in 'shush')
    *   شِ : (Shi as in 'ship')

14. **صَاد (Sad)**: A heavy, emphatic 's' sound, not found in English. Make your mouth rounder than for 'seen'.
    *   صَ : (Sa, emphatic 's')
    *   صُ : (Su, emphatic 's')
    *   صِ : (Si, emphatic 's')

15. **ضَاد (Dad)**: A heavy, emphatic 'd' sound, not found in English. Make your mouth rounder than for 'dal'.
    *   ضَ : (Da, emphatic 'd')
    *   ضُ : (Du, emphatic 'd')
    *   ضِ : (Di, emphatic 'd')

16. **طَاء (Ta)**: A heavy, emphatic 't' sound, not found in English. Make your mouth rounder than for 'ta'.
    *   طَ : (Ta, emphatic 't')
    *   طُ : (Tu, emphatic 't')
    *   طِ : (Ti, emphatic 't')

17. **ظَاء (Dha)**: A heavy, emphatic 'th' sound, like 'th' in 'this' but heavier. Not found in English.
    *   ظَ : (Dha, emphatic 'th')
    *   ظُ : (Dhu, emphatic 'th')
    *   ظِ : (Dhi, emphatic 'th')

18. **عَيْن (Ain)**: A deep guttural sound from the back of the throat, no English equivalent. Imagine a constricted 'a' sound.
    *   عَ : (Aa, deep guttural)
    *   عُ : (Uu, deep guttural)
    *   عِ : (Ii, deep guttural)

19. **غَيْن (Ghain)**: A guttural 'gh' sound, like a French 'r' or 'g' in gargle.
    *   غَ : (Gha, guttural 'gh')
    *   غُ : (Ghu, guttural 'gh')
    *   غِ : (Ghi, guttural 'gh')

20. **فَاء (Fa)**: Sounds like 'f' in 'fish'.
    *   فَ : (Fa as in 'fan')
    *   فُ : (Fu as in 'full')
    *   فِ : (Fi as in 'fill')

21. **قَاف (Qaf)**: A deep 'k' sound, made further back in the throat than a regular 'k'.
    *   قَ : (Qa, deep 'k')
    *   قُ : (Qu, deep 'k')
    *   قِ : (Qi, deep 'k')

22. **كَاف (Kaf)**: Sounds like 'k' in 'kite'.
    *   كَ : (Ka as in 'cat')
    *   كُ : (Ku as in 'cup')
    *   كِ : (Ki as in 'kit')

23. **لَام (Lam)**: Sounds like 'l' in 'lamp'.
    *   لَ : (La as in 'lap')
    *   لُ : (Lu as in 'lunar')
    *   لِ : (Li as in 'lid')

24. **مِيم (Mim)**: Sounds like 'm' in 'mat'.
    *   مَ : (Ma as in 'mat')
    *   مُ : (Mu as in 'muffin')
    *   مِ : (Mi as in 'mint')

25. **نُون (Nun)**: Sounds like 'n' in 'net'.
    *   نَ : (Na as in 'nap')
    *   نُ : (Nu as in 'nut')
    *   نِ : (Ni as in 'nip')

26. **هَاء (Ha)**: A soft 'h' sound, like 'h' in 'hat'.
    *   هَ : (Ha as in 'hat')
    *   هُ : (Hu as in 'huh')
    *   هِ : (Hi as in 'him')

27. **وَاو (Waw)**: Sounds like 'w' in 'water'.
    *   وَ : (Wa as in 'was')
    *   وُ : (Wu as in 'wool')
    *   وِ : (Wi as in 'win')

28. **يَاء (Ya)**: Sounds like 'y' in 'yes'.
    *   يَ : (Ya as in 'yam')
    *   يُ : (Yu as in 'yule')
    *   يِ : (Yi as in 'yip')
