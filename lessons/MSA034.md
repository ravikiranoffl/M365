# Day 34 - Pronoun reference in long sentences

## Lesson

1.  **What is Pronoun Reference?**
    *   Think of it like this: When you're talking, you don't keep repeating the same name over and over. You say, "Ahmad went to the school. **He** studied there. **His** book was on the desk."
    *   In these sentences, "He" and "His" refer back to "Ahmad." In Arabic, pronouns work the same way! They replace nouns to make sentences smoother and avoid repetition.
    *   **Pronoun reference** is simply figuring out *which noun* a pronoun is replacing or referring to.

2.  **The Golden Rule: Matching Gender and Number**
    *   A pronoun **ALWAYS** has to match the noun it refers back to in:
        *   **Gender:** Masculine or Feminine
        *   **Number:** Singular or Plural (We'll mostly focus on singular and how broken plurals are treated for now).
    *   If you're talking about a masculine singular student, you'll use a masculine singular pronoun for him (`هُوَ`, `ـهُ`).
    *   If you're talking about a feminine singular student, you'll use a feminine singular pronoun for her (`هِيَ`, `ـهَا`).
    *   **Special Case for Non-Human Broken Plurals (from Day 24):** Remember that a non-human broken plural (like `كُتُبٌ` - books, `غُرَفٌ` - rooms) is treated grammatically as **feminine singular**. This means any pronoun referring to it will also be feminine singular!

3.  **How Pronouns Refer in Sentences**

    Let's look at different kinds of pronouns and how they refer:

    *   **A) Independent Pronouns (`هُوَ`, `هِيَ`, `هُمْ`, `أَنَا`, `نَحْنُ`, `أَنْتَ`, `أَنْتِ`)**
        *   These usually refer to the subject that was just mentioned in the previous sentence or at the beginning of the current sentence.
        *   **Example:** `الطَّالِبُ يَكْتُبُ. هُوَ جَدِيدٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu yaktubu. Huwa jadeedun.)
            *   "The student writes. **He** is new."
            *   The `هُوَ` (he) clearly refers back to `الطَّالِبُ` (the student).
        *   **Example (with a non-human broken plural):** `الْكُتُبُ كَبِيرَةٌ. هِيَ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Al-kutubu kabeeratun. Hiya fawqa al-maktabi.)
            *   "The books are big. **They** are on the desk."
            *   The `هِيَ` (she/it) refers back to `الْكُتُبُ` (the books). Even though "books" are plural, because they are non-human broken plural, we use the feminine singular `هِيَ`.

    *   **B) Attached Possessive Pronouns (`ـهُ`, `ـهَا`, `ـكَ`, `ـكِ`, `ـِي`, `ـنَا`, `ـهُمْ`)**
        *   These pronouns attach to nouns (in an Idafa construct) to show possession ("his book," "her car"). They refer back to a possessor mentioned earlier.
        *   **Example:** `الطَّالِبُ جَدِيدٌ. كِتَابُهُ صَغِيرٌ.` (Aṭ-ṭālibu jadeedun. Kitābuhu ṣagheerun.)
            *   "The student is new. **His** book is small."
            *   The `ـهُ` (his) attached to `كِتَابُ` refers back to `الطَّالِبُ` (the student).
        *   **Example (with a non-human broken plural):** `الْغُرَفُ جَمِيلَةٌ. أَبْوَابُهَا صَغِيرَةٌ.` (Al-ghurafu jameelatun. Abwābuhā ṣagheeratun.)
            *   "The rooms are beautiful. **Their** doors are small."
            *   The `ـهَا` (her/its) attached to `أَبْوَابُ` refers back to `الْغُرَفُ` (the rooms). Even though "rooms" are plural, as a non-human broken plural, we use the feminine singular `ـهَا`.

    *   **C) Pronouns within Conjunctions (`لِأَنَّهُ`, `لِأَنَّهَا`)**
        *   You've learned `لِأَنَّهُ` (because he/it is) and `لِأَنَّهَا` (because she/it is) from Day 28. The attached pronoun (`ـهُ` or `ـهَا`) refers to the subject of the previous clause.
        *   **Example:** `هُوَ يَكْتُبُ لِأَنَّهُ طَالِبٌ.` (Huwa yaktubu li'annahu taalibun.)
            *   "He writes **because he is** a student."
            *   The `ـهُ` in `لِأَنَّهُ` refers back to the initial `هُوَ` (he).
        *   **Example:** `السَّيَّارَةُ جَمِيلَةٌ لِأَنَّهَا صَغِيرَةٌ.` (As-sayyāratu jameelatun li'annahā ṣagheeratun.)
            *   "The car is beautiful **because it is** small."
            *   The `ـهَا` in `لِأَنَّهَا` refers back to `السَّيَّارَةُ` (the car).

    *   **D) Pronouns in Relative Clauses (`الَّذِي`, `الَّتِي`)**
        *   These words themselves act as "pronouns" that refer back to the definite noun they describe (from Day 31). The verb that follows also implicitly refers back.
        *   **Example:** `الْمُدَرِّسُ الَّذِي يَكْتُبُ فِي الْجَامِعَةِ.` (Al-mudarrisu alladhī yaktubu fī al-jāmi'ati.)
            *   "The teacher **who** writes in the university."
            *   `الَّذِي` refers to `الْمُدَرِّسُ`. The implicit "he" in `يَكْتُبُ` also refers to `الْمُدَرِّسُ`.
        *   **Example:** `الْكِتَابُ الَّذِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ.` (Al-kitābu alladhī fawqa al-maktabi.)
            *   "The book **that** is on the desk."
            *   `الَّذِي` refers to `الْكِتَابُ`.

Understanding pronoun reference makes your reading much clearer and helps you build more logical sentences. Always ask yourself: "Who or what is this pronoun talking about?"

## Examples

Let's look at more complex sentences and identify the pronoun referents.

1.  `الطَّالِبَةُ تَدْرُسُ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ. هِيَ ذَكِيَّةٌ وَ مُدَرِّسَتُهَا جَمِيلَةٌ.`
    *   "The student (f) studies in the school. **She** is smart and **her** teacher is beautiful."
    *   The `هِيَ` (she) refers to `الطَّالِبَةُ` (the student - f).
    *   The `ـهَا` (her) in `مُدَرِّسَتُهَا` refers to `الطَّالِبَةُ` (the student - f).

2.  `الْكِتَابُ الْكَبِيرُ فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ. هُوَ أَجْمَلُ كُتُبِ الْجَامِعَةِ.`
    *   "The big book is on the desk. **It** is the most beautiful of the university's books."
    *   The `هُوَ` (he/it) refers to `الْكِتَابُ الْكَبِيرُ` (the big book).

3.  `نَحْنُ نَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ. نَحْنُ نُحِبُّهَا لِأَنَّهَا كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Let's simplify `نُحِبُّهَا` to just say "it is big" so we stick to known pronouns. `نُحِبُّهَا` is "we love it.")
    *   Let's rephrase: `نَحْنُ نَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ. هِيَ كَبِيرَةٌ.`
    *   "We go to the school every day. **It** is big."
    *   The `هِيَ` (she/it) refers to `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (the school - f).

4.  `هُوَ يَشْرَبُ الشَّايَ الْآنَ لِأَنَّهُ يَعْمَلُ كَثِيرًا.` (Let's simplify `يَعْمَلُ كَثِيرًا` to `هُوَ مُتْعَبٌ` for "he is tired", using familiar vocab, and `لِأَنَّهُ`.)
    *   Let's rephrase: `هُوَ يَشْرَبُ الشَّايَ الْآنَ لِأَنَّهُ مُتْعَبٌ.` (Huwa yashrabu ash-shāya al-āna li'annahu mut'abun.)
    *   "He is drinking tea now **because he is** tired."
    *   The `هُوَ` (he) at the beginning refers to some implied masculine singular person.
    *   The `ـهُ` in `لِأَنَّهُ` (because he is) refers back to the initial `هُوَ`.

5.  `هَذِهِ الْكُتُبُ الَّتِي دَرَسْتُهَا أَمْسِ. هِيَ جَدِيدَةٌ وَ مُفِيدَةٌ.` (Let's simplify `دَرَسْتُهَا` to just `دَرَسْتُ` and imply the object).
    *   Let's rephrase: `هَذِهِ الْكُتُبُ الَّتِي دَرَسْتُهَا أَمْسِ.` (Hādhihi al-kutubu allatī darastuhā amsi.)
    *   "These are the books **that** I studied yesterday." (The `ـهَا` attached to `دَرَسْتُ` means "I studied *them* (the books)"). This is an attached object pronoun, which we haven't covered comprehensively, so let's simplify for the first grader.
    *   Let's use a simpler structure: `الْكُتُبُ الَّتِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ جَمِيلَةٌ. هِيَ كَبِيرَةٌ.`
    *   "The books that are on the desk are beautiful. **They** are big."
    *   `الَّتِي` refers to `الْكُتُبُ` (non-human broken plural, treated as feminine singular for the relative pronoun).
    *   The `هِيَ` (she/it) refers to `الْكُتُبُ`.

6.  `الطَّالِبُونَ يَذْهَبُونَ إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ. هُمْ يَدْرُسُونَ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ.`
    *   "The students (m) go to the university. **They** study every day."
    *   The `هُمْ` (they) refers to `الطَّالِبُونَ` (the students - m.pl).

## Practice

Now, let's practice identifying pronoun references in longer sentences. I will give you a sentence, and you need to tell me what the highlighted pronoun refers to.

1.  `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ تَكْتُبُ قِصَّةً. **هِيَ** جَمِيلَةٌ.`
    *   What does `هِيَ` (she/it) refer to?

2.  `الْبَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ وَ أَبْوَابُ**هُ** جَدِيدَةٌ.`
    *   What does `ـهُ` (his/its) attached to `أَبْوَابُ` refer to?

3.  `أَنْتَ تَكْتُبُ الْكِتَابَ لِأَنَّ**كَ** طَالِبٌ مُجْتَهِدٌ.` (Let's simplify `مُجْتَهِدٌ` to just `طَالِبٌ`.)
    *   Let's rephrase: `أَنْتَ تَكْتُبُ الْكِتَابَ لِأَنَّ**كَ** طَالِبٌ.` (Anta taktubu al-kitaaba li'annaka taalibun.)
    *   What does `ـكَ` (you-m) in `لِأَنَّكَ` refer to?

4.  `الْمَكَاتِبُ الصَّغِيرَةُ فِي الْغُرْفَةِ. **هِيَ** فَوْقَ الْكُتُبِ.`
    *   What does `هِيَ` (she/it) refer to? (Remember the special rule for plurals!)

5.  `إِذَا دَرَسَ الطَّالِبُ، فَ**هُوَ** سَيَكْتُبُ.`
    *   What does `هُوَ` (he) in `فَهُوَ` refer to?

6.  `أَنَا أَشْرَبُ الشَّايَ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ. سَيَّارَتُ**نَا** جَمِيلَةٌ.` (This is a bit tricky, the `ـنَا` refers to "we", which is "I" + others). Let's make it simpler.
    *   `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ. مُدَرِّسَتُ**هَا** ذَكِيَّةٌ.`
    *   What does `ـهَا` (her/its) in `مُدَرِّسَتُهَا` refer to?

7.  `الْقَلَمُ الَّذِي **هُنَاكَ** أَزْرَقُ.` (Let's simplify `أَزْرَقُ` - blue. Just focus on the pronoun reference.)
    *   Let's rephrase: `الْقَلَمُ الَّذِي فَوْقَ الْمَكْتَبِ جَدِيدٌ.`
    *   What does `الَّذِي` refer to?
