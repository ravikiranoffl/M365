# Day 103 - Economic vocabulary

## Lesson

Understanding economic terms is crucial for anyone engaging with Arabic media or formal discussions. These words often appear in news reports about national development, international trade, and market conditions.

Let's learn some new and essential economic vocabulary!

### 1. `اِسْتِثْمَارٌ` (istihtmārun) - Investment

This noun refers to the act of committing money or capital to an endeavor (like a business or project) with the expectation of obtaining an additional income or profit.

*   **New Word:** `اِسْتِثْمَارٌ` (istihtmārun) - 'is-tith-mā-run'
    *   *Meaning:* Investment (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الِاسْتِثْمَارُ مُهِمٌّ لِلِاقْتِصَادِ.` (Al-istihtmāru muhimun li-l-iqtiṣādi.) - Investment is important for the economy.

### 2. `سَهْمٌ` (sahmun) - Share / Stock (and its plural)

A `سَهْمٌ` is a unit of ownership in a company, which can be bought and sold.

*   **New Word (Singular):** `سَهْمٌ` (sahmun) - 'sah-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Share, stock (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `أَسْهُمٌ` (ashumun) - 'as-hu-mun'
    *   *Meaning:* Shares, stocks (plural noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْكَثِيرُ مِنَ الْأَسْهُمِ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Al-katheeru mina al-ashumi jadeedatun.) - Many shares are new. (Remember `الْكَثِيرُ` from Day 100.)

### 3. `سُوقُ الْأَسْهُمِ` (sūqu al-ashumi) - Stock Market

This is the market where company shares are bought and sold. You already know `سُوقٌ` (market) from Day 51. This is an Idafa construction.

*   **New Phrase:** `سُوقُ الْأَسْهُمِ` (sūqu al-ashumi) - 'soo-qu al-as-hu-mi'
    *   *Meaning:* Stock market (Idafa construction).
    *   *Example:* `سُوقُ الْأَسْهُمِ كَبِيرٌ.` (Sūqu al-ashumi kabeerun.) - The stock market is big.

### 4. `مَالٌ` (mālun) - Money / Wealth

This is a fundamental term for currency or assets.

*   **New Word:** `مَالٌ` (mālun) - 'mā-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Money, wealth (masculine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الْمَالُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-mālu muhimun.) - Money is important.

### 5. `مَالِيٌّ` (māliyyun) - Financial / Monetary

This is an adjective (a nisba adjective, Day 49) derived from `مَالٌ`, meaning "related to money or finance."

*   **New Word (Masculine):** `مَالِيٌّ` (māliyyun) - 'mā-lee-yyun'
    *   *Meaning:* Financial, monetary (masculine adjective).
*   **New Word (Feminine):** `مَالِيَّةٌ` (māliyyatun) - 'mā-lee-yya-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Financial, monetary (feminine adjective).
    *   *Example:* `الْقَرَارُ مَالِيٌّ.` (Al-qarāru māliyyun.) - The decision is financial.

### 6. `تِجَارَةٌ` (tijāratun) - Trade / Commerce

This noun refers to the activity of buying and selling goods and services.

*   **New Word:** `تِجَارَةٌ` (tijāratun) - 'ti-jā-ra-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Trade, commerce (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `التِّجَارَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (At-tijāratu jadeedatun.) - The trade is new.

### 7. `صِنَاعَةٌ` (ṣināʿatun) - Industry / Manufacturing

This noun refers to the production of goods or services, typically in factories or large enterprises.

*   **New Word:** `صِنَاعَةٌ` (ṣināʿatun) - 'ṣi-nā-a-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Industry, manufacturing (feminine noun).
    *   *Example:* `الصِّنَاعَةُ مُهِمَّةٌ لِلدَّوْلَةِ.` (Aṣ-ṣināʿatu muhimmatun li-d-dawlati.) - Industry is important for the state.

These terms are foundational for understanding news and discussions about the economy.

## Examples

Let's integrate these new economic words into sentences using the grammar and structures you already know!

1.  **"It is certain that investment is important for the economy of the state."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الِاسْتِثْمَارَ مُهِمٌّ لِاقْتِصَادِ الدَّوْلَةِ.` (Mina al-mu'akkadi anna al-istihtmāra muhimun li-l-iqtiṣādi ad-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `الِاسْتِثْمَارَ` (Accusative after `أَنَّ`). `لِاقْتِصَادِ الدَّوْلَةِ` (for the economy of the state - Idafa, Day 97 `اِقْتِصَادٌ`, Day 101 `دَوْلَةٌ`).

2.  **"The company wants to undertake a new financial project."**
    *   `تُرِيدُ الشَّرِكَةُ أَنْ تَقُومَ بِمَشْرُوعٍ مَالِيٍّ جَدِيدٍ.` (Tureedu ash-sharikatū an taqūma bi-mashroo'in māliyyin jadeedin.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرِيدُ الشَّرِكَةُ` (known). `أَنْ تَقُومَ بِـ` (wants to undertake - Day 98, Subjunctive). `مَشْرُوعٍ مَالِيٍّ جَدِيدٍ` (a new financial project - `مَشْرُوعٌ` known, `مَالِيٍّ` and `جَدِيدٍ` are adjectives in Genitive after `بِـ`).

3.  **"Perhaps the minister (m.) will speak about trade and industry."**
    *   `رُبَّمَا يَتَكَلَّمُ الْوَزِيرُ عَنِ التِّجَارَةِ وَالصِّنَاعَةِ.` (Rubbamā yatakallamu al-wazeeru 'ani at-tijārati wa-ṣ-ṣināʿati.)
        *   *Notice:* `رُبَّمَا يَتَكَلَّمُ الْوَزِيرُ عَنِ` (perhaps the minister speaks about - Day 80, 97). `التِّجَارَةِ` (Genitive after `عَنِ`). `وَالصِّنَاعَةِ` (and the industry - also Genitive).

4.  **"It is known that the stock market is important for money."**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ سُوقَ الْأَسْهُمِ مُهِمٌّ لِلْمَالِ.` (Mina al-ma'roofi anna sūqa al-ashumi muhimun li-l-māli.)
        *   *Notice:* `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (Day 99). `سُوقَ الْأَسْهُمِ` (the stock market - `سُوقَ` is Accusative as subject of `أَنَّ`, `الْأَسْهُمِ` is Genitive as second term of Idafa). `مُهِمٌّ لِلْمَالِ` (important for money).

5.  **"The ambassador (f.) believes that the financial problem will become difficult."**
    *   `تَعْتَقِدُ السَّفِيرَةُ أَنَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْمَالِيَّةَ سَتُصْبِحُ صَعْبَةً.` (Ta'taqidu as-safeeratū anna al-mushkilata al-māliyyata satūṣbiḥu ṣa'batan.)
        *   *Notice:* `السَّفِيرَةُ` (Day 101). `تَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّ` (Day 79). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْمَالِيَّةَ` (the financial problem - `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` Accusative, `الْمَالِيَّةَ` feminine adjective also Accusative). `سَتُصْبِحُ صَعْبَةً` (will become difficult - Day 98 `يُصْبِحُ`, `صَعْبَةً` Accusative predicate).

6.  **"Fortunately, the new government made investment easy."**
    *   `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ، جَعَلَتِ الْحُكُومَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ الِاسْتِثْمَارَ سَهْلًا.` (Li-ḥusni al-ḥaẓẓi, ja'alati al-ḥukūmatu al-jadeedatu al-istihtmāra sahlan.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِحُسْنِ الْحَظِّ` (Day 99). `جَعَلَتِ` (she made - feminine verb, Day 98). `الْحُكُومَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ` (subject, Day 101). `الِاسْتِثْمَارَ سَهْلًا` (investment easy - both Accusative, Day 98).

These examples show how you can combine new vocabulary with the complex grammar you've learned to form rich and informative sentences.
