# Day 45 - News collocations

## Lesson

1.  **Introducing `خَبَرٌ` (khabarun) - "A News Item / A Report"**
    *   You actually know the word `خَبَرٌ` (khabarun) already! We learned it as "comment" or "predicate" in a nominal sentence (`الْخَبَرُ`).
    *   It also means "a piece of news" or "a report." It is a masculine singular noun.
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'kha-ba-run'.

2.  **Introducing `أَخْبَارٌ` (akhbārun) - "News" (Plural)**
    *   When we want to talk about "news" in general, or multiple items of news, we use the plural form: `أَخْبَارٌ` (akhbārun).
    *   **Crucial Rule:** `أَخْبَارٌ` is a **non-human broken plural**. What does that mean for adjectives that describe it? Remember from Day 24: non-human broken plurals are treated as **feminine singular** for adjectives!
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'akh-baa-run' (long 'aa').

3.  **New Verbs for Engaging with News:**

    *   **"To Read": `قَرَأَ` (qara'a) and `يَقْرَأُ` (yaqra'u)**
        *   Root: `ق ر أ` (q-r-a)
        *   Past Tense (he read): `قَرَأَ` (qara'a) - 'qa-ra-a'
        *   Present Tense (he reads): `يَقْرَأُ` (yaqra'u) - 'yaq-ra-u'
        *   Present Tense (I read): `أَقْرَأُ` (aqra'u) - 'aq-ra-u'
        *   Present Tense (she reads / you-m.s. read): `تَقْرَأُ` (taqra'u) - 'taq-ra-u'

    *   **"To Hear": `سَمِعَ` (sami'a) and `يَسْمَعُ` (yasma'u)**
        *   Root: `س م ع` (s-m-')
        *   Past Tense (he heard): `سَمِعَ` (sami'a) - 'sa-mi-a'
        *   Present Tense (he hears): `يَسْمَعُ` (yasma'u) - 'yas-ma-u'
        *   Present Tense (I hear): `أَسْمَعُ` (asma'u) - 'as-ma-u'
        *   Present Tense (she hears / you-m.s. hear): `تَسْمَعُ` (tasma'u) - 'tas-ma-u'

4.  **Common News Collocations (Words that go together):**

    *   **Important News:**
        *   Since `أَخْبَارٌ` is a non-human broken plural, its adjective `مُهِمٌّ` (important - m.s. from Day 43) becomes `مُهِمَّةٌ` (muhimmatun - f.s.).
        *   `أَخْبَارٌ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Akhbārun muhimmatun.) - Important news. (Note: indefinite for now)
        *   Or, if definite: `الْأَخْبَارُ الْمُهِمَّةُ.` (Al-akhbāru al-muhimmatu.) - The important news.

    *   **New News:**
        *   Similarly, `جَدِيدٌ` (new - m.s. from Day 3) becomes `جَدِيدَةٌ` (jadeedatun - f.s.).
        *   `أَخْبَارٌ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Akhbārun jadeedatun.) - New news.
        *   Or, if definite: `الْأَخْبَارُ الْجَدِيدَةُ.` (Al-akhbāru al-jadeedatu.) - The new news.

    *   **Big News:**
        *   `كَبِيرٌ` (big - m.s. from Day 3) becomes `كَبِيرَةٌ` (kabeeratun - f.s.).
        *   `أَخْبَارٌ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Akhbārun kabeeratun.) - Big news.

    *   **Reading the News:**
        *   `يَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ.` (Yaqra'u al-akhbāra.) - He reads the news. (`الْأَخْبَارَ` takes Fatha as it's the object.)
        *   `أَقْرَأُ الْخَبَرَ.` (Aqra'u al-khabara.) - I read the news item.

    *   **Hearing the News:**
        *   `يَسْمَعُ الْأَخْبَارَ.` (Yasma'u al-akhbāra.) - He hears the news.
        *   `تَسْمَعُ الْخَبَرَ.` (Tasma'u al-khabara.) - She hears the news item.

    *   **Writing the News:**
        *   You already know `يَكْتُبُ` (he writes) from Day 20.
        *   `يَكْتُبُ الْأَخْبَارَ.` (Yaktubu al-akhbāra.) - He writes the news.

You're now ready to talk about the news in Arabic! This introduces very common vocabulary and reinforces the special rule for non-human broken plurals.

## Examples

Let's use these news collocations in full sentences, combining them with grammar you know!

1.  **The news is important.**
    *   `الْأَخْبَارُ مُهِمَّةٌ.` (Al-akhbāru muhimmatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْأَخْبَارُ` (the news) is the subject, and `مُهِمَّةٌ` (important) is the feminine singular comment matching `الْأَخْبَارُ`.

2.  **I read the new news in the morning.**
    *   `أَقْرَأُ الْأَخْبَارَ الْجَدِيدَةَ فِي الصَّبَاحِ.` (Aqra'u al-akhbāra al-jadeedata fī aṣ-ṣabāḥi.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْأَخْبَارَ` is the object, so it takes Fatha. `الْجَدِيدَةَ` is its feminine singular adjective, also taking Fatha to match.

3.  **She wants to hear the big news.**
    *   `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَسْمَعَ الْأَخْبَارَ الْكَبِيرَةَ.` (Tureedu an tasma'a al-akhbāra al-kabeerata.)
        *   *Notice:* `تُرِيدُ` (she wants) from Day 44. `أَنْ تَسْمَعَ` (to hear) with `تَسْمَعُ` getting a Fatha ending because of `أَنْ` (Day 38, 44). `الْأَخْبَارَ الْكَبِيرَةَ` is the object with its adjective.

4.  **Indeed, he writes important news every day.**
    *   `إِنَّهُ يَكْتُبُ أَخْبَارًا مُهِمَّةً كُلَّ يَوْمٍ.` (Innahu yaktubu akhbāran muhimmatan kulla yawmin.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِنَّهُ` (indeed, he is) from Day 37. `أَخْبَارًا مُهِمَّةً` are indefinite objects with matching adjective, taking Tanween Fatha.

5.  **If I read the news, then I will go to the university.**
    *   `إِذَا قَرَأْتُ الْأَخْبَارَ، فَأَنَا سَأَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ.` (Idhā qara'tu al-akhbāra, fa'anā sa'adhhabu ilā al-jāmi'ati.)
        *   *Notice:* `إِذَا قَرَأْتُ` (if I read - past tense for the condition) from Day 32. `فَأَنَا سَأَذْهَبُ` (then I will go) for the result.

6.  **He said that the news is new.**
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْأَخْبَارَ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (Qāla anna al-akhbāra jadeedatun.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَ` (he said) and `أَنَّ` (that) from Day 39. `الْأَخْبَارَ` gets a Fatha because of `أَنَّ`. `جَدِيدَةٌ` is the feminine singular comment.

*   خَبَرٌ (khabarun) - news (item), report (m.s. noun)
*   أَخْبَارٌ (akhbārun) - news (plural)
*   قَرَأَ (qara'a) - he read (past tense verb)
*   يَقْرَأُ (yaqra'u) - he reads (present tense verb)
*   أَقْرَأُ (aqra'u) - I read (present tense verb)
*   تَقْرَأُ (taqra'u) - she reads / you (m.s.) read (present tense verb)
*   قِرَاءَةٌ (qirā'atun) - reading (verbal noun / Masdar for `قَرَأَ`)
*   سَمِعَ (sami'a) - he heard (past tense verb)
*   يَسْمَعُ (yasma'u) - he hears (present tense verb)
*   أَسْمَعُ (asma'u) - I hear (present tense verb)
*   تَسْمَعُ (tasma'u) - she hears / you (m.s.) hear (present tense verb)
*   سَمَاعٌ (samā'un) - hearing (verbal noun / Masdar for `سَمِعَ`)
