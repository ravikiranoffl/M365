# Day 29 - Describing Daily Routines

## Lesson

1.  **Talking About "Every Day"**
    *   To say "every day" in Arabic, we use a new phrase: **كُلَّ يَوْمٍ (kulla yawmin)**.
        *   `كُلَّ` (kulla) means "every" or "all".
        *   `يَوْمٍ` (yawmin) means "day". It has Tanween Kasra here because it follows `كُلَّ`, a rule you'll fully understand later, but for now just learn `كُلَّ يَوْمٍ` as a fixed phrase.
    *   We use this phrase to show that an action happens habitually, like a routine.

2.  **New Verbs for Daily Actions (Present Tense)**
    *   We'll learn four new common verbs that describe actions we do regularly. Remember how present tense verbs use prefixes to show who is doing the action (Day 19-20)? We'll use those same patterns!

    *   **A) To Go (Root: ذ ه ب - dh-h-b)**
        *   `يَذْهَبُ` (yadhhabu) - **He goes / He is going.** (Prefix يَـ)
        *   `تَذْهَبُ` (tadhhabu) - **She goes / She is going.** (Prefix تَـ) (Also: You (m.s.) go / are going)
        *   `أَذْهَبُ` (adhhabu) - **I go / I am going.** (Prefix أَـ)

    *   **B) To Return (Root: ر ج ع - r-j-')**
        *   `يَرْجِعُ` (yarji'u) - **He returns / He is returning.** (Prefix يَـ)
        *   `تَرْجِعُ` (tarji'u) - **She returns / She is returning.** (Prefix تَـ) (Also: You (m.s.) return / are returning)
        *   `أَرْجِعُ` (arji'u) - **I return / I am returning.** (Prefix أَـ)

    *   **C) To Eat (Root: أ ك ل - a-k-l)**
        *   `يَأْكُلُ` (ya'kulu) - **He eats / He is eating.** (Prefix يَـ)
        *   `تَأْكُلُ` (ta'kulu) - **She eats / She is eating.** (Prefix تَـ) (Also: You (m.s.) eat / are eating)
        *   `آكُلُ` (aakulu) - **I eat / I am eating.** (Prefix آـ)
            *   *Notice*: For "I eat," `أَأْكُلُ` becomes `آكُلُ` with a long 'aa' sound. This is a special way Arabic handles two Hamza letters together. Just remember `آكُلُ` for "I eat."

    *   **D) To Drink (Root: ش ر ب - sh-r-b)**
        *   `يَشْرَبُ` (yashrabu) - **He drinks / He is drinking.** (Prefix يَـ)
        *   `تَشْرَبُ` (tashrabu) - **She drinks / She is drinking.** (Prefix تَـ) (Also: You (m.s.) drink / are drinking)
        *   `أَشْرَبُ` (ashrabu) - **I drink / I am drinking.** (Prefix أَـ)

3.  **Times of the Day (New Adverbial Phrases)**
    *   We'll use prepositions you already know (`فِي` - in) with nouns for parts of the day to say "in the morning," "at noon," etc. Remember the Kasra Rule (Day 10) for nouns after prepositions!
    *   `فِي الصَّبَاحِ` (fī aṣ-ṣabāḥi) - **in the morning** (`صَبَاحٌ` - morning)
    *   `فِي الظُّهْرِ` (fī aẓ-ẓuhri) - **at noon** (`ظُهْرٌ` - noon)
    *   `فِي الْمَسَاءِ` (fī al-masā'i) - **in the evening** (`مَسَاءٌ` - evening)
    *   `فِي اللَّيْلِ` (fī al-layli) - **at night** (`لَيْلٌ` - night)

4.  **New Nouns (as Objects)**
    *   When these verbs take an object (e.g., "eat *food*"), the object will end with a Fatha ( َ ) or Tanween Fatha ( ً ).
    *   `الطَّعَامَ` (aṭ-ṭa'āma) - **the food**
    *   `الشَّايَ` (ash-shāya) - **the tea**
    *   `الْمَاءَ` (al-mā'a) - **the water**
    *   Remember `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (the school) and `الْبَيْتِ` (the house) from previous lessons. We'll use them with `إِلَى` (to) and `مِنْ` (from).

Now you have all the pieces to start building sentences about daily routines!

## Examples

Let's put all these new words and rules into practice to describe simple daily routines!

1.  **I go to the school every day.**
    *   `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ.` (Ana adhhabu ilā al-madrasati kulla yawmin.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَذْهَبُ` for "I go," `إِلَى` makes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` take Kasra.

2.  **He returns from the school in the afternoon.**
    *   `هُوَ يَرْجِعُ مِنَ الْمَدْرَسَةِ فِي الظُّهْرِ.` (Huwa yarji'u mina al-madrasati fī aẓ-ẓuhri.)
        *   *Notice*: `يَرْجِعُ` for "he returns," `مِنْ` (from) makes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` take Kasra. `فِي الظُّهْرِ` for "at noon."

3.  **She eats the food in the evening.**
    *   `هِيَ تَأْكُلُ الطَّعَامَ فِي الْمَسَاءِ.` (Hiya ta'kulu aṭ-ṭa'āma fī al-masā'i.)
        *   *Notice*: `تَأْكُلُ` for "she eats," `الطَّعَامَ` takes Fatha as the object.

4.  **I drink the water in the morning.**
    *   `أَنَا أَشْرَبُ الْمَاءَ فِي الصَّبَاحِ.` (Ana ashrabu al-mā'a fī aṣ-ṣabāḥi.)

5.  **He goes to the house every day and he eats the food.**
    *   `هُوَ يَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ وَ هُوَ يَأْكُلُ الطَّعَامَ.` (Huwa yadhhabu ilā al-bayti kulla yawmin wa huwa ya'kulu aṭ-ṭa'āma.)
        *   *Notice*: We used `وَ` (and) to connect two actions in his routine!

6.  **I study in my room at night and I drink tea.**
    *   `أَنَا أَدْرُسُ فِي غُرْفَتِي فِي اللَّيْلِ وَ أَنَا أَشْرَبُ الشَّايَ.` (Ana adrusu fī ghurfatī fī al-layli wa ana ashrabu ash-shāya.)
        *   *Notice*: `غُرْفَتِي` (my room) is an Idafa.

You are now constructing full sentences about daily activities! This is incredibly useful for real-world communication.
