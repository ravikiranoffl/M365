# Day 113 - Write 400-word article

## Lesson

Writing a 400-word article in Arabic might seem like a huge task, but we'll break it down. An article, especially a news-style or analytical one, follows a clear structure:

1.  **Introduction:** Sets the stage, introduces the main topic.
2.  **Body Paragraphs:** Develop the main ideas, provide details, arguments, and different perspectives.
3.  **Conclusion:** Summarizes the main points, offers a final thought or outlook.

The key to writing a good article at your stage is to consciously use the sophisticated tools you've learned. This isn't about perfectly replicating a native speaker's style yet, but about demonstrating your ability to deploy:

*   **Diverse Vocabulary:** From government, legal, economic, diplomatic, and conflict categories.
*   **Verb + Preposition Collocations:** To express specific actions.
*   **Light Verb Constructions:** To show changes of state or the performance of actions.
*   **Stance Markers:** To express certainty, common knowledge, doubt, or emotions.
*   **Advanced Connectors:** To link ideas logically, show contrast, add information, or draw conclusions.
*   **Morphology in action:** Using active/passive participles, Masdars, and correct verb tenses.
*   **Grammatical Accuracy:** Ensuring correct noun case endings (Nominative, Accusative, Genitive) and verb conjugations (Indicative, Subjunctive, Jussive).

**How to approach writing an article:**

1.  **Choose a Topic:** Select a topic that allows you to integrate your learned vocabulary. A news report about a fictional country's political or economic situation is perfect. For example: "The New Government's Plans for the Economy and International Relations."
2.  **Outline Your Ideas:**
    *   **Introduction:** What is the main point of your article? (e.g., A new government is in power, facing challenges.)
    *   **Body Paragraph 1 (e.g., Government & Economy):** What are the government's economic plans? (e.g., Investment, stock market, trade, industry). How will it make things easy/difficult?
    *   **Body Paragraph 2 (e.g., Diplomacy & Conflict):** How are international relations? Are there conflicts? Negotiations? What about human rights or security?
    *   **Conclusion:** What's the outlook? Is the situation hopeful or difficult?
3.  **Draft Sentence by Sentence:** Don't worry about perfection initially. Focus on getting your ideas down, using at least one new grammatical structure or vocabulary item in each sentence where appropriate.
4.  **Review and Refine:**
    *   **Check Case Endings:** Are all subjects, objects, and words after prepositions or in Idafa constructions in the correct case?
    *   **Verb Forms:** Are your verbs in the correct tense and mood (Indicative, Subjunctive, Jussive)?
    *   **Adjective Agreement:** Do your adjectives match the nouns in gender, number, and case?
    *   **Flow:** Do your connectors (`عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ`, `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ`, `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ`, etc.) make sense and help the article flow smoothly?
    *   **Word Count:** Adjust by adding more details or combining simpler sentences.

This exercise is designed to help you synthesize all your knowledge. Don't be afraid to make mistakes; that's how we learn!

فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، يَعْتَقِدُ الْقَاضِي الْعَالِمُ، عُمَرُ، أَنَّ حِفْظَ حُقُوقِ الْإِنْسَانِ وَتَطْبِيقَ الْعَدَالَةِ بِشَكْلٍ كَامِلٍ وَسَرِيعٍ مُهِمٌّ جِدًّا لِتَحْقِيقِ الْأَمْنِ الشَّامِلِ وَالسَّلَامِ فِي الْبَلَدِ. لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، الْجَرَائِمُ مُتَزَايِدَةٌ بِشَكْلٍ مَلْحُوظٍ فِي الْمُدُنِ الْكَبِيرَةِ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُوَاجِهُ الشُّرْطَةُ وَالْمَحْكَمَةُ تَحَدِّيَاتٍ قَانُونِيَّةً وَاجْتِمَاعِيَّةً صَعْبَةً فِي حَلِّ هَذِهِ الْمَشَاكِلِ. لَكِنَّ الْجَمِيعَ يَأْمُلُ أَنْ تُصْبِحَ الدَّوْلَةُ أَقْوَى وَأَكْثَرَ عَدَالَةً وَتَقَدُّمًا فِي الْمُسْتَقْبَلِ الْقَرِيبِ، بِفَضْلِ الْعَمَلِ الْجَمَاعِيِّ الْمُتَوَاصِلِ وَالتَّفْكِيرِ الْإِبْدَاعِيِّ الْجَدِيدِ.

---
**English Translation:**

**Article: The New Government and Its Challenges**

It is known that the Arab state is witnessing a significant political change at this time. A new government has been formed after very long and difficult negotiations in the parliament, and it wants to undertake great work for all citizens, especially since many citizens desire positive change. There is no doubt that this time is very important for determining the future of the country, especially since the national economy requires deep study and new designs for financial solutions.

The new government believes that continuous and targeted investment is very important for improving the overall economic situation. And accordingly, the Financial Minister, Fatima, will announce a new and comprehensive financial plan to make the stock market stronger and more attractive to direct foreign investments. Moreover, the parliament desires to study the importance of free trade and advanced industry in rural areas, especially since the current money is not enough for all upcoming national projects. The government wants the state to become a leading center for developed technological industries.

On the other hand, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, led by the knowing Ambassador Muhammad, continues the serious search for global peace and the resolution of regional conflicts. Despite the fact that the old conflict created a great humanitarian crisis in some neighboring regions, the strong representative, Ali, wants to undertake new political negotiations to solve the increasing crises and stop the war. And at the same time, the Ambassador is working to improve diplomatic relations with friendly states, especially since some relations have become very weak because of the continuous terrorist threats facing national security.

In light of that, the knowing Judge, Omar, believes that the protection of human rights and the full and swift application of justice is very important for achieving comprehensive security and peace in the country. Unfortunately, crimes are noticeably increasing in big cities. And accordingly, the police and the court face difficult legal and social challenges in solving these problems. But everyone hopes that the state will become stronger, more just, and more advanced in the near future, thanks to continuous collective work and new creative thinking.
