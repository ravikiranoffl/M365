# Day 60 - Subjunctive and jussive moods

## Lesson

You already know that when a present tense verb is just by itself, or just saying what someone *does*, it usually ends with a **Damma** (ُ). This is its normal "happy" or "telling" mood, called the **Indicative Mood**.

For example, `يَذْهَبُ` (yadhhabu) - "he goes." See the Damma at the end?

But sometimes, special words come before the verb and change its ending. Today, we're learning two new "moods" or "hats" for our verbs: the Subjunctive and the Jussive moods!

### 1. The Subjunctive Mood (الْمَنْصُوبُ - al-manṣūbu) - The "Open" Mood

This mood is like the verb is saying "I *want* to do this," or "I *plan* to do this," or "I *will not* do this in the future." It's about desires, intentions, or things that might happen.

**How it looks:**
*   For most verbs that usually end with a **Damma** (ُ), the Damma changes to a **Fatha** (َ). It's like the verb opens its mouth wide!

**Special Words (Particles) that make a verb Subjunctive:**

1.  **`أَنْ` (an) - "that" or "to"**
    *   You've seen this before! When `أَنْ` comes before a present tense verb, it makes the verb take a Fatha.
    *   *Example:* `أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَذْهَبَ.` (Ureedu an adhhaba.) - I want *to go*. (`أَذْهَبُ` becomes `أَذْهَبَ`).

2.  **`لِـ` (li-) - "in order to"**
    *   You've seen this too! When `لِـ` comes before a present tense verb to show *purpose*, it makes the verb take a Fatha.
    *   *Example:* `أَذْهَبُ لِأَكْتُبَ.` (Adhhaba li'aktuba.) - I go *in order to write*. (`أَكْتُبُ` becomes `أَكْتُبَ`).

3.  **`لَنْ` (lan) - NEW! "will not" or "never"**
    *   This is a new word for saying something *will not* happen in the future. It's like `لَا` but for the future, and it makes the verb Subjunctive.
    *   *Example:* `لَنْ أَذْهَبَ.` (Lan adhhaba.) - I *will not go*.

**What about verbs that end with a `ن` (nūn)?**
*   Some verbs for "you (feminine singular)" or "they (masculine plural)" end with a `ن` (nūn), like `تَذْهَبِينَ` (you-fs go) or `يَكْتُبُونَ` (they write).
*   In the Subjunctive mood, this `ن` (nūn) **disappears**! It's like the Fatha is so strong, it pushes the `ن` away.
    *   *Example:* `أُرِيدُ أَنْ تَذْهَبِي.` (Ureedu an tadhhabee.) - I want *you (f.s.) to go*. (`تَذْهَبِينَ` becomes `تَذْهَبِي`).
    *   *Example:* `لَنْ يَكْتُبُوا.` (Lan yaktuboo.) - They *will not write*. (`يَكْتُبُونَ` becomes `يَكْتُبُوا`).

**What about verbs that end with a weak letter like `ي` (Yā')?**
*   Some verbs, like `يَأْتِي` (he comes), end in a `ي`.
*   In the Subjunctive, the `ي` stays, but it gets a Fatha!
    *   *Example:* `أُرِيدُ أَنْ يَأْتِيَ.` (Ureedu an ya'tiya.) - I want *him to come*. (`يَأْتِي` becomes `يَأْتِيَ`).

### 2. The Jussive Mood (الْمَجْزُومُ - al-majzūmu) - The "Cut Off" Mood

This mood is like the verb is saying "this *did not* happen" in the past, or giving a *command* to "do not do this!" It's about negating past actions or giving strong prohibitions.

**How it looks:**
*   For most verbs that usually end with a **Damma** (ُ), the Damma changes to a **Sukoon** (ْ). It's like the ending sound is suddenly "cut off" or stopped.

**Special Words (Particles) that make a verb Jussive:**

1.  **`لَمْ` (lam) - NEW! "did not" or "has not"**
    *   This is a very important new word for negating actions in the past, but it uses a *present tense* verb!
    *   *Example:* `لَمْ يَذْهَبْ.` (Lam yadhhab.) - He *did not go*. (`يَذْهَبُ` becomes `يَذْهَبْ`).

2.  **`لَا` (lā) - NEW! "do not" (for prohibition)**
    *   You know `لَا` for "he does not write" (`لَا يَكْتُبُ`). But this `لَا` is different! It's used to tell someone *not* to do something. It's a command to stop!
    *   *Example:* `لَا تَذْهَبْ!` (Lā tadhhab!) - *Do not go!* (to a masculine singular 'you'). (`تَذْهَبُ` becomes `تَذْهَبْ`).

**What about verbs that end with a `ن` (nūn)?**
*   Just like in the Subjunctive mood, the `ن` (nūn) at the end of verbs like `تَذْهَبِينَ` or `يَكْتُبُونَ` **disappears** in the Jussive mood!
    *   *Example:* `لَمْ تَذْهَبِي.` (Lam tadhhabee.) - You (f.s.) *did not go*. (`تَذْهَبِينَ` becomes `تَذْهَبِي`).
    *   *Example:* `لَا تَكْتُبُوا!` (Lā taktūboo!) - *Do not write!* (to a masculine plural 'you'). (`تَكْتُبُونَ` becomes `تَكْتُبُوا`).

**What about verbs that end with a weak letter like `و` or `ي` (Weak verbs)?**
*   For verbs like `يَأْتِي` (he comes), the final weak letter (`ي` or `و`) **disappears completely** in the Jussive mood! It's like the Sukoon "cuts off" the weak letter.
    *   *Example:* `لَمْ يَأْتِ.` (Lam ya'ti.) - He *did not come*. (`يَأْتِي` becomes `يَأْتِ`).

This is a lot to remember, but don't worry! We will practice it many times. The most important thing is to see how the ENDING of the verb changes.

## Examples

Let's see our verbs change their "hats" and "shoes"!

Here is the verb `يَذْهَبُ` (yadhhabu) - "he goes" in different moods:

*   **Indicative (Normal Damma):**
    *   `هُوَ يَذْهَبُ.` (Huwa yadhhabu.) - He goes.

*   **Subjunctive (Fatha or Nūn-dropping):**
    *   With `أَنْ`: `أُرِيدُ أَنْ يَذْهَبَ.` (Ureedu an yadhhaba.) - I want *him to go*.
    *   With `لِـ`: `هُوَ يَذْهَبُ لِيَشْرَبَ.` (Huwa yadhhabu liyashraba.) - He goes *to drink*.
    *   With `لَنْ`: `هُوَ لَنْ يَذْهَبَ.` (Huwa lan yadhhaba.) - He *will not go*.
    *   (For feminine singular `تَذْهَبِينَ`): `لَنْ تَذْهَبِي.` (Lan tadhhabee.) - You (f.s.) *will not go*.
    *   (For masculine plural `يَذْهَبُونَ`): `لَنْ يَذْهَبُوا.` (Lan yadhhaboo.) - They *will not go*.

*   **Jussive (Sukoon or Nūn-dropping or weak letter dropping):**
    *   With `لَمْ`: `لَمْ يَذْهَبْ.` (Lam yadhhab.) - He *did not go*.
    *   With `لَا` (prohibition for `أَنْتَ`): `لَا تَذْهَبْ!` (Lā tadhhab!) - *Do not go!*
    *   (For feminine singular `تَذْهَبِينَ`): `لَمْ تَذْهَبِي.` (Lam tadhhabee.) - You (f.s.) *did not go*.
    *   (For masculine plural `يَذْهَبُونَ`): `لَمْ يَذْهَبُوا.` (Lam yadhhaboo.) - They *did not go*.

Here is the verb `يَأْتِي` (ya'tī) - "he comes" (a weak verb) in different moods:

*   **Indicative (Normal ending with `ي`):**
    *   `هُوَ يَأْتِي.` (Huwa ya'tī.) - He comes.

*   **Subjunctive (Fatha on `ي`):**
    *   `أُرِيدُ أَنْ يَأْتِيَ.` (Ureedu an ya'tiya.) - I want *him to come*.
    *   `لَنْ يَأْتِيَ.` (Lan ya'tiya.) - He *will not come*.

*   **Jussive (`ي` drops):**
    *   `لَمْ يَأْتِ.` (Lam ya'ti.) - He *did not come*.
    *   `لَا تَأْتِ!` (Lā ta'ti!) - *Do not come!* (to a masculine singular 'you').

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   لَنْ (lan) - will not, never (Subjunctive particle)
*   لَمْ (lam) - did not (Jussive particle for past negation)
*   لَا (lā) - do not (Jussive particle for prohibition)
