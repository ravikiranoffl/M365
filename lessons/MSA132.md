# Day 132 - Fluency: Argumentation structures

## Lesson

"Fluency: Argumentation structures" today is all about expanding and refining your ability to construct sophisticated arguments in Arabic. You already know the core argumentative "moves" from Day 125: stating claims, adding support, presenting counter-arguments, drawing conclusions, and expressing sentiment. Today, we'll introduce some new, powerful phrases to enhance these moves, allowing for even greater nuance and impact in your arguments.

Think of these as advanced gears in your argumentative car, helping you navigate complex discussions with precision and authority.

We'll revisit the same five categories of argumentative moves and introduce new ways to express them:

### 1. Stating a Claim or Main Point (Revisiting & Enhancing)

You know phrases like `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (It is certain that...) and `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (It is clear that...). Let's add a phrase for a claim that is hard to dispute:

*   **New Argumentative Phrase:** `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ` (Lā yunkaru anna) - 'lā yun-ka-ru an-na'
    *   *Meaning:* It cannot be denied that... / Undoubtedly...
    *   *Explanation:* This phrase uses the passive form of `يُنْكِرُ` (he denies, Form IV), making it a strong way to state a universally accepted or undeniable fact.

### 2. Adding Support or Elaboration (Revisiting & Enhancing)

You use `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover...) to add information. To specifically refer to factual proof, you'll need the word for "evidence":

*   **New Word (Singular):** `دَلِيلٌ` (daleelun) - 'da-lee-lun'
    *   *Meaning:* Evidence, proof (masculine noun).
*   **New Word (Plural):** `أَدِلَّةٌ` (adillatun) - 'a-dil-la-tun'
    *   *Meaning:* Evidences, proofs (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote).
    *   *Example Phrase:* `وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ...` (Wa-ad-daleelu 'alā dhālika anna...) - And the evidence for that is that...
        *   *Explanation:* This allows you to explicitly introduce concrete support for your claim. Note that `أَدِلَّةٌ` is a diptote, so it behaves like `تَقَالِيدُ` (Day 122) and `مَوَارِدُ` (Day 124) when it comes to case endings.

### 3. Presenting a Counter-Argument or Contrasting View (Review)

These phrases help you acknowledge different perspectives or conflicting facts.

*   `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (Min jihatin ukhrā) - On the other hand... (Day 108)
*   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Bi-r-raghmi min anna) - Despite the fact that... (Day 107)
*   `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (Wa-ma'a anna) - Although... (Day 108 practice)
*   `وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (Wa-fī al-waqti nafsihi) - At the same time (can imply contrast or parallel action) (Day 108)

### 4. Drawing a Conclusion or Consequence (Revisiting & Enhancing)

You've used `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (And accordingly...) and `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (In light of that...). For a simpler, more direct conclusion, you can use:

*   **New Argumentative Word:** `لِذَلِكَ` (li-dhālika) - 'li-dhā-li-ka'
    *   *Meaning:* Therefore, for that reason, so...
    *   *Explanation:* This is a concise and frequently used word to introduce a direct consequence or conclusion.

You can also make a recommendation or state a necessity, using `يَنْبَغِي أَنْ` (it is necessary that / should) from Day 125, which we'll practice more today.

### 5. Expressing Sentiment or Evaluation (Review)

These convey your feelings or judgment about a situation within your argument.

*   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi) - Unfortunately... (Day 99)
*   `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (Mina al-ghareebi anna) - It is strange that... (Day 99)

**Mastering Argumentation Flow:**

The art of argumentation is not just knowing these phrases but combining them skillfully to build a cohesive and persuasive narrative. Remember to use all your specialized vocabulary (cultural, scientific, environmental, etc.) and maintain impeccable grammatical accuracy (case endings, agreements, Idafa, diptotes, `أَنَّ`/`أَنْ`).

## Examples

Let's look at some examples that seamlessly integrate these new and old argumentation structures with the vocabulary you've learned. Try to identify the function of each argumentative move and the vocabulary categories employed!

1.  **"It cannot be denied that climate change is a global challenge. And the evidence for that is that scientific research indicates a big increase in air pollution. Therefore, environmental protection needs more international cooperation."**
    *   `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ تَحَدٍّ عَالَمِيٌّ. وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ تُشِيرُ إِلَى زِيَادَةٍ كَبِيرَةٍ فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ. لِذَلِكَ، تَحْتَاجُ حِمَايَةُ الْبِيئَةِ إِلَى مَزِيدٍ مِنَ التَّعَاوُنِ الدَّوْلِيِّ.`
    *   *Translation:* (Lā yunkaru anna taghayyura al-munākhi taḥaddin 'ālamiyyun. Wa-ad-daleelu 'alā dhālika anna al-abḥātha al-'ilmiyyata tusheeru ilā ziyādatin kabeeratin fī talawwuthi al-hawā'i. Li-dhālika, taḥtāju ḥimāyatu al-bee'ati ilā mazeedin mina at-ta'āwuni ad-dawliyyi.)
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Starts with a strong, undeniable claim (`لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ`), provides direct evidence (`وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ`), and then draws a clear, concise conclusion/recommendation (`لِذَلِكَ`). Integrates environmental and scientific vocabulary.

2.  **"Many believe that cultural diversity is a source of strength for society. However, despite the fact that the government supports festivals, foreign influences are increasing noticeably. Therefore, it is necessary to protect national traditions."**
    *   `يَعْتَقِدُ الْكَثِيرُ أَنَّ التَّنَوُّعَ الثَّقَافِيَّ مَصْدَرُ قُوَّةٍ لِلْمُجْتَمَعِ. مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ تَدْعَمُ الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ، تَزْدَادُ التَّأْثِيرَاتُ الْأَجْنَبِيَّةُ بِشَكْلٍ مَلْحُوظٍ. لِذَلِكَ، يَنْبَغِي أَنْ نَحْمِيَ التَّقَالِيدَ الْوَطَنِيَّةَ.`
    *   *Translation:* (Ya'taqidu al-katheeru anna at-tanawwu'a ath-thaqāfiyya maṣdaru quwwatin li-l-mujtama'i. Min jihatin ukhrā, bi-r-raghmi min anna al-ḥukūmata tad'amu al-muhrajānāti, tazdādu at-ta'theerātu al-ajnabiyyatu bi-shaklin malḥūẓin. Li-dhālika, yanbaghī an naḥmiya at-taqāleeda al-waṭaniyyata.)
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Begins with a widely held belief (`يَعْتَقِدُ الْكَثِيرُ أَنَّ`), introduces a counter-point with a concession (`مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ`), and concludes with a strong recommendation using `لِذَلِكَ` and `يَنْبَغِي أَنْ`. Integrates cultural, government, and social vocabulary. (`مَصْدَرُ قُوَّةٍ` - source of strength, `يَدْعَمُ` - supports).

3.  **"Unfortunately, the report shows a big decrease in funding for scientific research. And the evidence for that is that the number of new experiments is not enough. In light of that, it cannot be denied that this will affect future development negatively."**
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، يُظْهِرُ التَّقْرِيرُ نَقْصًا كَبِيرًا فِي تَمْوِيلِ الْأَبْحَاثِ الْعِلْمِيَّةِ. وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ عَدَدَ التَّجَارِبِ الْجَدِيدَةِ لَيْسَ كَافِيًا. فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ هَذَا سَيُؤَثِّرُ فِي التَّطَوُّرِ الْمُسْتَقْبَلِيِّ بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ.`
    *   *Translation:* (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, yuẓhiru at-taqrīru naqṣan kabeeran fī tamweeli al-abḥāthi al-'ilmiyyati. Wa-ad-daleelu 'alā dhālika anna 'adada at-tajāribi al-jadeedati laysa kāfiyan. Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, lā yunkaru anna hādhā sayu'aththiru fī at-taṭawwuri al-mustaqbaliyyi bi-shaklin salbiyyin.)
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Starts with a negative sentiment (`لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ`), provides specific evidence (`وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ`), and draws a firm, undeniable conclusion about future consequences (`فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ`). Integrates scientific, economic, and data vocabulary. (`عَدَدَ` - number, `مُسْتَقْبَلِيٌّ` - future, `بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ` - negatively).

These examples demonstrate how to create nuanced and impactful arguments by strategically deploying your enhanced argumentative structures.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ (lā yunkaru anna) - It cannot be denied that... (phrase)
*   دَلِيلٌ (daleelun) - evidence, proof (masculine noun)
*   أَدِلَّةٌ (adillatun) - evidences, proofs (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote)
*   لِذَلِكَ (li-dhālika) - therefore, for that reason, so... (conjunction/adverb)
*   جُهُودٌ (juhoodun) - efforts (masculine plural noun)
*   نُوَازِنَ (nuwāzina) - we balance (present tense verb, subjunctive)
*   مَصْدَرُ قُوَّةٍ (maṣdaru quwwatin) - source of strength (Idafa)
*   يَدْعَمُ (yad'amu) - he supports (present tense verb)
*   تَعَاوُنٌ دَوْلِيٌّ (ta'āwunun dawliyyun) - international cooperation (masculine noun phrase)
*   عَدَدٌ (ʿadadun) - number (masculine noun)
*   مُسْتَقْبَلِيٌّ (mustaqbaliyyun) - future (masculine adjective)
*   تَحْتَاجُ إِلَى (taḥtāju ilā) - she needs (verb + preposition)
