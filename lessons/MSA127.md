# Day 127 - Fluency: Translation exercises

## Lesson

"Fluency: Translation exercises" is a critical step in consolidating your advanced Arabic skills. You've learned to build complex sentences, express nuanced meanings, and report on diverse topics. Now, the challenge is to seamlessly translate from English to Arabic, ensuring every grammatical rule, every case ending, and every word choice is precisely correct.

Think of yourself as a master architect, now tasked with building exact replicas of intricate designs using all the specialized materials and techniques you've acquired. This exercise will test your ability to:

1.  **Recall and apply diverse vocabulary:** Select the most appropriate words from all the specialized categories you've studied (e.g., government, economy, environment, culture, science, legal, interview language).
2.  **Master grammatical structures:** Accurately implement `أَنَّ` and `أَنْ` clauses, subjunctive verbs, light verb constructions, verb + preposition collocations, Idafa constructions, and correctly handle plural forms (human/non-human, sound/broken, diptote).
3.  **Utilize connectors and stance markers:** Strategically place advanced connectors (`عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ`, `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ`, `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ`, etc.) and stance markers (`مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ`, `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ`, `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ`, etc.) to ensure the translated text flows logically and conveys the intended tone and emphasis.
4.  **Demonstrate morphological understanding:** Use the correct forms of verbs, Masdars, active, and passive participles derived from roots.
5.  **Apply editing skills:** Pay meticulous attention to details like noun case endings (Nominative, Accusative, Genitive), adjective agreement (especially with non-human plurals), and verb-subject agreement.

Each sentence will be a mini-puzzle, requiring you to carefully consider how to best express the English meaning using your advanced Arabic toolkit. Remember to take your time, think through the grammar, and write your translations with full Harakat.

## Examples

Let's work through a few examples together. Read the English sentence, try to translate it in your mind (or aloud!), and then compare your version with my Arabic translation and the breakdown.

1.  **English Sentence:** "It is certain that the government wants to promote cultural festivals. Moreover, the scientific data indicates a big interest in old traditions."
    *   **Arabic Translation:** `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ الْحُكُومَةَ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تُعَزِّزَ الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُشِيرُ الْبَيَانَاتُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ إِلَى اهْتِمَامٍ كَبِيرٍ بِالتَّقَالِيدِ الْقَدِيمَةِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (It is certain that - Stance marker, Day 99).
        *   `الْحُكُومَةَ` (the government - Accusative, subject of `أَنَّ`, Day 101).
        *   `تُرِيدُ أَنْ تُعَزِّزَ` (wants to promote - `تُرِيدُ` (Day 79); `أَنْ تُعَزِّزَ` (to promote, new verb for context, subjunctive, Day 60; related to `تَعْزِيزٌ` Masdar from Day 126 vocab).
        *   `الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ الثَّقَافِيَّةَ` (cultural festivals - Accusative direct object, `الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ` (Day 122), `الثَّقَافِيَّةَ` (Day 122) feminine plural adjective).
        *   `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `تُشِيرُ الْبَيَانَاتُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ إِلَى` (the scientific data indicates - `تُشِيرُ إِلَى` (Day 123) feminine singular verb for non-human plural `الْبَيَانَاتُ` (Day 115); `الْعِلْمِيَّةُ` (Day 123) feminine singular adjective).
        *   `اهْتِمَامٍ كَبِيرٍ بِالتَّقَالِيدِ الْقَدِيمَةِ` (a big interest in old traditions - `اهْتِمَامٍ` (Day 122) Genitive after `إِلَى`; `كَبِيرٍ` (Day 54) Genitive adjective; `بِالتَّقَالِيدِ الْقَدِيمَةِ` (in old traditions) `بِـ` preposition, `التَّقَالِيدِ` (Day 122) Genitive (diptote with `الـ` takes Kasra), `الْقَدِيمَةِ` (Day 54) feminine singular adjective for non-human plural).

2.  **English Sentence:** "Despite the fact that the environment faces difficult challenges, the experts believe that the reduction of air pollution is possible. And accordingly, new laws are needed."
    *   **Arabic Translation:** `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ الْبِيئَةَ تُوَاجِهُ تَحَدِّيَاتٍ صَعْبَةً، يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ أَنَّ تَقْلِيلَ تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ مُمْكِنٌ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُطْلَبُ قَوَانِينُ جَدِيدَةٌ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `الْبِيئَةَ تُوَاجِهُ تَحَدِّيَاتٍ صَعْبَةً` (the environment faces difficult challenges - `الْبِيئَةَ` (Day 124) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `تُوَاجِهُ` (she faces, Day 113) verb; `تَحَدِّيَاتٍ` (challenges, Day 105) Accusative direct object, sound feminine plural; `صَعْبَةً` (Day 27) Accusative feminine singular adjective for non-human plural).
        *   `يَعْتَقِدُ الْخُبَرَاءُ أَنَّ` (the experts believe that - `يَعْتَقِدُ` (Day 79); `الْخُبَرَاءُ` (Day 123) Nominative subject).
        *   `تَقْلِيلَ تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ مُمْكِنٌ` (the reduction of air pollution is possible - `تَقْلِيلَ` (Day 124) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`, first term of Idafa; `تَلَوُّثِ الْهَوَاءِ` (Day 124) Idafa, `الْهَوَاءِ` Genitive; `مُمْكِنٌ` (possible, Day 120) Nominative predicate).
        *   `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (And accordingly - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `تُطْلَبُ قَوَانِينُ جَدِيدَةٌ` (new laws are needed/requested - `تُطْلَبُ` (is requested, passive voice of `يَطْلُبُ` (Day 116), feminine singular verb); `قَوَانِينُ` (laws, new word plural for `قَانُونٌ` (Day 102), broken plural diptote, Nominative subject of passive verb); `جَدِيدَةٌ` (Day 54) feminine singular adjective for non-human plural).

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   تُعَزِّزَ (tu'azziza) - she promotes (present tense verb, subjunctive)
*   الْمُهْرَجَانَاتِ (al-muhrajānāti) - the festivals (feminine plural noun, accusative/genitive)
*   الثَّقَافِيَّةَ (ath-thaqāfiyyata) - the cultural (feminine adjective, accusative)
*   الْقَدِيمَةِ (al-qadeemati) - the old (feminine adjective, genitive)
*   تُطْلَبُ (tuṭlabu) - she is requested/needed (present tense verb, passive)
*   قَوَانِينُ (qawāneenu) - laws (masculine plural noun, diptote, nominative)
*   جَدِيدَةٌ (jadeedatun) - new (feminine adjective, nominative)
*   أَنْشِطَةٌ (anṣhiṭatun) - activities (feminine plural noun, broken plural)
*   إِجْرَامِيَّةٌ (ijrāmiyyatun) - criminal (feminine adjective)
