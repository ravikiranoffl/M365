# Day 32 - Conditional Sentences

## Lesson

1.  **What is a Conditional Sentence?**
    *   A conditional sentence tells us that *if* something happens (the **condition**), *then* something else will happen or is true (the **result**).
    *   In English, we say things like: "If it rains, then I will stay home." or "If you study, you will succeed."

2.  **The "If" Word: `إِذَا` (idhā)**
    *   In Arabic, one of the most common words for "if" (especially for conditions that are likely or certain to happen) is **`إِذَا` (idhā)**.
    *   You place `إِذَا` at the beginning of the part of the sentence that states the condition.
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'i-dhaa', with a long 'aa' sound.

3.  **The Verb After `إِذَا` (The Condition)**
    *   This is a special rule in Arabic! When you use `إِذَا` to talk about a condition (even if you mean it in the present or future, like "If he *studies*"), the verb that immediately follows `إِذَا` **must be in the past tense.**
    *   So, even for "If he studies," you will use `دَرَسَ` (he studied).

4.  **The "Then" Word: `فَـ` (fa-)**
    *   After you state the condition, you connect it to the result using a small conjunction: **`فَـ` (fa-)**.
    *   `فَـ` means "so" or "then" and usually attaches directly to the next word (which is often the verb or a pronoun).
    *   **Pronunciation hint:** 'fa-', like the 'fa' in 'father'.

5.  **The Verb in the Result Clause (The "Then" Part)**
    *   The verb in the result clause (the "then" part) will usually be in the **present tense** or the **future tense**. It describes what will happen or what is happening as a result of the condition.

Let's break it down with a simple structure:
**`إِذَا` + [PAST TENSE VERB (for condition)] + `فَـ` + [PRESENT or FUTURE TENSE VERB (for result)]**

This might feel like a lot of rules, but let's see them in action with some examples. The key is remembering `إِذَا` for "if" and that the first verb is past tense, and `فَـ` for "then".

## Examples

Let's build some conditional sentences using the verbs and pronouns you've learned!

1.  **If he studied, then he writes.** (Meaning: If he studies, then he writes.)
    *   `إِذَا دَرَسَ، فَهُوَ يَكْتُبُ.` (Idhā darasa, fahuwa yaktubu.)
        *   *Notice*: `إِذَا` (if). `دَرَسَ` (he studied) is past tense for the condition. `فَـ` (then). `يَكْتُبُ` (he writes) is present tense for the result. `هُوَ` is included for clarity here.

2.  **If she wrote, then she will study.** (Meaning: If she writes, then she will study.)
    *   `إِذَا كَتَبَتْ، فَهِيَ سَتَدْرُسُ.` (Idhā katabat, fahiya satadrusu.)
        *   *Notice*: `كَتَبَتْ` (she wrote) is past tense. `سَتَدْرُسُ` (she will study) is future tense (using `سَـ`).

3.  **If I go to the school, then I study there.** (Meaning: If I go to school, then I study there.)
    *   `إِذَا ذَهَبْتُ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ، فَأَنَا أَدْرُسُ هُنَاكَ.` (Idhā dhahabtu ilā al-madrasati, fa'anā adrusu hunāka.)
        *   *Notice*: `ذَهَبْتُ` (I went) is past tense. `أَدْرُسُ` (I study) is present tense. `هُنَاكَ` (there) is an adverb of place.

4.  **If we eat the food, then we will drink the tea.** (Meaning: If we eat the food, then we will drink the tea.)
    *   `إِذَا أَكَلْنَا الطَّعَامَ، فَنَحْنُ سَنَشْرَبُ الشَّايَ.` (Idhā akalnā aṭ-ṭa'āma, fanaḥnu sanashrabu ash-shāya.)
        *   *Notice*: `أَكَلْنَا` (we ate) is past tense. `سَنَشْرَبُ` (we will drink) is future tense.

5.  **If you (masculine) return from the university, then you will write your name.** (Meaning: If you return from the university, then you will write your name.)
    *   `إِذَا رَجَعْتَ مِنَ الْجَامِعَةِ، فَأَنْتَ سَتَكْتُبُ اِسْمَكَ.` (Idhā raja'ta mina al-jāmi'ati, fa'anta sataktubu ismaka.)
        *   *Notice*: `رَجَعْتَ` (you-m returned) is past tense. `سَتَكْتُبُ` (you-m will write) is future tense.

6.  **If she doesn't study, then she will not write.** (Meaning: If she doesn't study, then she won't write.)
    *   `إِذَا مَا دَرَسَتْ، فَهِيَ لَا تَكْتُبُ.` (Idhā mā darasat, fahiya lā taktubu.)
        *   *Notice*: `مَا دَرَسَتْ` (she did not study) is past tense negation for the condition. `لَا تَكْتُبُ` (she does not write) is present tense negation for the result.

You can now express conditional relationships in Arabic! This is a powerful way to expand your communication.

## Practice

Now, let's practice forming conditional sentences. I will give you an English "if... then..." sentence, and you will translate it into Arabic. Remember the rules for `إِذَا`, the past tense in the condition, `فَـ`, and the present/future in the result.

1.  If he writes, then he studies. (`كَتَبَ`, `يَدْرُسُ`)
2.  If I go to the house, then I will eat the food. (`ذَهَبْتُ`, `سَآكُلُ`)
3.  If she drinks the water, then she returns. (`شَرِبَتْ`, `تَرْجِعُ`)
4.  If you (masculine) study the book, then you will go to the university. (`دَرَسْتَ`, `سَتَذْهَبُ`)
5.  If they (masculine) return from the school, then they will drink tea. (`رَجَعُوا`, `سَيَشْرَبُونَ`)
6.  If I don't eat, then I will drink. (`مَا أَكَلْتُ`, `سَأَشْرَبُ`)
7.  If the teacher (feminine) writes the story, then she studies it (the story). (For "studies it", use `تَدْرُسُهَا`. We haven't explicitly covered object pronouns attached to present tense, but `ـهَا` for "it" (f) is consistent with `اِسْمَهَا` from Day 21).
