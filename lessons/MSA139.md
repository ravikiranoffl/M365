# Day 139 - Fluency: Argumentation structures

## Lesson

"Fluency: Argumentation structures" today is all about expanding and refining your ability to construct sophisticated arguments in Arabic. You already know the core argumentative "moves" from Days 125 and 132: stating claims, adding support, presenting counter-arguments, drawing conclusions, and expressing sentiment. Today, we'll introduce a couple of new, powerful phrases to enhance these moves, allowing for even greater nuance and impact in your arguments.

Think of these as advanced gears in your argumentative car, helping you navigate complex discussions with precision and authority.

We'll revisit the same five categories of argumentative moves and introduce new ways to express them:

### 1. Stating a Claim or Main Point (Review)

You know phrases like `مِنَ الْمُؤَكَّدِ أَنَّ` (It is certain that...), `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (It is clear that...), and `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ` (It cannot be denied that...). These are all powerful ways to introduce your main point.

### 2. Adding Support or Elaboration (Enhancing)

You use `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover...) and `وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ` (And the evidence for that is that...) to add information or factual proof. To make your support more concrete and illustrative, you can now add examples:

*   **New Argumentative Phrase:** `عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ` (ʿalā sabeeli al-mithāl) - 'a-laa sa-bee-li al-mi-thaal'
    *   *Meaning:* For example, by way of example.
    *   *Explanation:* This phrase uses `سَبِيلٌ` (way, path) and `مِثَالٌ` (example, Day 99). It's incredibly useful for introducing specific instances or illustrations that support your general claim.

### 3. Presenting a Counter-Argument or Contrasting View (Enhancing)

You use `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (On the other hand...) and `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that...) to introduce contrasting ideas. To explicitly state the opposite, you can use:

*   **New Argumentative Word:** `وَبِالْعَكْسِ` (wa-bi-l-'aks) - 'wa-bi-l-`aks'
    *   *Meaning:* And on the contrary, conversely.
    *   *Explanation:* This word (from `عَكْسٌ` - opposite, new word for today) is a concise way to introduce a statement that is the reverse or direct opposite of what was just said.

### 4. Drawing a Conclusion or Consequence (Review)

You've used `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (And accordingly...), `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (In light of that...), and `لِذَلِكَ` (Therefore...). These are all excellent for summarizing your argument or stating what logically follows. You can also make a recommendation using `يَنْبَغِي أَنْ` (it is necessary that / should).

### 5. Expressing Sentiment or Evaluation (Review)

These convey your feelings or judgment about a situation within your argument: `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Unfortunately...) and `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (It is strange that...).

**Mastering Argumentation Flow:**

The art of argumentation is not just knowing these phrases but combining them skillfully to build a cohesive and persuasive narrative. Remember to use all your specialized vocabulary (cultural, scientific, environmental, etc.) and maintain impeccable grammatical accuracy (case endings, agreements, Idafa, diptotes, `أَنَّ`/`أَنْ`).

## Examples

Let's look at some examples that seamlessly integrate these new and old argumentation structures with the vocabulary you've learned. Try to identify the function of each argumentative move and the vocabulary categories employed!

1.  **"It cannot be denied that scientific research is crucial for future development. And the evidence for that is that new discoveries, for example in medicine, lead to many solutions. Therefore, governments should support this field."**
    *   `لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ الْأَبْحَاثَ الْعِلْمِيَّةَ مُهِمَّةٌ لِلتَّطَوُّرِ الْمُسْتَقْبَلِيِّ. وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ الِاكْتِشَافَاتِ الْجَدِيدَةَ، عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ فِي الطِّبِّ، تُؤَدِّي إِلَى حُلُولٍ كَثِيرَةٍ. لِذَلِكَ، يَنْبَغِي أَنْ تَدْعَمَ الْحُكُومَاتُ هَذَا الْمَجَالَ.`
    *   *Translation:* (Lā yunkaru anna al-abḥātha al-'ilmiyyata muhimmatun li-t-taṭawwuri al-mustaqbaliyyi. Wa-ad-daleelu 'alā dhālika anna al-iktishāfāti al-jadeedata, 'alā sabeeli al-mithāli fī aṭ-ṭibbi, tu'addī ilā ḥuloolin katheeratin. Li-dhālika, yanbaghī an tad'ama al-ḥukūmātu hādhā al-majāla.)
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Starts with an undeniable claim (`لَا يُنْكَرُ أَنَّ`), provides evidence (`وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ`), and *illustrates* it with a specific example (`عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ`), then concludes with a recommendation (`لِذَلِكَ، يَنْبَغِي أَنْ`). Integrates scientific, government, and economic vocabulary.

2.  **"Many believe that the reduction of pollution is a global priority. However, despite the fact that some countries are working on this, others, on the contrary, are increasing their industrial output. In light of that, collective international work is needed."**
    *   `يَعْتَقِدُ الْكَثِيرُ أَنَّ تَقْلِيلَ التَّلَوُّثِ أَوْلَوِيَّةٌ عَالَمِيَّةٌ. مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ بَعْضَ الدُّوَلِ تَعْمَلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ، فَإِنَّ دُوَلًا أُخْرَى، وَبِالْعَكْسِ، تَزِيدُ إِنْتَاجَهَا الصِّنَاعِيَّ. فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ، تَحْتَاجُ الْبِيئَةُ إِلَى عَمَلٍ جَمَاعِيٍّ دَوْلِيٍّ.`
    *   *Translation:* (Ya'taqidu al-katheeru anna taqleela at-talawwuthi awlawiyyatun 'ālamiyyatun. Min jihatin ukhrā, bi-r-raghmi min anna ba'ḍa ad-duwali ta'malu 'alā dhālika, fa-inna duwalan ukhrā, wa-bi-l-'aks, tazeedu intājahā aṣ-ṣinā'iyya. Fī ḍaw'i dhālika, taḥtāju al-bee'atu ilā 'amalin jamā'iyyin dawliyyin.)
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Begins with a widely held belief, introduces a counter-point with a concession (`مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ`), and then uses `وَبِالْعَكْسِ` to sharply contrast the actions of other entities before drawing a conclusion (`فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ`). Integrates environmental, government, and economic vocabulary. (`أَوْلَوِيَّةٌ` - priority, `فَإِنَّ` - indeed, for emphasis after `أَنَّ`, `الصِّنَاعِيَّ` - industrial, adjective of `صِنَاعَةٌ` (industry, Day 103)).

3.  **"Unfortunately, the lack of support for cultural events affects national identity. And the evidence for that is that old traditions, for example traditional arts, are decreasing among the young generation. Therefore, the Ministry of Culture should present new ambitious plans."**
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، يُؤَثِّرُ نَقْصُ دَعْمِ الْأَحْدَاثِ الثَّقَافِيَّةِ فِي الْهَوِيَّةِ الْوَطَنِيَّةِ. وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ التَّقَالِيدَ الْقَدِيمَةَ، عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ الْفُنُونَ التَّقْلِيدِيَّةَ، تَقِلُّ بَيْنَ الْجِيلِ الصَّغِيرِ. لِذَلِكَ، يَنْبَغِي عَلَى وِزَارَةِ الثَّقَافَةِ أَنْ تُقَدِّمَ خُطَطًا جَدِيدَةً طَمُوحَةً.`
    *   *Translation:* (Li-sū'i al-ḥaẓẓi, yu'aththiru naqṣu da'mi al-aḥdāthi ath-thaqāfiyyati fī al-hawiyyati al-waṭaniyyati. Wa-ad-daleelu 'alā dhālika anna at-taqāleeda al-qadeemata, 'alā sabeeli al-mithāli al-funūna at-taqleediyyata, taqillu bayna al-jeeli aṣ-ṣagheeri. Li-dhālika, yanbaghī 'alā wizaarati ath-thaqāfati an tuqaddima khuṭaṭan jadeedatan ṭamooḥatan.)
    *   *Argumentative Impact:* Starts with a negative sentiment (`لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ`), provides evidence (`وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَنَّ`) *with a concrete example* (`عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ`), and concludes with a strong recommendation (`لِذَلِكَ، يَنْبَغِي أَنْ`). Integrates cultural, government, and social vocabulary. (`بَيْنَ` - between, among, Day 106; `طَمُوحَةً` - ambitious, Day 133).

These examples demonstrate how to create nuanced and impactful arguments by strategically deploying your enhanced argumentative structures.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ (ʿalā sabeeli al-mithāl) - For example (phrase)
*   سَبِيلٌ (sabeelun) - way, path (masculine noun, in context of `عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْمِثَالِ`)
*   وَبِالْعَكْسِ (wa-bi-l-'aks) - And on the contrary, conversely (phrase)
*   عَكْسٌ ('aksun) - opposite (masculine noun, in context of `وَبِالْعَكْسِ`)
*   أَوْلَوِيَّةٌ (awlawiyyatun) - priority (feminine noun)
*   فَإِنَّ (fa-inna) - indeed, for emphasis (particle following `فَـ` + `أَنَّ`)
*   الصِّنَاعِيَّ (aṣ-ṣinā'iyya) - industrial (masculine adjective)
*   تُؤَدِّي إِلَى (tu'addī ilā) - she leads to (present tense verb + preposition)
*   يَدْعَمُ (yad'amu) - he supports (present tense verb)
*   مَجَالٌ (majālun) - field, domain (masculine noun)
*   بَيْنَ (bayna) - between, among (preposition)
*   أَوْلَوِيَّةٌ عَالَمِيَّةٌ (awlawiyyatun 'ālamiyyatun) - global priority (noun + adjective)
*   وَعْيٌ بِيئِيٌّ (wa'yun bee'iyyun) - environmental awareness (noun + adjective)
*   شَرْطٌ أَسَاسِيٌّ (sharṭun asāsiyyun) - essential condition (noun + adjective)
*   بَرَامِجُ تَعْلِيمِيَّةٌ (barāmiju ta'leemiyyatun) - educational programs (noun + adjective)
*   بِطَالَةٌ (biṭālatun) - unemployment (feminine noun)
*   اتِّصَالٌ عَالَمِيٌّ (ittiṣālun 'ālamiyyun) - global communication (noun + adjective)
*   كَيْفِيَّةٌ (kayfiyyatun) - how, manner (feminine noun)
*   يَلْعَبُ دَوْرًا (yal'abu dawran) - he plays a role (verb + Masdar, idiomatic)
*   يُحَسِّنُ (yuḥassinu) - he improves (present tense verb)
*   فَائِدَةٌ (fā'idatun) - usefulness, benefit (feminine noun)
