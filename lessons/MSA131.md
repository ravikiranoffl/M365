# Day 131 - Fluency: Environment reporting

## Lesson

"Fluency: Environment reporting" today is all about integrated application. You already possess a strong vocabulary for discussing the environment itself, pollution, climate, protection efforts, clean resources, and the Earth. The goal now is to use these terms within even more complex sentences, connecting environmental ideas to political, economic, scientific, or cultural contexts, and employing your full range of advanced connectors and stance markers.

Think of it as writing a sophisticated article or giving an oral report where you seamlessly blend environmental observations with analytical points, drawing connections between different aspects of a society.

To achieve this, remember to:

1.  **Recall `Environment Reporting` Vocabulary (Day 124):**
    *   `بِيئَةٌ` (environment)
    *   `تَلَوُّثٌ` (pollution)
    *   `مُنَاخٌ` (climate)
    *   `يَحْمِي` / `تَحْمِي` (he/she protects)
    *   `حِمَايَةٌ` (protection)
    *   `مُلَوَّثٌ` / `مُلَوَّثَةٌ` (polluted)
    *   `نَظِيفٌ` / `نَظِيفَةٌ` (clean)
    *   `مَوَارِدُ` (resources)
    *   `مَاءٌ` (water)
    *   `هَوَاءٌ` (air)
    *   `أَرْضٌ` (earth, land)
    *   `تَقْلِيلٌ` (reduction, decrease)

2.  **Integrate `Argumentation Structures` (Day 125):** Use phrases to state claims, add support, present counter-arguments, and draw conclusions.
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (It is clear that...)
    *   `يَعْتَقِدُ ... أَنَّ` (... believes that...)
    *   `لَا شَكَّ أَنَّ` (There is no doubt that...)
    *   `مِنَ الْمَعْرُوفِ أَنَّ` (It is known that...)
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Unfortunately...)
    *   `مِنَ الْغَرِيبِ أَنَّ` (It is strange that...)

3.  **Utilize `Advanced Connectors` (Days 107-108):** Ensure smooth transitions and logical relationships between ideas.
    *   `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover...)
    *   `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (On the other hand...)
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that...)
    *   `فِي ضَوْءِ ذَلِكَ` (In light of that...)
    *   `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (And accordingly...)
    *   `خَاصَّةً وَأَنَّ` (Especially since...)
    *   `وَفِي الْوَقْتِ نَفْسِهِ` (At the same time...)

4.  **Maintain `Grammatical Accuracy` (Days 118-119):** Pay meticulous attention to noun case endings, adjective agreement (especially with non-human plurals), verb-subject agreement, Idafa constructions, `أَنَّ` vs `أَنْ`, and diptote behavior.

This lesson will help you articulate nuanced and sophisticated insights about the environment, linking it to various aspects of society and demonstrating a high level of Arabic proficiency.

## Examples

Let's look at some examples that weave together environment reporting with other learned concepts. Try to identify the various structures and vocabulary in each sentence!

1.  **"It is clear that climate change affects the economy. Moreover, scientific research shows a big increase in water and air pollution."**
    *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ يُؤَثِّرُ فِي الِاقْتِصَادِ. عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، تُظْهِرُ الْأَبْحَاثُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْمَاءِ وَالْهَوَاءِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `مِنَ الْوَاضِحِ أَنَّ` (It is clear that - Stance marker, Day 125).
        *   `تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ يُؤَثِّرُ فِي الِاقْتِصَادِ` (climate change affects the economy - `تَغَيُّرَ الْمُنَاخِ` (change of climate, Day 113/124) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `يُؤَثِّرُ فِي` (affects, Day 122); `الِاقْتِصَادِ` (the economy, Day 103) Genitive after `فِي`).
        *   `عَلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (Moreover - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `تُظْهِرُ الْأَبْحَاثُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْمَاءِ وَالْهَوَاءِ` (scientific research shows a big increase in water and air pollution - `تُظْهِرُ الْأَبْحَاثُ الْعِلْمِيَّةُ` (shows the scientific research, Day 115/123) Nominative subject + verb; `زِيَادَةً كَبِيرَةً` (big increase, Day 115/54) Accusative object; `فِي تَلَوُّثِ الْمَاءِ وَالْهَوَاءِ` (in pollution of water and air, Day 124) Genitive after `فِي`, Idafa).

2.  **"Despite the fact that environmental challenges are big, the government wants to protect natural resources. And accordingly, it announced a new plan for sustainable development."** (Use `تَنْمِيَةٌ مُسْتَدَامَةٌ` - sustainable development, new for this context, feminine noun + adjective).
    *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ التَّحَدِّيَاتِ الْبِيئِيَّةَ كَبِيرَةٌ، تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحْمِيَ الْمَوَارِدَ الطَّبِيعِيَّةَ. وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ، أَعْلَنَتْ عَنْ خُطَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ لِلتَّنْمِيَةِ الْمُسْتَدَامَةِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `بِالرَّغْمِ مِنْ أَنَّ` (Despite the fact that - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `التَّحَدِّيَاتِ الْبِيئِيَّةَ كَبِيرَةٌ` (environmental challenges are big - `التَّحَدِّيَاتِ الْبِيئِيَّةَ` (challenges, environmental, Day 105/124) Accusative subject of `أَنَّ`; `كَبِيرَةٌ` (big, Day 54) Nominative predicate for non-human plural).
        *   `تُرِيدُ الْحُكُومَةُ أَنْ تَحْمِيَ الْمَوَارِدَ الطَّبِيعِيَّةَ` (the government wants to protect natural resources - `تُرِيدُ` (Day 79); `الْحُكُومَةُ` (Day 101) Nominative subject; `أَنْ تَحْمِيَ` (to protect, Day 124) Subjunctive; `الْمَوَارِدَ الطَّبِيعِيَّةَ` (natural resources, Day 124) Accusative object).
        *   `وَبِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ` (And accordingly - Advanced connector, Day 107).
        *   `أَعْلَنَتْ عَنْ خُطَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ لِلتَّنْمِيَةِ الْمُسْتَدَامَةِ` (it announced a new plan for sustainable development - `أَعْلَنَتْ عَنْ` (she announced about, Day 106); `خُطَّةٍ جَدِيدَةٍ` (new plan, Day 113/54) Genitive after `عَنْ`; `لِلتَّنْمِيَةِ الْمُسْتَدَامَةِ` (for sustainable development - `لِـ` preposition, new `تَنْمِيَةِ` (development) Genitive, new `الْمُسْتَدَامَةِ` (sustainable) Genitive adjective).

3.  **"Unfortunately, the lack of clean water affects health. On the other hand, experts are working to find scientific solutions for pollution reduction."**
    *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ، نَقْصُ الْمَاءِ النَّظِيفِ يُؤَثِّرُ فِي الصِّحَّةِ. مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى، يَعْمَلُ الْخُبَرَاءُ عَلَى إِيجَادِ حُلُولٍ عِلْمِيَّةٍ لِتَقْلِيلِ التَّلَوُّثِ.`
    *   **Breakdown:**
        *   `لِسُوءِ الْحَظِّ` (Unfortunately - Stance marker, Day 99).
        *   `نَقْصُ الْمَاءِ النَّظِيفِ يُؤَثِّرُ فِي الصِّحَّةِ` (lack of clean water affects health - `نَقْصُ الْمَاءِ النَّظِيفِ` (lack of clean water, Day 115/124) Idafa, Nominative subject; `يُؤَثِّرُ فِي` (affects, Day 122); `الصِّحَّةِ` (health, Day 126) Genitive after `فِي`).
        *   `مِنْ جِهَةٍ أُخْرَى` (On the other hand - Advanced connector, Day 108).
        *   `يَعْمَلُ الْخُبَرَاءُ عَلَى إِيجَادِ حُلُولٍ عِلْمِيَّةٍ لِتَقْلِيلِ التَّلَوُّثِ` (experts are working to find scientific solutions for pollution reduction - `يَعْمَلُ الْخُبَرَاءُ` (experts work, Day 116/123); `عَلَى إِيجَادِ` (on finding - `إِيجَادٌ` (finding, new for this context) Genitive Masdar after `عَلَى`); `حُلُولٍ عِلْمِيَّةٍ` (scientific solutions - `حُلُولٌ` (solutions, Day 105) Genitive, broken plural diptote takes Kasra Tanween here, but when indefinite it should take Fatha, so `حُلُولٍ` is actually correct if it's not a diptote, it's `حُلُولٌ` pattern `فُعُولٌ` not `فَعَائِلُ`. Let's assume it is a regular broken plural here for simplicity as it was not explicitly taught as a diptote; `عِلْمِيَّةٍ` (scientific, Day 123) Genitive adjective); `لِتَقْلِيلِ التَّلَوُّثِ` (for pollution reduction, Day 124) Idafa.

*   تَحْمِي (taḥmee) - she protects (present tense verb)
*   حِمَايَةٌ (ḥimāyatun) - protection (feminine Masdar/noun)
*   مُلَوَّثٌ (mulawwathun) - polluted (masculine adjective)
*   مُلَوَّثَةٌ (mulawwathatun) - polluted (feminine adjective)
*   نَظِيفٌ (naẓeefun) - clean (masculine adjective)
*   نَظِيفَةٌ (naẓeefatun) - clean (feminine adjective)
*   مَوَارِدُ (mawāridu) - resources (masculine plural, broken plural, diptote)
*   مَاءٌ (mā'un) - water (masculine noun)
*   هَوَاءٌ (hawā'un) - air (masculine noun)
*   أَرْضٌ (arḍun) - earth, land (feminine noun)
*   تَقْلِيلٌ (taqleelun) - reduction, decrease (masculine Masdar/noun)
*   تَنْمِيَةٌ مُسْتَدَامَةٌ (tanmiyatun mustadāmatun) - sustainable development (feminine noun phrase)
*   دُوَلٌ (duwalun) - countries (feminine plural, broken plural, diptote)
*   تَقِلُّ (taqillu) - she decreases (present tense verb)
*   عَمَلٌ جَمَاعِيٌّ دَوْلِيٌّ (ʿamalun jamāʿiyyun dawliyyun) - collective international work (masculine noun phrase)
*   كَوْكَبٌ (kawkabun) - planet (masculine noun)
*   بِشَكْلٍ سَلْبِيٍّ (bi-shaklin salbiyyin) - in a negative way (prepositional phrase)
