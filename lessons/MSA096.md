# Day 96 - Intensive listening to Arabic news (Transcription Day 6)

## Lesson

Today, your mission is to tackle a final set of complex, grammatically intertwined sentences that are typical of news reports. The goal remains the same: to sharpen your ear by "listening" (reading and comprehending) these news-style sentences and actively transcribing them. This intensive practice is key to developing your fluency and confidence in understanding Arabic.

**How to approach transcription:**

1.  **Read the Arabic text slowly, multiple times.** Imagine it being spoken naturally and clearly. Try to pronounce it aloud as accurately as you can, even if just softly.
2.  **Try to identify each word.** Break the sentence into smaller chunks if necessary. Look for known verbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and noun phrases.
3.  **Pay close attention to case endings (Damma, Fatha, Kasra) and Sukoon.** These subtle markings carry significant grammatical information that helps you understand who is doing what to whom, or how words relate in a sentence.
4.  **Write down what you hear (or read).** Focus on getting the Harakat (vowel markings) correct for each word.
5.  **Then, compare your transcription with the correct version and explanation.** Reflect on any differences and why they occurred. This self-correction is where the real learning happens!

Remember, this isn't a test for a perfect score, but an exercise to improve your recognition skills. The more you practice, the faster your brain will process these forms and the clearer Arabic will become to you.

## Examples

Here is a complex news-style sentence. Read it, imagine hearing it, and try to identify each word and its ending.

**Arabic Text:**

`وَمَعَ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ تُرِيدُ الرِّبْحَ، يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ الْفَاهِمَ مُحَمَّدًا لَنْ يُعْلِنَ الْمَشْرُوعَ الْمُشْتَرَكَ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ الْمَكْتُوبَةِ.`

*(Take your time to read and identify the words and their endings. When you are ready, continue below for the breakdown.)*

---

**Breakdown and Explanation:**

The sentence is: `وَمَعَ أَنَّ الشَّرِكَةَ تُرِيدُ الرِّبْحَ، يَبْدُو أَنَّ الْوَزِيرَ الْفَاهِمَ مُحَمَّدًا لَنْ يُعْلِنَ الْمَشْرُوعَ الْمُشْتَرَكَ بِسَبَبِ الْمُشْكِلَةِ الْمَكْتُوبَةِ.`

Let's break it down word by word, identifying each one and its grammatical features:

1.  **`وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (wa-ma'a anna):**
    *   **Word:** `وَمَعَ أَنَّ` (although / even though).
    *   **Type:** Contrast structure (Day 81).
    *   **Grammar:** `أَنَّ` makes the subject of the following clause take Fatha.
    *   **Meaning:** Although.

2.  **`الشَّرِكَةَ` (ash-sharikatā):**
    *   **Word:** `شَرِكَةٌ` (company - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the subject of `أَنَّ` (Day 64, Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** the company.

3.  **`تُرِيدُ` (tureedū):**
    *   **Word:** `تُرِيدُ` (she wants).
    *   **Type:** Present tense verb (Day 44).
    *   **Meaning:** wants.

4.  **`الرِّبْحَ` (ar-ribḥā):**
    *   **Word:** `رِبْحٌ` (profit - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the direct object of `تُرِيدُ` (Day 89, Core Vocabulary).
    *   **Meaning:** the profit.

5.  **`يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (yabdū anna):**
    *   **Word:** `يَبْدُو أَنَّ` (it seems that).
    *   **Type:** Hedging language (Day 80).
    *   **Grammar:** `أَنَّ` makes the subject of the following clause take Fatha.
    *   **Meaning:** it seems that.

6.  **`الْوَزِيرَ` (al-wazeerā):**
    *   **Word:** `وَزِيرٌ` (minister - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the subject of `أَنَّ` (Day 44, Day 39).
    *   **Meaning:** the minister.

7.  **`الْفَاهِمَ` (al-fāhimā):**
    *   **Word:** `فَاهِمٌ` (understanding - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`), active participle (Day 88) acting as an adjective agreeing with `الْوَزِيرَ`.
    *   **Meaning:** the understanding.

8.  **`مُحَمَّدًا` (Muḥammadān):**
    *   **Word:** `مُحَمَّدٌ` (Muhammad - masculine proper noun).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (proper noun).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـًا`) because it's in apposition to `الْوَزِيرَ` (Day 85).
    *   **Meaning:** Muhammad.

9.  **`لَنْ يُعْلِنَ` (lan yu'linā):**
    *   **Word:** `لَنْ يُعْلِنَ` (he will not announce).
    *   **Type:** Negation for future tense with subjunctive verb (Day 60).
    *   **Grammar:** `لَنْ` makes the present tense verb `يُعْلِنُ` (he announces) take Fatha (Subjunctive mood) (Day 64).
    *   **Meaning:** will not announce.

10. **`الْمَشْرُوعَ` (al-mashroo'ā):**
    *   **Word:** `مَشْرُوعٌ` (project - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`) as the direct object of `يُعْلِنَ` (Day 79).
    *   **Meaning:** the project.

11. **`الْمُشْتَرَكَ` (al-mushtarakā):**
    *   **Word:** `مُشْتَرَكٌ` (joint - m.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Accusative (Fatha - `ـَ`), participial adjective agreeing with `الْمَشْرُوعَ` (Day 67, Day 88).
    *   **Meaning:** joint.

12. **`بِسَبَبِ` (bi-sababi):**
    *   **Word:** `بِسَبَبِ` (because of).
    *   **Type:** Prepositional phrase / Cause/effect connector (Day 86).
    *   **Grammar:** Followed by a noun in the Genitive case.
    *   **Meaning:** because of.

13. **`الْمُشْكِلَةِ` (al-mushkilatī):**
    *   **Word:** `مُشْكِلَةٌ` (problem - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Genitive (Kasra - `ـِ`) after `بِسَبَبِ` (Day 50).
    *   **Meaning:** the problem.

14. **`الْمَكْتُوبَةِ` (al-maktūbatī):**
    *   **Word:** `مَكْتُوبَةٌ` (written - f.).
    *   **Definiteness:** Definite (`الـ`).
    *   **Case:** Genitive (Kasra - `ـِ`), passive participle (Day 88) acting as an adjective agreeing with `الْمُشْكِلَةِ`.
    *   **Meaning:** the written.

**Full Sentence Meaning:** Although the company wants the profit, it seems that the understanding Minister Muhammad will not announce the joint project because of the written problem.

    *   **Correct Transcription & Explanation:**
        *   `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā): Adverb (perhaps / maybe) (Day 80).
        *   `يُرِيدُ` (yureedū): `يُرِيدُ` (he wants), present tense verb (Day 44).
        *   `الطَّالِبُ` (aṭ-ṭālibū): `طَالِبٌ` (student), definite, Nominative (Damma) as the subject (Day 19).
        *   `الدَّارِسُ` (ad-dārisū): `دَارِسٌ` (studying), definite, Nominative (Damma), active participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الطَّالِبُ` (Day 88).
        *   `عُمَرُ` (ʿUmaru): `عُمَرُ` (Omar - proper noun), definite, Nominative (Damma), appositive to `الطَّالِبُ` (Day 85). (Note: `عُمَرُ` is a diptote).

        *   `يَعُودَ` (ya'oodā): `يَعُودُ` (he returns), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `أَنْ` (Day 78).
        *   `إِلَى` (ilā): Preposition (to / towards) (Day 10).
        *   `الْقَاهِرَةِ` (al-qāhiratī): `قَاهِرَةٌ` (Cairo), definite, Genitive (Kasra) after `إِلَى` (Day 87).
        *   `حَتَّى` (ḥattā): Conjunctive particle (in order to), makes verb Subjunctive (Day 86).
        *   `يَسْأَلَ` (yas'alā): `يَسْأَلُ` (he asks), present tense verb, Subjunctive (Fatha) because of `حَتَّى` (Day 79).

        *   `الْعَالِمَ` (al-ʿālimā): `عَالِمٌ` (knowing/scholar), definite, Accusative (Fatha), active participle acting as an adjective agreeing with `الْمُدَرِّسَ` (Day 88).
