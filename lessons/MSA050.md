# Day 50 - Diplomacy vocabulary

## Lesson

Today, we'll learn some key nouns and a new verb related to diplomacy. Pay close attention to the gender of each noun, as this will affect how adjectives describe them and how they're used in sentences!

1.  **`سَلَامٌ` (salāmun) - Peace**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'sa-laa-mun'.
    *   Example: `السَّلَامُ مُهِمٌّ.` (As-salāmu muhimun.) - Peace is important. (Using `مُهِمٌّ` - important, from Day 43).

2.  **`حَرْبٌ` (ḥarbun) - War**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ḥar-bun'.
    *   Example: `الْحَرْبُ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Al-ḥarbu kabeeratun.) - The war is big. (Using `كَبِيرَةٌ` - big, from Day 3).

3.  **`سَفِيرٌ` (safīrun) - Ambassador**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. An ambassador is a high-ranking official who represents their country in another country.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'sa-fee-run'.
    *   Example: `السَّفِيرُ ذَكِيٌّ.` (As-safīru dhakiyyun.) - The ambassador is smart. (Using `ذَكِيٌّ` - smart, from Day 37).

4.  **`سِفَارَةٌ` (sifāratun) - Embassy**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun. This is the building or office where an ambassador and their staff work.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'si-faa-ra-tun'.
    *   Example: `السِّفَارَةُ جَدِيدَةٌ.` (As-sifāratu jadeedatun.) - The embassy is new. (Using `جَدِيدَةٌ` - new, from Day 3).

5.  **`اجْتِمَاعٌ` (ijtimā'un) - Meeting, Conference**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. You'll often hear about diplomatic meetings or conferences.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'ij-ti-maa-'un'.
    *   Example: `الِاجْتِمَاعُ مُهِمٌّ.` (Al-ijtimā'u muhimun.) - The meeting is important.

6.  **`اتِّفَاقٌ` (ittifāqun) - Agreement**
    *   This is a **masculine** noun. Often, the goal of diplomacy is to reach an agreement.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'it-ti-faa-qun'.
    *   Example: `الِاتِّفَاقُ كَبِيرٌ.` (Al-ittifāqu kabeerun.) - The agreement is big.

7.  **`مُشْكِلَةٌ` (mushkilatun) - Problem**
    *   This is a **feminine** noun. Diplomacy often aims to solve problems between countries.
    *   *Pronunciation hint:* 'mush-ki-la-tun'.
    *   Example: `الْمُشْكِلَةُ صَغِيرَةٌ.` (Al-mushkilatū ṣagheeratun.) - The problem is small. (Using `صَغِيرَةٌ` - small, from Day 3).

8.  **New Verb: "To Solve / To Resolve"**
    *   This verb is very useful for talking about solving problems. It's a "geminated" verb, meaning it has a double letter in its root.
    *   **Root:** `ح ل ل` (ḥ-l-l)
    *   **Past Tense (he solved):** `حَلَّ` (ḥalla) - 'ḥal-la'.
    *   **Present Tense (he solves):** `يَحُلُّ` (yaḥullu) - 'ya-ḥul-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (I solve):** `أَحُلُّ` (aḥullu) - 'a-ḥul-lu'.
    *   **Present Tense (she solves / you-m.s. solve):** `تَحُلُّ` (taḥullu) - 'ta-ḥul-lu'.
    *   Example: `هُوَ يَحُلُّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Huwa yaḥullu al-mushkilata.) - He solves the problem. (`الْمُشْكِلَةَ` takes Fatha as it's the direct object).

You've just learned a powerful set of words to start discussing diplomatic topics! Remember to use them with their correct genders and case endings.

## Examples

Let's combine our new vocabulary with grammar and sentence structures you've learned from previous lessons.

1.  **Peace is important for the people.** (Nominal sentence + preposition)
    *   `السَّلَامُ مُهِمٌّ لِلشَّعْبِ.` (As-salāmu muhimun li-sh-sha'bi.)
        *   *Notice:* `السَّلَامُ` (peace) is masculine, so `مُهِمٌّ` (important) is masculine. `لِـ` (for) is a preposition (Day 10), so `الشَّعْبِ` (the people - Day 48) takes a Kasra.

2.  **The ambassador is in the embassy of the state.** (Idafa construct + prepositional phrase)
    *   `السَّفِيرُ فِي سِفَارَةِ الدَّوْلَةِ.` (As-safīru fī sifārati ad-dawlati.)
        *   *Notice:* `سِفَارَةِ الدَّوْلَةِ` (the embassy of the state). `سِفَارَةِ` (embassy of) takes Kasra after `فِي`, and `الدَّوْلَةِ` (the state - Day 48) takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa.

3.  **The minister wants to understand the problem.** (Nominalization with `أَنْ`)
    *   `الْوَزِيرُ يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَفْهَمَ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Al-wazīru yureedu an yafhama al-mushkilata.)
        *   *Notice:* `الْوَزِيرُ` (the minister - Day 48) `يُرِيدُ` (he wants - Day 44). `أَنْ يَفْهَمَ` (to understand - `يَفْهَمُ` from Day 48, takes Fatha due to `أَنْ`). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (the problem) takes Fatha as the object.

4.  **Indeed, the agreement is certainly new.** (Emphasis particles `إِنَّ` and `لَـ`)
    *   `إِنَّ الِاتِّفَاقَ لَجَدِيدٌ.` (Inna al-ittifāqa lajadeedun.)
        *   *Notice:* `الِاتِّفَاقَ` (the agreement) takes Fatha because of `إِنَّ` (Day 37). `لَجَدِيدٌ` (certainly new) has `لَـ` attached.

5.  **He said that the war is big.** (Reported speech + `أَنَّ`)
    *   `قَالَ أَنَّ الْحَرْبَ كَبِيرَةٌ.` (Qāla anna al-ḥarba kabeeratun.)
        *   *Notice:* `قَالَ` (he said - Day 39). `أَنَّ` makes `الْحَرْبَ` (the war) take Fatha. `كَبِيرَةٌ` (big) is the feminine comment matching `الْحَرْبَ`.

6.  **I go to the meeting to solve the problem.** (Purpose clause + new verb)
    *   `أَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الِاجْتِمَاعِ لِأَحُلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ.` (Ana adhhaba ilā al-ijtimā'i li'aḥulla al-mushkilata.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِأَحُلَّ` (in order for me to solve) where `أَحُلُّ` takes Fatha because of `لِـ` (Day 40). `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` takes Fatha as object.

7.  **It is probable that the president understands politics.** (Modal expression `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ`)
    *   `مِنَ الْمُحْتَمَلِ أَنْ يَفْهَمَ الرَّئِيسُ السِّيَاسَةَ.` (Mina al-muḥtamali an yafhama ar-ra'īsu as-siyāsata.)
        *   *Notice:* `يَفْهَمَ` takes Fatha because of `أَنْ`. `الرَّئِيسُ` (the president - Day 48) is the subject of `يَفْهَمَ`. `السِّيَاسَةَ` (politics - Day 48) is the object.

8.  **The embassy is for the new ambassador.** (Fronted comment)
    *   `لِلسَّفِيرِ الْجَدِيدِ السِّفَارَةُ.` (Li-s-safīri al-jadeedi as-sifāratu.)
        *   *Notice:* `لِلسَّفِيرِ الْجَدِيدِ` is the fronted comment (`لِـ` is a preposition, so `السَّفِيرِ` takes Kasra, and its adjective `الْجَدِيدِ` matches). `السِّفَارَةُ` is the delayed topic (Day 36).
