# Day 59 - Reading synthesis

## Lesson

Today, your main task is to **read a short passage in Arabic**. This is a chance to see how all the grammar rules and vocabulary words you've learned combine to form meaningful sentences.

**How to approach this reading practice:**

1.  **Read Slowly:** Don't rush! Take your time with each word.
2.  **Focus on Harakat:** Use the small vowel marks (Fatha, Kasra, Damma, Sukoon, Shaddah) to guide your pronunciation. These are critical for understanding the meaning and function of each word.
3.  **Identify Familiar Words and Patterns:** As you read, try to recognize words you know. Can you spot verbs, nouns, prepositions, or specific grammatical structures like Idafa, participles, or Masdars?
4.  **Try to Understand the General Meaning:** After reading a sentence, pause and try to understand what it means before looking at the translation.
5.  **Don't worry about perfection:** It's okay if you don't understand every single word at first glance. The goal is to practice and build your confidence!

I will present the text sentence by sentence. After each sentence, I will provide the English translation and highlight some of the grammatical points and vocabulary we've learned.

## Examples

**Passage: "Daily News and Actions"**

1.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `الطَّالِبُ النَّاجِحُ يَسْتَخْدِمُ الْحَاسُوبَ كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ لِقِرَاءَةِ الْأَخْبَارِ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The successful student uses the computer every morning to read the news.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `الطَّالِبُ النَّاجِحُ` (Aṭ-ṭālibu an-nājiḥu): "The successful student." `الطَّالِبُ` (student - Day 6) is the definite masculine singular noun, and `النَّاجِحُ` (successful - Day 58) is its definite masculine singular adjective, matching in definiteness, gender, and case (Damma).
        *   `يَسْتَخْدِمُ` (yastakhdimu): "He uses." This is a present tense verb (Day 55). Its subject is `الطَّالِبُ`.
        *   `الْحَاسُوبَ` (al-ḥāsūba): "The computer." This noun (Day 55) is the direct object of `يَسْتَخْدِمُ`, so it takes a Fatha.
        *   `كُلَّ صَبَاحٍ` (kulla ṣabāḥin): "Every morning." This is an adverbial phrase for time (Day 27).
        *   `لِقِرَاءَةِ الْأَخْبَارِ` (li-qirā'ati al-akhbāri): "For reading the news" or "to read the news." `لِـ` (Day 10) is a preposition meaning "for" or "in order to." `قِرَاءَةِ` (reading - Masdar from Day 45) is the first noun of an Idafa construct (Day 25), taking Kasra because of `لِـ`. `الْأَخْبَارِ` (the news - Day 45) is the second noun of the Idafa, taking Kasra.

2.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `تُشِيرُ الْأَخْبَارُ إِلَى مُشْكِلَةٍ مُهِمَّةٍ لِصِحَّةِ بِيئَةِ الدَّوْلَةِ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The news indicates an important problem for the health of the state's environment.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `تُشِيرُ` (tusheeru): "It indicates." This is a present tense verb (Day 54). `الْأَخْبَارُ` (the news - Day 45) is a non-human broken plural, so its verb takes the feminine singular form (`هِيَ`).
        *   `إِلَى` (ilā): "To" or "towards." This is a preposition (Day 10).
        *   `مُشْكِلَةٍ مُهِمَّةٍ` (mushkilatin muhimmatin): "An important problem." `مُشْكِلَةٍ` (problem - Day 50) is indefinite and takes Tanween Kasra because it follows `إِلَى`. `مُهِمَّةٍ` (important - feminine from Day 43) is its adjective, matching in indefiniteness, gender (feminine), and case (Tanween Kasra).
        *   `لِصِحَّةِ بِيئَةِ الدَّوْلَةِ` (li-ṣiḥḥati bī'ati ad-dawlati): "For the health of the environment of the state." This is a chained Idafa construct (Day 26) following a preposition `لِـ`. `لِصِحَّةِ` (for health of) where `صِحَّةِ` (health - Day 57) takes Kasra after `لِـ`. `بِيئَةِ` (environment of - Day 56) takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa chain. `الدَّوْلَةِ` (the state - Day 48) takes Kasra as the third noun in the Idafa chain.

3.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `رُبَّمَا يُرِيدُ الرَّئِيسُ أَنْ يَحُلَّ الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْكَبِيرَةَ فِي اجْتِمَاعٍ جَدِيدٍ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    Perhaps the president wants to solve the big problem in a new meeting.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `رُبَّمَا` (rubbamā): "Perhaps" or "maybe." This is a modal expression (Day 38).
        *   `يُرِيدُ` (yureedu): "He wants." This is a present tense verb (Day 44).
        *   `الرَّئِيسُ` (ar-ra'eecu): "The president." This noun (Day 48) is the subject of `يُرِيدُ`.
        *   `أَنْ يَحُلَّ` (an yaḥulla): "To solve." `أَنْ` (Day 38) makes the following present tense verb take a Fatha ending. `يَحُلُّ` (he solves - Day 50) becomes `يَحُلَّ`. This phrase acts as the object of `يُرِيدُ`.
        *   `الْمُشْكِلَةَ الْكَبِيرَةَ` (al-mushkilata al-kabeerata): "The big problem." `الْمُشْكِلَةَ` (the problem - Day 50) is the direct object of `يَحُلَّ`, taking Fatha. `الْكَبِيرَةَ` (the big - feminine from Day 3) is its adjective, matching in definiteness, gender, and case (Fatha).
        *   `فِي اجْتِمَاعٍ جَدِيدٍ` (fī ijtimā'in jadeedin): "In a new meeting." `فِي` is a preposition. `اجْتِمَاعٍ` (meeting - Day 50) is indefinite and takes Tanween Kasra. `جَدِيدٍ` (new - Day 3) is its adjective, matching in indefiniteness, gender (masculine), and case (Tanween Kasra).

4.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `إِنَّهُ يُرْسِلُ رِسَالَةً إِلَى الْوَزِيرِ عَنْ إِحْصَاءِ النَّقْصِ فِي الْمَاءِ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    Indeed, he sends a message to the minister about the statistics of the decrease in water.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `إِنَّهُ` (innahu): "Indeed, he" or "He is indeed." This is the emphasis particle `إِنَّ` (Day 37) combined with the attached pronoun `هُو` (he), taking a Fatha.
        *   `يُرْسِلُ` (yursilu): "He sends." This is a present tense verb (Day 55).
        *   `رِسَالَةً` (risālatan): "A message." This feminine noun (Day 55) is the direct object of `يُرْسِلُ`, taking Tanween Fatha because it's indefinite.
        *   `إِلَى الْوَزِيرِ` (ilā al-wazeeri): "To the minister." `إِلَى` (Day 10) is a preposition, so `الْوَزِيرِ` (minister - Day 48) takes Kasra.
        *   `عَنْ إِحْصَاءِ النَّقْصِ فِي الْمَاءِ` (an iḥṣā'i an-naqṣi fī al-mā'i): "About the statistics of the decrease in water." `عَنْ` (about - Day 48) is a preposition. `إِحْصَاءِ النَّقْصِ` (statistics of the decrease) is an Idafa. `إِحْصَاءِ` (statistics - Day 54) takes Kasra after `عَنْ`. `النَّقْصِ` (the decrease - Day 54) takes Kasra as the second noun in the Idafa. `فِي الْمَاءِ` (in the water) is a prepositional phrase, with `الْمَاءِ` (water - Day 56) taking Kasra after `فِي`.

5.  **Read this sentence aloud slowly:**
    `قَالَتِ الْمُدَرِّسَةُ أَنَّ الْقَانُونَ لِعَدْلِ الشَّعْبِ.`
    *(Pause for you to read and process)*

    Here's the translation:
    The teacher (f.) said that the law is for the justice of the people.

    *   *Let's break it down:*
        *   `قَالَتِ` (qālati): "She said." This is a past tense verb (Day 39) for feminine singular. It becomes `قَالَتِ` with a Kasra because it is followed by `الـ` (the definite article).
        *   `الْمُدَرِّسَةُ` (al-mudarrisatu): "The teacher (f.)." This feminine noun (Day 58) is the subject of `قَالَتِ`.
        *   `أَنَّ الْقَانُونَ` (anna al-qānūna): "That the law." `أَنَّ` (Day 39) is a particle that makes the following noun (its subject) take a Fatha. So `الْقَانُونَ` (the law - Day 51) has a Fatha.
        *   `لِعَدْلِ الشَّعْبِ` (li-'adli ash-sha'bi): "For the justice of the people." `لِـ` (Day 10) is a preposition. `عَدْلِ` (justice - Day 51) is the first noun of an Idafa, taking Kasra after `لِـ`. `الشَّعْبِ` (the people - Day 48) is the second noun of the Idafa, taking Kasra.
